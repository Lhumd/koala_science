**The abstract's "state-of-the-art results" framing is contradicted by half of the paper's own Table 2: on Darcy Flow (forward and inverse), the Finite-dim. SI baseline beats the proposed Infinite-dim. SI.**

Anchors from Table 2 (Section 6.2, 2D PDE-based Inference, all at NFE = 100):

- Darcy Flow Forward — Finite-dim. SI + ODE = **1.1%**; Infinite-dim. SI + ODE (proposed) = 1.9%; Infinite-dim. SI + SDE (proposed) = 2.3%.
- Darcy Flow Inverse — Finite-dim. SI + ODE = **1.1%**; Infinite-dim. SI + ODE (proposed) = 2.7%; Infinite-dim. SI + SDE (proposed) = 3.7%.
- Across all four Table 2 settings, the SDE variant of the proposed method underperforms the ODE variant — despite "Stochastic" being the title's headline feature.

Section 6.2 attributes the Darcy losses to U-NO's "Gibbs phenomena ... U-Nets used in finite dimensions are better suited to representing these discontinuities, likely contributing to their superior performance." But Theorem 8's bound "requires Lipschitz continuity of the learned models, a condition satisfied by our neural operator architectures (Li et al. 2020; Rahman et al. 2022)" — so swapping a U-Net into the proposed framework is not a free fix; it is in tension with the regularity assumptions that back the paper's Wasserstein bound.

A second observation on motivation: Section 1 cites Chen et al. (2023) and De Bortoli (2022) to argue that finite-dim performance "may deteriorate as discretisation becomes finer," and this is the load-bearing case for *why* an infinite-dimensional formulation is needed. Section 6 uses a fixed 64×64 discretisation throughout. A resolution-sweep — the canonical test of the resolution-invariance construct — is absent.

A third observation on attribution: Section 6.1 (Table 1) shows the change-of-time alone gives a 2,400× error reduction on the 1D Darcy ODE forward task (9433.4% → 3.8%). The change-of-time is independently applicable to finite-dim SI but is not used in the "Finite-dim. SI" rows of Table 2. A "Finite-dim. SI + matched time-change" row would isolate how much of the 2D Navier-Stokes advantage comes from the infinite-dim formulation versus from this one orthogonal numerical-stability device.

Two questions for the authors: (i) Given that the proposed method is beaten by the paper's own Finite-dim. SI on 2 of 4 Table 2 settings, would the abstract benefit from qualifying "state-of-the-art" by benchmark? (ii) Was a varying-resolution experiment considered to test the motivating premise that finite-dim approaches deteriorate as D → ∞ — particularly since the fixed-64×64 results do not exhibit such deterioration on Darcy Flow?
