# Phase A draft comment — b8458ab2

The headline efficacy claim — that residualizing dense embeddings reduces bias and RMSE in text-as-treatment CATE estimation (Tables 2–9, Fig. 7) — appears to rest on a simulation whose data-generating process is defined in terms of the residualized covariates themselves. App. E.1 (lines 1011–1018) constructs the synthetic outcome as y_i = μ_0(x̃_i) + τ(x̃_i)·T_{φ,i} + ε_i, with μ_0 and τ random compositions of {sin, tanh, x², x} on partitioned columns of X̃ — so the "true CATE" τ(x̃) is a function of X̃ by construction. Estimating it from X̃ then wins almost tautologically against estimating it from raw X. None of the three labelled datasets (civility, party affiliation, populism) is used as a real outcome Y in any CATE evaluation; only the synthetic outcome is used. Was a control DGP — one where y is a function of raw X (or of an independent latent) rather than of X̃ — also tested? Without it, the "raw vs residualized" comparison cannot distinguish "residualization mitigates bias in text-as-treatment problems" from "residualization helps when the simulator is parameterized in terms of residualized covariates."

A second concern: the IC score in Sec. 6.3 — described as "a rigorous metric for identifying well-behaved features" — has its intensity component I*(φ) defined via cosine similarity between activations and W_dec(φ), the very SAE column whose intervention is being evaluated (Eq. 6), while the coherence component J* uses an LLM judge instructed to "disregard profanities, aggressive language, sentiment, tone, or any other stylistic artifacts" (App. C). Neither factor measures whether the steered text actually contains the targeted concept as judged by humans or an independent classifier. The App. B.3 examples ("Insults" feature at α=0.2 generates no insults; "Marxism" at α=0.2 produces only generic working-class talk) seem to corroborate the gap — texts the IC score would rank well for use as treatments may not exhibit the concept the researcher actually wants to vary. Has the IC score been calibrated against human judgements of concept presence in steered text on any subset of features?
