The headline reasoning result — "JAEGER further achieves 99.2% accuracy on joint audio-visual reasoning in simulated multi-speaker physical environments" (Sec. 1) — is operationalized as a 3-way Left/Center/Right multi-choice classification, with sample QA from Tab. 1: *"Q: Can you tell which speaker the male voice originates from? A: Center."* In Tab. 2, the random baseline reaches 45.6% / 47.4% for Reasoning 1-speaker / 2-speaker, and the *"Ours w/o FOA Encoder"* ablation in Tab. 5 reaches 43.8% / 47.6% — statistically indistinguishable from random.

Two consequences worth a paragraph:

1. **The metric appears to discriminate "has spatial-audio information" vs "doesn't"**, with no intermediate-difficulty regime. With FOA-derived DoA + RGB-D-localized speaker centroids, the matching task reduces to angular comparison: estimate target azimuth/elevation, look up the nearest visible speaker, output its categorical label. The ~50-point gap between random (≈47%) and JAEGER (99.2%) is consistent with a near-perfect geometric pipeline; it is less direct evidence for "complex physical reasoning tasks" (Conclusion). A construct-spanning evaluation would include difficulty bins stratified by candidate angular separation, or distractors at similar azimuth, so the metric measures reasoning quality rather than information presence.

2. **Tab. 1 lists the 3-candidate Task E subset (5K samples) separately from the 2-candidate subset (24K), but Tab. 2 reports a single aggregate "Reasoning 2-speaker" number.** The 3-candidate variant is the more probative measurement (random = 33%, not 50%) but is buried in the average — and at ~17% of Task E samples, an aggregate 99.2% is consistent with 3-cand accuracy as low as ~95% or as high as ~100%; readers can't tell.

Two related framing points: (a) the Sec. 5.3 conclusion that *"standard monaural representations are fundamentally insufficient for spatial disambiguation"* is shown by removing FOA entirely; the harder-to-refute claim would compare to monaural-plus-visual systems with lip-motion attention rather than to a no-spatial-audio condition. (b) JAEGER's 13.13° vs BAT's 19.09° on Overlap DoA conflates 4-channel FOA with a model-architecture difference — Tab. 3 isolates Neural-vs-Classical IV cleanly, but a within-FOA non-Neural-IV baseline (e.g., a SELDnet on the same 4-channel input) would isolate the architecture choice from the channel-count advantage.

Could the authors add the 3-cand-only accuracy on Task E and a difficulty-stratified breakdown by candidate angular separation?
