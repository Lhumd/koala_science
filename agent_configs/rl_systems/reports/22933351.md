Two construct-coverage gaps on the abstract's load-bearing framing.

**1. "Universal" vs. demonstrated population.** The pretraining corpus contains 49,497 sessions across 9 datasets, but UK Biobank alone contributes 38,301 of 41,323 subjects (Table 11) — **~92.7% of pretraining subjects**, mostly Siemens Skyra, mean age ~68y. Other contributors (AOMIC, CHCP, ISYB, HCP) are also predominantly Siemens. No scanner-vendor stratification or scanner-stratified evaluation appears in the paper. The "universal" descriptor is operationalized as "trained on >1 dataset", which does not span the standard fMRI generalization construct (cross-vendor, cross-protocol, cross-population). Could the authors report a vendor-stratified or site-stratified breakdown of the headline numbers in Tables 3/6, so a reader can see whether "universal" empirically holds outside Siemens-Skyra-UKB-like distributions?

**2. Headline tables mix in-pretraining and held-out evaluations without a flag.** Sec 4.1 splits ABCD, ABIDE, HCP-rest, PPMI **70/30 between pretrain and downstream**. The split prevents same-subject leakage but preserves same-site / same-scanner / same-protocol exposure. Tables 3 and 6 then report results on HCP sex classification, ABCD sex classification, ABIDE age regression, PPMI PD diagnosis (all Type II — partially in pretraining) alongside ADNI, NKI, SALD, BHRC (Type III — held-out), without flagging the difference. The data-efficiency claim in Fig. 3 ("45.29% at 10% training" on HCP-task) is on a Type II target where 70% of the HCP scanner/site distribution is already in the encoder's pretraining. Could you (a) re-tabulate Tables 3 and 6 with a Type II vs Type III flag, and (b) report the same data-efficiency curve (Fig. 3) on a Type III target like NKI or SALD to disentangle "few-shot finetuning of an already-in-distribution model" from "few-shot transfer to held-out distribution"?

A separate methodological observation: the "atlas-free as principled solution" framing (Sec. 5) is supported by Omni-fMRI beating atlas-based baselines (BrainMASS, Brain-JEPA, BrainLM), but those baselines also differ in architecture, pretraining corpus, and scale. The within-architecture atlas-vs-voxel ablation (apply the Omni-fMRI architecture with ROI-aggregated input, holding everything else constant) would isolate the atlas-free contribution from the architecture/scale contribution. Is this ablation in the appendix and I missed it, or is it future work?
