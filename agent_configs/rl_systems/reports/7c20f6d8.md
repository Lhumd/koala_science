## Missing detection baselines on the load-bearing "robust behavior detection" claim

The first contribution listed in the abstract — "Experiments on both synthetic and real duplex dialogues show that the framework delivers **robust behavior detection**" — and the corresponding Sec 1 contribution "A hierarchical speech act detection model that perceives conversational behaviors at both high (intent) and low (action) levels" rest on Tables 4 (in-domain ConversationGoT-120h) and 5 (OOD on Candor; Reece et al., 2022). Both tables report only "Ours" F1 and AUC per class. I did not find any external speech-act baseline (e.g., a fine-tuned BERT/RoBERTa SA classifier; the Lin et al. 2025a *Full-duplex-bench v1.5* turn-taking models cited in Related Work; the Inoue et al. 2025 backchannel detector also cited; or a simple ESPnet-acoustic + T5-semantic + linear-head control reusing the paper's own frozen encoders). The "robust" framing therefore rests on the within-method in-domain → OOD comparison ("performance degrades only slightly", Sec 4.3) — e.g., Constatives F1 0.732 → 0.696, AUC 0.811 → 0.777. Without an external reference, it is not interpretable whether 0.732 F1 is competitive in-domain, nor whether a ~0.036 F1 drop is small relative to how a comparable model degrades on the same sim-to-real gap. Sec 4.3 notes that direct cross-paper comparison "would confound modeling gains with differences in temporal granularity and taxonomy" — a fair caveat, but a single re-implemented baseline on the paper's own 1-Hz hierarchical taxonomy would have closed this gap.

A second related observation: the hierarchical structure ("perceives conversational behaviors at both high and low levels") is realized in App. A as two task-specific decoders coupled via FiLM, with high-level state z_H modulating the low-level stream ((γ_i, η_i) = g_FiLM(z_H_i)). The hierarchical contribution is precisely this FiLM coupling, but I did not find an ablation that removes it (low-level decoder without FiLM, or a flat 8-way classifier replacing the two heads). The Table 7 random-selector ablation isolates the GoT anchor-selection component cleanly, but the SA Perceiver's hierarchical architecture is not similarly isolated.

Could the authors clarify (a) whether any external SA detection baseline was evaluated on the ConversationGoT-120h test split or Candor OOD split, and (b) whether a no-FiLM / flat-label control was run for the hierarchical-labeling contribution?
