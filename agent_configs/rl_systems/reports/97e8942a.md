The framework's primary safety claims (Theorem 4.5, gCRC for non-monotonic FDR on MedLFQA) are well-supported, with multi-axis reporting, three subclaim-scoring methods, and head-to-head comparisons to monotonized CRC and LTT. I want to ask about the secondary "can also improve performance" claim from the abstract — specifically the active-learning result in Fig. 5 ("Surprisingly, in some cases the risk-controlled data selection policy attains lower test MSE than the uncontrolled policy").

Two parts of the experimental setup look coupled in a way that makes attribution to CPC's calibration mechanism difficult to isolate:

1. The synthetic feasibility constraint (Appendix E.2, Eq. 73) is constructed by exponential tilting of rank of the *first principal component* of the covariate matrix: `p_i^{feasible} = σ(rank(z̃_i)/n; μ, s)` with `z̃_i = exp(γ · z_i^{norm})` where `z_i` is the PC1 projection. So infeasibility is by construction concentrated on low-PC1 records.

2. The initial training pool is sampled with the *same* exponential tilting toward high PC1 (Appendix E.3: "exponential tilting toward high PC1 values, using the same bias parameter γ from Section E.2"). So the GP's reliable-prediction region and the high-feasibility region are co-located on the PC1 axis by construction.

Under this setup, any constraint that biases acquisition toward high-PC1 (i.e., toward the safe policy's support) would simultaneously (a) reduce infeasibility and (b) keep queries near the training-data manifold where the GP is well-supported, lowering test MSE — independent of whether the constraint threshold β is conformally calibrated.

A fixed-β clipping baseline (CPC's clipping construction without the conformal calibration) appears to be missing from Fig. 5 — the comparators are "Uncontrolled" and the Lekeufack et al. (2024) Conformal Controller. Would the authors be willing to comment on whether the "lower test MSE under risk control" effect persists when β is fixed (uncalibrated) rather than chosen by gCRC, to isolate the calibration mechanism from the more generic "stay near training-data manifold" effect? A similar question applies to the moderate-α stabilization observed in Fig. 6 for sequence optimization with DPO.
