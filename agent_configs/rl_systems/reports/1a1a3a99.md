The headline claim that TIC-VLA "consistently outperforms prior VLA models" (Abstract; Table 1) is hard to attribute cleanly because the proposed contribution is two coupled novelties — the delayed semantic-control interface (Sec 3.2) and the latency-consistent training pipeline of Sec 3.3 (Stage 2: IL with delayed inference; Stage 3: RL with asynchronous guidance) — and the ablations isolate only the first.

Two specific gaps that may interest the authors:

1. **Baseline training-procedure parity.** Sec 4.1 states that NaVILA, Uni-NaVid, DualVLN, and NavDP "are fine-tuned on the same datasets to ensure a fair and controlled comparison." This documents data parity but not training-procedure parity. Were the baselines also trained with TIC-VLA's delay-injection imitation learning (Algorithm 1, where Δt ~ U(0, 10) seconds is injected into the demonstrations)? If not, the comparison conflates the architectural contribution with the training-pipeline contribution. The paper's own Table 1 reinforces this concern: TIC-VLA (Sync.) — the same architecture run synchronously — drops to SR=16.31, lower than NaVILA (28.24), DualVLN (30.59), and Uni-NaVid (22.35). The architecture in isolation underperforms the baselines; only the architecture+latency-aware-training combination wins. A row showing a baseline trained with the delay-injection pipeline would settle whether the gain travels with the training methodology rather than with the architecture.

2. **Isolating the training-pipeline contribution.** Table 1 ablates inference-time synchrony (Sync vs full) and the RL stage (no RL vs full RL), but does not include "TIC-VLA architecture trained with synchronous IL (no delay injection), deployed asynchronously." Without that row, the +30 SR gap between TIC-VLA-Sync and TIC-VLA-no-RL cannot be partitioned between the asynchronous interface and the delay-injection training itself.

These would also help readers interpret Fig 5's latency-robustness curve: is the post-RL flatness a property of the architecture or of the training distribution?
