**AffordanceGrasp-R1's ablation (Table 4) shows GRPO RL improves gIoU by only 0.3 points over a second SFT stage, leaving the specific contribution of RL exploration unsupported.**

The paper frames its post-training strategy as a "synergistic combination of SFT and RL with task-oriented reward functions, significantly enhancing the reasoning capability" (abstract). Table 4 permits a direct comparison:

- **CoT-SFT + SFT**: Main gIoU/cIoU = 65.2/64.2; Reasoning = 64.5/64.3
- **CoT-SFT + GRPO (RL)**: Main gIoU/cIoU = 65.5/65.0; Reasoning = 65.1/64.1

The gIoU difference between GRPO and a second SFT stage is **+0.3 on Main and +0.6 on Reasoning subsets** — well within the range attributable to evaluation stochasticity or training seed variance (neither is reported). "Significantly enhancing reasoning capability" (abstract) implies GRPO provides a qualitative advantage over continued SFT; the ablation does not support this reading.

A critical missing baseline: **CoT-SFT + SFT + SAM2-LoRA**. The final system's dominant gain appears to come from SAM2-LoRA fine-tuning, which takes CoT-SFT+GRPO from 64.5/65.9 (All gIoU/cIoU) to 66.9/67.1 — a +2.4/1.2 gain. Without testing whether CoT-SFT+SFT+SAM2-LoRA achieves the same final performance, it is unclear whether GRPO is necessary at all or whether two SFT stages plus SAM2-LoRA would match the headline numbers.

The real-world experiments (Table 3) compare only against AffordanceNet, while Table 1 lists seven other baselines (LISA, Segzero, VisionReasoner, etc.) that are not evaluated on the robot arm. Was there a practical reason these baselines were excluded from the real-world evaluation?
