# Phase A draft comment — RIGA-Fold (5c5508af)

The headline result for RIGA-Fold* (61.39% Recovery / 3.37 PPL on CATH 4.2 All, Table 1) couples four simultaneous additions on top of base RIGA-Fold: (a) ESM-IF as a frozen structural prior, (b) ESM-2 as a frozen sequence prior, (c) iterative recycling at T=3, and (d) cross-entropy summed across all recycling steps (Eq 10). The intro frames the second contribution as "an enhanced variant featuring a closed-loop inference strategy. By leveraging dual-stream feedback from ESM-2 and ESM-IF, it dynamically updates semantic priors", and Sec 4.2 attributes the 6.34pp jump from RIGA-Fold (55.05%) to RIGA-Fold* (61.39%) to "the Iterative Refinement mechanism, RIGA-Fold* effectively leverages the 'semantic intuition' from ESM-2".

The Table 3 ablation, however, isolates only components inside base RIGA-Fold — GAU vs GCN/GAT/QKV/AttMLP, Edge Update on/off, Context Bridge on/off — and reports only RIGA-Fold (55.05%) variants. It does not include any of the rows that would attribute the RIGA-Fold* gain: RIGA-Fold + ESM-2 only (T=1), RIGA-Fold + ESM-IF only (T=1), RIGA-Fold + dual-stream at T=1 (no recycling), or RIGA-Fold + recycling at T=3 (no PLMs). Without these, "ESM-2 corrects errors via recycling" is asserted rather than demonstrated.

A related framing question: the abstract's "surpassing leading methods like PiFold" implicitly compares against PiFold (51.66%, no PLM), giving a ~10pp gap. The closest PLM-using baseline LM-Design (55.65%) leaves a ~5.7pp gap, and Knowledge-Design (60.77%, no PLM) leaves ~0.6pp — within rounding of on-par. Could the authors add the PiFold+ESM-2 / ScFold+ESM-2 / RIGA-Fold-with-recycling-only controls, and clarify which component(s) of the dual-stream + recycling stack drive the gain?
