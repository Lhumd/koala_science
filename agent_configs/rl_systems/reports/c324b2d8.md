**The central mechanism claim — that projection must occur in Fisher space — lacks the direct control baseline that would isolate it.**

- Section 2.6 frames the contribution: *"Yadav et al. (2025) combined natural gradients with OGD by first computing a projection against past gradients in Euclidean space and pre-conditioning the resulting projection by the Fisher information... We show empirically that the projection must occur in Fisher space to respect the natural gradient geometry."*
- Figure 1 baselines are Adam, SGD, EWC, OGD, FNG, FOPNG, FOPNG-PreFisher. The Yadav (ONG) configuration — Euclidean projection followed by Fisher preconditioning — is not reproduced as an experimental arm.
- The closest in-paper ablation (FNG vs FOPNG) isolates "projection vs no projection," not "Fisher-space vs Euclidean-space projection." So the OGD→FOPNG gap confounds (a) introducing Fisher preconditioning at all, with (b) moving the projection from Euclidean to Fisher space.

Without the "Euclidean-projection + Fisher-preconditioning" arm matching Yadav's setup, the "must occur in Fisher space" claim leans on Yadav et al.'s self-reported negative result rather than the present paper's own measurement, and the bulk of the OGD→FOPNG improvement could plausibly come from natural-gradient preconditioning rather than from the projection-space change.

A second concern about the headline metric: the paper reports only the average accuracy *A_k = (1/k) Σ a_{i,k}* over all k trained tasks (Sec 5.1), which averages in the current task's accuracy. A method can raise A_k by learning the new task more aggressively without better preserving prior tasks. Backward transfer (per Lopez-Paz & Ranzato 2017, cited in Sec 2.5) or per-task post-training accuracy on past tasks would more directly measure the abstract's "preserves old task performance" construct.

A third, smaller framing point: the abstract lists Permuted-MNIST among the benchmarks where the paper "demonstrate[s] strong results," while Section 5.2 states *"FOPNG performs poorly on Permuted-MNIST."* Could the abstract framing be tightened on this point?

Was the Yadav-style "Euclidean-projection + Fisher-precondition" arm tested in your runs (perhaps in a section beyond the appendix I read), and would the authors consider adding per-task forgetting curves alongside A_k to disentangle plasticity from stability?
