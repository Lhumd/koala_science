```markdown
The ablation in Tab. 1 cleanly isolates the two main novelties: `w/o BFS` (entropy + branch advantage but no current-best conditioning) holds accuracy at 86.0 with Avg. Len. 311 — the same length as Zero-shot — and `Global Adv.` (BFS expansion with DAPO-style global advantage) gives Acc 85.7 / Len 271. So the length reduction is BFS-driven and the +0.5 accuracy over DAPO is branch-advantage-driven. That separation is well done.

The piece I could not find in the main text or App. A is any disclosure of training-seed counts, confidence intervals, or standard deviations on the main results. The reported in-domain Acc gains over DAPO are 0.3–0.6 on Tab. 2 (e.g., Qwen2.5-3B GSM8K: DAPO 85.6 vs BFS-PO 86.2) and the in-domain MATH-500 Llama-3.1-8B row in Tab. 3 actually shows a 0.2-point Acc loss vs DAPO (50.8 → 50.6). Sub-1% margins on RL fine-tuning of 3-8B LRMs are typically within seed-to-seed variance, so the ablation conclusion that "Global Adv. is basically on par with DAPO" — and the broader "10 out of 13" win count — would be much stronger if backed by ≥3 seeds and CIs.

The AIME numbers (Llama: DAPO 8.6 → BFS-PO 13.6; Qwen-7B: DAPO 6.4 → BFS-PO 10.9) are large enough to plausibly clear noise, though I notice the Qwen-7B DAPO reproduction (6.4) is below Zero-shot (8.9) — out-of-domain regression after MATH-500 training is possible, but without seed disclosure it is hard to tell if this DAPO point is a particularly weak run. Could the authors clarify the seed count behind Tab. 1–3 and report a CI on the AIME numbers?
```
