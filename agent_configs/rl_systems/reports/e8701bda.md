**The "≥10× speedup" claim is sampler-only on a different hardware tier, while the paper's own pipeline still relies on SUAVE for verification.**

- App. G reports MixeDiT-MaskeDiT at **195.6 s for 100 samples on an A100-PCIE-40GB GPU**, "with the majority of time devoted to loading the model checkpoints," versus SUAVE at **2523 s on an Intel Xeon E5-2680 v4 (14 cores, CPU-only, no MPI)."
- Sec. 5.3.1 verifies posterior predictive performance by running the generated designs *through SUAVE itself* — i.e., the operational pipeline is "diffusion sampler → SUAVE re-simulation," not "diffusion replaces SUAVE."
- The companion claim that the "speedup is more significant for larger batch sizes" is asserted in Sec. 5.3.1 with no batch-vs-time curve or table.

The abstract frames the contribution as "significantly accelerating design generation," which a reader naturally interprets as pipeline-level acceleration. The benchmark instead measures one component of one stage, on heterogeneous hardware, with model-load overhead included — and the paper notes that overhead dominates the wall-clock. Could the authors report (a) MixeDiT-MaskeDiT on the same Xeon CPU, (b) sampler kernel time with model-load amortized, and (c) the batch-size scaling curve they reference?

A related, smaller framing question on Sec. 5.2: the claim that "MaskeDiT maintains a similarly low MMD … with only a modest tradeoff in per-topology fidelity" reads as parity, but Tab. 3 (App. H) shows NLE wins on MMD (0.0021 vs 0.0040), joint C2ST (0.7445 vs 0.7773), mean marginal C2ST (0.5961 vs 0.6142), median marginal C2ST (0.5614 vs 0.5973), and min marginal C2ST (0.4963 vs 0.5065) — five of six reported metrics — and App. H confirms the MMD gap is statistically significant at the 5% level. The unification-across-topologies value is real, but the reported comparison is in-distribution per-topology — does MaskeDiT also generalize to held-out topologies, where the unification benefit would be most concrete?
