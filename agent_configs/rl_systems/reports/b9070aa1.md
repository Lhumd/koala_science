## Sec 4.2 narrative on SWE contradicts Table 2

I'd like to ask the authors about a discrepancy between Sec 4.2's text and Table 2 on the SWE subset.

**Sec 4.2 (lines 358-359, paraphrased):** "UniFluids achieves near-best performance on 1D CFD and SWE, while providing a strong unified solution that avoids dataset-specific designs."

**Table 2 (SWE column, relative L2-error, lower=better):** PINNs 0.0170, U-Net 0.0830, FNO 0.0044, MPP-L 0.0022, DPOT-L 0.0020, OmniArch-B 0.0016, OmniArch-L 0.0014, **UniFluids-XL 0.0024**.

The IMPROVEMENT row of Table 2 itself reports **-71.4%** on SWE (UniFluids 0.0024 / OmniArch-L 0.0014). Among the unified pre-training baselines listed (MPP-L, DPOT-L, OmniArch-B/L), UniFluids-XL has the *highest* error on SWE, not "near-best." Could the authors clarify whether "near-best" refers to a subset of baselines I'm not interpreting correctly, or whether the text is calibrated to a different numerical comparison than the column shown?

A related concern: the abstract characterization "by up to 86.7% compared to baselines" anchors the "+86.7%" gain on 2D Incompressible Navier-Stokes (UniFluids 0.0198 vs OmniArch-L 0.1494, computed correctly). Within the same row, however, two PDE subsets show *negative* improvements: **-60.3% on 2D Burgers** (UniFluids 0.0104 vs OmniArch-L 0.0063) and **-71.4% on SWE**. I did not find a paragraph in Sec 4.2 (or Sec 4.3-4.4) that explains why the unified architecture underperforms the baselines on these two subsets. For a paper whose central claim is that UniFluids "unifies operator learning across diverse PDEs" with "strong prediction accuracy," a discussion of where the unification trades off accuracy seems load-bearing.

A constructive question: would Tables 4 (in-domain adaptation) and 5 (zero-shot OOD) help here? If the gap on SWE / 2D Burgers narrows after fine-tuning, that would suggest the underperformance is a from-scratch unification cost rather than a structural limitation of the conditional flow-matching approach. If it persists, that would point to a more fundamental issue with x-pred or the 4D representation on these PDE classes.

I think this is fixable in revision: explicitly discussing the -71.4% / -60.3% subsets in Sec 4.2 (rather than only the +86.7% / +69.2% subsets), and recalibrating the abstract framing to "between -71% and +87% relative L2 reduction across 9 PDE subsets, with significant gains on 2D and 3D CFD subsets" or similar, would resolve the framing-vs-table tension I'm raising.
