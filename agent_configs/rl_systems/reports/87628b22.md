The paper frames SoMA as a "force-driven" simulator that learns "physically meaningful dynamics" (§1, §4.2.2) and reports a "20% improvement" headline (Abstract). I would appreciate clarification on two construct-vs-evaluation gaps.

**1. The evaluation never measures any physical quantity.** §5.1 lists PSNR, SSIM, LPIPS for RGB and Abs Rel + RMSE for depth — all appearance metrics. The image loss (Eq. 12) and the evaluation are both restricted to the visible mask region (Appendix B.4: "All image metrics are computed...within the object regions only"). The momentum-consistency term L_mom (Eq. 13) is a *training* regularizer, not an evaluation diagnostic. Could the authors report, even in supplementary, any direct physical-fidelity measurement: end-effector contact-onset timing error, penetration depth between robot proxy and reconstructed object, area/volume conservation for the cloth/T-shirt, or per-frame drift curves? Without this, a simulator that produces visually plausible but physically inconsistent occluded-region motion is indistinguishable from one that does not, and the "force-driven" framing is supported only by appearance metrics.

**2. The ablation does not isolate the force-driven dynamics.** Tab. 3 ablates the multi-resolution training schedule (w/o MRT) and the blended-supervision loss (Img-Only) on cloth, but does not include a "GausSim with SoMA's training stack (multi-resolution + blended supervision + 4× super-resolution preprocessing)" baseline. As reported, the gain over GausSim conflates (a) force-conditioning, (b) hierarchical GS clustering with momentum propagation, (c) the multi-resolution schedule, (d) the masked/blended supervision, and (e) the 4× image super-resolution preprocessing (Appendix B.2). Without an A-only (force-conditioning, otherwise GausSim-equivalent) row, attributing the headline to the force-driven dynamics is unsupported by the ablation table.

A related question: §5.1 states "No existing method directly supports robot-manipulated scenes with complex interactions and occlusions" as the rationale for restricting baselines to PhysTwin and GausSim, yet §2 cites MPM (Jiang 2016), Isaac Sim (NVIDIA 2023), and DiffTaichi (Hu 2019) as physics-based simulators applicable to deformables. Was an MPM/Isaac-Sim baseline with hand-set physical parameters considered for at least the cloth or rope task, where the underlying physics is well-understood?
