The empirical study is comprehensive (Tables 1–10 spanning standard, in-the-wild, unseen-generator, perturbation, recapture, reconstruction, editing, and PEFT-fine-tuning protocols). Two calibration points on the abstract phrasing.

1. **"Boosting accuracy by striking margins of over 30%."** Table 2 (in-the-wild) shows DINOv3-Linear average 0.940 vs DDA 0.850 — a ~10% gap to the strongest specialized baseline — and 0.940 vs CNNSpot 0.571 / NPR 0.560 / Effort 0.553 — ~37–39% gaps to legacy CNN-based detectors. Sec. 3.3 itself reports the distinction honestly ("surpassing DDA by nearly 10% and traditional detectors by over 30%"). The abstract collapses these to the larger figure; readers may interpret "over 30%" as a SOTA-vs-VFM gap rather than a legacy-detector-vs-VFM gap.

2. **"Emergent property driven by the massive scale of pre-training data containing synthetic content."** The DINOv3-Web vs DINOv3-Sat counterfactual (Table 6) varies three axes simultaneously: pre-training domain (web vs satellite imagery), pre-training scale (1.6B vs 493M, ~3.3×), and synthetic content presence. Attributing the GenImage gap (0.965 vs 0.706) specifically to synthetic exposure requires controlling for the first two. Sec. 6 acknowledges "fully controlled pre-training ablations are beyond the scope of this work" and frames the conclusion as "indirectly suggest." The abstract's causal language ("driven by," "trace the source of this capability") is more declarative than the body's hedged interpretation.

The Table 5 text-image similarity probing is a stronger, more direct piece of evidence on the VLM side (literally retrieving "AI generated" tokens for synthetic images) and substantially mitigates the confounded-counterfactual concern for VLMs. Mechanism (II) for SSL still relies primarily on Table 6.

Neither concern undermines the main empirical finding (modern VFM linear probes are highly competitive on in-the-wild AIGI detection); both are calibration on the abstract framing.
