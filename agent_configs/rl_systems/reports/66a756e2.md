The paper's title and headline contribution are *Instance-aware Separation Guidance* (ISG), with SGU framed as a stabilizer. But the Tab.2 ablation does not isolate ISG. The three rows shown are `w/o ISG, w/o SGU` (SSR 0.697, LCD 0.627), `w/ ISG, w/o SGU` (SSR 0.674, LCD 0.623), and `w/ ISG, w/ SGU` (SSR 0.809, LCD 0.598). The fourth cell — `w/o ISG, w/ SGU` — is not reported.

Two consequences:

1. **ISG alone underperforms the no-guidance baseline on SSR**: 0.674 < 0.697. The only setting where ISG appears to "help" the headline instance-distinctiveness metric is the joint condition, where SGU may be doing the load-bearing work. Without the missing `w/o ISG, w/ SGU` cell, the attribution to ISG (the named title contribution) is unsupported. Could the authors add this ablation cell, and report whether SGU applied to raw cross-attention (no ISG anchoring) recovers most of the SSR gain?

2. **SSR is a count-match metric**: Eq. 10 defines `SSR = min(N_pred, N_gt) / max(N_pred, N_gt)`. It scores 1.0 whenever the instance count is right, regardless of whether predicted instances correspond to GT ones in identity, position, or shape. As the only "instance distinctiveness" metric in Tab.1, it does not directly evidence "distinct local instances" — only "right-cardinality outputs". The CD-O / FS-O metrics depend on a predicted-to-GT instance matching procedure that the paper does not specify in App. A; clarifying the matching (Hungarian on centroids? nearest-neighbor?) would help readers interpret the ~3% FS-O gap over MIDI.

Two related framing points worth a sentence: (a) the abstract describes the input as "a single image", but Sec. 5.1 reveals instance masks come from Grounded-SAM, so the pipeline consumes (image, masks) and is sensitive to mask quality, which is not ablated; (b) four hyperparameters (l ≤ 4, t ≤ 15, α = 0.1, σ = 1.5) are selected via sweeps on the same evaluation datasets used for Tab.1, with the chosen values being best-on-eval-set in Tabs. 3, 4, 6, 7 — this is a soft form of training and somewhat undermines the strict "training-free" comparison against MIDI.
