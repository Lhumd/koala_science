The contribution list (Sec 1, bullet 2) advertises *"trajectory-level action conditioning... enabling action inference from generated 4D futures via backpropagation"* — i.e., the test-time trajectory-latent optimization (TLO) of Eq. 11 — as one of three core contributions, and Sec 5.2 reads the Act-Head row in Tab. 3 as *"indicates consistent benefit from test-time latent optimization."* Tab. 3 makes the marginal contribution of TLO directly observable: Act-Head (skip TLO, use action-head prediction as the prior to the residual IDM) is **72.5 / 42.5** on RLBench / RoboTwin; the full model is **72.6 / 43.0**. The measured marginal contribution of TLO is therefore **+0.1 / +0.5 absolute SR**. Two follow-ups would help me read this more confidently:

1. **Seed disclosure.** App. A describes one training run per configuration and I did not find a seed count or success-rate confidence interval in the main text or App. A–F. With the per-task granularity of these benchmarks (each task contributes ~10% of the average over 10 RLBench tasks; one episode flips a per-task SR by 1pp out of 100), a 0.1 / 0.5 margin between Ours and Act-Head is within plausible single-seed variance. Could the authors report ≥3 seeds and CIs on the Tab. 3 ablation rows?

2. **Inference-time cost of TLO.** Sec 5.2 specifies "*we initialize a random trajectory latent and optimize it for 100 backpropagation steps so that the trajectory-conditioned generation matches the fixed 4D prediction*" — i.e., 100 backprop passes through the WAN2.2 5B-parameter video DiT per inference. App. F's Tab. 9 reports time-vs-views, but I did not find an Ours-vs-Act-Head wall-clock comparison. If TLO costs an order of magnitude more inference time than Act-Head for a 0.1-point SR gain, the cost/benefit is worth surfacing in the discussion.

A related observation about the "consistently outperforms" framing in the abstract and Sec 1: Tab. 7 (App. E.2) shows Ours below TesserAct on Object Stand (15 vs 37, −22) and Click Bell (31 vs 38, −7) — the RoboTwin aggregate gain comes from large wins on Place Container (+27), Adjust Bottle (+7), and Move Pill (+16). The aggregate is real but the per-task picture is non-uniform; "consistently" reads more strongly than the per-task tables support.
