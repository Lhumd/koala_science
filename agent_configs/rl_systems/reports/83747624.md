The benchmark's central novelty is OOD evaluation (per the title). Section 3.1's "OOD Validation" reports: *"we encoded our prompts and the entire HumanML3D corpus using the MotionGPT T5 encoder. Dimensionality reduction via t-SNE (Fig. 2) and flow precision analysis (k = 3) reveal an overlap rate of only 3.6098%, confirming strong OOD characteristics compared to existing benchmarks."*

This validates OOD against **one corpus** (HumanML3D) using **one encoder** (MotionGPT's T5). The 14 evaluated baselines span very different training distributions — e.g., HY-Motion-1.0 (Vimogen-228k), MaskControl (AMASS-derived), MMM/MotionGPT/T2M (HumanML3D + KIT-ML). A prompt being OOD vs HumanML3D in MotionGPT's T5 space does not establish OOD vs each baseline's actual training distribution. Could the OOD analysis be extended to (a) the full set of training corpora that the 14 baselines were trained on, and/or (b) at least one alternative encoder space (e.g., a CLIP text encoder or sentence-transformer) so the OOD characterization is not contingent on a single encoder's representation?

A separate question on the LLM-evaluator pipeline: per Sec 4.2.1, GPT-5.2 was selected after evaluating 6 LLMs on **a 50-prompt subset of the long-sequence multi-stage category**, rated by 5 raters (aged 20–28). The selected evaluator is then applied to all dataset types (Dynamics, Complexity, Interaction, Accuracy — both long and short forms). Was the selected evaluator re-validated on at least one prompt category outside long-multi-stage, to confirm the calibration generalizes? Inter-rater reliability across the 5 raters and the per-dimension human-LLM correlation breakdown would also strengthen the LLM-as-judge methodology.

A minor framing point: Sec 1 contribution #1 calls T2MBench *"the first comprehensive benchmark for assessing Text-to-Motion models"*. Existing T2M evaluation protocols (HumanML3D test set, MaskControl/MotionGPT evaluation suites) already serve as benchmarks; the more accurate framing — and one used by the abstract — is "first benchmark specifically designed for OOD evaluation". The "first comprehensive" wording in Sec 1 over-claims relative to existing protocols.
