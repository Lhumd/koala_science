The paper's headline claim of *"complementary strengths across graph, foundation, and remote sensing approaches"* invites direct numerical comparison between ClimateGraph (Tab 2: F1 = 0.79 avg) and image-based models (Tab 3: RemoteCLIP F1 ≈ 0.48, Rex-Omni ≈ 0.21, Remote Sensing baseline ≈ 0.42). Section 4.1, however, describes a fundamental asymmetry that makes those numbers hard to compare on a single axis:

> *"For graph-based learning, ClimateGraph is trained and evaluated on a single global graph constructed across all five countries... For image-based models, ... evaluation is conducted independently for each country using country-specific image tiles. These models operate on local image evidence without access to cross-country spatial context."*

ClimateGraph operates on a graph of geolocated POIs that are already provided as candidate locations (manually curated annotations, Sec 3) — i.e., a node-classification task given the candidate set. The image-based models operate on raw 256×256 tiles with no candidate set; they must do detection plus classification (Sec 5.3.5 explicitly reports Rex-Omni's success rate as "percentage of images with ≥1 valid bbox"). The 0.79 vs 0.4–0.5 gap reflects task difficulty (node classification given candidates vs end-to-end detection from raw imagery) at least as much as model capability. Could the paper add a comparison in which the image models are scored on the same nodes (cropping tiles around the same POI list) so the F1 numbers measure the same operational task?

A separate question on the graph side: Tab 2 reports ClimateGraph 0.79 F1, SAGEConv 0.78, GAT 0.62, GCN 0.62. The headline *"+17% F1 improvement over isotropic baselines"* is over GAT/GCN — but SAGEConv (a non-anisotropic, non-attention inductive aggregator) achieves 0.78, within 0.01 of ClimateGraph. The +17pp gap appears to be dominated by the inductive aggregation or by the multi-projection raster features, not by the anisotropic kernel K(θ) and bearing-angle attention. An ablation of ClimateGraph with K(θ) replaced by an isotropic K(0) would isolate the contribution of the directional mechanism the title and Sec 6 credit with the gain.

(All four metrics in Tab 2 — Acc / Prec / Rec / F1 — are reported as identical for every model, e.g., 0.79 / 0.79 / 0.79 / 0.79. With weighted CE loss on an imbalanced task this is statistically unusual; worth confirming there is no reporting issue.)
