The abstract claims DeltaKV maintains *"near-lossless accuracy on LongBench, SCBench, and AIME"* (conjunctive across all three benchmarks). The paper's own tables contradict this on two of the three.

**SCBench R.KV (Table 2, Llama-3.1-8B):** Full Cache = 79.0, OmniKV = 72.2, **DeltaKV = 58.0** — a 21-point absolute drop from Full Cache (26.6% relative degradation). On Qwen2.5-7B: Full = 70.4, **DeltaKV = 59.4** (15.6% relative). Sec 5.2 acknowledges this as "slightly larger drops on this task" attributed to SSID-like distribution mismatch — but a 21-point absolute drop on a key retrieval subtask is not "near-lossless" by the conventional usage in the KV-compression literature.

**AIME (Table 3, DeepSeek-Qwen-7B):** Full = 50.0, OmniKV = 46.7, **DeltaKV = 43.3** — a 6.7-point drop from Full Cache (13.4% relative), and 3.4 points behind the training-free OmniKV baseline. The body softens to "remains effective on mathematical reasoning benchmarks", which matches the data better than the abstract's framing.

**LongBench (Table 1)** is genuinely near-lossless across all three backbones (Δ ≤ 0.7). The abstract's framing is accurate for one of three benchmarks. Could it be tightened — e.g., "near-lossless on LongBench, with measurable degradation on retrieval-heavy SCBench subtasks and on AIME reasoning"?

A separate question on the throughput side: the *"up to 2× throughput improvement over vLLM"* headline is supported at 512k (Sparse-vLLM DeltaKV† BS=4 at 67.7 t/s vs vLLM Full Attn BS=2 at 33.1 t/s, Table 4). This comparison uses **different batch sizes** — Sparse-vLLM at BS=4, vLLM at BS=2. At equal BS=8 at 128k, the ratio is 148.4 / 143.2 = 1.04×. At equal BS=4 at 256k, the ratio is 115.9 / 70.2 = 1.65× (honest). What is the per-batch throughput ratio at 512k at the same BS — i.e., how much of the 2× is per-batch compute speedup vs batch-fitting capacity?
