The abstract claims dnaHNet "outperforms leading architectures" and Section 2.1 enumerates the field of genomic foundation models — DNABERT, DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Evo, Evo 2, and megaDNA. Yet Section 4.2 ("Models and Baselines") narrows the actual comparison set to exactly two: StripedHyena2 (described as the architecture *underlying* Evo 2, but not Evo 2 itself) and an optimized decoder-only Transformer.

Tables 5 and 6, which carry the load-bearing zero-shot SOTA claim, contain no entry for DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Caduceus, Evo, or Evo 2. The headline number is Spearman ρ = 0.327 on MaveDB versus SH2 at 0.311 and Transformer at 0.156 — a Transformer score less than half of SH2's, which raises the question of how tuned that baseline actually is. The "state-of-the-art zero-shot performance on protein variant effect prediction" framing in Section 1 is operationalized as "best of two baselines we ran ourselves" rather than benchmarked against any published prior-FM number on the same MaveDB partition.

This matters most because Evo 2 is explicitly named as a prior-FM target, exists at 7B parameters on a much broader (eukaryotic + prokaryotic) corpus, and presumably has published MaveDB-style fitness scores; the dnaHNet ceiling is 1B and prokaryotic-only.

Two questions for the authors:
1. Could Tables 5 and 6 be extended with at least the published zero-shot Spearman/AUROC numbers from DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Evo, and Evo 2 on the same MaveDB partition (or a clearly noted equivalent), so the "state-of-the-art" framing is anchored against the field rather than against two in-house baselines?
2. The Transformer baseline's 0.156 Spearman on MaveDB is markedly weaker than SH2's 0.311 at matched FLOPs. Was per-architecture hyperparameter tuning performed for the Transformer at each compute budget, or are the baselines using a single shared HP set?

The architectural contribution and the wall-clock efficiency story (Fig 7) are well-executed. The concern is purely about the comparison set behind the SOTA framing.
