The headline +4.7% gain on DeepScaler-5K (Abstract; Tab. 1) decomposes into two contributions in the same table. P²O Self-Ref, which uses the policy model itself (Qwen3-4B) as the GEPA mutation operator, reaches 62.4 vs GRPO's 60.5 — a +1.9 gap. P²O Teacher-Ref, which substitutes Kimi-K2 (Sec 4.1) as the mutation operator, reaches 65.2 — an additional +2.8 over Self-Ref. About 60% of the headline gain is therefore attributable to access to a stronger external model in the prompt-evolution step rather than to the joint policy + prompt optimization mechanism the abstract credits.

A directly comparable baseline that would isolate the joint-optimization contribution from the teacher-distillation contribution is missing: Kimi-K2-only SFT on the same hard samples — i.e., take the trajectories Kimi-K2 generates while serving as the GEPA mutation operator and use them as straightforward supervised data, without GEPA and without the two-phase loop. If GEPA's evolutionary loop is doing meaningful work over and above "give the policy access to Kimi-K2 trajectories on hard samples," that comparison would surface it. As Tab. 2 stands, the ablations test the context-distillation mechanism (cleanly) and group prompt diversity, but neither isolates the role of GEPA-specifically (versus any non-trivial external CoT source).

The DeepMath-5K row is consistent with this teacher-confound interpretation: Self-Ref dominates Teacher-Ref (61.7 vs 57.9), which the paper attributes to misalignment between Kimi-K2 priors and the policy's reasoning style (Sec 4.2). When the external teacher is unhelpful, the joint-optimization recipe loses ground.

Two related observations: (i) the AIME24/AIME25 headline gains (+12.9, +11.7) are computed on 30-problem benchmarks averaged over 16 rollouts; Tab. 1 reports no standard deviations or seed counts, making it hard to distinguish "consistent improvement" from "favorable seed allocation." (ii) Fig. 3's x-axis counts policy-update samples; the Kimi-K2 reflection calls and dev-set evaluations consumed by GEPA (Alg. 3) are not budgeted into any matched-compute axis. Reporting per-dataset seed variance and a Kimi-K2-SFT baseline at matched compute would tighten the joint-optimization claim considerably.
