The empirical section operationalizes "demonstrate its effectiveness on standard IM benchmarks" (abstract) as (a) trajectory snapshots showing the iCEM-MPC controller swings up the single pendulum, cart-pole, and double pendulum (Fig. 2a–c), and (b) CIP value curves rising over iCEM iterations (Fig. 2d). I'd appreciate the authors' view on a few specific gaps in linking that evidence to the "effectiveness" claim.

1. **CIP rising is the optimization objective.** Algorithm 1, line 5 selects elite action sequences by `J_i = H_KS^{ol} - H_KS^{cl}`, then iCEM fits a Gaussian to the elites. That CIP scores rise across iCEM iterations (Fig. 2d) is tautological with respect to the planner working as intended; it does not by itself argue CIP is a useful IM signal versus any alternative.

2. **No comparison to the prior IM methods the paper cites.** Empowerment (Klyubin 2005, Salge 2014, Tiomkin 2024), DIAYN (Eysenbach 2019), MaxInfoRL (Bhavya 2025), and Active Inference (Parr 2022) are introduced in Sec. 2 and cited as predecessors evaluated on similar pendulum systems. None of them appear in Fig. 2 under the same iCEM-MPC controller. Without even one head-to-head, "effectiveness on standard IM benchmarks" — relative to what?

3. **Counter-hypothesis for the off-vertical double-pendulum stabilization.** Sec. 5.2 reads stabilization "at a slight angle rather than the perfectly vertical configuration" as evidence of edge-of-chaos seeking. A simpler chaos quantity — e.g., a single open-loop positive-Lyapunov-exponent estimate — optimized with the same iCEM-MPC might produce qualitatively identical trajectories. Was that comparison run, or is the demonstrated behavior consistent with "iCEM-MPC + any chaos quantity" rather than CIP specifically?

I note Sec. 6.5 explicitly defers learning-based and large-scale experiments to future work, scoping the current empirical content modestly. Given that scoping, would the authors prefer to characterize the present experiments as a proof-of-concept that the iCEM planner can drive these systems to high-CIP states, rather than a benchmark-style "effectiveness" claim against the prior IM signals the paper cites?
