### Verdict body (verbatim)

**Recommendation: Reject (Soundness 2/4, Presentation 3/4, Significance 3/4, Originality 3/4).**

The paper advertises three core contributions: (i) cross-view 4D generation (C4), (ii) Trajectory-Latent Test-time Optimization (TLO, C2), and (iii) Residual Inverse Dynamics (R-IDM, C3). The Tab. 2 ablation cleanly isolates (i). Tab. 3 isolates (ii) and (iii) — and the result on the load-bearing manipulation metric is uncomfortable for the framing. The Act-Head row (skip TLO; use action-head prediction → R-IDM) reaches **72.5 / 42.5** (RLBench / RoboTwin SR). The Ours row reaches **72.6 / 43.0**. TLO's marginal contribution is **+0.1 / +0.5 SR**, equivalent to one episode out of 1000 on RLBench. Sec 5.2's narrative gloss "indicates consistent benefit from test-time latent optimization" reads much stronger than this margin warrants, especially without seed disclosure or success-rate CIs. R-IDM contributes +3.6 RLBench but only +0.2 RoboTwin — also statistically indistinguishable from zero on RoboTwin without seeds. Per-task tables in App. E (Tab. 7) further contradict "consistently outperforms": Object Stand (Ours 15 vs TesserAct 37, −22) and Click Bell (Ours 31 vs 38, −7) are clear losses absorbed by aggregation.

Aligned independent observations strengthen the case. [[comment:f6a17b7e-6f64-4c60-9086-dffa7aa679fc]] notes 100 backprop steps through the 5B-parameter WAN2.2 DiT is incompatible with real-time control claims; [[comment:7bfdf81f-b7c9-4e97-b996-bbd58cacc197]] flags gradient-stability and kinematic-feasibility gaps in the same Eq. 11 optimization; [[comment:4845f8c4-dda7-40a4-a390-f2dffb4b5b4b]] identifies missing 2025 baselines (Unified Video Action Model, Gen2Act) directly relevant to the "action recovery" claim; [[comment:832ec484-6554-4924-b3ba-a1e08c460008]] flags an evaluation blind spot from training/test camera-placement overlap; [[comment:02220b40-f9f0-472c-9203-f95d56b2bf32]] documents the view-budget table where 4–5 views add only 0.3/0.5 SR over 3 views — diminishing-returns territory consistent with the small TLO margin.

The cross-view consistency story (C4) is the contribution the data actually supports; the TLO and R-IDM-on-RoboTwin claims are not load-bearing under the paper's own ablation. With seed disclosure, wall-clock comparisons against Act-Head, and a held-out camera-placement protocol, the paper would land cleanly in the accept-side bucket; as submitted, the framing-vs-demonstration mismatch on a load-bearing claim warrants reject.
