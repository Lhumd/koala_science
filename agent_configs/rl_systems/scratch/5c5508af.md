Paper: "RIGA-Fold: A General Framework for Protein Inverse Folding via Recurrent Interaction and Geometric Awareness"

Supported by Table 1 (RIGA-Fold = 55.05% Recovery / 4.13 PPL on CATH 4.2 All; vs PiFold 51.66%/4.55, ScFold 52.22%/4.61).

Supported by Table 1 (RIGA-Fold* = 61.39% / 3.37 on CATH 4.2 All; closest PLM-using baseline LM-Design = 55.65% / 4.05; closest non-PLM baseline Knowledge-Design = 60.77% / 3.46) and Table 2 (TS50 65.74%/2.95, TS500 70.15%/2.77).

Implicitly attributes the 6.34pp gap (RIGA-Fold* 61.39 minus RIGA-Fold 55.05) to ESM-IF + ESM-2 + recycling collectively.

5. **C5 (Robustness across protein lengths from Global Context Bridge + Recycling).** Sec 4.3: "We attribute this robustness to two factors: the Global Context Bridge, which effectively shortcuts long-range dependencies, and the Recycling strategy, which iteratively corrects errors that typically accumulate in longer chains."

6. **C6 (Structural validity of generated sequences).** Sec 4.6 + Fig 4: "RIGA-Fold* consistently achieves higher sequence recovery and lower RMSD compared to the base model, particularly on complex all-β topologies." Supported by 3 hand-picked AlphaFold3 refolds (1a6m, 2giy, 2ffy) with RMSD ≤ 0.412.

## Construct inventory

- **C1 — Construct:** "highly competitive" geometric framework.
  - **Operational metric:** Perplexity, Recovery % on CATH 4.2 (Table 1), TS50/TS500 (Table 2).
  - **Unmeasured aspect:** No seed count, no confidence intervals, no statistical significance tests on Recovery deltas. The base RIGA-Fold (55.05%) vs Knowledge-Design (60.77%) gap is ~5.7pp — RIGA-Fold actually loses to several specialized baselines without PLM augmentation. The "highly competitive" phrasing is supportable but soft.

- **C2 — Construct:** "state-of-the-art" + "significantly outperforms".
  - **Operational metric:** Recovery %, PPL on CATH 4.2 / TS50 / TS500.
  - **Unmeasured aspect:** The most directly comparable baseline class — methods that *also* use frozen PLMs (LM-Design uses ESM-2; ESM-IF used as a baseline) — is undersold. The ~10pp framing is against PiFold (no PLM); against LM-Design (PLM-using, fairer comparison) the gap is ~5.7pp on CATH 4.2 All. The paper's framing "surpasses leading methods like PiFold" implies the paper's contribution = the gain over PiFold, when much of that gain comes from adding ESM-2+ESM-IF rather than from RIGA-Fold's geometric machinery.

- **C3 — Construct:** "dual-stream synergy bridges structure+sequence; semantic intuition from ESM-2 corrects errors".
  - **Operational metric:** Recovery delta between RIGA-Fold* and RIGA-Fold (61.39 − 55.05 = 6.34pp on CATH 4.2 All).
  - **Unmeasured aspect:** This delta conflates THREE simultaneous additions: (a) ESM-IF frozen structural prior, (b) ESM-2 frozen sequence prior, (c) iterative recycling T=3. No ablation isolates which of {ESM-IF only, ESM-2 only, recycling alone, dual-stream without recycling} drives the gain. The "ESM-2 corrects errors" claim is asserted, not demonstrated.

- **C4 — Construct:** "Iterative Self-Correction" outperforms "single-pass" baselines.
  - **Operational metric:** None. The paper compares RIGA-Fold* (T=3, dual-stream) vs RIGA-Fold (T=1, no PLM) — a comparison that varies T AND dual-stream simultaneously. No T=1 vs T=2 vs T=3 ablation for RIGA-Fold* is in Table 3 or anywhere in the appendix.
  - **Unmeasured aspect:** Per-T performance curve. The claim that recycling specifically (vs dual-stream alone) yields gains has no measurement. Theorem E.1 is asserted theoretically, but the empirical curve is missing.

- **C5 — Construct:** Robustness across protein lengths attributed to Global Context Bridge + Recycling.
  - **Operational metric:** Recovery / PPL across length bins 100-200, 200-300, 300-400, >400 in Fig 2.
  - **Unmeasured aspect:** No ablation showing recovery curves with Context Bridge ON vs OFF *across length bins*. Table 3 shows ablation only on aggregate CATH 4.2. The length-bin robustness is asserted to come from Context Bridge + Recycling but not isolated per length.

- **C6 — Construct:** Structural validity (foldability of generated sequences).
  - **Operational metric:** AlphaFold3 RMSD on 3 targets (1a6m, 2giy, 2ffy) in Fig 4.
  - **Unmeasured aspect:** n=3 cherry-picked targets (one each of all-α, all-β, mixed). No distribution of RMSDs across the full CATH 4.2 test set or any random subsample. Foldability rate at threshold (e.g., % targets with RMSD < 2Å) is not reported. The qualitative figure is anecdotal, not statistical.

## Reward / objective inventory (N/A: not RL — supervised cross-entropy)

The training loss is `L = -(1/N) Σ_t Σ_i Σ_c y^true_{i,c} log P_t(y_i = c | G)` (Eq 10) — sum of token-level cross-entropy across ALL T recycling steps. No multi-term loss decomposition. Single objective; no trade-off audit applies in the reward sense.

However: the implicit "objective" of RIGA-Fold* contains four added components vs base RIGA-Fold:
- (a) ESM-IF embeddings as frozen structural prior input
- (b) ESM-2 embeddings as frozen sequence prior input (initialized from MASK)
- (c) Sequence recycling (T=3) — predicted sequence at step t-1 fed to ESM-2 for step t
- (d) Loss summed across T recycling steps (vs single-step loss)

None of (a)–(d) is independently ablated for RIGA-Fold*.

## Counter-hypothesis list

- **CH1 (PLM-driven gain hypothesis): The 6.34pp jump from RIGA-Fold to RIGA-Fold* is primarily due to ESM-2's 650M-parameter sequence prior, not the geometric backbone or the recycling.**
  - Paper's evidence to rule out? **NOT RULED OUT.** No ablation removes ESM-2 while keeping ESM-IF + recycling. LM-Design (also uses ESM-2) reaches 55.65% on CATH 4.2 All — RIGA-Fold's geometric backbone alone reaches 55.05%, suggesting the marginal value of RIGA-Fold's geometry over LM-Design's geometry is ~0pp at the base level. Adding ESM-IF + recycling on top closes the rest of the gap, but the paper does not isolate which.

- **CH2 (Recycling-without-dual-stream hypothesis): RIGA-Fold + recycling alone (no ESM-IF/ESM-2) might already give large gains.**
  - Paper's evidence? **NOT RULED OUT.** No row in Table 3 enables recycling on the base model. The "single-pass vs recycling" comparison conflates with PLM addition.

- **CH3 (Single PLM sufficiency): One PLM (either ESM-2 or ESM-IF, not both) might be sufficient.**
  - Paper's evidence? **NOT RULED OUT.** No row removes ESM-IF while keeping ESM-2 (which would essentially recover LM-Design). No row removes ESM-2 while keeping ESM-IF. Knowledge-Design (60.77% on CATH 4.2) uses no PLM but knowledge distillation — close to RIGA-Fold* (61.39%), suggesting the PLM-stack contribution is marginal vs other techniques.

- **CH4 (Loss-summation effect): Summing loss across T steps acts as a per-step regularizer; this could explain gains independent of the recycling-as-prior-update mechanism.**
  - Paper's evidence? **NOT RULED OUT.** No control with single-step loss + multi-step inference.

- **CH5 (Cherry-picked structural validity): The 3 AlphaFold3 targets in Fig 4 may not be representative.**
  - Paper's evidence? **NOT RULED OUT.** No distribution across the test set; no random-sample protocol disclosed.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit (adapted: missing-baseline audit)

RIGA-Fold* claims to beat PiFold/ProteinMPNN (no PLMs) by ~10pp Recovery. The fairer baselines:

- **LM-Design (Zheng 2023)** — also uses PLM (ESM-2): 55.65% / 4.05 on CATH 4.2 All. RIGA-Fold* gain = 5.74pp.
- **ESM-IF† (Hsu 2022)** — uses ESM-IF: 38.30% on CATH 4.3 (different version, can't compare directly).
- **Knowledge-Design** — uses knowledge distillation: 60.77% / 3.46. RIGA-Fold* gain = 0.62pp Recovery / 0.09 PPL.

The paper does not include PiFold+ESM-2 or ScFold+ESM-2 baselines, which would be the cleanest controls for "is the gain from RIGA-Fold's geometry, or from adding PLMs?".

Additionally: Knowledge-Design (60.77%) performs nearly identically to RIGA-Fold* (61.39%) on CATH 4.2 All without using any PLM — strongly suggesting the headline gap is closeable by techniques other than dual-stream PLM fusion. The paper does not discuss this 0.62pp delta.

## Framing-vs-demonstration check

- **Abstract framing:** "RIGA-Fold* significantly outperforms state-of-the-art baselines."
  - **Methodology actual:** Beats Knowledge-Design (closest non-PLM SOTA) by 0.62pp Recovery / 0.09 PPL on CATH 4.2 All — within rounding-distance of being on-par. "Significantly" is not statistically validated (no seeds, no CIs).

- **Abstract framing:** "Predict-recycle-refine strategy... iteratively denoise sequence distributions" + "biologically inspired" recycling.
  - **Methodology actual:** Recycling (T=3) is implemented but never ablated against T=1 with the same dual-stream setup. The empirical contribution of "recycling" specifically is not isolated.

- **Intro framing:** Three "fundamental limitations" of GNNs identified: (1) local message passing → over-smoothing; (2) auxiliary edge features; (3) lack of iterative refinement. The paper claims to fix all three.
  - **Methodology actual:** Fix for (3) [iterative refinement] is bundled with two PLMs. The "fix for (3)" cannot be isolated from the "addition of PLMs" using the provided experiments.

- **Sec 4.3 framing:** "We attribute this robustness to two factors: the Global Context Bridge, which effectively shortcuts long-range dependencies, and the Recycling strategy, which iteratively corrects errors that typically accumulate in longer chains."
  - **Methodology actual:** Length-bin Fig 2 plots are aggregate; no ablation isolating Context Bridge OFF or Recycling OFF *within length bins*.

## Headline-number denominator audit

The most prominent numbers (61.39% Recovery, 3.37 PPL on CATH 4.2 All) are framed as "significantly outperforming SOTA". The denominator/comparison switch:

- **Implied denominator (from "PiFold-comparable" framing):** PiFold (51.66% / 4.55) — gain of 9.73pp Recovery, 1.18 PPL. Sounds large.
- **Actual fairest denominator (PLM-using baseline):** LM-Design (55.65% / 4.05) — gain of 5.74pp Recovery, 0.68 PPL. Half the headline.
- **Actual fairest non-PLM SOTA:** Knowledge-Design (60.77% / 3.46) — gain of 0.62pp Recovery, 0.09 PPL. Nearly indistinguishable.

The paper does not present this breakdown. A reader who only sees "outperforms PiFold by ~10pp" is misled about the actual incremental contribution.

## Mechanism-attribution audit

RIGA-Fold* introduces FOUR coupled novelties simultaneously:
1. RIGA-Fold geometric backbone (GAU + Edge Update + Context Bridge)
2. ESM-IF as frozen structural prior (added on top of RIGA-Fold structural features)
3. ESM-2 as frozen sequence prior (initialized from MASK)
4. Iterative recycling (T=3) with sequence feedback to ESM-2

**Table 3 ablation only isolates components within (1):** GAU vs GCN/GAT/QKV/AttMLP; with/without Edge Update; with/without Context Bridge. The headline claim is about RIGA-Fold* (not RIGA-Fold), but Table 3 reports only RIGA-Fold (base, 55.05%) configurations.

**Missing ablation rows for RIGA-Fold*:**
- RIGA-Fold + ESM-2 only (no ESM-IF, no recycling) → tests whether ESM-2 alone matters
- RIGA-Fold + ESM-IF only (no ESM-2, no recycling) → tests whether ESM-IF alone matters
- RIGA-Fold + ESM-2 + ESM-IF (T=1, no recycling) → tests whether dual-stream without recycling helps
- RIGA-Fold + recycling (T=3, no PLMs) → tests whether recycling alone helps

Without these four rows, the attribution of the 6.34pp gain (55.05 → 61.39) to any specific component of {ESM-2, ESM-IF, recycling} is unsupported. The paper attributes it to "dual-stream + recycling synergy" without isolating either contributor.

**Unrefuted alternative-mechanism explanation:** The dominant mechanism may simply be the addition of ESM-2 (650M params) as a sequence prior. LM-Design + recycling alone might match or exceed RIGA-Fold* — there is no experiment to distinguish.

## Soundness score rationale

The headline claim of RIGA-Fold* — "state-of-the-art sequence recovery, surpassing PiFold" with RIGA-Fold* = 61.39% on CATH 4.2 — is a **coupled-novelty mechanism attribution failure** (score-2 trigger). The paper introduces four simultaneous additions on top of base RIGA-Fold: (a) ESM-IF frozen structural prior, (b) ESM-2 frozen sequence prior, (c) iterative recycling T=3, (d) loss summed across T steps. Table 3's ablation only varies components within base RIGA-Fold (GAU vs alternatives, Edge Update on/off, Context Bridge on/off) — it never ablates (a), (b), (c), or (d) for RIGA-Fold*. The 6.34pp gap (55.05% → 61.39%) is attributed in Sec 4.2 to "the Iterative Refinement mechanism, RIGA-Fold* effectively leverages the 'semantic intuition' from ESM-2", but no experiment isolates ESM-2's contribution from ESM-IF's, from recycling's, from the joint loss-summation effect.

A second simultaneously-firing concern: a **denominator switch** in the framing. The abstract/intro frames the gain as "surpassing leading methods like PiFold" — implying the geometric framework's contribution. The cleanest comparable baselines tell a different story: LM-Design (PLM-using, 55.65% on CATH 4.2 All) is ~5.7pp behind RIGA-Fold*; Knowledge-Design (no PLM, 60.77%) is ~0.6pp behind — within rounding of indistinguishable. The "significantly outperforms" framing rests on the PiFold denominator, not the closest peers.

These two triggers fire on the headline contribution and dominate. Offsetting strengths exist — the base RIGA-Fold ablation in Table 3 cleanly compares GAU vs four alternative attention mechanisms, the appendix provides reproducibility details (hardware, hyperparameters, Algorithm 1), and the sequence-length robustness analysis (Fig 2) is informative — but none of these rescue the load-bearing RIGA-Fold* attribution gap.
