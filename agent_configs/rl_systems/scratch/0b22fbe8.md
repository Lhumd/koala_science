## Construct inventory

- Construct: "consistent superiority over all baselines" (KI-VQA accuracy).
  - Operational metric: VQA accuracy on E-VQA, InfoSeek, A-OKVQA — Tables 1, 2.
  - Aspect partially confounded: Table 1 mixes base-model architectures across rows. REAL evaluates with LLaVA-1.5-7B / InternVL3.5-8B / Qwen3-VL-2B / Qwen3-VL-8B; baselines use LLaVA-1.5-7B (weakest), LLaMA3-8B, mPLUG-Owl3-8B, Flan-T5-11B, OPT-66B, GPT-3, Claude 3.5 (varied). The largest absolute gains for REAL appear on Qwen3-VL-8B, where no comparable baseline runs Qwen3-VL-8B as its base. The architecture-matched cell — REAL(InternVL3.5-8B)=39.2 vs VLM-PRF(InternVL3-8B)=39.2 on E-VQA All — shows ZERO gain on the closest architecture match.
- Construct: "discrimination accuracy" (Table 3) — well-spanned, MCC + F1 across 4 datasets, multiple base models. This is the most rigorous evidence in the paper.

## Reward / objective inventory

- REAL = RPA-SFT (training on constructed REAL-VQA dataset) + RPGD (decoding-time strategy with patch shuffle / adaptive gating / Gram-Schmidt). Coupled novelty.
- Table 5 decomposition (Qwen3-VL-8B, InfoSeek All): Zero-shot 19.2 → RPA-SFT only 38.7 → +RPGD 44.1. So RPA-SFT delivers 19.5 / 24.9 = 78% of the cumulative gain over zero-shot; RPGD adds 5.4.
- The ablation (Table 6) within RPGD components shows each one removed loses 1-3 pts; cumulative removal of all RPGD components corresponds to RPA-SFT-only at 38.1.

## Counter-hypotheses considered

1. Gains are from base-model heterogeneity in Table 1, not the method? Yes, partial confound. The architecture-matched comparison (InternVL3.5 vs InternVL3 baselines on E-VQA All: 39.2 = 39.2) shows zero gain. On InfoSeek, same architecture comparison: REAL(InternVL3.5) 43.8 vs VLM-PRF(InternVL3) 42.5 = +1.3. Real but smaller than the abstract's framing implies.
2. Gains are from data construction (REAL-VQA), not the algorithmic contribution? Plausible — RPA-SFT alone delivers most of the gain. But Table 3 shows RPA-SFT discriminator generalizes to held-out E-VQA / ScienceQA / MMKC benchmarks, which is non-trivial and suggests the data construction methodology has substance.
3. Coupled-novelty mechanism attribution? RPA-SFT and RPGD are isolated independently in Table 5 (RPA-SFT alone) and Table 6 (RPGD components). This IS proper isolation. Not a coupled-novelty failure.

## Multi-objective trade-off

No measured trade-off — paper does not report inference cost, latency, or training cost overhead from RPA-SFT/RPGD. RPGD's adaptive gating + Gram-Schmidt + patch shuffle adds decoding-time computation that's not measured. Minor.

Either the average is taken over more datasets I haven't seen, or there's a denominator subtlety.

## Mechanism-attribution audit

- RPA-SFT and RPGD are properly isolated (Tables 5-7). Not a coupled-novelty failure.
- The discriminator generalization (Table 3-4) is strong evidence that the framework is doing genuine work, not just memorizing REAL-VQA.

## Soundness score rationale

The paper has a substantial, well-structured empirical study: multi-model (LLaVA, InternVL, Qwen), multi-benchmark (E-VQA, InfoSeek, A-OKVQA, REAL-VQA, ScienceQA, MMKC), proper ablations (Tables 5, 6, 7) that isolate the two coupled novelties (RPA-SFT, RPGD), and convincing discriminator-generalization results (Table 3 MCC up to 98.1, F1 up to 99.1, generalizing to unseen benchmarks).

Two concerns:

1. **Comparison-fairness confound in Table 1.** Baselines use heterogeneous base MLLMs (LLaVA-1.5-7B, LLaMA3, mPLUG-Owl, Flan-T5, GPT-3). REAL with Qwen3-VL-8B has no Qwen3-baseline in the same table, inflating the perceived gain for that row. The architecture-matched cell (REAL InternVL3.5-8B = VLM-PRF InternVL3-8B = 39.2 on E-VQA All) shows the gain is much smaller when controlling for base model. The abstract's framing benefits from the heterogeneity.
2. **Inconsistent baseline framing across sections.** Abstract's "+14.68%" lacks transparent denominator anchoring; §5.2 and §5.4 use very different baselines (SOTA vs zero-shot).

Neither concern fires a clean score-2 trigger because:
- The architecture-matched comparison still shows real (modest) gains, not zero.
- The discriminator results in Table 3 are striking and don't depend on base-model confound.
- Ablations properly isolate RPA-SFT and RPGD.
- The paper does report the architecture matrix; the confound is visible in the table.

This is a clearly score-3 trigger (single-axis-comparison-on-multi-axis-claim soft variant: comparison fairness is partial) with offsetting strength (Table 3, ablations). Score 4 fits — one major flaw with offsetting strength.
