# Phase A scratch — df5a92f8 (DeltaKV: Residual-Based KV Cache Compression via Long-Range Similarity)

## Headline empirical claims (load-bearing)

1. **Abstract (verbatim):** *"Experiments show that DeltaKV reduces KV cache memory to 29% of the original while maintaining near-lossless accuracy on LongBench, SCBench, and AIME."* — **conjunctive accuracy claim across all three benchmarks**.
2. **Abstract (verbatim):** *"When integrated with Sparse-vLLM, it achieves up to 2× throughput improvement over vLLM in long-context scenarios."*
3. **Sec 5.2:** *"DeltaKV achieves competitive performance across various models and scales on LongBench, matching the results of OmniKV."*

## Construct inventory

- **"Near-lossless accuracy on LongBench, SCBench, AND AIME"**
  - Operational metric: aggregate F1/EM/accuracy on each benchmark.
  - **LongBench (Table 1, Llama-3.1-8B):** Full = 50.0; OmniKV (KR=100, CR=30) = 50.3; **DeltaKV (KR=45, CR=30) = 50.2**. ✓ Near-lossless.
  - **LongBench (Qwen2.5-7B):** Full 47.6; OmniKV 47.4; **DeltaKV 47.1.** Δ = -0.5. ✓ Near-lossless.
  - **LongBench (Qwen2.5-32B):** Full 48.4; OmniKV 48.1; **DeltaKV 47.7.** Δ = -0.7. ✓ Near-lossless.
  - **SCBench (Table 2, Llama-3.1-8B), avg:** Full = 50.4; OmniKV = 49.2; **DeltaKV = 45.0; +DeltaKV† = 46.3.** Δ = -5.4 (10.7% relative).
  - **SCBench R.KV subtask (Llama):** Full = 79.0; OmniKV = 72.2; **DeltaKV = 58.0.** Δ = -21.0 (26.6% relative).
  - **SCBench R.KV (Qwen-7B):** Full = 70.4; OmniKV = 69.2; **DeltaKV = 59.4.** Δ = -11.0 (15.6% relative).
  - **AIME (Table 3, DeepSeek-Qwen-7B):** Full = 50.0; OmniKV = 46.7; **DeltaKV = 43.3.** Δ = -6.7 (13.4% relative); 3.4 points behind training-free OmniKV.
  - **Aspect contradicted by demonstration:** "Near-lossless" framing is supported on LongBench but not on SCBench (especially the R.KV subtask) or AIME. The paper's body softens to "preserves most of its performance" (SCBench) and "remains effective" (AIME), language that matches the data better than the abstract's "near-lossless".

- **"Up to 2× throughput improvement over vLLM in long-context scenarios"**
  - Operational metric: tokens/s decode throughput (Table 4).
  - Verbatim Table 4 entries:
    - 128k Avail.MaxBS=8: vLLM Full Attn = 143.2 t/s; **Sparse-vLLM DeltaKV = 148.4** t/s. Same BS → 1.04×.
    - 128k Avail.MaxBS=16: **Sparse-vLLM DeltaKV† = 187.0** t/s. (vLLM cannot fit BS=16.)
    - 256k BS=4: vLLM = 70.2; **Sparse-vLLM DeltaKV = 115.9; DeltaKV† = 120.6.** Same BS → 1.65× / 1.72×. ✓ Honest.
    - 512k BS=2 vLLM = 33.1; **Sparse-vLLM DeltaKV† at BS=4 = 67.7** t/s. **DIFFERENT BS** — vLLM cannot fit BS=4 at 512k. Headline "2.05×" mixes per-batch compute speedup with batch-fitting capacity.
    - At 512k same BS=2: Sparse-vLLM DeltaKV at BS=2 not in the table. Sparse-vLLM DeltaKV at BS=4 = 32.1 t/s — *slower per-batch than vLLM at BS=2 = 33.1 t/s*.
  - **Aspect not measured:** at equal BS at 512k, the per-batch throughput speedup is unclear. The "2×" headline at the largest contexts is partly batch-fitting capacity, not per-batch compute speedup.
  - Has "up to" qualifier in abstract — so technically defensible. Borderline framing issue but secondary.

## Reward / objective audit

- DeltaKV training: L = L_rec + L_ntp (MSE + Next-Token Prediction)
- Compared baselines: OmniKV is **training-free** dynamic context selection; SnapKV is training-free static eviction; Palu is training-free low-rank.
- DeltaKV requires 8 GPU hours per backbone (160M Fineweb-Edu tokens), 14 GPU hours for 32B model.
- **Hidden cost:** every new model variant needs its own DeltaKV modules trained. Not folded into "efficiency" claim.

## Counter-hypothesis list

1. **Throughput gain at 512k is mostly batch-fitting capacity, not per-batch compute speedup.**
   - Evidence (paper's own Tab 4): at equal BS at 128k, Sparse-vLLM DeltaKV/vLLM = 1.04×. At equal BS at 256k, ratio = 1.65×. At 512k, comparison is at different BS so per-batch speedup is unmeasured.
   - **Status:** unrefuted by the paper's evidence. The "2×" headline is the largest-context, mixed-BS comparison.

2. **DeltaKV is consistently slightly worse than training-free OmniKV.**
   - LongBench: DeltaKV behind OmniKV by 0.1-0.4 points across 3 backbones (acceptable trade for halved memory).
   - SCBench: DeltaKV behind OmniKV by 1.8-4.2 points avg (more concerning; on R.KV subtask 13-15 points behind).
   - AIME: DeltaKV behind OmniKV by 3.4 points.
   - **Status:** the paper acknowledges this in tables but the abstract framing claims "near-lossless" without qualifying that this is achieved at the cost of training overhead AND inferior performance vs the training-free baseline on multi-turn and reasoning benchmarks.

3. **Light decompressor (DeltaKV†) is the configuration that achieves the headline speedup.** The standard MLP decompressor (DeltaKV) is slower. Table 4 shows DeltaKV† consistently outperforms DeltaKV in throughput. Acknowledged in Sec 5.3.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit

- "Balance between accuracy, compression, and hardware efficiency" claim.
- Accuracy is **multi-axis** (LongBench, SCBench, AIME) and the paper measures all three but the framing collapses them to "near-lossless".
- Compression: KR≈45%, CR=30% — measured.
- Hardware efficiency: throughput at three context lengths — measured.
- **Indexing/training cost** (8 GPU hours per backbone) is disclosed but not folded into the efficiency narrative.

## Framing-vs-demonstration check

- **Framing (Abstract):** "near-lossless accuracy on LongBench, SCBench, and AIME"
- **Demonstration (Tab 1):** LongBench near-lossless ✓
- **Demonstration (Tab 2):** SCBench Llama avg drop -5.4; R.KV subtask drop -21.0 (26.6% relative). **Not near-lossless.**
- **Demonstration (Tab 3):** AIME drop -6.7 (13.4% relative). **Not near-lossless.**
- **Conclusion:** the abstract's conjunctive "near-lossless on all three" claim is contradicted by the paper's own Tables 2 and 3.

- **Framing (Abstract):** "up to 2× throughput improvement"
- **Demonstration (Tab 4):** "up to 2×" defended at 512k, but with different batch sizes between systems. At equal BS=8 at 128k, ratio is 1.04×. The "up to" qualifier is honest; the framing is borderline.

## Headline-number denominator audit

- "29% of original" memory: denominator = full BF16 KV cache. Reasonable, no switch.
- "2× throughput": denominator = vLLM at smaller batch size. **Mixed denominator (BS varies between numerator and denominator).** Soft denominator switch, mitigated by "up to" qualifier.

## Mechanism-attribution audit

- DeltaKV introduces: (a) residual encoding via MLP/linear compressor with strided refs, (b) hybrid MSE+NTP loss, (c) Sparse-vLLM inference kernels.
- Ablations:
  - Table 5 + Tab 11-12 isolate (a): "w/o f_c, f_d" (eviction-only) drops 3.5 avg LongBench points on Llama; "w/o Ref. Tokens T" drops 4.3 points. Both components contribute.
  - Figure 6 isolates (b): NTP-only vs MSE-only vs both. Both losses needed.
  - Sparse-vLLM kernel ablations not directly compared, but Tab 4 shows the framework cost (135.0 vs 143.2 t/s overhead at 128k Full Attn).
- Mechanism attribution is reasonably well-isolated. Not a coupled-novelty failure.
