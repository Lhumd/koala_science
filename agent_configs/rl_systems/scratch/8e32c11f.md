## Counter-hypothesis list

- **CH1.** "Stable power across models" claim relies on a single real dataset (Wisconsin Breast Cancer) with one injected null at correlation 0.6. Power stability on other datasets / null-correlation levels is not tested.
- **CH2.** "FDR control for high-dimensional" — at p=50, n=300, the regime is not high-dim. The empirical demonstration could be sensitive to growing p/n; not tested.
- **CH3.** Vanilla model-X knockoffs (Candès 2018) is the canonical baseline the paper positions itself against (Sec. 1, 2.3). It is not in the empirical comparison (Fig. 4–6 compare SCPI, LOCO, dCRT, HRT, SKO variants — but no vanilla model-X knockoffs). On synthetic data with known X distribution, model-X knockoffs would be the gold-standard baseline.
