# Scratch: BFS-PO (8b923d8f)

## Load-bearing claim inventory
1. **Abstract:** "BFS-PO can simultaneously increase the LRM accuracy and shorten its answers." → supported by Tab. 2/3 Acc + Avg.Len columns.
2. **Sec 5.3:** "BFS-PO consistently achieves the highest AES, across all datasets, LRM models, and both with in-domain and out-of-domain scenarios." → supported by Tab. 2/3 AES column.
3. **Sec 5.3:** "BFS-PO also achieves the highest accuracy in 10 out of 13 experiments." → counted across Tab. 2/3.
4. **Sec 4.2:** "1.37× speedup with respect to DAPO." → Sec. 4.2 prose (no dedicated table).

## Construct inventory
- **"Increase accuracy"** → measured by Acc (correctness rate) on GSM8K, MATH-500, MMLU-STEM, AIME, MINERVA. Construct adequately spans the metric (standard usage). What's *not* measured: per-seed CI / confidence intervals on the small (0.3–0.6%) margins.
- **"Shorten answers"** → measured by avg token count per output. Direct measurement.
- **"Speedup"** → Sec 4.2 reports 1.37× wall-clock vs DAPO. Adequate as a measurement; not in a table.

## Reward / objective inventory
- BFS-PO inherits DAPO objective (Eq. 4) including length penalty `R_l(o)` (Eq. 3). New: branch advantage replaces global GRPO advantage.
- Ablation `w/o R_l(·)` (Tab. 1): Acc 86.1, Len 275 — almost identical to full BFS-PO (86.2/269), so the **length penalty is NOT load-bearing for the claimed gains** once BFS exploration is on.
- Ablation `w/o BFS` (entropy + branch advantage but no current-best selection): Acc 86.0, Len 311 (same length as zero-shot 315) — confirms length reduction is BFS-driven.
- Ablation `Global Adv.` (BFS expansion + GRPO global advantage): Acc 85.7, Len 271 — accuracy on par with DAPO. Branch advantage is what drives the +0.5 acc.

Verdict on reward audit: ablation cleanly attributes mechanism. Not a reward-explained outcome.

## Counter-hypothesis list
- *Hypothesis: Length-penalty alone explains the gains.* Refuted by `w/o R_l(·)` ablation (Tab. 1).
- *Hypothesis: Tree expansion alone (without best-first selection) explains gains.* Refuted by `w/o BFS` row.
- *Hypothesis: Reduced training tokens (BFS uses fewer tokens/batch than DAPO per Sec 4.2) is gradient regularization.* Not directly addressed; would need a same-tokens-per-step DAPO control. Minor unrefuted.
- *Hypothesis: Single-seed lottery on small (0.3-0.6%) margins.* Not addressed — paper reports point estimates only, no seed count, no CI.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit
The proposed reward IS the DAPO reward — they use exactly DAPO's reward (Eq. 4) including length penalty. So no protective penalty is dropped. Not a multi-axis-claim problem.

## Framing-vs-demonstration check
- Abstract: "simultaneously **increase** the LRM accuracy and shorten its answers."
- Tab. 3, Llama-3.1-8B-Instruct, MATH-500 (in-domain): DAPO Acc 50.8, BFS-PO Acc 50.6 — **a 0.2-point loss** on the in-domain accuracy axis for one of three base models tested.
- Sec 5.3 hedges this with "highest accuracy in 10 out of 13 experiments". So the abstract framing is broader than the methodology supports, but the paper itself discloses the actual count. Reader interprets "increase" as universal; "10/13" reveals 3 ties or losses. Mild framing tension, not a clean denominator switch.

## Headline-number denominator audit
- The "10 out of 13" denominator: 13 = (Tab. 2: 3 base models × 1 in-domain GSM8K) + (Tab. 3: 2 base models × {MATH-500 in-domain + 4 out-of-domain}) = 3 + 10 = 13. ✓ honest count.
- Tab 2 against DAPO baseline (the closest competitor): 3/3 BFS-PO wins. Tab 3 against DAPO: 7/10 wins, 1 loss (Llama MATH-500), 2 ties. Aggregated: 10/13. ✓ reasonable.

## Mechanism-attribution audit
Three novel components: (a) BFS exploration via current-best conditioning, (b) entropy-based backtracking-token selection, (c) branch-advantage formulation. Tab. 1 provides:
- BFS off, branch advantage on, length penalty on → "w/o BFS"
- BFS on, branch advantage off (global advantage), length penalty on → "Global Adv."
- BFS on, branch advantage on, length penalty off → "w/o R_l(·)"
- All three on → BFS-PO

This isolates components (a) and (c) cleanly. **Component (b) — entropy-based vs alternative backtracking-token selection — is NOT ablated.** The paper cites Wang et al. 2025 to justify entropy as a forking-token signal but does not compare against TreeRL's negative-log-likelihood criterion or random selection. Minor flag — would matter only if entropy choice is the dominant factor, which is not asserted.

## Single-seed / variance disclosure
The paper does NOT report:
- Number of training seeds run.
- Confidence intervals or standard deviations on Tab. 1, 2, or 3.
- Statistical significance tests on the 0.3–0.6% Acc margins.

The AIME setup (30 questions × 32 sampling repeats) gives sample variance only, not training-seed variance. With reported margins of 0.3–0.6 on GSM8K/MATH-500 in-domain and ~5 points on AIME, the small in-domain margins are within typical RL-training seed noise (typically ±0.5–1.0 on 8B-scale RL fine-tuning).

This is a **score-3-tier flaw**: low seed count without variance discussion violates RLC/MLRC norms. Not a score-2 trigger because (i) AIME +5 gain is large enough to plausibly clear noise even at single seed, (ii) the ablation pattern is internally consistent across multiple settings.

## Other notes
- DAPO-Qwen2.5-7B on AIME (Tab. 3): Acc 6.4 vs Zero-shot 8.9. DAPO is BELOW the zero-shot baseline on this out-of-domain task. This is a 2.5-point regression. It's plausible (out-of-distribution degradation after RL on MATH-500) but worth flagging — without seed variance, can't tell if this DAPO reproduction is unusually weak.
- Tree of Thought (Tab. 2, Llama, GSM8K) at 74.0 vs Zero-shot 86.2 — this 12-point drop is taken from McDonald et al. 2025. Suggests either TOT genuinely hurts on these benchmarks at this scale OR the McDonald reproduction is unfavorable. Minor — TOT is not the main baseline.

## Soundness score rationale
No score-2 trigger fires cleanly:
- Construct coverage is OK (acc + length + AES + wall-clock).
- Mechanism attribution via Tab. 1 ablation is clean for (a) and (c), gap on (b).
- No reward-overlap problem (uses identical DAPO reward).
- No silent baseline omission (TokenSkip, DAPO, TOT are present).
- Cost claim is measured (1.37× speedup).
- No circular evaluation.
- Framing-vs-demonstration tension exists (abstract "increase" vs methodology "10/13") but the paper discloses the actual count in Sec 5.3, so it's not a clean denominator switch.

Score-3-tier flaws present:
1. **No seed variance disclosure** on RL-training results with sub-1% effect sizes (RLC norm violation).
2. **Component-(b) entropy-vs-alternative selection** is not ablated; relies entirely on a citation.

Offsetting strength: clean ablation table, multi-model + multi-benchmark + in/out-of-domain coverage, wall-clock measurement provided, AIME gains (+4-5%) are large enough to plausibly clear any seed-noise floor.

→ **Score 4, confidence 4** (one major score-3 trigger — seed variance — with the rest of the methodology sound and at least one clearly well-executed section, the ablation in Tab. 1).
