## Construct inventory

For each load-bearing claim: construct → operational metric → unmeasured aspects.

### Claim #1, #4, #6: "state-of-the-art" on genomic FM benchmarks

- **Construct claimed**: SOTA / "leading" performance against the field of genomic foundation models. Section 2.1 explicitly enumerates the field: DNABERT (Ji et al., 2021), DNABERT-2 (Zhou et al., 2023), Nucleotide Transformer (Dalla-Torre et al., 2024), HyenaDNA (Nguyen et al., 2023), Evo (Nguyen et al., 2024), Evo 2 (Brixi et al., 2025), megaDNA (Shao & Yan, 2024). The paper itself frames these as the "leading architectures."
- **Operational metric**: Spearman ρ on MaveDB (Table 5: dnaHNet 0.3266 vs SH2 0.3110 vs Transformer 0.1555); AUROC on DEG (Table 6: dnaHNet (3,2) 0.6050 vs SH2 0.5759).
- **Unmeasured aspects**:
  - **Comparison vs DNABERT, DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Evo, Evo 2, MegaDNA — every one of the "leading architectures" identified in Section 2.1 is omitted from Tables 5, 6.** Only SH2 and a vanilla decoder-only Transformer baseline appear.
  - The Transformer baseline at 0.1555 Spearman is suspiciously weak (less than half SH2's score) — this raises the question of whether it is a tuned baseline or a strawman.
  - No comparison against actual published Spearman/AUROC numbers from these prior FMs on MaveDB / DEG.

### Claim #2: "outperforms leading architectures including StripedHyena2 in scaling"

- **Construct claimed**: scaling efficiency superior to "leading" genomic FMs.
- **Operational metric**: power-law fit α=0.06 (dnaHNet) vs α=0.04 (SH2) vs α=0.01 (Transformer), Fig 3 + §4.3.
- **Unmeasured aspects**:
  - α only computed against the same two-baseline set (SH2, Transformer) as above. None of HyenaDNA, Caduceus, DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, Evo are scaled at matched compute.
  - The compute range tested is 4×10^18 to 8×10^19 FLOPs — far below the regime of the published SH2/Evo/Evo2 systems (Evo 2 is 7B parameters, the dnaHNet ceiling is 1B). Extrapolation to "leading FM" scale is unsupported.
  - Param-count comparisons (e.g. Fig 2 dnaHNet 218M vs SH2 166M) are mismatched in size; the dnaHNet config is 31% larger.

### Claim #3, #7: ">3× inference speedup over Transformers"

- **Construct claimed**: wall-clock inference advantage from recursive compression.
- **Operational metric**: Fig 2 (FLOPs vs sequence length), Fig 7 (wall-clock throughput, memory, latency on H100).
- **Unmeasured aspects**: Fig 7 measures dnaHNet vs SH2 wall-clock — Transformers are not in the wall-clock benchmark, only the FLOP comparison. The "3× speedup over Transformers" is a FLOP claim, not a measured wall-clock speedup. Reasonable but should be acknowledged as theoretical-FLOP, not measured.

### Claim #5: "automatically discovers hierarchical biological structures without supervision"

- **Construct claimed**: emergent biologically meaningful segmentation.
- **Operational metric**: Table 1 — selection rates by genomic region (promoters, codons, etc.) and codon-position periodicity.
- **Unmeasured aspects**:
  - No comparison against null/random chunking baseline (would a random chunker also exhibit 35% global selection rate by chance? — answer obviously not, but the absence is worth noting).
  - No quantitative metric like "agreement with annotated codon boundaries" — only rate-of-selection per region, which is a coarse summary.
  - Generalization to a non-prokaryotic genome is acknowledged as not done (§5.1).

## Reward / objective inventory

The training objective (Eq. 3, 4) is straightforward:

- **Proposed**: ℒ = ℒ_NLL + α · ℒ_rate (Eq. 3, 4). α ≈ 0.01–0.05 (scheduled, Tables 3, 4). ℒ_rate is a chunking-rate regularizer guiding compression toward target ratio R_s.
- **H-Net baseline objective**: same NLL + ratio loss (paper inherits this directly from Hwang et al. 2025).
- **SH2 baseline objective**: standard NLL only (no chunking, no ratio loss).

**Reward-overlap check**: dnaHNet does not "drop" any baseline reward terms; it adds the chunking-rate regularizer. So reward-overlap is NOT a failure mode here. The mechanism is architectural, not reward-shaping.

## Counter-hypothesis list

1. **Counter-hypothesis: the SH2 baseline is undertrained at matched compute, not architecturally inferior.** §4.3 reports SH2 trains on 68B tokens at 8×10^19 FLOPs vs dnaHNet's 140B tokens at the same FLOPs (because compression halves the effective sequence length). Paper attributes this to dnaHNet "extracting more signal per raw nucleotide" but an alternative is that SH2 simply needs the same data-to-parameter ratio dnaHNet uses, and the comparison conflates "compute-optimal for dnaHNet's scaling laws" with "compute-optimal in general." Paper notes its scaling differs from Chinchilla but does not run an apples-to-apples 140B-token SH2 control.

   **Refuted?**: No. The paper does not report SH2 at matched-tokens (only matched-FLOPs).

2. **Counter-hypothesis: dnaHNet's 218M vs SH2's 166M parameter mismatch (Fig 2) explains the 3.89× FLOP gap.** dnaHNet has 31% more parameters but achieves "fewer FLOPs at 10^6 nucleotides" — this is the architectural compression effect. Reasonable claim, not a failure mode.

   **Refuted?**: Yes — the FLOP analysis is per-architecture-design-point and isn't a parameter-count confound.

3. **Counter-hypothesis: Transformer baseline at Spearman 0.1555 is undertrained or untuned.** Such a low score (less than half SH2's) is unusual for a Transformer at 8×10^19 FLOPs on MaveDB. If the Transformer is a strawman, the "3× more compute" framing for SH2 to match dnaHNet may also be inflated.

   **Refuted?**: No — paper does not document Transformer hyperparameter search or per-architecture HP optimization.

4. **Counter-hypothesis: The "SOTA" claim collapses if any of DNABERT-2 / Nucleotide Transformer / Evo / Evo 2 / HyenaDNA / Caduceus reach Spearman > 0.33 on MaveDB or AUROC > 0.61 on DEG.**

   **Refuted?**: Not addressed. Evo 2 is a 7B-param model trained on prokaryotic+eukaryotic genomes including MaveDB-relevant variants; it would be surprising if dnaHNet at 1B beats Evo 2 zero-shot on MaveDB. Without explicit comparison, the SOTA claim is unsubstantiated.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit

dnaHNet does not drop any baseline objective terms — only adds the chunking-rate regularizer. No multi-objective trade-off concern.

However, on the **performance axes**: paper claims SOTA on (a) protein VEP, (b) gene essentiality. Other standard genomic FM benchmarks (Genomic Benchmarks suite, Nucleotide Transformer Benchmarks, BEND, GUE) are not evaluated. A genomic FM claiming SOTA without these is single-axis.

## Framing-vs-demonstration check

- **Abstract framing**: "state-of-the-art tokenizer-free autoregressive model that segments and models genomic sequences end-to-end ... outperforms leading architectures including StripedHyena2." (l.020-029)
- **Methodology demonstration (§4)**: Comparison set is **two architectures only** — StripedHyena2 and a decoder-only Transformer. Section 2.1 enumerates the actual "leading architectures" as DNABERT, DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Evo, Evo 2, megaDNA, of which **zero are evaluated**. The framing "outperforms leading architectures" is contradicted by §4.2's two-baseline comparison set.
- **Verbatim quote of mismatch**:
  - Abstract: "Pretrained on prokaryotic genomes, dnaHNet outperforms leading architectures including StripedHyena2 in scaling and efficiency." (l.027-029)
  - §4.2 Models and Baselines: "We compare dnaHNet against two leading long-sequence architectures. StripedHyena2 ... is the convolutional multi-hybrid architecture underlying Evo 2 ... We also compare against an optimized Transformer++ architecture (Nguyen et al., 2024) designed for long-context genomic sequences." (verbatim)

  The plural "leading architectures" in the abstract suggests a comparison set that materializes in §4.2 as exactly two — and the SOTA-incumbents Evo, Evo 2, DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Caduceus are all absent. SH2 is the architecture *underlying* Evo 2, but is not Evo 2 itself.

## Headline-number denominator audit

- "**3.89× fewer FLOPs than StripedHyena2**" (Fig 2 caption): denominator is SH2 (166M) at 10^6 nucleotides. dnaHNet here is 218M — larger model, fewer FLOPs. Fair comparison if FLOP counting methodology matches; paper documents FLOP accounting for dnaHNet (sum of encoder+main+decoder, l.306-309) but does not document FLOP counting for SH2's hybrid architecture in equivalent detail.
- "**3.75× less efficiency**" for SH2 to match dnaHNet's perplexity (l.301-303): based on power-law extrapolation from compute range 4×10^18 to 8×10^19. Extrapolation gap from 8×10^19 to 3×10^20 is modest (4×) and credible.
- **"State-of-the-art zero-shot performance"** (Abstract): denominator is **only the SH2/Transformer baselines reported**. Not denominated against any other published FM. This is the headline-denominator switch — "SOTA" implies field-level supremacy but is operationalized as "best of two baselines I ran myself."

## Mechanism-attribution audit

dnaHNet introduces multiple coupled novelties simultaneously:

1. Tokenizer-free dynamic chunking (inherited from H-Net architecture).
2. Recursive hierarchical compression (2-stage 3×2 = 6× compression).
3. Genomics-specific design choices: 30% encoder-decoder param share (vs 15% in language H-Net), biologically motivated R1=3 / R2=2 ratios, α≈0.01 (vs ≥0.03 for language).
4. Increased data-to-param ratio relative to Chinchilla (140B vs 68B at matched FLOPs).
5. Layer-wise LR multipliers (2.0/1.5/1.0).

**Ablations actually run:**
- (2,2) hierarchy vs (3,2) hierarchy (Table 6) — partial isolation of compression-ratio choice.
- 1-stage vs 2-stage variants of dnaHNet (Fig 2) — partial isolation of recursive depth.
- Discussed in text: parameter allocation 15% vs 30%, depth selection 1–4 stages, α value, target compression ratios.

**Ablations missing:**
- No "H-Net-vanilla applied to genomics without genomics-specific tweaks" baseline. The genomics-specific α/parameter-allocation tweaks could be doing all the work; "applying H-Net to genomics" alone is not isolated from "genomics-tuned H-Net."
- No "SH2 trained with the same 140B-token budget as dnaHNet" control.
- No isolation of layer-wise LR multipliers vs uniform LR.

The mechanism-attribution gap is moderate, not severe — the ablations cover the headline architectural choice (hierarchy depth, compression ratios) but not the genomics-specific tuning that distinguishes "we made H-Net work on DNA" from "H-Net works on DNA out of the box."

## Soundness score rationale

The dominant Soundness flaw is **silent baseline omission on the load-bearing SOTA claim**. The paper's abstract frames it as outperforming "leading architectures" and Section 2.1 names those leading architectures explicitly: DNABERT, DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Evo, Evo 2, megaDNA. The headline tables (Tables 5, 6) compare only against StripedHyena2 (the architecture *underlying* Evo 2 but not Evo 2 itself) and a vanilla decoder-only Transformer. Critically:

- **DNABERT-2** has published MaveDB Spearman correlations.
- **Nucleotide Transformer** is benchmarked on protein variant effect prediction.
- **HyenaDNA** is benchmarked on DEG-style tasks.
- **Evo** and **Evo 2** are explicitly mentioned and have published zero-shot fitness numbers.

None of these appear in Tables 5, 6. The SOTA claim is operationalized as "best of two architectures we ran ourselves," with the Transformer baseline at Spearman 0.1555 — half of SH2's score — looking suspiciously like a strawman. This is a textbook **silent baseline omission on a load-bearing claim**: if any one of the omitted FMs reaches Spearman > 0.33 on MaveDB or AUROC > 0.605 on DEG (which is plausible — Evo 2 alone is 7B params on a vastly broader corpus), the SOTA claim fails.

This is a verbatim-anchored, load-bearing, unrefuted gap. Score 2.

Offsetting: the paper has clean wall-clock measurements (Fig 7), well-documented FLOP accounting (l.306-309), and reasonable hierarchy ablations (Table 6). These belong as caveats in the comment, not as a score-rescue.

**Score: 2. Confidence: 4** (read full paper including appendix; rationale is anchored to specific section/table labels; did not cross-check against published numbers from omitted FMs).
