## Construct inventory

- **Construct claimed:** "Effectiveness on standard IM benchmarks." In the IM literature this paper cites (Empowerment, DIAYN, MaxInfoRL, Active Inference, Tiomkin 2024), "effectiveness" typically means: (i) competitive or superior intrinsic-driven exploration relative to other IM signals, (ii) downstream task adaptation / sample efficiency, (iii) coverage of the state space, or (iv) qualitative behavior matching some target (swing-up, balancing, etc).
- **Operational metric used:** (a) trajectory visualization in Fig. 2a–c (visual inspection of swing-up/stabilization on three pendulums under MPC-iCEM); (b) CIP value curves over iCEM iterations in Fig. 2d.
- **Aspects of the construct NOT measured:**
  - **No comparison to prior IM signals.** The paper cites Empowerment, DIAYN, MaxInfoRL, Active Inference, Tiomkin 2024 — all studied on similar pendulum tasks — but none appear in Fig. 2 under the same iCEM-MPC controller for head-to-head comparison.
  - **No downstream task transfer.** A canonical IM benchmark is "intrinsic phase → frozen policy → measure performance on extrinsic task" (e.g., reward-free pretraining → swing-up). The paper does not run this.
  - **No state-space coverage / diversity metric.** State visitation, trajectory entropy, or basin coverage are standard IM-effectiveness proxies; not measured.
  - **No sample efficiency / wall-clock metric.** iCEM samples N action sequences per planning step; cost not reported.
  - **No quantitative scoring of swing-up.** No reward-free task-success rate, no time-to-stable, no terminal-angle distribution across seeds.

## Reward / objective inventory

- **Proposed objective:** `J_i = H_KS^{ol}(x, u_{0:T-1}^{(i)}) - H_KS^{cl}(x, u_{0:T-1}^{(i)})` (Eq. unnumbered in Algorithm 1 line 5). The CIP score for a candidate action sequence.
- **Optimizer:** iCEM (Pinneri et al. 2021) — fits a Gaussian to the top-K elite sequences ranked by `J_i`.
- **Baseline objectives:** none. The paper does not compare CIP-based MPC against Empowerment-based MPC, MaxInfoRL-based MPC, or any other IM-objective MPC.

## Counter-hypothesis list

- **CH1 (untested):** Maximizing any chaos-related quantity under iCEM-MPC — e.g., a single open-loop positive Lyapunov-exponent estimate — would yield qualitatively similar behavior on the three pendulum systems (swing-up + stabilization at sensitive equilibria). This would localize the demonstrated behavior to "iCEM-MPC + any chaos quantity," not to CIP specifically.
- **CH2 (untested):** A goal-conditioned MPC with a hand-coded swing-up reward would produce essentially the same trajectory snapshots in Fig. 2a–c. The paper offers the snapshots as evidence the IM signal is non-trivial, but absent a comparison to alternatives, the snapshots don't disambiguate "CIP is special" from "iCEM-MPC swings these systems up under almost any moderately reasonable objective."
- **CH3 (untested):** The iCEM elite-fitting procedure with a CIP score is mode-seeking — it finds a single high-CIP action mode and locks in. The paper offers no diversity test (e.g., distribution of trajectories over multiple resets) to distinguish "controllable chaos" from "iCEM converges to one chaos-rich mode."

## Soundness score rationale

Three score-2 triggers fire on the load-bearing empirical claim:

1. **Construct-coverage gap.** The construct "effectiveness on standard IM benchmarks" in the IM literature this paper itself cites typically means relative comparison + downstream-task transfer + state-space coverage. The operational metric used here measures none of those — it shows (a) visual swing-up and (b) the objective's own value rising. At least three standard facets of "effectiveness" are unmeasured.

2. **Circular evaluation (partial).** Fig. 2d's CIP curves are the optimization objective being maximized by iCEM. Showing that the optimizer increases its objective is tautological with the planner working, not evidence that CIP is a useful IM signal.

3. **Framing-vs-demonstration mismatch.** Abstract says "demonstrate effectiveness on standard IM benchmarks" (relative-comparison framing); methodology says "induces non-trivial and interpretable behavior" (descriptive only).

The paper is honest in Sec. 6.5 ("we deliberately adopt a simple controller architecture; experiments with arbitrary large scale environments are beyond scope... deferred to follow up work"). That partly mitigates the gap — the paper acknowledges empirical scope is limited. But the abstract phrasing overreaches relative to what is actually shown.

The theoretical content (CIP positivity, Riccati connection, decomposition lemma) is mathematically substantive and is the paper's principal contribution — but per CLAUDE.md, theoretical proof correctness is not Soundness's lane and does not enter this score.

**Soundness: 2.** A load-bearing empirical claim from the abstract is not adequately supported by the demonstration provided.
