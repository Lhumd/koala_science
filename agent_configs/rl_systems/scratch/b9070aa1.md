## Construct inventory

- Construct: "strong prediction accuracy" / "unified" operator learning across diverse PDEs.
  - Operational metric: relative L2 error on 9 PDEBench subsets (1D CFD, 1D Adv, 1D Burgers, 2D CFD M=1, 2D CFD M=0.1, 2D CFD All, 2D SWE, 2D Incompressible, 3D CFD Turb) — Table 2.
  - **Aspect not measured / contradicted:** the unified accuracy claim is contradicted by the IMPROVEMENT row of Table 2, which shows UniFluids-XL is **WORSE** than the best baseline on 2 of 9 subsets:
    - **2D Burgers: -60.3%** (UniFluids-XL 0.0104 vs OmniArch-L 0.0063)
    - **2D SWE: -71.4%** (UniFluids-XL 0.0024 vs OmniArch-L 0.0014)
  - The "+86.7%" cited in the abstract is the BEST single-subset result (2D Incompressible vs OmniArch-L). The "up to 86.7%" framing selectively emphasizes the favorable subset and omits the negative results.

## Counter-hypotheses considered

1. The "up to 86.7%" claim is a cherry-pick across 9 subsets? YES — on 2/9 subsets, UniFluids underperforms. The +86.7% is the best, with -71.4% as a counterpoint not surfaced in abstract or section narrative.
2. SWE is a special case where the base model is genuinely good (multiple baselines cluster near 0.0014-0.0022)? Possibly — SWE is a relatively simple shallow-water equation, and OmniArch-L's 0.0014 may be the natural floor. But the paper's narrative claims "near-best" on SWE when UniFluids is the WORST among unified pre-training methods (UniFluids 0.0024 vs MPP-L 0.0022, DPOT-L 0.0020, OmniArch-B 0.0016, OmniArch-L 0.0014). "Near-best" is verbatim contradicted by the table.
3. The 2D Burgers underperformance is acknowledged elsewhere? Searching the body — no mention of why 2D Burgers degrades by -60.3% vs OmniArch-L. Sec 4.2 narrative discusses "+86.7% on 2D Incompressible" but not the negative results.
