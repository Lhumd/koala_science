## Construct inventory

- **C1 — "consistently outperforms"** → operational metric: average task success rate over 100 episodes per task (Sec 5.2).
  - Aspects measured: aggregate SR on RLBench (10 tasks) + RoboTwin (10 tasks) + 6 real-world task categories.
  - Aspects NOT measured: success rate confidence intervals; number of training seeds (no seed count disclosed in App. A or main text).
  - **Aspect violated by the data**: "consistently" is contradicted by Tab. 7 RoboTwin per-task. Object Stand: Ours 15 vs TesserAct 37 (Ours loses by 22 points). Click Bell: Ours 31 vs TesserAct 38 (Ours loses by 7 points). The aggregate RoboTwin row in Tab. 3 (Ours 43.0 vs TesserAct 33.9) wins because gains on Place Container (+27 vs TesserAct), Adjust Bottle (+7), and Move Pill (+16) dominate the per-task losses on Object Stand and Click Bell.

- **C2 — "Test-time action optimization that backpropagates through the generative model to infer a trajectory-level latent best matching the predicted future"** → operational metric: comparison of full model (Ours) vs Act-Head (skips latent optimization, directly uses an action-head prediction as the trajectory prior for the residual IDM).
  - Aspects measured: aggregate SR on RLBench + RoboTwin.
  - **Tab. 3 rows verbatim**: Act-Head 72.5 (RLBench), 42.5 (RoboTwin); Ours 72.6 (RLBench), 43.0 (RoboTwin). Margin: **+0.1 / +0.5 absolute SR**.
  - **Aspect NOT measured**: inference-time cost of TLO. Per Sec 5.2: "We then initialize a random trajectory latent and optimize it for 100 backpropagation steps so that the trajectory-conditioned generation matches the fixed 4D prediction." 100 backprop steps through a video diffusion model is a non-trivial cost; the cost-vs-benefit trade-off for 0.1 SR is never reported.
  - **Aspect NOT measured**: seed variance. With margins of 0.1–0.5 on success rate, the "indicates consistent benefit from test-time latent optimization" framing in Sec 5.2 (Effect of Trajectory Latent Optimization paragraph) cannot be distinguished from seed-to-seed noise.

- **C3 — "Residual inverse dynamics for action refinement"** → operational metric: w/o R-IDM vs Full IDM vs Ours.
  - Aspects measured: SR. **Tab. 3**: w/o R-IDM 69.0 (RL), 42.8 (RT); Full IDM 68.8 (RL), 41.7 (RT); Ours 72.6 (RL), 43.0 (RT). The R-IDM contributes +3.6 on RLBench, but only +0.2 on RoboTwin vs the w/o-R-IDM ablation.
  - On RoboTwin, all six ablation rows (Act-Head 42.5, Full IDM 41.7, w/o R-IDM 42.8, Ours 43.0, plus baselines TesserAct 33.9, 4DGen 40.2) cluster in a 1.3-point band — both novelty components fall within one absolute SR point on this benchmark. Without seed CIs, none of the C2/C3 claims can be cleanly attributed on RoboTwin.

- **C4 — "Cross-view consistent appearance and geometry"** → operational metric: Tab. 2 ablation (w/o view, EA, w/o mod, Ours) on RoboTwin.
  - Aspects measured: PSNR, SSIM, FVD, AbsRel, RMSE, δ1, CD, EMD.
  - This ablation is **clean** on RoboTwin: the deformable cross-view attention contributes ~1.4 PSNR and 1.5 EMD over the EA baseline. C4 is well-supported.

## Counter-hypothesis list

1. **TLO is mechanism-orthogonal to the SR gains.** Tab. 3 directly tests this: Act-Head (= action-head prediction → residual IDM, no TLO) gets 72.5 / 42.5 vs Ours 72.6 / 43.0. The TLO step contributes 0.1 / 0.5 absolute SR. The paper's claim "indicates consistent benefit from test-time latent optimization" is the verbatim narrative gloss on a 0.1-point margin without seed CI. **Status: unrefuted — the paper's own ablation supports this counter-hypothesis.**

2. **The "consistent" framing is partially refuted by per-task tables.** Tab. 7 shows Ours below TesserAct on 2 of 10 RoboTwin tasks (Object Stand -22, Click Bell -7). Aggregate gain comes from a few tasks. **Status: unrefuted; per-task tables in appendix expose the gap.**

3. **R-IDM contribution is benchmark-dependent.** Tab. 3 shows R-IDM contributes +3.6 on RLBench but +0.2 on RoboTwin. Without seed variance, R-IDM's RoboTwin contribution is statistically indistinguishable from zero. **Status: unrefuted.**

## Mechanism-attribution audit

Three claimed contributions: (i) cross-view + cross-modality fusion (C4); (ii) trajectory-latent test-time optimization (C2); (iii) residual IDM (C3).

- (i) is **cleanly isolated** by Tab. 2 (w/o view, EA, w/o mod ablations) on the 4D-generation metrics. ✓
- (ii) is **isolated by the Act-Head row in Tab. 3**: skips TLO, uses action-head prediction as prior, then R-IDM. Result: 0.1 / 0.5 SR margin = TLO contribution. The paper attributes "consistent benefit" to TLO, but the magnitude is within plausible seed noise.
- (iii) is **isolated by w/o R-IDM and Full IDM rows in Tab. 3**: clean +3.6 RLBench / +0.2 RoboTwin contribution. R-IDM is benchmark-asymmetric.

**The TLO is the load-bearing claim where the score-2 trigger fires.** The paper attributes one of three core contributions to a component that the ablation shows contributes 0.1–0.5 SR — the TLO is mechanism-orthogonal to the SR gains.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit
- Inference cost: Tab. 9 (number-of-views) reports time but not Ours-vs-Act-Head time. The TLO costs 100 backprop steps through the video diffusion backbone — likely the dominant inference cost component — but not measured against the alternative that achieves equivalent SR.
- Generation quality: TLO is described as recovering actions from an *imagined* 4D rollout. Whether the optimized actions are smoother / more executable / lower-jerk than the action-head prediction is not measured (only SR).

## Framing-vs-demonstration check

- **"consistently outperforms strong baselines on downstream manipulation"** (Sec 1, contribution bullet 4): contradicted by Tab. 7 Object Stand and Click Bell (both worse than TesserAct), and by the very tight 1.3-point ablation cluster on RoboTwin (Act-Head 42.5, Full IDM 41.7, w/o R-IDM 42.8, Ours 43.0).
- **Real-world Tab. 1 PSNR**: Ours 21.82 vs TesserAct 22.27 — TesserAct wins on PSNR by 0.45 in real-world. SSIM: Ours 89.98 vs TesserAct 91.50 — TesserAct wins by 1.52 SSIM. The abstract aggregation ("better appearance quality") is true on synthetic but reversed on real-world for these two metrics (FVD and depth metrics still favor Ours).

## Headline-number denominator audit
- "consistently outperforms strong baselines" denominator: per-task SR averaged over 100 episodes per task (sim) or unspecified per task (real). Real-robot per-task numbers in Tab. 4 (e.g., 50, 33, 56, 23, 63, 50) read as percentages but the trial count is not stated explicitly in the table caption — App. C describes "for each trial, we first verify whether the predicted video completes the task" but does not state N per task. SOFT — interpretable as integer counts on a small N or as percentages on a typical N=100 protocol.

## Soundness score rationale

**Score-2 trigger check.**

The clearest trigger is **mechanism-orthogonal outcome on a load-bearing claim**. The paper's three contributions are: (i) cross-view 4D world model, (ii) trajectory-latent test-time optimization (TLO), (iii) residual IDM. The Act-Head ablation in Tab. 3 — which the paper itself constructs as the clean test of (ii) — shows the TLO contributes 0.1 SR on RLBench and 0.5 SR on RoboTwin. The paper's narrative gloss in the "Effect of Trajectory Latent Optimization" paragraph claims "consistent benefit from test-time latent optimization." A 0.1-point margin on a benchmark with 10 tasks at 10% per-task granularity (1 trial out of 100), and 0.5 on a similarly granular benchmark, is **within plausible single-seed noise** and is *not* a robust demonstration of the load-bearing claim that the TLO is doing meaningful work.

The TLO is presented as architecturally significant (Sec 4.3 motivates it through several pages: "Optimizing in the learned low-dimensional trajectory latent space is typically more stable and yields smoother, more executable actions"; Eq. 11 is given prominence). Sec 5.2 calls Act-Head "indicates consistent benefit from test-time latent optimization" — but the only quantitative anchor for "consistent benefit" is 0.1 / 0.5 absolute SR, with no seed disclosure.

**Per the rubric** (mechanism-orthogonal outcome): "*The headline metric improvement is fully explained by reward-term overlap with the baseline OR by a component orthogonal to the paper's claimed mechanism.*" The aggregate Ours-vs-baseline gain on RLBench (Ours 72.6 vs TesserAct 67.3 = +5.3) is *explained almost entirely* by the residual IDM (+3.6 from w/o-R-IDM ablation) and the cross-view world model (the Tab. 1 4D generation gain that improves the rollout that all action-conversion methods consume). The TLO's marginal contribution is 0.1. The paper attributes a substantial fraction of the contribution narrative to a component that is mechanism-orthogonal to the measured improvement.

**Distribution sanity check.** Last 5 papers I scored were all 4. The forcing function flags: do I have a sharp, verbatim-anchored critique on a load-bearing claim that I am rounding up to score 4 because "the 4D generation cross-view ablation in Tab. 2 is clean"? Yes — I am tempted to do exactly that. But the 4D generation ablation supports (i), not (ii). The score is on the paper's *load-bearing claim portfolio*. (ii) is explicitly listed as one of three core contributions and the ablation that isolates it shows it contributes within single-seed noise.

**Score 2, confidence 4.**

(Offsetting strengths exist — the Tab. 2 cross-view ablation is clean, the per-task disclosure in Tabs. 6/7 is honest, the real-robot data collection is non-trivial — but per Calibration Principle 2, "a score-2 trigger fires regardless of offsetting strength.")
