## Construct inventory

### Construct A: "joint audio-visual reasoning" / "complex physical reasoning" (C3, C5)

- **Operational metric:** Accuracy on multi-choice classification with answer space {Left, Center, Right}. Sample QA from Tab 1: "Q: Can you tell which speaker the male voice originates from? A: Center". Task D = single-source 2/3-candidate matching (10K + 4K samples). Task E = overlap-source 2/3-candidate matching (24K + 5K samples). Tab 2 reports Reasoning 1-speaker = 99.5% and Reasoning 2-speaker = 99.2%.
- **Aspects of "reasoning" NOT captured by this metric:**
  1. **Continuous spatial reasoning** — the answer space is categorical (Left/Center/Right), not the actual 3D position the FOA could in principle support. The model could produce correct categorical labels while having coarse continuous localization.
  2. **Ambiguity-stratified difficulty** — the metric does not bin samples by candidate-position similarity. With FOA + RGB-D access, the matching task reduces to angular comparison; difficulty depends on candidate angular separation, but no separation-difficulty bin is reported.
  3. **3-candidate vs 2-candidate breakdown** — Tab 1 shows Task E has 24K 2-cand + 5K 3-cand samples. Tab 2 reports a single aggregate "Reasoning 2-speaker" number. The harder 3-candidate subset (where random=33%) is buried in the average and not reported separately, even though it would be the more probative measurement.
  4. **Compositional/multi-step inference** — "reasoning" in the abstract framing implies multi-step inference; the operational task is single-step geometric matching.
  5. **Robustness probes** — no test of reasoning under acoustic occlusion, similar-spectrum interferers, or with adversarially chosen candidates.

### Construct B: "monaural representations are fundamentally insufficient for spatial disambiguation" (C4)

- **Operational metric:** Tab 5 — "Ours w/o FOA Encoder" hits 43.8% / 47.6% on Reasoning 1-spk / 2-spk; "Random" baseline (Tab 2) is 45.6% / 47.4%.
- **Aspect not captured:** This is essentially a tautology — without ANY spatial-audio signal, you cannot tell which speaker is talking from monaural audio. The "catastrophic drop" is not an empirical discovery about model limitations; it's a definitional consequence of the input pipeline. The interesting ablation would be: monaural audio + visual + lip motion / mouth-region attention, which models like Qwen2.5-Omni and several AV speaker-diarization systems demonstrate can solve speaker-source attribution from RGB alone in many cases. That's not tested.

### Construct C: "JAEGER... outperforms BAT in the more challenging overlapping-source task, reducing the MAE from 19.09° to 13.13°" (C1)

- **Operational metric:** Tab 2 "Overlap DoA" column.
- **Aspect not captured:** BAT uses binaural audio (2-channel). JAEGER uses 4-channel FOA. The comparison is across audio-modality, not across model architectures within the same modality. A within-FOA comparison (e.g., a non-Neural-IV FOA SELDnet baseline) is absent. Tab 3's "Classical IV" comparison is *internal* to JAEGER's architecture, so it doesn't isolate model architecture from spatial-representation choice.

## Reward / objective inventory

Not applicable — JAEGER is supervised instruction-tuning (LoRA on Qwen2.5-Omni), no reward shaping. The closest analogue is the multi-task loss balancing across Tasks A-E. Sec 5.1 says "We fine-tune specific components selectively: the audio encoder, Neural IV, and projector for Audio DoA (Tasks A-B); the visual encoder and projector for Visual Grounding (Task C); and all modality encoders and projectors for Joint Reasoning (Tasks D-E)." So different tasks are trained on different parameter subsets — joint reasoning gets full encoder+projector training, perception tasks only train audio-side or visual-side. This task-specific routing is a form of curriculum that could explain why headline "joint reasoning" numbers saturate: the joint task gets the most permissive training schedule.

## Counter-hypothesis list

- **CH1 (information-presence detector vs reasoning):** With FOA + RGB-D, the matching task reduces to "estimate DoA from FOA, look up nearest visible speaker centroid, output its position label." The 99.2% accuracy is consistent with a near-perfect geometric pipeline, not "reasoning." The paper's evidence does NOT rule this out — there is no harder reasoning probe (compositional QA, multi-step inference, distractor-with-similar-direction).
- **CH2 (saturated-by-design):** Tab 2 shows the 2-cand baseline is ~50% (random for 2 choices). The 3-cand baseline would be ~33%. The aggregate is ~45-47%. The chosen task evaluates an information-presence boundary (random vs full info) rather than a continuous difficulty curve. Not refuted.
- **CH3 (FOA-vs-binaural confound on Overlap DoA):** JAEGER beats BAT on overlap-source MAE (13.13° vs 19.09°), but JAEGER has 4-channel FOA while BAT has binaural. The improvement could come from the additional channels rather than from the Neural IV architecture. The paper's only architecture-isolated comparison is within JAEGER (Classical IV vs Neural IV in Tab 3), which keeps FOA constant — useful but doesn't address the BAT comparison.

Not directly applicable in a reward-shaping sense. The closest analogue: JAEGER trades model capacity (LoRA on a 7B+ Qwen2.5-Omni base) and modality access (RGB-D + 4-ch FOA) for the headline gains. ## Headline-number denominator audit

C3 (99.2%) — the denominator is "all Task E samples (overlap-source matching)" averaged over 2-candidate and 3-candidate subsets (24K + 5K). The 3-candidate variant (where random is 33%, not 50%) is not reported separately. Implied audience reading "99.2% accuracy on joint audio-visual reasoning" sees a uniformly hard reasoning task; actual evaluation is dominated by 2-cand samples (~83% of Task E). This is a soft denominator concern — not a flagrant switch, but a relevant disclosure gap.

## Mechanism-attribution audit

Two coupled novelties: Neural IV (learned FOA spatial representation) + 3D-aware positional encoding (depth-projected sinusoidal PE on RGB tokens).

- **Tab 3** (Neural IV vs Classical IV, single vs overlap, train/test cross-eval): solid isolation of the IV-architecture choice while keeping all else fixed. Good ablation.
- **Tab 4** (with vs without depth encoding for Task C visual grounding): isolates depth contribution for grounding only. Good.
- **Tab 5** (component analysis on reasoning): four conditions — Neural IV w/ Depth, Classical IV w/ Depth, Neural IV w/o Depth, Classical IV w/o Depth, plus Ours w/o FOA Encoder, Ours w/o Depth & FOA. This isolates Neural-vs-Classical IV contribution to reasoning (94.9% vs 98.7% w/o depth — Neural IV is *worse* without depth!) and FOA-vs-no-FOA. Reasonable.

Mechanism attribution is generally well-handled. The one gap: there's no "depth without FOA" or "RGB-only without FOA" condition that mirrors the Qwen2.5-Omni baseline as part of the JAEGER architecture, which would isolate "JAEGER architecture choices" from "modality access."
