# Scratch — Strong Linear Baselines Strike Back (f4a3c3d6)

## Load-bearing claims

- **Abstract:** "OLS regression consistently matches or outperforms state-of-the-art deep detectors"
- **Abstract:** "achieves superior accuracy while requiring orders of magnitude fewer computational resources"
- **Sec. 4.2:** OLS lowest average rank across F1/B-F-5/E-F-5; mean E-F-5=0.6963, B-F-5=0.7335, F1=0.9399 (slightly below KANAD 0.9559).

## Construct inventory

- **Construct: "consistently matches or outperforms"** — measured via 3 metrics (F1, B-F-5, E-F-5) on 6 univariate + 5 multivariate datasets vs 21 univariate / 6 multivariate baselines. Multi-axis reporting is good. Sec. 4.3 honestly notes pattern-shape and pattern-trend anomalies favor KANAD; OLS lags markedly on E-F-5 there (Table 3: pattern-trend OLS E-F-5 0.2525 vs KANAD 0.3063).
- **Construct: "orders of magnitude fewer computational resources"** — measured in Appendix E. OLS 3.35s vs Anomaly Transformer 73.65s ≈ 22× (one order of magnitude, not "orders"). Memory: OLS 0.02MB vs typical Transformer ~100MB ≈ 5000× (orders of magnitude in memory).

## Counter-hypothesis list

- **CH1.** Best F1 (used in column F1) is point-adjusted and known to be inflated (Wu & Keogh 2021b — cited by the paper itself). The paper mitigates by also reporting B-F-5 and E-F-5; OLS still ranks 1.83 on E-F-5. Counter-hypothesis substantially refuted.
- **CH2.** Multivariate Table 2 has only 6 baselines vs 21 on univariate. Classical methods (AR, PCA, MatrixProfile) would be applicable on multivariate via per-channel application but are absent. Mild thinning, not a load-bearing omission since multivariate-specific deep methods are the natural comparison.
- **CH3.** RRR uses dataset-tuned rank + window (Appendix B, Table 4: rank ∈ {8,16,32}, window ∈ {32,64,128,256}). The "no-hyperparameter" framing is partially eroded for RRR, though OLS itself uses single window-size (Sec. C). Acknowledged honestly in the paper.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit

The paper claims "matches or outperforms." Table 3 shows OLS strongly outperforms on point anomalies (point-global, point-context) and lags on pattern-shape, pattern-trend. The abstract's "consistently" is mildly overstated relative to Sec. 4.3's honest discussion. Within the paper's own multi-axis reporting, the trade-off is visible.

## Framing-vs-demonstration check

- "Orders of magnitude fewer computational resources" — true for memory (~5000×), borderline for time (~22×). Mild ambiguity.
- "Consistently matches or outperforms" — qualified by Sec. 4.3 honest acknowledgment of pattern-anomaly weakness. Mildly overstated in abstract.
