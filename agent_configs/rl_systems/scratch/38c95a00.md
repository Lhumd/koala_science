## Phase A scratch — Brain Alignment & Abstraction (38c95a00)

### Load-bearing claim inventory

1. **C1 (abstract):** "models construct higher-order linguistic features in their middle layers, cued by a peak in the layerwise intrinsic dimension".
2. **C2 (abstract):** "a layer's intrinsic dimension strongly predicts how well it explains fMRI and ECoG signals". Backed by Fig 2B: ρ=0.76 (fMRI) / 0.43 (ECoG).
3. **C3 (abstract):** "the relation between intrinsic dimension and brain predictivity arises over model pre-training". Fig 2 (bottom) tracks Pythia-6.9b checkpoints.
4. **C4 (abstract, causal):** "finetuning models to better predict the brain causally increases both representations' intrinsic dimension and their semantic content". Backed by Fig 5: WavLM-base-plus layer 9 only.
5. **C5 (Sec. 4.4):** "Id does not cause model-brain predictivity"; Id is "likely an epiphenomenon" of feature-richness. RFF analysis (Fig 6).

### Construct vs operational metric audit

**For C4 — "models" (plural) causal claim:**
- Construct: causal manipulation across "models" (plural, abstract framing).
- Operational: finetuning **WavLM-base-plus only**, on **layer 9 only**, in **2 fMRI subjects** (UTS03 main, UTS02 appendix). Sec. 4.3 verbatim: "we causally manipulate the layerwise encoding performance by directly finetuning WavLM-base-plus—the model whose encoding performance was most driven by low-level input properties—on fMRI for each subject."
- Aspect not measured: causal increase in Id and semantic content for any LLM (OPT, Pythia, Whisper). Single-model causal evidence with the abstract's "models" framing is broader than the demonstration.

**For C5 — "Id is epiphenomenon" / "Id does not cause model-brain predictivity":**
- Construct: Id is a *symptom* (not a *cause*) of feature richness; the underlying mechanism is feature richness.
- Operational: Random Fourier Feature spaces with growing Id achieve only R ≈ 0.04 at high Id, vs LLM layers at lower Id achieving R > 0.1.
- Aspect not measured: a feature space with **fixed semantic content but artificially varied Id**. The RFF comparison conflates Id-source (random vs learned) with Id-magnitude. The data supports "Id is necessary but not sufficient" (which Sec. 5 says explicitly) but not "Id is epiphenomenon" / "Id does not cause" (Sec. 4.4 framing). To establish Id is symptom-not-mechanism, the design would need at least one cell where high-Id and high-feature-richness are **decoupled** (e.g., compress LLM features to lower Id while preserving semantic content; check if encoding performance survives).

### Reward / objective inventory

- Encoding model: ridge regression from layer activations → voxel/electrode response, R as evaluation metric.
- Brain-tuning: layerwise finetuning of WavLM layer 9 against voxelwise responses (Appendix F).
- No proposed-vs-baseline reward comparison; this is a measurement paper, not an RL/NAS paper.

### Counter-hypothesis list

- **CH1: The Id-encoding correlation is driven by base-architecture similarity rather than learning.** Paper rules this out: training-dynamics experiments (Fig 2C-E) show Id peak emerges *over* training (not at init), so it's not architectural prior. Refuted by Sec. 4.2.
- **CH2: WavLM-base-plus's causal result doesn't generalize to LLMs.** Paper acknowledges WavLM-base-plus is the "exception" (Sec. 4.2: "lone exception" where encoding performance was driven by low-level input properties), and chooses to finetune precisely this exception in Sec. 4.3. The choice is reasonable as a worst-case demonstration but does mean the "models" claim has N=1 model. Not refuted.
- **CH3: RFF baseline confounds Id-source with Id-magnitude.** Paper does not address. Not refuted.

### Multi-objective / framing-vs-demonstration audit

- Abstract: "finetuning models to better predict the brain causally increases both representations' intrinsic dimension and their semantic content" → demonstrated on N=1 model (WavLM-base-plus), N=1 layer (9), N=2 subjects. **Borderline framing mismatch** ("models" plural vs N=1).
- Sec. 4.4 title "Dimensionality and encoding performance: mechanism or symptom?" + claim "Id is likely an epiphenomenon" → RFF result supports "Id is necessary but not sufficient" (Sec. 5 wording), not "Id is symptom-not-mechanism" (Sec. 4.4 wording). **Borderline framing mismatch**.

### Headline-number denominator audit

- ρ=0.76 (fMRI), ρ=0.43 (ECoG) global correlations between Id and encoding. Significance at α=0.05 reported. Per-subject results Tables 1, H.1.
- ρ=0.96 (Id vs encoding over Pythia-6.9b training, fMRI). One model.
- ρ=1.0 RFF Id-vs-encoding (perfect, but plateauing at R≈0.04). Asymmetric comparison.

No denominator switches.

### Mechanism-attribution audit

- C5's "Id is epiphenomenon" couples two novelties: (a) RFF as Id-only control, (b) the absence of high-Id-with-semantic control. Attributing the gap between RFF (R≈0.04) and LLM (R>0.1) to "Id is symptom" is mechanism-attribution-lite — without the second control, the claim that semantic richness is the *mechanism* is one of several explanations.

### Soundness score rationale

Strengths: cleanly distinguishes correlation (Sec. 4.2) from causation (Sec. 4.3), transparently identifies WavLM-base-plus as exception, replicates across 2 fMRI subjects + 9 ECoG subjects, includes RFF control (even if confounded).

Weaknesses (all moderate, none Score-2-triggering on a load-bearing claim):
- Single-model causal finetuning supporting a "models" plural claim (mild framing mismatch).
- RFF design conflates Id-source with Id-magnitude; the "Id is epiphenomenon" framing in Sec. 4.4 is stronger than the data supports (the abstract's "cued by" framing and Sec. 5's "necessary but not sufficient" are well-calibrated; only the Sec. 4.4 framing overshoots).
- N=2 fMRI subjects (field-norm).
- No quantitative co-localization test for "Id-peak ≈ semantic decodability peak" (visual inspection of Fig 3 only).
