# DDP-WM (1b797ddb) — Phase A scratch

## Load-bearing claims

1. **C1 (efficiency, abstract):** "DDP-WM achieves an approximately 9× inference speedup… on the challenging Push-T task." Supported by FLOPs (Table 2: 23G→2.5G, 9.2×), throughput (Table 3: 170→1563 samples/s, 9.2×), wall-clock (Table 4: 120s→16s, 7.5×). Genuine.

2. **C2 (closed-loop SR, abstract+Sec. 1 contributions):** "improves the MPC success rate from 90% to 98% compared to state-of-the-art dense models." Supported in Table 1 (DINO-WM 90%, DDP-WM 98%). Attributed to the decoupled dynamics framework (Contribution 1+2: "Disentangled Dynamics Paradigm" + LRM).

3. **C3 (general SR, Table 1):** "matches or surpasses the SOTA dense model on all tasks": Maze 100/98, Push-T 98/90, Wall 98/96, Rope 0.31/0.41 CD, Granular 0.24/0.26 CD.

## Construct inventory

- **"Closed-loop planning success" → SR ↑.** Operational on the 5 sim tasks. Reasonable construct match for benchmark performance, but: no real-robot, no perturbation tests, no generalization to OOD scenes, single fixed-seed eval (Sec. F: "fixed random seed").

- **"Efficiency" → FLOPs, throughput, MPC decision time.** Operational and well-measured.

## Reward / objective inventory

The world-model loss is MSE on foreground/background features (Sec. H). Not really an RL reward setup — this is supervised dynamics learning + CEM planning at test time.

CEM cost (Sec. 3.4):
- Conventional: `LMPC = MSE(ẑT, zgoal)`
- Proposed: `LMPC = MSE(ẑT ⊙ Mtask, zgoal ⊙ Mtask)` where `Mtask` = pixel-diff mask between obs and goal.

The Sparse MPC Cost Mask is **planner-side, not architecture-side**. It is goal-image-conditioned and orthogonal to the world model. It can be applied to any world model.

## Counter-hypothesis list

- **CH1 (mechanism attribution):** The closed-loop SR gain on Push-T (90→98%) is explained by the Sparse MPC Cost Mask (a planner-side cost reweighting), not by the decoupled dynamics framework.

  Evidence from Table 7 (Push-T):
  - LRM ✓, MPC Mask ✓ → 98% (full DDP-WM)
  - LRM ✓, MPC Mask ✗ → 90% (DDP-WM minus MPC Mask)
  - LRM ✗, MPC Mask ✓ → 70%
  - LRM ✗, MPC Mask ✗ → 62% (Naive Sparse)

  The "LRM ✓, MPC Mask ✗" variant achieves 90% — exactly DINO-WM's number. The 8-point gain is fully in the MPC Mask cell.

  CH1 is **unrefuted**: paper does not test "DINO-WM + Sparse MPC Cost Mask". If that variant hits 96%+, the headline SR gain attributable to the decoupled framework collapses to noise level.

- **CH2 (LRM as gradient regularizer):** The LRM may act as a generic feature-space smoother, not as a low-rank-structure capturer specifically. The PCA argument in Appendix A shows the LRM-produced updates have similar PCA structure to ground-truth updates, but a control experiment with a non-low-rank smoothing module (e.g., a small dense correction MLP) would isolate whether the low-rank inductive bias matters or any background-update module of comparable scale would do. Not tested.

## Mechanism-attribution audit

- The paper presents two novelties: (a) decoupled dynamics framework (LRM + sparse predictor + dynamic localization), (b) Sparse MPC Cost Mask (planner-side).
- The ablation Table 7 isolates them on Push-T.
- The headline closed-loop SR claim against DINO-WM (Table 1, Push-T) does not include "DINO-WM + Sparse MPC Cost Mask" as a baseline.
- The "LRM ✓, MPC Mask ✗" row is at 90%, matching DINO-WM. The marginal contribution of the decoupled framework over DINO-WM, holding the planner-side trick fixed (both off), is 0 percentage points on Push-T.
- The MPC Mask alone (LRM ✗, MPC Mask ✓) gives 70%, while LRM alone gives 90%. Both novelties contribute relative to Naive Sparse (62%), but the MPC Mask is doing the load-bearing closed-loop work in the comparison-against-DINO-WM frame.

## Framing-vs-demonstration check

- Abstract: "improves the MPC success rate from 90% to 98% compared to state-of-the-art dense models" → reader interprets the comparison as the decoupled framework vs the dense framework, with the MPC Mask as a small auxiliary detail. The ablation reveals the comparison is dense framework + no MPC Mask vs decoupled framework + MPC Mask, where the MPC Mask is the dominant contributor.
- Sec. 1 Contribution 3 frames DDP-WM as establishing "a new state of the art in both performance and efficiency." Performance gain attribution is unsupported.

## Headline-number denominator audit

- 9× speedup denominator is DINO-WM under matched-environment matched-CEM conditions. Genuine.
- 90% → 98% Push-T denominator is DINO-WM without MPC Mask vs DDP-WM with MPC Mask. Asymmetric setup.

## Soundness score rationale

The closed-loop performance headline (Contribution 3) is offered as evidence for the decoupled dynamics framework, but the paper's own Table 7 ablation localizes the entire 8-point Push-T improvement to the planner-side Sparse MPC Cost Mask — a goal-image-conditioned cost reweighting orthogonal to the world model. The DDP-WM architecture without the MPC Mask achieves 90% on Push-T, identical to DINO-WM. The matched comparison "DINO-WM + Sparse MPC Cost Mask" is silent baseline omission. This is two simultaneous score-2 triggers: (a) silent baseline omission on a load-bearing comparison, (b) framing-vs-demonstration mismatch. The 9× efficiency claim is independently well-supported, but the load-bearing closed-loop SR claim does not survive a fair claim-vs-evidence audit.
