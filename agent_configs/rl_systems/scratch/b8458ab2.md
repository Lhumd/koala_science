## Construct inventory

- **Construct A — "Controlled variation in textual features."**
  - Operational metric: ex-post intensity I(ν, φ) = mean token-level cosine similarity between activations and the relevant decoder column (Eq. 6, p. 5). The IC score is Eq. 9: IC(φ) = I*(φ) · J*(φ), with I* a normalized linear-regression slope of intensity vs. steering factor α (Eq. 7), J* an LLM-as-judge coherence rating (Eq. 8 + App. C, 0–3 scale).
  - **Unmeasured aspect:** *external* validation that the steered text actually exhibits the targeted concept as judged by humans or an independent semantic classifier. The IC metric uses I*(φ), itself defined via cosine similarity to the SAE decoder column W_dec(φ) — i.e., the same SAE direction whose intervention is being evaluated. This is a near-circular evaluation: a feature with a strong projection on its own direction will be deemed "well-behaved." App. B.3 examples are anecdotal and unblinded; the "Insults" feature at Steered (Mid), α=0.2, generates a measured response with no insult content, while the "Marxism" feature at Steered (Mid) produces only generic working-class talk. The IC score ranks these features highly, but humans would not score Mid as exhibiting the targeted concept. The construct gap: text-level concept presence (the thing a researcher actually wants to vary) is never measured; only activation-space self-projection is.

- **Construct B — "Significant bias" and "mitigates estimation error."**
  - Operational metric: |Bias|(X) and RMSE(X) on the **semi-synthetic** simulation in Sec. 8.4 / App. E.1. The DGP is: y_i = μ_0(x̃_i) + τ(x̃_i)·T_{φ,i} + ε_i, with μ_0 and τ randomly sampled compositions of {sin(x), tanh(x), x², x} on partitioned subsets of the residualized embedding columns X̃ (Eq. App. E.1). The "true CATE" is then defined by construction in terms of X̃ — i.e., the residualized covariates the residualization step produces. The headline comparison is between estimating CATE using raw X vs residualized X̃.
  - **Unmeasured aspect:** *real-world* downstream outcomes. None of the three labelled datasets (the civility label on Local Govt Speech, the party label on Political Ads, the populism/discrimination label on Us vs Them) are ever used as the outcome Y in a CATE evaluation. The empirical evaluation only uses synthetic outcomes that are constructed as a function of X̃. Construct gap: a researcher applying this pipeline to real social-science data would estimate a CATE on a real outcome variable; nothing in the paper measures whether the residualization helps in that regime.

- **Construct C — "Naive estimation suffers from significant bias" / positivity violation.**
  - Operational metric: out-of-sample classification accuracy of T_φ from raw X vs X̃ (Fig. 5); |Bias| and RMSE on simulated CATE (Tables 2–9).
  - **Unmeasured aspect:** baseline strategies *other* than naive raw-X full-embedding control. The paper compares against (i) raw X (naive control on full embedding) and (ii) two flavours of residualization. There is no comparison against established text-as-control baselines from the cited prior work — Veitch, Sridhar & Blei (2020) propose adapting embeddings for causal inference; Imai & Nakamura (2025) is named as "particularly relevant to our work" yet not run as a baseline; Gui & Veitch (2022) handles overlap violations; matching with text data (Mozer et al., 2020) is referenced as a baseline option but not implemented. The headline efficacy claim against "naive estimation" elides the comparison against the actual state of the art for the same problem.

N/A — not RL.

## Counter-hypothesis list

- **CH1 (synthetic-DGP self-fulfilling).** The simulated y is constructed as y = μ_0(x̃) + τ(x̃)·T + ε where μ_0 and τ are functions of the residualized X̃, not of the raw embeddings X. Trivially, controlling for X̃ should beat controlling for X — because the true outcome model is a function of X̃ by construction. The "true CATE" used to compute |Bias| and RMSE is τ(x̃), defined on the same X̃. The paper notes the comparison structure but does not run a control DGP where y = μ_0(x_raw) + τ(x_raw)·T to test whether the result is artefactual. Not refuted by anything in the paper. **High-severity unrefuted counter-hypothesis on the headline empirical claim.**

- **CH2 (IC threshold cherry-picking).** Tables 2–5 (in main App. E.2) present results filtered to "Features with IC > 0.5"; Tables 6–9 are "IC < 0.5." On IC > 0.5 the residualization win is clean. On IC < 0.5 (Tables 6–9), the dimension-by-dimension residualization shows enormous instability — Qwen 2.5 7B Instruct |Bias|(X̃^DD) = 379.38 ± 1278.9 and RMSE(X̃^DD) = 1.74e5 ± 5.0e5 (Table 6). Embedding-Gemma rows show |Bias|(X̃^DD) = 219.95 ± 1058.4, RMSE 9.07e4 ± 3.6e5 (Table 7). Local Govt Speech IC<0.5: |Bias|(X̃^DD) = 568.13 ± 1531.9, RMSE = 2.61e5 ± 6.0e5 (Table 8). The residualization method as given is catastrophically unstable on low-IC features but the headline narrative does not disclose this.

- **CH3 (steering coherence is judged by an LLM trained to rate fluency, not concept presence).** The J* coherence rubric in App. C tells the judge to *"disregard profanities, aggressive language, sentiment, tone, or any other stylistic artifacts. Very aggressive or derogatory language should still get a high score."* So a coherent text that lacks the concept the SAE direction was supposed to encode will score high on coherence and high on intensity (because intensity is measured against the decoder column itself). No human-validation step that the steered text actually contains the target concept.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit

Headline narrative: residualization "improves causal estimation." Trade-off side effects unmeasured:
- Variance/instability on low-IC features (see CH2: 5-orders-of-magnitude blow-up in RMSE for X̃^DD on Embedding-Gemma).
- Coverage of true CATE (no calibration of confidence intervals reported; the simulated CATE estimator is point-estimate-only).
- Computational cost of the pipeline (no wall-clock or sample efficiency reported).

## Framing-vs-demonstration check

- Abstract: *"Our empirical results show that our pipeline effectively induces variation in target features and mitigates estimation error..."* — implies real-data validation. Reality: all CATE estimates use **synthetic** outcomes; the labelled outcomes (civility, party, populism) are used only for hypothesis-generation probes, never as the outcome Y of a CATE evaluation.
- Sec. 6.3: *"The IC Score serves as a rigorous metric for identifying well-behaved features."* — implies a domain-general usefulness claim. Sec. 8 only validates IC-as-filter at the IC > 0.5 vs < 0.5 boundary (App. E.2 split); no continuous calibration curve, no human-validated downstream accuracy against the IC score.
- Conclusion: *"Our results provide a rigorous new foundation for experimentation with latent treatments..."* — "foundation" framing for an evaluation that never touches a real outcome variable.

## Headline-number denominator audit

- Bias reduction figure (Local Govt Speech, IC > 0.5, Table 4): "naive" |Bias|(X) = 3.89 ± 1.0 vs |Bias|(X̃^Drop) = 0.78 ± 0.5 → ~5x reduction. **Denominator caveat:** "naive" here is full-embedding regression on the X (high-dim PCA-rotated embedding). It is not "the obvious thing a practitioner would do" — there is no ridge regression baseline, no propensity-score calibration, no overlap-trimming, no comparison against Veitch–Sridhar–Blei (2020). The reader interprets "5x bias reduction over naive" as evidence the method dominates standard practice; the comparison is actually against an unregularized degenerate baseline that exhibits the positivity violation the method is built to fix.

## Mechanism-attribution audit

Coupled novelties:
1. The steering / IC-filtering pipeline (Sec. 5–6).
2. The covariate residualization step (Sec. 7.3, 8.2).
3. The R-learner CATE estimator (Sec. 7.4).

The headline empirical comparison (raw X vs residualized X̃ in Sec. 8.4 + App. E.2) holds (1) and (3) fixed and varies only (2). This *does* isolate the residualization step's effect on the simulated CATE estimator. **However**, the gain may be entirely explained by the synthetic DGP being parameterized as y = f(X̃) — i.e., (2) is what defines the ground-truth functional form, so beating raw X is tautological. An independent control is missing: a DGP where y is a function of raw X (or of an independently-defined latent), residualized vs raw covariates, and check whether residualization still helps. Without this, the attribution that "residualization mitigates bias *in real text-as-treatment problems*" rests on a simulation that bakes the answer in.

## Soundness score rationale

Two score-2 triggers fire on load-bearing claims:

**(i) Circular evaluation on the headline efficacy claim.** The paper's central empirical contribution (Sec. 8, Tables 2–9, Figures 5–7) compares raw-X vs residualized-X̃ control on a semi-synthetic DGP defined as y = μ_0(x̃) + τ(x̃)·T + ε (App. E.1, lines 1011–1018). The "true CATE" is τ(x̃), a function of X̃ by construction. Estimating it with residualized X̃ wins by construction; the simulation cannot test the alternative hypothesis that residualization fails when the true outcome model is a function of raw X. None of the three labelled datasets (civility, party, populism) is used as a real outcome. The Soundness rubric explicitly lists circular evaluation as a score-2 trigger; the softer form ("decomposing M into components and using those components as ground truth") matches almost exactly: residualization defines X̃, X̃ defines the true CATE, residualization "wins" against the alternative.

**(ii) Construct-coverage gap on the IC score and the steering machinery.** Sec. 6.3 claims IC is "a rigorous metric for identifying well-behaved features" for use as treatments. The intensity component I*(φ) is defined as cosine similarity between LLM activations and W_dec(φ) — the very SAE column whose intervention is being evaluated (Eq. 6). The coherence J* explicitly excludes concept-presence judgements from the rubric (App. C: *"You must disregard profanities, aggressive language, sentiment, tone, or any other stylistic artifacts"*). Neither component measures whether human readers would identify the concept in the text. The App. B.3 examples confirm the gap: at α=0.2 on the "Insults" feature the steered output contains no insulting content, and at α=0.2 on "Marxism" the text contains no Marxist references — yet I increases monotonically with α and these features are deemed well-behaved by the IC criterion. The construct ("controlled variation in targeted *text features*") and the metric (activation-space self-projection) are not the same thing.

Either trigger alone is sufficient for score 2. The paper has clear strengths — the formal residualization framework is clean, the LLM/dataset/embedding-model coverage is broad, the catastrophic-instability disclosure is at least present in App. E.2 — but those rescue score 4 from score 3, not score 2 from score 4. Score: **2**.
