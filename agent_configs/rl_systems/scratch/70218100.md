## Construct inventory (per claim, with one specific aspect not measured)
- Construct: "state-of-the-art" generative performance on PDE benchmarks (Abstract).
  Operational metric: relative L2 error (%) at fixed 64×64 discretisation, Table 2 (Section 6.2).
  Aspect NOT measured: behavior at *varying* resolutions — the very property the framework is designed for. Also not measured: 50% of reported Table 2 settings actively contradict the SOTA claim (Darcy Flow forward and inverse, where Finite-dim. SI + ODE wins).
- Construct: "infinite-dim is rigorously needed because finite-dim deteriorates as D → ∞" (Section 1 motivation).
  Operational metric: single fixed-resolution comparison (64×64) in Table 2.
  Aspect NOT measured: a resolution-sweep experiment showing that the proposed method's *relative* advantage over finite-dim SI grows as discretisation gets finer. Without this, the motivating premise is asserted, not demonstrated.
- Construct: "stochastic interpolants" — stochasticity is the headlined feature (paper title, Abstract).
  Operational metric: SDE-variant performance in Table 2.
  Aspect NOT measured: SDE consistently *underperforms* the ODE variant of the proposed method on all four Table 2 settings (Darcy Forward 2.3 vs 1.9; Darcy Inverse 3.7 vs 2.7; NS Forward 1.4 vs 1.0; NS Inverse 6.1 vs 4.6). The ε > 0 stochasticity machinery does not visibly help.

## Reward / objective inventory
Two losses (Eqs. 18, 19): velocity Lφ = E[wφ(t)||α̇x0 + β̇x1 − φθ(xt,t)||²_H]; denoiser Lη = E[wη(t)||z − ηϕ(xt,t)||²_H]. These are the standard Albergo et al. 2023a SI losses lifted to H. The proposed framework's overlap with finite-dim SI is total at the loss level — the only differences are (i) trace-class noise z ∼ N(0,C), (ii) HC-Lipschitz drift, (iii) time-change θ(t) for stability. The "infinite-dim" branding does not change what the network optimises.

## Counter-hypothesis list
- Navier-Stokes wins might be attributable to U-NO architecture vs. U-Net for finite-dim, not to the infinite-dim formulation. Paper uses U-NO for proposed and U-Net for "Finite-dim. SI" (Section 6.2). No apples-to-apples comparison where the same architecture is used for both variants on Navier-Stokes. Section 6.2 itself acknowledges U-Net is better than U-NO for the Darcy discontinuous-coefficient case — by the same logic, U-NO might be better than U-Net for the smoother Navier-Stokes case, independent of dimensional formulation. UNREFUTED.
- Trace-class noise is an inductive bias that smooths gradients during training. Wins might come from this regularisation rather than from the well-posedness machinery. Not tested via a finite-dim baseline with matched smooth (non-isotropic) noise. UNREFUTED.

## Framing-vs-demonstration check
- Abstract: "achieves state-of-the-art results." Table 2 reality: SOTA on Navier-Stokes forward (1.0) and inverse (4.6); LOSES on Darcy Flow forward (1.9 vs 1.1 finite-dim) and inverse (2.7 vs 1.1 finite-dim). The paper achieves SOTA on 2 of 4 reported settings. The abstract does not qualify the claim by benchmark.
- Section 6.2 attribution: "U-NO ... poorly captured by spectral convolutions ... due to Gibbs phenomena ... U-Nets used in finite dimensions are better suited." This is a candid admission, but Theorem 8's HC-Lipschitz requirement constrains architecture choice toward neural operators (Li et al. 2020; Rahman et al. 2022 cited at end of Section 4.2). The "use a U-Net instead" workaround would break the framework's Lipschitz-continuity requirement on the learned models.
- The motivating premise (Section 1) "performance may deteriorate as discretisation becomes finer" is never empirically verified. A resolution-sweep is the canonical test and is absent.
