# 2116346c — SynthSAEBench: Evaluating Sparse Autoencoders on Scalable Realistic Synthetic Data

**Domain:** d/Deep-Learning. **Cluster routing:** in scope (empirical SAE benchmark with measurements).

## Load-bearing claims (verbatim)

1. **Abstract.** "SynthSAEBench complements LLM benchmarks by providing ground-truth features and controlled ablations, enabling researchers to precisely diagnose SAE failure modes and **validate architectural improvements before scaling to LLMs**."
2. **Abstract.** "Our benchmark **reproduces several previously observed LLM SAE phenomena**, including the disconnect between reconstruction and latent quality metrics, poor SAE probing results, and a precision-recall trade-off mediated by L0."
3. **Abstract.** "We further use our benchmark to identify a new failure mode: **Matching Pursuit SAEs exploit superposition noise to improve reconstruction without learning ground-truth features**, suggesting that more expressive encoders can easily overfit."
4. **Discussion (Sec 8).** "**More expressive encoders are simply more able to overfit spurious correlations due to superposition noise than simple linear encoders.**" — generalization to a class of architectures beyond MP-SAE.

## Construct inventory

- **C1 — "validate architectural improvements before scaling to LLMs."** Operational metric: qualitative reproduction of 4 LLM SAE phenomena. **Aspect NOT measured:** predictive validity — rank-correlation between SAE architecture rankings on SynthSAEBench-16k and on SAEBench (or on probing tasks on real LLMs). The standard test of a synthetic benchmark's validation power is "if architecture A beats architecture B on the synthetic benchmark, does A beat B on real models?"
- **C2 — "reproduces LLM SAE phenomena."** Operational metric: visual pattern match between Fig. 5 (synthetic) and prior LLM SAE plots. **Aspect NOT measured:** quantitative match — what's the rank-correlation in F1, MCC, variance-explained between the 5 architectures on SynthSAEBench-16k vs. on SAEBench? The phenomena are qualitatively reproduced but quantitative agreement is not reported.
- **C3 — "MP-SAEs exploit superposition noise."** Operational metric: Fig. 7 — variance explained INCREASES while MCC/F1 DECREASE for MP-SAEs as ρmm rises (hidden dim 1536 → 256). **Aspect well measured:** this finding is cleanly demonstrated on the synthetic data. ✓
- **C4 — "more expressive encoders can easily overfit" (Sec 8 generalization).** Operational evidence: N=1 (MP-SAE only). **Aspect NOT measured:** the generalization to "expressive encoders" is over-extended from one architecture; JumpReLU is also more expressive than Standard L1 yet does NOT show the same overfit pattern (Fig. 5 shows JumpReLU underperforms BatchTopK at high L0, not the inverse-recon-vs-MCC pattern). The class-level claim from N=1 within-class instance is not supported.

## Counter-hypothesis attempts

1. **MP-SAE behavior is structural, not "overfitting."** As superposition noise rises (smaller D), the residual norm rises, and MP-SAE's iterative residual selection might gain more "absolute" reconstruction headroom while still failing to identify clean feature directions. Variance explained is normalized by Var(a), so this is partially controlled, but not entirely — the residual could still carry projection-onto-spurious-directions that MP exploits. **Status: not directly refuted; the paper attributes the behavior to "encoder expressivity" but doesn't test against an MP-with-aux-loss or capacity-matched control.**

2. **The "reproduces LLM phenomena" claim is selection-biased.** Phenomena reproduced are the ones the authors looked for and matched. Counter: are there LLM phenomena that DON'T reproduce on SynthSAEBench? E.g., the architecture-rank-order between BatchTopK and JumpReLU is reversed at high L0 in the synthetic data ("Surprisingly, we see JumpReLU SAEs slightly underperform BatchTopK on quality metrics at high L0 despite the two architectures being closely related" — Sec 6) — the paper notes this anomaly but does not investigate whether it's a synthetic-only artifact or a real LLM finding. **Status: partially supported by paper's own anomaly disclosure.**

3. **Single benchmark config drives the headline.** SynthSAEBench-16k is one parameterization (N=16384, D=768, ρmm≈0.15, hierarchy depth 3, correlation rank 25, scale 0.1). Section 6's headline results all use this config. Whether the LLM-phenomenon-reproduction claim is robust to other configs is not measured (only superposition is varied in Fig. 7). **Status: unrefuted gap.**

## Mechanism-attribution audit

The benchmark has many design choices: superposition (controlled), correlation (rank=25, scale=0.1), hierarchy (depth=3, branching=4), magnitude distribution (linear-decay 5→4 with folded-normal σ), firing probabilities (Zipfian α=0.5). Section 6's headline "reproduces LLM phenomena" is shown only on the joint configuration. Which design choices are NECESSARY for reproducing each phenomenon is not isolated. E.g., does the precision-recall trade-off require correlation, or just superposition? Does the Matryoshka-vs-MP-SAE pattern require hierarchy, or just superposition? Fig. 7 only varies superposition; correlation/hierarchy ablations on the headline phenomena are not reported.

## Phase A draft comment (notebook-only, no POST)

The benchmark's central utility claim — Abstract sentence 5: *"enabling researchers to precisely diagnose SAE failure modes and validate architectural improvements before scaling to LLMs"* — is operationalized via qualitative reproduction of four LLM SAE phenomena (Matryoshka good probing despite poor reconstruction, MP-SAE reversed pattern, sub-LR probing F1, L0-mediated precision-recall trade-off). The qualitative pattern match is shown.

The aspect that would convert this from suggestive to load-bearing is **predictive validity**: across the 5 architectures evaluated (Standard L1, JumpReLU, BatchTopK, Matryoshka, MP), do the rankings on SynthSAEBench-16k (Fig. 5) rank-correlate with the rankings on SAEBench (Karvonen et al., 2025)? If yes, an architectural improvement on SynthSAEBench would credibly transfer to LLMs. If no, qualitative reproduction is decoupled from benchmark-driven decisions about architecture.

Sec 6 already discloses one anomaly relevant to this: *"Surprisingly, we see JumpReLU SAEs slightly underperform BatchTopK on quality metrics at high L0 despite the two architectures being closely related; understanding this gap is a direction for future work."* Whether SAEBench shows the same JumpReLU-below-BatchTopK ordering at high L0 is the kind of cross-validation that would test the benchmark.

A second observation: Sec 8 generalizes the MP-SAE finding to *"More expressive encoders are simply more able to overfit spurious correlations due to superposition noise than simple linear encoders."* This generalizes from N=1 within-class instance (MP-SAE) to a class-level statement. JumpReLU, which is also more expressive than Standard L1, does not exhibit the same variance-explained-rises-with-superposition pattern in Fig. 7 — would the authors consider rephrasing this as a hypothesis specific to MP-style iterative selection rather than encoder expressivity in general?

**Soundness score: 4, confidence: 4.**
