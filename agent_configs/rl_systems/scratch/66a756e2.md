## Scratch — TIMI: Training-Free Image-to-3D Multi-Instance Generation with Spatial Fidelity (66a756e2)

### Load-bearing claim inventory

1. **Abstract / Sec.1**: "TIMI ... achieves *high spatial fidelity*" via a *training-free* framework. Headline numbers Tab.1: best LCD (0.598), best FS-O (0.353), best SSR (0.809), inference ~59s.
2. **Sec.5.2.2**: "Despite being training-free, TIMI consistently outperforms the training-based approach MIDI in both global and local spatial fidelity." Load-bearing because it underwrites the entire "training-free" framing.
3. **Sec.1**: "ISG and SGU jointly promote local instance separation and global layout preservation." Mechanism attribution — the paper credits its two named modules.
4. **Sec.5.2.2**: "TIMI also maintains high inference efficiency (~59s), comparable to the base model Hunyuan3D, and is significantly faster than both the training-based MIDI (~90s) and the compositional DPA (~783s)." Load-bearing speed claim.

### Construct inventory — what is "spatial fidelity"?

- **Construct claimed**: "high spatial fidelity" (abstract, repeated throughout). Defined informally as "accurate global layout and distinct local instances" (Sec.1).
- **Operational metrics** (Sec.5.1, Appendix A):
  - LCD (centroid Chamfer Distance): how close predicted-instance centroids are to GT centroids (Eq. 8-9).
  - CD-S, FS-S: scene-level Chamfer Distance / F-Score on the whole point cloud.
  - CD-O, FS-O: object-level CD/FS — but the paper *never explains* how predicted instances are matched to GT instances for CD-O computation. Hungarian assignment? Nearest-centroid? This is unspecified.
  - **SSR** (Separation Success Rate) = `min(N_pred, N_gt) / max(N_pred, N_gt)`. **This is a count-match metric.** It scores 1.0 whenever the instance count is right, regardless of whether the predicted instances correspond to the GT ones, are positioned correctly, or are even the right objects.
- **Aspects of "spatial fidelity" NOT measured by these metrics:**
  - **Depth ordering / front-back relationships** between instances (no metric for relative depth).
  - **Inter-instance contact / interpenetration** (whether instances correctly overlap, support, or avoid each other in 3D — never measured).
  - **Per-instance correspondence to the input image**: SSR doesn't check identity; CD-O depends on an unspecified matching procedure.
  - **Orientation / pose consistency** of instances (e.g., chairs all facing the table).
  - **Scene-level scale/extent fidelity** (Tab.1 reports CD-S which is a sum of point-cloud distances; doesn't separate size error from position error).
- **SSR is the only "instance distinctiveness" metric** and it is a count-match, not a separation or correctness metric. Lead trigger.

### Mechanism-attribution audit (coupled novelty)

The paper introduces TWO modules (ISG, SGU) and credits both jointly. **Tab.2** ablation:
- w/o ISG, w/o SGU (baseline Hunyuan3D 2.0 with no intervention): LCD 0.627, SSR 0.697, FS-O 0.339.
- w/ ISG, w/o SGU: LCD 0.623, SSR **0.674**, FS-O 0.351. → **ISG alone DEGRADES SSR** vs the no-guidance baseline (0.674 < 0.697). LCD only marginally improves.
- w/ ISG, w/ SGU: LCD 0.598, SSR 0.809, FS-O 0.353.

There is **no "w/o ISG, w/ SGU" condition** in Tab.2. So the paper cannot demonstrate that ISG contributes anything: the only condition where ISG is "shown to help" is the joint condition, where SGU might be doing all the work. The paper's central novelty is "Instance-aware Separation Guidance via cross-attention anchoring", but the only ablation showing it helps is when paired with SGU — and ISG-alone *degrades* the headline SSR metric. Coupled-novelty mechanism attribution failure.

Tab.5 (SGU sub-ablation) shows SGU's components (GM, SR) progressively help; this further suggests SGU is the load-bearing component, not ISG. The naming "Instance-aware Separation Guidance" in the title and abstract is therefore credit-misallocated.

### Framing-vs-demonstration: "training-free" with hidden test-set tuning

Abstract: "*training-free* framework ... no additional fine-tuning". Sec.5.1 lists hyperparameters: l ≤ 4 (ISG cross-attention layer count), t ≤ 15 (ISG timesteps), α = 0.1 (SGU modulation strength), σ = 1.5 (SGU smoothing). These four are introduced as "demonstrating the generalizability of TIMI".

But Tab.3 (l), Tab.4 (t), Tab.6 (α), Tab.7 (σ) are full ablation sweeps over each hyperparameter, and the **chosen value in each case is the one that wins on the eval set** (highlighted/bolded as best). For example Tab.6 shows α=0.1 wins LCD/SSR/FS-S, but α=0.2 wins FS-O — so "best" here means "best on this exact test set". Tab.7 picks σ=1.5 by inspecting the same numbers. There is no held-out validation set. The "training-free" claim is contradicted by **hyperparameter selection on the test set itself** — selecting four hyperparameters on the eval data is a soft form of training, and the "generalizability" claim ("these parameters work well across most cases") is not supported by any out-of-distribution validation experiment.

This is the same pattern as a "no fine-tuning" RL paper that quietly does per-task reward-weight tuning on the eval set. Score-2-tier framing-vs-demonstration mismatch.

### Hidden input requirement: instance masks from Grounded-SAM

Sec.5.1: "Instance masks are extracted using Grounded-SAM (Ren et al., 2024)." The pipeline takes (image, instance masks) as input — not just an image. Fig.1's caption says "TIMI generates multiple 3D instances *from a single image*", and the abstract says "from a single image". The dependence on Grounded-SAM mask quality is silent in the abstract. If Grounded-SAM merges two adjacent instances or splits one, downstream SSR / CD-O would degrade — but no ablation tests robustness to mask quality. The "from a single image" framing therefore overstates input-side autonomy.

### Silent baseline omission

Cited but not benchmarked in Tab.1:
- Tang et al. 2024b/2025 (Diffuscene, geometric/textural consistency 3D scene generation) — explicitly mentioned in Related Works (Sec.2.2) as compositional baselines.
- Zhou et al. 2024 — "Zero-shot scene reconstruction from single images with deep prior assembly" — directly relevant compositional method.
- Rahamim et al. 2024 (Lay-A-Scene) — multi-instance 3D arrangement from text-to-image priors.
- Han et al. 2025 (Reparo) — differentiable 3D layout alignment.
- Yao et al. 2025 (CAST) — component-aligned 3D scene reconstruction from RGB image.
- Ardelean et al. 2025 — generalizable 3D scene reconstruction from single view.

Of these, CAST and Lay-A-Scene are particularly directly comparable. The paper compares only against MIDI, DPA, and Hunyuan3D 2.0 (a single-instance baseline that is not even an I2MI method). Three baselines for a 70-scene benchmark with 7+ uncited recent multi-instance/scene methods is a thin comparison.

### Statistical / variance audit

- 30 synthetic + 20 real + 20 stylized scenes = 70 evaluation scenes. **Zero std deviations, zero confidence intervals reported in any table.** The headline gap on SSR between Ours (0.809) and MIDI (0.737) is reported without any indication of seed variance or per-scene variance.
- **Tab.6** α-sensitivity: LCD goes 0.598 (α=0.1) → 0.523 (α=0.2) → 0.562 (0.3) → 0.611 (0.4) → 0.570 (0.5). **α=0.2 actually beats α=0.1 on LCD by 13%** but the paper reports α=0.1 as "the best balance". If LCD is the headline global-fidelity metric (it leads Tab.1), why is the chosen α not the LCD-optimal one? Inconsistent — picking α=0.1 looks like a post-hoc tradeoff justification.
- **User study**: n participants unreported, blinded condition unreported, statistical test unreported.

### Counter-hypotheses the paper does not rule out

1. **Mask-quality leakage**: TIMI's wins might largely reflect Grounded-SAM mask quality on the chosen scenes; baselines (MIDI, DPA) may not consume masks the same way, so the comparison conflates "ISG/SGU is better" with "the mask input gives TIMI a strong prior the baselines lack".
2. **SGU-alone explains the gain**: as noted, the missing "w/o ISG, w/ SGU" cell in Tab.2 means a smoothing+momentum update on the latent might explain most of the improvement, with ISG's named contribution near-orthogonal.
3. **Hyperparameter overfitting**: the four parameters (l, t, α, σ) selected by sweeps on the eval set could explain the gap to MIDI; cross-dataset fixed-hyperparameter results not shown.

### Soundness score rationale

Multiple score-2 triggers fire on load-bearing claims:

- **Construct-coverage gap on "spatial fidelity"**: SSR is count-only; depth ordering, contact/interpenetration, and per-instance correspondence are not measured. The paper's central construct is operationalized via a count match plus aggregate Chamfer distances.
- **Coupled-novelty mechanism attribution failure**: ISG (the title's headline contribution) alone *degrades* SSR vs the baseline (Tab.2: 0.674 < 0.697); the paper credits ISG without testing the SGU-alone condition that would isolate the actual mechanism.
- **Framing-vs-demonstration mismatch on "training-free"**: four hyperparameters selected by eval-set sweeps (Tabs.3, 4, 6, 7); no held-out validation set; "from a single image" framing hides the Grounded-SAM mask requirement.
- **Silent baseline omission**: 6+ multi-instance/scene generation methods cited in Related Works but absent from Tab.1; only one I2MI method (MIDI) is benchmarked.
- **Headline metric variance not reported**: 70-scene benchmark with no error bars, no seeds, no significance test.

Score: **2**. Confidence: **5** — every assertion above is anchored to a specific table cell or sentence in the paper.
