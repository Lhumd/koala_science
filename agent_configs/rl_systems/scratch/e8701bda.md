# Scratch — e8701bda — "Do Diffusion Models Dream of Electric Planes?"

## Load-bearing claim inventory
1. **Abstract / Contributions**: "Our approach rediscovers known trends and governing physical laws in aircraft design, while significantly accelerating design generation." Supported by Sec. 5.3.2 case studies (qualitative) and Sec. 5.3.1 / App. G (10× speedup).
2. **Contributions**: "MaskeDiT's topology-conditioned masking supports inference over variable-dimensional design spaces, extending SBI beyond fixed-structure simulators." Supported by Tab. 1 (MaskeDiT vs NLE).
3. **Posterior predictive performance**: "in all cases the posterior distribution's mean agrees closely to the input observed value" (Sec. 5.3.1), Fig. 5.
4. **Marginal sampling fidelity**: KL=0.0505 vs uniform 1.4607 (Sec. 5.1).
5. **Pushing SBI to D=136** (Sec. 2).

## Construct inventory + aspect-not-measured

### Claim: "significantly accelerating design generation"
- Operational metric: wall-clock for 100 unconditional samples — App. G: SUAVE 2523s on Intel Xeon E5-2680 v4 (14 cores, CPU-only, no MPI) vs MixeDiT-MaskeDiT 195.6s on A100-PCIe-40GB GPU "with the majority of time devoted to loading the model checkpoints" — quoted self-described 10× in Sec. 5.3.1.
- **Aspect not measured**: same-hardware comparison (MixeDiT-MaskeDiT on the Xeon CPU, OR SUAVE on… well SUAVE is CPU-only — so a fair benchmark would amortize model-load overhead across larger batches, isolate kernel time, or compare per-design throughput on equalized FLOPs). The paper's own footnote "majority of time devoted to loading the model checkpoints" admits the headline number is overhead-dominated.
- **Aspect not measured**: end-to-end pipeline cost in the realistic deployment setting. Sec. 5.3.1 uses SUAVE itself to verify posterior predictive performance — the diffusion model produces *candidate* designs whose physical validity is asserted only via SUAVE re-simulation. So the actual workflow is `MixeDiT-MaskeDiT (sample) → SUAVE (verify)`, not `MixeDiT-MaskeDiT replaces SUAVE`. The speedup claim is for the sampler component alone, not for the design-generation pipeline as framed.
- **Aspect not measured**: "more significant for larger batch sizes" is claimed verbatim with zero supporting curve / table.

### Claim: "demonstrating strong multi-topology generalization with only a modest tradeoff in per-topology fidelity" (Sec. 5.2)
- Operational metric: Tab. 1 (MaskeDiT vs NLE on 10 topologies with >200 test points each).
  - MMD: MaskeDiT 0.004±0.001 vs NLE 0.002±0.001 → NLE 2× better
  - Joint C2ST: 0.777±0.040 vs 0.744±0.023 → NLE better
  - Mean Marg. C2ST: 0.614±0.016 vs 0.596±0.010 → NLE better
  - Max Marg. C2ST: 0.770±0.076 vs 0.818±0.043 → MaskeDiT better
- App. H: "Using a 5% rejection rate we find that the better MMD found in NLE is significant."
- **Aspect not measured**: zero-shot generalization to held-out topologies (the actual operational value of unification — could MaskeDiT score on a topology absent from training?). The reported comparison is all in-distribution.
- **Aspect not measured**: total compute cost — NLE requires 144 separate models in principle. The paper gives no training-time accounting that would make the "unification value" quantitative.
- **Framing-vs-demonstration**: "comparable C2ST scores" + "modest tradeoff" understates that NLE is statistically-significantly better (per the paper's own p-value test) on MMD and wins 3 of 4 C2ST variants.

### Claim: "rediscovers known trends and governing physical laws"
- Operational metric: 4 case studies (A–D) showing pairplots and 3D renders of conditional samples.
- **Aspect not measured**: any quantitative agreement metric between diffusion-model trend and physics-based ground truth. E.g., for the recovered "near-quadratic relationship between lift coefficient and drag coefficient" (CD = CD,0 + KCL²) in Case B, no fitted K, no residual, no comparison to physics. The trends are visual, not measured.

### Claim: "in all cases the posterior distribution's mean agrees closely to the input observed value" (Sec. 5.3.1, Fig. 5)
- Operational metric: posterior mean of 3 of 10 observation variables (Avg. Lift, Total Mass, State of Charge).
- **Aspect not measured**: 7 of 10 observation variables. App. F. quietly notes "wing weight may be the variable that is least well predicted by the model" without reporting the gap quantitatively, and never extends Fig. 5 to the other 7 observations.
- **Aspect not measured**: posterior calibration. SBI standard practice (cited Deistler 2025, Lueckmann 2021) requires SBC, TARP, or coverage tests; the paper reports only the *mean* of one-shot conditional samples. Mean alignment ≠ calibrated posterior.

## Counter-hypothesis list
- For the speedup: hardware mismatch (CPU vs A100 GPU) trivially explains the gap. A same-hardware benchmark could collapse the speedup. Unrefuted.
- For "rediscovers physical laws": the dataset itself was generated from SUAVE physics. The model is trained to fit the data distribution, so reproducing the trends present in the training distribution is data-fitting, not "rediscovery". The paper offers no held-out-physics test (e.g., trends absent from training data).
- For NLE comparison: NLE could plausibly be tuned (the paper used "default hyperparameters" per App. H) — undertuned baseline.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit
- The "unification" value of MaskeDiT comes at a per-topology fidelity cost. Paper acknowledges qualitatively but does not measure the converse axis NLE doesn't span (cross-topology transfer).

## Framing-vs-demonstration check
- Abstract: "significantly accelerating design generation". Sec. 5.3.1: speedup comparison includes model-loading overhead and is CPU-vs-GPU. The framing implies pipeline-level acceleration; the demonstration is sampler-component acceleration on different hardware.
- Sec. 5.2: "comparable C2ST scores… modest tradeoff" framing vs Tab. 1 + App. H confession that NLE is statistically-significantly better on MMD and wins 3/4 C2ST metrics.
