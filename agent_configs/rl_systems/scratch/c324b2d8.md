# Scratch — c324b2d8 (FOPNG, Continual Learning)

## Load-bearing claim inventory

1. **(Abstract / Sec 1)** "FOPNG... preserves old task performance while learning new tasks." Operationalized via "average accuracy on all trained tasks": Ak = (1/k) Σ a_{i,k} (Sec 5.1, Evaluation).
2. **(Abstract / Sec 2.6)** Mechanism claim: projecting in Fisher-space (vs Euclidean) is what matters. Verbatim Sec 2.6: "Yadav et al. (2025) combined natural gradients with OGD by first computing a projection against past gradients in Euclidean space and pre-conditioning the resulting projection by the Fisher information... We show empirically that the projection must occur in Fisher space to respect the natural gradient geometry."
3. **(Abstract / Sec 5)** "demonstrate strong results on standard continual learning benchmarks such as Permuted-MNIST, Split-MNIST, Rotated-MNIST, Split-CIFAR10, and Split-CIFAR100."
4. **(Sec 2.4 / 5.3)** Hyper-parameter stability: "FOPNG achieves more stable performance across hyperparameters, requiring minimal tuning" (Sec 2.4); "unlike EWC, performance is much less sensitive to hyperparameter choice in FOPNG" (Sec 5.3).

## Construct-coverage audit

- Claim: "preserves old task performance" (catastrophic forgetting reduction). Operational metric: Ak averages over ALL k tasks INCLUDING the current task. **Aspect not measured:** backward transfer (BWT) and per-task post-training accuracy on past tasks specifically. A method can improve Ak by being better on the new task while preserving the old ones less. Standard CL papers (Lopez-Paz & Ranzato 2017 GEM, Chaudhry 2019a A-GEM) decompose into BWT/FWT precisely to avoid this confound. Paper reports neither separately; only Ak.
- Claim: "Fisher-space projection is what matters." Operational comparison: OGD (Euclidean projection, no Fisher precondition anywhere) vs FOPNG (Fisher projection + Fisher precondition). **Aspect not measured:** the closest control — Yadav 2025's explicit formulation of Euclidean projection followed by Fisher pre-conditioning — is not run as an experimental arm. The OGD→FOPNG gap confounds (a) introducing Fisher preconditioning at all, with (b) moving the projection from Euclidean to Fisher space.

## Coupled-novelty / mechanism-attribution audit

FOPNG simultaneously introduces two mechanisms relative to OGD:
- M1: Fisher preconditioning of the gradient (natural gradient).
- M2: Projection-space change from Euclidean to Fisher.

The paper's mechanism claim is specifically about M2. Their ablation set is OGD (no M1, no M2), FNG (M1 only, no projection), FOPNG (M1+M2). The proper ablation isolating M2 is "Euclidean projection + Fisher precondition" (M1 + Euclidean projection), i.e., Yadav 2025. This arm is absent. The paper relies on Yadav's self-reported failure (Sec 2.6) as evidence for "must occur in Fisher space" rather than reproducing the comparison.

## Silent-baseline omission

- GEM / A-GEM / Experience Replay are standard CL baselines. Sec 2.5 explicitly excludes them on the grounds that FOPNG addresses "the continual-learning setup in which we do not have access to past task data." But Sec 4.3 stores 80 gradients per task; this is gradient memory, not raw-data-free. The framing-vs-demonstration on memory access is also a soft mismatch — gradients of past-task losses ARE a form of past-task data, just compressed.
- Yadav et al. (2025) baseline (above).

## Hyperparameter-stability sub-claim

- Paper sweeps EWC λ over [10, 50, 100, 400] (4 coarse log-points) vs FOPNG λ over [1e-4, 5e-4, 1e-3, 1e-2] (4 finer log-points). Different sweep granularity per method.
- α robustness shown only on Split-MNIST and Rotated-MNIST (App C), not on the harder Split-CIFAR10/100 or Permuted-MNIST. They fix α=0.5 for all reported experiments — this is single-axis evidence on a multi-benchmark stability claim.
- Sec 5.2: "FOPNG performs poorly on Permuted-MNIST" — yet the abstract lists Permuted-MNIST among benchmarks where the paper "demonstrate[s] strong results." Soft framing-vs-demonstration mismatch.

## Counter-hypothesis list

- The bulk of the FOPNG-vs-OGD gap may come from M1 (Fisher preconditioning) rather than M2 (Fisher-space projection). Unrefuted: no Yadav-style intermediate baseline.
- The Ak gap may be driven by FOPNG learning the new task more aggressively (the trust-region constraint v⊤Fnew v = η^2 explicitly bounds in Fisher metric of new task), with old-task accuracy not directly inspected. Unrefuted: no per-task forgetting curve.
- Permuted-MNIST under-performance attributed to "highly out-of-distribution tasks" (Farquhar & Gal 2018) — but EWC/Adam/OGD work fine on the same benchmark per their own Figure 1, so the OOD argument doesn't explain why FOPNG specifically fails. Unrefuted alternative: the Fisher-orthogonality projection itself fails when consecutive tasks are nearly orthogonal in Fisher geometry.

## Soundness score rationale

Two score-2 triggers fire on load-bearing claims:
- **Coupled-novelty mechanism attribution failure** for the central "must occur in Fisher space" claim (Sec 2.6) — the Yadav-style "Euclidean projection + Fisher precondition" arm is missing, so OGD→FOPNG confounds M1 and M2.
- **Construct-coverage gap** on the "preserves old task performance" claim — Ak averages across all tasks including the current task; BWT and per-task forgetting curves (the standard direct measurements) are not reported.

Plus secondary score-3-tier issues: framing-vs-demonstration mismatch on Permuted-MNIST, single-axis (2-of-5 benchmarks) evidence for the α-stability claim, mismatched λ sweep granularity for the EWC-vs-FOPNG hyper-stability comparison, and silent omission of the Yadav 2025 baseline whose self-reported failure is the paper's main evidence for its central mechanism claim. Score is 2; the rest of the empirical study is competent (5 seeds, CIs reported, hyperparameter sweeps documented in App B), but score-2 triggers do not get rounded up by offsetting strength. Confidence 4 — read methodology, baselines list, App B hyperparameter table, and ablations end-to-end; did not cross-check against Yadav 2025's actual paper.
