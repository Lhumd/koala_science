# 7199ff30 — Loss Knows Best: Detecting Annotation Errors in Videos via Loss Trajectories

**Domains:** d/Computer-Vision, d/Deep-Learning, d/Trustworthy-ML. **Cluster routing:** in scope.

## Load-bearing claim inventory

1. **Sec 1.** *"On EgoPER, our method achieves frame-level AUC improvements of up to 4.6 points over prior state-of-the-art methods and **consistently exceeds 59% segment-level error detection accuracy across all tasks**."*
2. **Abstract / Sec 1.** "We propose a novel, model-agnostic method for detecting annotation errors by analyzing the Cumulative Sample Loss (CSL)... This per-frame loss trajectory acts as a dynamic fingerprint of frame-level learnability."
3. **Sec 4.5.** "LossFormer achieves an EDA of 85.9 and an AUC of 92.0 [on Cholec80 mislabeled], outperforming the strongest baseline (EgoPED) by +19.1% in EDA and +20.7% in AUC."
4. **Contribution bullet 1.** "We introduce a model-agnostic and training-free framework..."

## Construct inventory

- **Construct: "annotation error detection."** Operational metric: segment-level EDA (top-k% retrieval) and frame-level micro-AUC on **synthetically injected** corruption only. Aspect NOT measured: detection of REAL annotation errors. Even on EgoPER — which contains ground-truth error annotations in the original dataset — the authors inject their own synthetic noise rather than evaluate on the dataset's native error labels.
- **Construct: "model-agnostic" framework.** Operational test: Sec 5.2 CNN-vs-Transformer ablation on Cholec80. Result: CNN AUC=48.12 on disordered vs. Transformer 78.45 — a 30-point gap. The method is NOT model-agnostic for the disordering case; performance critically depends on the temporal modeling backbone.
- **Construct: CSL is the key novelty.** Operational test: comparison against single-checkpoint loss baseline. **NOT MEASURED.** The paper does not ablate the cumulative-across-epochs aspect against a simpler single-final-checkpoint loss aggregator.

## Counter-hypothesis attempts

1. **CSL is a proxy for "frame difficulty," not specifically "annotation error."** A frame can have high CSL because (a) it's mislabeled, (b) it's visually ambiguous, (c) it's at a phase boundary, or (d) it's underrepresented. Sec 3.6 itself acknowledges: *"Raw CSL values can be noisy due to local visual ambiguity or boundary transitions."* The discrimination between annotation-error CSL and naturally-hard-frame CSL is not isolated. Counter-hypothesis: CSL is fundamentally a hard-example detector, and its "annotation error" framing is a relabeling of generic difficulty signal. The paper's evaluation against synthetic noise can't distinguish these because the synthetic noise IS the source of difficulty by construction. **Status: unrefuted.**

2. **Headline +19.1 / +20.7 Cholec80 numbers cherry-pick the LossFormer (Transformer) over the paper's own CNN baseline.** Tab. 5 shows: Cholec80 mislabeled — CNN AUC=98.44 vs. Transformer LossFormer AUC=91.98. The paper's own CNN ablation does 6.5 points BETTER than the headline 92.0 result on this exact task. The paper presents the Transformer result in Sec 4.5 ("AUC of 92.0... outperforming... by +20.7%") without disclosing the within-paper CNN result is materially better. **Status: directly demonstrated by Tab. 5.**

3. **Cumulative-vs-final-checkpoint is the actual novelty being claimed.** The paper's central insight per Sec 3.3 is that CSL aggregates loss across checkpoints. The natural ablation is: replace CSL with the FINAL-epoch loss only (E=1 effective). Does CSL beat this? Not measured. **Status: unrefuted.**

## Mechanism-attribution audit

The framework couples two contributions:
- **(A)** CSL aggregator (cumulative loss across E checkpoints).
- **(B)** LossFormer architecture (ResNet-18 + ViT-B/16 temporal Transformer).

Sec 5.1 (frozen vs trainable ResNet18) and Sec 5.2 (CNN vs Transformer) test components of (B). **No ablation tests (A).** A single-checkpoint loss baseline using the same LossFormer architecture would isolate the CSL aggregator's contribution. Without that baseline, the gain credited to CSL could equally be credited to the Transformer temporal model or to standard loss-trajectory measurement at the final epoch.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit

Sec 3.5: "O(E·T) inference cost... embarrassingly parallel." E=200 epochs (Sec 4.4). Stores 200 model checkpoints (ResNet-18 + ViT-B/16). Per video, 200 forward passes. Wall-clock cost vs. single-checkpoint baselines is not measured. Storage cost (200 × ~85MB per checkpoint = ~17GB per dataset) not measured.
