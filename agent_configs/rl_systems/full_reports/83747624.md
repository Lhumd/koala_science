## 2. Construct-validity audit

### Construct A: OOD nature of the prompt dataset (the title-level novelty)

- Operational metric (Sec 3.1, verbatim): t-SNE overlap with HumanML3D in MotionGPT's T5 encoder space + "flow precision analysis (k = 3)" → 3.6098% overlap rate.
- **Single-corpus validation:** only HumanML3D used as the comparison distribution. The 14 baselines were trained on diverse training corpora:
  - HumanML3D + KIT-ML: T2M, MotionGPT, MMM, MDM, etc.
  - AMASS-derived: MaskControl
  - Vimogen-228k: HY-Motion-1.0
  - Motion-X (cited): a major training corpus
  - The OOD characterization w.r.t. HumanML3D does not establish OOD w.r.t. each baseline's actual training distribution.
- **Single-encoder validation:** MotionGPT's T5 encoder is one of many text-encoder choices. Different baselines internally use different text encoders (CLIP-based, sentence-transformers, T5 variants). OOD in one encoder space ≠ OOD in another.
- **Single-tool validation:** t-SNE distortions are well-documented; "overlap rate" in t-SNE space is a noisy proxy for distributional overlap. The supplementary "flow precision analysis (k = 3)" is referenced but not defined in the main text.

### Construct B: LLM-as-judge calibration (load-bearing for all LLM-based scores)

- Operational metric (Sec 4.2.1, verbatim): 6 LLMs evaluated on a 50-prompt subset of the long-sequence multi-stage category, rated by 5 raters (aged 20-28). GPT-5.2 selected as evaluator.
- Tab 2 verbatim (smaller-is-better discrepancy with human reference):
  - GPT-5.2: ENA 1.28, AC 3.16, MOC 1.24, BPU 1.44, PP 1.48
  - Gemini-3: 2.92, 4.16, 2.70, 2.88, 2.58
  - GLM-4.6v: 2.20, 3.59, 2.25, 2.59, 2.95
  - Nemotron: 2.00, 3.79, 2.24, 2.51, 2.81
  - Qwen3: 3.54, 4.48, 2.76, 2.94, 2.92
  - Claude: 1.36, 4.34, 1.64, 1.92, 1.66
- **Single-category validation:** the 50-prompt selection set is drawn only from long-multi-stage prompts. Calibration on Dynamics, Complexity, Interaction-short, and Accuracy categories is not separately validated.
- **Inter-rater reliability not reported** in the main text. With 5 raters of narrow demographic (age 20-28), the human reference may not be representative.
- The selected evaluator (GPT-5.2) is then applied to all dataset types (~10× the validation set size) without re-checking calibration.

### Construct C: "Two datasets that excel in physical and semantic attributes"

- Operational metric: PHC+ tracking success rate (Tab 4): ours 91.0% / 93.8% / 98.0% (Dynamics / Complexity / Interaction); random baseline 68.9% / 71.9% / 74.0%.
- The "tracking success rate >90%" claim is supported by the data. ✓
- This is genuine validation that the physical-quality-selected motions are tractable for downstream physics-based control.

### Construct D: "First comprehensive benchmark for assessing Text-to-Motion models"

- Existing T2M evaluation protocols (HumanML3D test set, KIT-ML test set, MotionGPT/MaskControl/MDM evaluation suites) already function as benchmarks.
- The more accurate framing (used in the abstract) is "first benchmark specifically designed for OOD evaluation". The "first comprehensive" Sec 1 wording over-claims.

## 3. Counter-hypothesis attempts

### CH1: OOD characterization is encoder-specific and corpus-specific.

- Supporting evidence (Sec 3.1): only HumanML3D + MotionGPT T5 encoder used.
- **Status:** unrefuted. The OOD validation is narrow relative to the breadth of baselines evaluated. A prompt OOD vs HumanML3D in MotionGPT's T5 space may be in-distribution vs MaskControl's training data in MaskControl's encoder space.

### CH2: GPT-5.2 evaluator was selected on a narrow prompt category and applied broadly.

- Supporting evidence (Sec 4.2.1, Tab 2): selection done on 50 long-multi-stage prompts; evaluator applied to all dataset types.
- **Status:** unrefuted. Generalization is assumed without re-validation.

### CH3: t-SNE-based overlap is a noisy proxy.

- t-SNE is well-known to distort high-dim distances. The "overlap rate" computed from t-SNE projections is sensitive to perplexity, learning rate, and initialization.
- **Status:** acknowledged as a soft methodological concern. Not a sharp critique.

### CH4: Manual quality control of the prompt dataset is the actual quality guarantee.

- Sec 3.1: "All prompts underwent LLM-based refinement for linguistic naturalness followed by manual quality filtering."
- This is reasonable QA, not a Soundness concern. Counter-defense to over-emphasizing the OOD-validation gap.

## 4. Multi-objective trade-off audit

For a benchmark paper this is less central. The multi-factor framework (semantic alignment, generalizability, physical quality, fine-grained accuracy) covers multiple axes appropriately. The radar charts (Fig 4-5) show baselines have distinct strengths/weaknesses, suggesting the benchmark differentiates models effectively.

## 5. Mechanism-attribution audit

For a benchmark paper, "mechanism" attribution is replaced by "does the benchmark differentiate models?" Yes — Tab 3 shows Vimogen and HY achieve top scores across categories, while different baselines win different sub-metrics (Sec 4.2.1: SALAD wins on Matching Score, MMM/MDM/MotionGPT win on R-Pre@1, etc.). The benchmark exhibits useful differentiation.

## 6. Framing-vs-demonstration audit

- **Framing (Title + Abstract):** "OOD text-to-motion generation" benchmark
- **Demonstration:** OOD validated only vs HumanML3D in one encoder space; not validated vs all baselines' training distributions or in alternative encoder spaces.
- **Mismatch severity:** narrow validation but defensible methodology; the paper does not over-claim that the dataset is OOD vs every possible distribution, only that t-SNE overlap with HumanML3D is low.

- **Framing (Sec 1):** "first comprehensive benchmark for assessing Text-to-Motion models"
- **Demonstration:** existing T2M evaluation protocols already serve as benchmarks. The "first comprehensive" framing over-claims; the "first OOD" framing is honest.

## 7. Headline-number denominator audit

- "1,025 prompts", "14 baselines", "tracking success rate >90%" — supported by the corresponding tables. No denominator switches.

## 8. Weaknesses found

- **[Major]** OOD validation (the title-level novelty) is single-corpus and single-encoder. A prompt being OOD in MotionGPT's T5 space vs HumanML3D does not establish OOD vs each of the 14 baselines' actual training distributions.
- **[Moderate]** LLM evaluator selected on a 50-prompt subset of one category (long-multi-stage) and applied to all dataset types without re-validation.
- **[Moderate]** "First comprehensive benchmark" framing in Sec 1 over-claims relative to existing T2M evaluation protocols.
- **[Moderate]** "Flow precision analysis (k=3)" referenced for the 3.6098% overlap rate but not defined in the main text.
- **[Minor]** Inter-rater reliability of 5 human raters not reported; rater demographic narrow (age 20-28).
- **[Minor]** No seed counts or confidence intervals on baseline scores in Tab 3.
- **[Minor]** GPT-5.2 used both for ground-truth extraction in fine-grained accuracy AND as the evaluator (creates dependency on a single LLM); mitigated by manual verification (Sec 3.3.3).

## 9. Defense paragraph

Verbatim evidence available if challenged:
- **Sec 3.1 (OOD Validation):** "we encoded our prompts and the entire HumanML3D corpus using the MotionGPT T5 encoder. Dimensionality reduction via t-SNE (Fig. 2) and flow precision analysis (k = 3) reveal an overlap rate of only 3.6098%, confirming strong OOD characteristics compared to existing benchmarks."
- **Sec 4.2.1:** "we recruited five raters (aged 20–28) with normal cognitive abilities to independently evaluate the 50 samples. We then averaged the scores across the five raters to obtain the final human preference reference score."
- **Tab 2 verbatim:** GPT-5.2 wins all 5 dimensions of the LLM-vs-human discrepancy on the 50-prompt set.
- **Tab 4 verbatim:** PHC+ tracking — 91.0 (Dynamics), 93.8 (Complexity), 98.0 (Interaction); random 68.9 / 71.9 / 74.0.

If the authors respond that the OOD validation is sufficient because HumanML3D is the canonical T2M training corpus, my counter is: HY-Motion-1.0 (one of the top-performing baselines, Tab 3) is trained on Vimogen-228k (Lin et al., 2025) and ViMoGen also uses non-HumanML3D data. The benchmark's OOD claim should be qualified relative to the specific training distributions of the evaluated baselines.

If the authors respond that LLM evaluator generalization across categories is reasonable to assume, my counter is: the selected evaluator's lead over runners-up varies substantially (Tab 2: GPT-5.2 vs Claude on AC = 3.16 vs 4.34 = 1.18-point gap; on PP = 1.48 vs 1.66 = 0.18-point gap). The category mix matters; long-multi-stage prompts may favor different LLM strengths than short Accuracy prompts.

## 10. Score

**Soundness = 3, confidence = 4.**

One major score-3 trigger fires: narrow OOD validation on the title-level novelty (single corpus, single encoder, t-SNE proxy). Multiple minor flaws (LLM-evaluator selection narrowness, "first comprehensive" framing, undefined "flow precision" metric).

Offsetting strengths:
- 14 baselines is comprehensive coverage
- PHC+ tracking validation gives concrete physical-quality evidence (Tab 4)
- Multi-factor framework (semantic + generalizability + physical + fine-grained) is principled
- Manual quality control of prompts is described (Sec 3.1)
- Benchmark genuinely differentiates baselines (radar charts Fig 4-5)

The OOD construct-coverage gap is the central concern but not catastrophic — the methodology is well-described and the offsetting strengths are real. The score-3-tier critique fires on the load-bearing OOD claim but the paper has clearly well-executed sections elsewhere.
