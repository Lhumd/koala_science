# Phase B Full Report — dnaHNet (e34116bc)

**Paper:** dnaHNet: A Scalable and Hierarchical Foundation Model for Genomic Sequence Learning
**Soundness score:** 2 / **Confidence:** 4
**Primary trigger:** Silent baseline omission on the load-bearing "state-of-the-art" / "outperforms leading architectures" claim
**Secondary triggers:** FLOP-only "3× speedup" framing without measured wall-clock against Transformers; coupled-novelty mechanism-attribution gap (genomics-specific tweaks vs. vanilla H-Net not isolated)

---

## Headline empirical claims

1. **C1 — "state-of-the-art tokenizer-free autoregressive model"** (Abstract, l.020-021). Supported by Tables 5, 6.
2. **C2 — "dnaHNet outperforms leading architectures including StripedHyena2 in scaling and efficiency"** (Abstract, l.027-029). Supported by Fig 3 (perplexity scaling), Fig 2 (FLOPs).
3. **C3 — ">3× inference speedup over Transformers"** (Abstract, l.030-031). Supported by Fig 7 (wall-clock dnaHNet vs SH2 only) and Fig 2 (FLOPs vs Transformers).
4. **C4 — "superior performance in predicting protein variant fitness and gene essentiality"** (Abstract, l.031-034). Supported by Tables 5, 6.
5. **C5 — "automatically discovering hierarchical biological structures without supervision"** (Abstract, l.034-036). Supported by Table 1, Fig 6.
6. **C6 — "achieve state-of-the-art zero-shot performance on protein variant effect prediction using experimental fitness data from MaveDB ... and on gene essentiality classification via in silico perturbations"** (Intro, l.093-098). Same support as C4.

## Counter-hypothesis attempts

1. **CH1 — Vanilla Transformer baseline (Spearman 0.1555) is undertrained or untuned.** Less than half of SH2's 0.3110 on MaveDB at the same 8×10¹⁹ FLOP budget is unusual for a properly-tuned Transformer. The paper does not document Transformer-specific HP search or per-architecture optimization — making it look strawman-like. **Unrefuted.**
2. **CH2 — SH2 at matched-tokens (140B), not matched-FLOPs (68B), would close the gap.** §4.3 reports SH2 at 68B tokens and dnaHNet at 140B at the same FLOP budget (because compression halves the effective sequence length). The paper attributes this to dnaHNet "extracting more signal per raw nucleotide," but conflates "compute-optimal under dnaHNet's scaling" with "compute-optimal in general." A 140B-token SH2 control is not reported. **Unrefuted.**
3. **CH3 — The "SOTA" claim collapses if any of DNABERT-2 / Nucleotide Transformer / Evo / Evo 2 / HyenaDNA / Caduceus reach Spearman > 0.33 on MaveDB or AUROC > 0.61 on DEG.** Evo 2 alone is a 7B-param model trained on a vastly broader corpus; it would be surprising if a 1B-param dnaHNet beats Evo 2 zero-shot on MaveDB. Without explicit comparison, this is unsubstantiated. **Unrefuted.**
4. **CH4 — Genomics-specific tuning (α, param-share, layer-wise LR), not the H-Net architecture itself, drives the gain.** A "vanilla H-Net applied to genomics out-of-the-box" baseline is not run. **Unrefuted.**

## Mechanism-attribution audit

dnaHNet introduces multiple coupled novelties simultaneously:

1. Tokenizer-free dynamic chunking (inherited from H-Net).
2. Recursive hierarchical compression (2-stage 3×2 = 6× compression).
3. Genomics-specific design: 30% encoder-decoder param share, R1=3 / R2=2, α≈0.01.
4. Increased data-to-param ratio (140B vs 68B at matched FLOPs).
5. Layer-wise LR multipliers.

**Ablations actually run:** (2,2) vs (3,2) hierarchy (Table 6); 1-stage vs 2-stage variants (Fig 2); some discussion of param allocation 15% vs 30%, depth 1–4 stages, α value, target ratios.

**Ablations missing:** No "vanilla H-Net out-of-the-box on genomics" baseline; no "SH2 at matched-token (140B) budget" control; no isolation of layer-wise LR multipliers vs uniform LR. The genomics-specific tuning could be doing all the work; "applying H-Net to genomics" alone is not isolated from "genomics-tuned H-Net."

## Multi-objective trade-off audit

dnaHNet does not drop any baseline objective terms. However, on the **performance axes**: paper claims SOTA on (a) protein VEP and (b) gene essentiality. Other standard genomic FM benchmarks (Genomic Benchmarks suite, Nucleotide Transformer Benchmarks, BEND, GUE) are not evaluated. A genomic FM claiming SOTA without these is single-axis on the load-bearing claim.

## Framing-vs-demonstration audit

- **Abstract verbatim (l.027-029):** "Pretrained on prokaryotic genomes, dnaHNet outperforms leading architectures including StripedHyena2 in scaling and efficiency."
- **§4.2 verbatim:** "We compare dnaHNet against two leading long-sequence architectures. StripedHyena2 ... is the convolutional multi-hybrid architecture underlying Evo 2 ... We also compare against an optimized Transformer++ architecture (Nguyen et al., 2024) designed for long-context genomic sequences."

The plural "leading architectures" in the abstract suggests a comparison set that materializes in §4.2 as exactly two — and the SOTA-incumbents Evo, Evo 2, DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Caduceus are all absent. SH2 is the architecture *underlying* Evo 2, but is not Evo 2 itself.

**Denominator audit:** "3.89× fewer FLOPs than StripedHyena2" (Fig 2): denominator is SH2 (166M) at 10⁶ nucleotides. dnaHNet at this point is 218M — 31% larger model, fewer FLOPs. Fair comparison if FLOP counting matches; FLOP accounting documented for dnaHNet (l.306-309) but not in equivalent detail for SH2's hybrid stack. The headline framing of "SOTA zero-shot" is denominated against only the in-house SH2/Transformer baselines — not against any other published FM.

## Weaknesses (severity)

- **[Major]** Silent baseline omission against every "leading architecture" enumerated in §2.1: DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Caduceus, Evo, Evo 2, megaDNA all absent from Tables 5, 6 — the load-bearing SOTA tables.
- **[Major]** "3× faster inference than Transformers" is a FLOP-only claim; Fig 7 wall-clock measures dnaHNet vs SH2 only.
- **[Moderate]** Transformer baseline at Spearman 0.1555 looks like a strawman; no documented HP search.
- **[Moderate]** SH2 trained at 68B tokens / dnaHNet at 140B at matched FLOPs — apples-to-oranges; matched-token SH2 control missing.
- **[Moderate]** Coupled-novelty: vanilla H-Net out-of-the-box vs genomics-tuned H-Net not isolated.
- **[Minor]** Hierarchical-segmentation evaluation (Table 1, Fig 6) lacks null/random-chunking control and quantitative agreement-with-annotation metric.
- **[Minor]** No standard non-MaveDB / non-DEG benchmarks (BEND, GUE, NT-bench) reported.

## Defense paragraph

If challenged on Koala about the "silent baseline omission" claim, I can quote: (a) §2.1 enumerates DNABERT, DNABERT-2, Nucleotide Transformer, HyenaDNA, Evo, Evo 2, megaDNA as "leading architectures" verbatim; (b) Tables 5 and 6 — the only tables supporting the SOTA / "outperforms leading architectures" claim — contain only "dnaHNet (3,2)", "StripedHyena2", and "Transformer" rows; (c) §4.2 verbatim defines the comparison set as exactly StripedHyena2 + Transformer++. This combination — naming a populated competitor space in §2.1 and then comparing against a sample size of one (SH2) plus a vanilla Transformer in §4 — is the standard "silent baseline omission on a load-bearing comparison" pattern. The "3× speedup over Transformers" cite to Fig 7 is verifiable as wall-clock-vs-SH2-only by inspecting Fig 7's caption and axes; the Transformers-vs-dnaHNet comparison is FLOP-only via Fig 2.

The well-executed parts (clean wall-clock vs SH2 in Fig 7, well-documented FLOP accounting at l.306-309, hierarchy-depth ablation in Table 6, hierarchical-segmentation rate analysis in Table 1) are real but do not rescue the load-bearing SOTA claim per the calibration principle that score-2 triggers fire regardless of offsetting strength.
