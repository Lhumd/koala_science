## Construct Inventory

**Construct: "Generalizable safe reasoning" / "generalization"**
- Verbatim claim: "RAPO successfully generalizes multiple LRMs' safe reasoning adaptively across diverse attack prompts" (Abstract); "generalizing defenses across jailbreak attacks of varying complexity" (Contributions, p.3)
- Operational metric: ASR on WildJailbreak (WJ) and SorryBench (with attacks), plus JailbreakBench (JBB) and HarmBench for harmlessness
- **Unmeasured aspect #1:** "Generalization" to genuinely held-out attack types — the training RL data uses WildTeaming [13] prompts (300 samples, Sec 5.1, p.8). WildJailbreak is derived from WildTeaming [13]. The paper states "we replace the harmful dataset with 300 prompts sampled from WildTeaming [13]" for RL training and evaluates on WildJailbreak [13] — these are drawn from the same source distribution. This is a train-test distribution overlap: the "held-out" evaluation benchmark shares its source with the training data, meaning WildJailbreak ASR improvements may reflect memorization rather than generalization to unseen attack patterns.
- **Unmeasured aspect #2:** No evaluation on entirely novel, structurally distinct attack families not present in StrataSword or WildTeaming — e.g., multi-modal attacks, cipher-based attacks, or attacks not represented in the training taxonomy.
- **Unmeasured aspect #3:** Generalization across domains/topics beyond those covered in the 300 WildTeaming training prompts.

**Construct: "Preserving general utility"**
- Verbatim claim: "this enhanced safety and robustness do not come at the expense of the model's overall utility" (Intro, p.2)
- Operational metric: XsTest compliance rate (over-refusal) + MMLU-Pro accuracy
- **Unmeasured aspect:** Instruction-following on open-ended, free-form generation tasks; code generation; mathematical reasoning quality; helpfulness for edge-case but legitimate requests. MMLU-Pro is a multiple-choice benchmark — it captures factual recall but not generative helpfulness or instruction-following compliance on benign task diversity.

**Construct: "Safe reasoning adequacy" (the reward signal)**
- Verbatim definition: "The reasoning trace is safety-relevant and perfectly matched to the risk level (no undertthinking or overthinking)" → Reward = 1 (Adequate); "The reasoning trace is safety-related but inadequate (superficial analysis fails to uncover core risks)" → Reward = 0 (Fair); "Excessive" → 0; "Invalid" → -1 (Table 3)
- Operational metric: LLM-as-a-Judge (base LRM with system prompt shown in Figure 5) evaluates extracted safe reasoning traces
- **Unmeasured aspect:** Whether the adequacy judgment aligns with actual defense success. The reward judge is the same base model (f0) used for training. Section 4.4 states: "we leverage the original base LRM f0 with system prompts (details in Appendix C) to build the agents and obtain the rewards." The model being evaluated IS the same model family (or instance) providing the reward signal — a form of self-rewarding that can drift. No validation of judge accuracy against human annotation or an independent safety classifier is provided.

**Construct: "Safe reasoning" proportion (motivating construct)**
- Verbatim: "we split the thinking content into individual sentences and determine whether each sentence is safety-related" (Sec 3.3, p.5)
- Operational metric: Keyword-matching pipeline (Appendix B, Table 8) — a sentence is "safe reasoning" if it contains any keyword from: "harm", "danger", "risk", "toxicity", "malicious", "illegal", "violence", "abuse", "hate", "discrimination", "ethic", "careful", "can't", "cannot", "safe", "should not", "offensive", "inappropriate", "caution", "warn", "refrain", "avoid"
- **Unmeasured aspect:** Keyword presence does not verify that the reasoning is substantively safety-relevant — a sentence like "I should avoid making this too long" would be classified as safe reasoning (contains "avoid"). The construct "quality of safe reasoning" is reduced to keyword count. The paper uses this flawed proxy to construct the entire motivating empirical observation in Table 1 (that safe reasoning proportion correlates with refusal success).

## Reward / Objective Inventory

**RAPO reward (Algorithm 1, Sec 4.3):**
- Total reward: A_p = R(s_p) + G(r_p)
- R(s_p) ∈ {−1, 0, 1}: Risk-aware reward on safe reasoning traces (adequacy judgment by LLM judge)
- G(r_p) ∈ {±1}: General reward — binary: +1 if response refuses harmful prompt (or answers benign helpfully), −1 otherwise

**Compared to baselines (Sec 5.1):**
- TARS [14]: fine-grained safe dimension decomposition reward
- IPO [45]: corrective interventions on reasoning trajectories (preference learning)
- GRPO: standard general reward only (no risk-aware component)
- STAR, Intent-Aware: SFT-based

**Mechanism attribution issue:** RAPO = SFT warm-up + GRPO + risk-aware reward + general reward. GRPO is included as a baseline ("we also considered directly using GRPO to optimize the general reward as a baseline"). Table 5 ablation compares: Base, RAPO-SFT, RAPO. Missing ablations:
1. GRPO + General Reward only (this IS included as "GRPO" in Table 4)
2. RAPO-SFT + General Reward (no risk-aware reward) — NOT directly isolated in Table 5 as such; Table 5 compares "Risk-Aware Reward" (ablate general reward) vs "General Reward" (ablate risk-aware reward), but these are within the full RAPO RL stage, not isolating the SFT contribution to each
3. Whether the SFT format alignment alone (without the specific risk-aware RL) explains the WJ improvement — RAPO-SFT already gets WJ ASR = 36.1% vs Base 70.9% (Table 5), showing SFT alone provides substantial WJ reduction

**Key observation from Table 5 ablation:**
- RAPO-SFT WJ ASR: 36.1%
- RAPO (full) WJ ASR: 15.8%
- "Risk-Aware Reward only" WJ ASR: 34.4% (ablating general reward → very close to RAPO-SFT alone)
- "General Reward only" WJ ASR: 22.3%

This means: the general reward alone (without the paper's claimed novel mechanism, risk-aware reward) already achieves 22.3% WJ ASR — closer to the full RAPO's 15.8% than the SFT-only baseline. The specific mechanism RAPO claims (risk-aware reward calibrating safe reasoning length/depth to attack complexity) is partially redundant with simply adding a safety-oriented general reward on top of SFT.

## Counter-Hypothesis List

**Counter-hypothesis 1 (Train-test overlap):** The WildJailbreak evaluation set is derived from WildTeaming [13], which is also the source of 300 RL training prompts. Framing this as "generalization" to novel attacks is misleading — the model may be learning to recognize WildTeaming's specific attack patterns rather than generalizing to structurally distinct threats. The paper does not report ASR on attack datasets whose construction methodology is entirely distinct from the training distribution.
- Ruled out? No. The paper does not acknowledge or address this overlap.

**Counter-hypothesis 2 (Keyword-based safe reasoning metric is confounded):** The motivating empirical claim in Table 1 (that higher safe reasoning proportion correlates with successful refusal) uses keyword matching. A model could increase keyword coverage in thinking traces without substantively reasoning about safety — the correlation might reflect formatting artifacts (refusal responses systematically using more hedging language) rather than genuine safety reasoning quality.
- Ruled out? Not directly. The paper notes it "found has sufficiently good accuracy" for this classification (Appendix B) without providing human-agreement rates.

**Counter-hypothesis 3 (Self-rewarding drift):** The risk-aware reward judge uses the base LRM f0 (Figure 5, Sec 4.4). As the policy improves during RL, the same base model f0 acts as judge. The paper does not provide evidence that the judge's adequacy assessment remains calibrated across training iterations (Table 6 shows distribution of judgments in the final epoch but not across training, and does not validate against human assessment).
- Ruled out? No. Only final-epoch distribution shown (Table 6).

**Counter-hypothesis 4 (Benchmark saturation explains JBB/HarmBench 0%):** JailbreakBench and HarmBench ASRs of 0.0% for Qwen-8B and Qwen-1.7B could reflect that these benchmarks have been saturated (most strong baselines also reach 0%). The paper's headline improvement is primarily on WildJailbreak; JBB/HarmBench 0% is shared by STAR, IPO, and Intent-Aware (Table 4). This makes WildJailbreak the load-bearing claim — which is also the train-test overlap concern above.
- Ruled out? Partially — the paper acknowledges JBB 0% is matched by some baselines; WJ is highlighted as the differentiator (Sec 5.2, p.9).

**Counter-hypothesis 5 (SFT format priming explains much of the gain):** The safe reasoning format (placing safety analysis at the start of thinking content) constrains the model's behavior structurally. Any SFT that instills this format may produce similar WJ improvements, independent of the risk-aware reward. RAPO-SFT achieves WJ 36.1% vs RAPO full 15.8% — the SFT step already delivers more than half of the improvement from Base (70.9%).
- Ruled out? No explicit ablation isolates the format effect from the content effect of the SFT stage.

## Multi-Objective Trade-Off Audit

The paper evaluates three dimensions: Harmlessness (JBB, HarmBench ASR), Robustness (SorryBench, WildJailbreak ASR), and Utility (XsTest, MMLU-Pro).

**What is NOT measured:**
- Instruction-following quality on open-ended generation tasks
- Calibration of over-refusal on domain-specific benign queries (e.g., medical, legal, security research contexts where the model might now over-refuse due to heightened safety sensitivity)
- Latency/throughput impact of longer safe reasoning traces — the model generates more thinking tokens on complex prompts; the paper does not report inference cost or wall-clock time comparison
- Quality degradation in reasoning tasks that require engaging with potentially sensitive topics legitimately (e.g., analysis of historical atrocities, fictional violence in literature)

The XsTest benchmark checks for exaggerated safety behaviors on clearly benign queries, but it is a narrow test set. The paper does not test on more adversarial benign scenarios where safety training typically regresses.

## Mechanism-Attribution Audit

RAPO has two coupled novel components:
1. **SFT warm-up with risk-complexity-labeled data** (format alignment + prior on safe reasoning granularity)
2. **Risk-aware RL reward** (LLM judge evaluates safe reasoning adequacy vs. attack complexity)

These are introduced jointly. Ablations in Table 5 compare:
- Base → RAPO-SFT → RAPO (sequential addition)
- Within RAPO: Risk-Aware Reward only vs. General Reward only (within the RL stage)

**Missing ablation:** RAPO-SFT + standard GRPO without risk-aware reward, but WITH SFT-instilled format. This would isolate whether the risk-aware reward adds value beyond: (a) the SFT format, and (b) standard binary safety reward. The "General Reward only" ablation in Table 5 uses the SFT-initialized model + GRPO + general reward, which IS roughly this ablation — and achieves WJ 22.3% vs RAPO 15.8%. So the marginal contribution of the specific risk-aware reward (beyond SFT + general reward) is approximately 22.3% → 15.8% = 6.5pp on WildJailbreak.

**The paper's claimed mechanism** is that adaptively scaling safe reasoning depth to attack complexity drives the gains. But the ablation shows:
- SFT format alone: 70.9% → 36.1% (major reduction)
- Adding general reward: 36.1% → 22.3% (secondary reduction)
- Adding risk-aware reward on top: 22.3% → 15.8% (marginal reduction)

The risk-aware reward — the claimed differentiating mechanism — provides only the smallest increment. Attribution of generalization to the risk-aware calibration mechanism is weakly supported.

**Primary trigger (Score 2): Train-test distribution overlap on the load-bearing "generalization" claim.**

The paper's central contribution is "generalizable safe reasoning." The primary evidence for generalization on complex attacks is WildJailbreak ASR improvement. The training RL data is 300 prompts from WildTeaming [13]. WildJailbreak IS the WildTeaming benchmark [13] (same reference, same authors, same paper). Table 1's correlation between "safe reasoning proportion" and defense success uses keyword matching (Appendix B, Table 8) — a sentence containing "avoid" or "cannot" counts as "safe reasoning" regardless of content. The risk-aware reward — the claimed core mechanism — provides only 6.5pp marginal improvement on WJ over SFT + general reward (Table 5). The bulk of the gain is attributable to SFT format priming, not the novel risk-aware calibration.
