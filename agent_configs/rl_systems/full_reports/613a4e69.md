## 2. Construct-validity audit

### C1: "Out-of-distribution improvements (+4.7%)"
- **Construct claimed:** OOD generalization beyond training distribution.
- **Operational metric:** average accuracy across {AIME24, AIME25, AMC, MATH500, Minerva, Olympiad} after training on DeepScaler-5K.
- **Aspect not measured:** distributional separation between training and eval. AIME, AMC, MATH500, etc. are standard math reasoning benchmarks with substantial overlap (in topic, in problem-construction patterns) with the kind of problems in DeepScaler. The paper has no held-out non-math domain or any quantitative distance measurement. The "OOD" framing is loose.

### C2/C3: "Consistently outperforms" / "AIME +12.3% avg"
- **Construct claimed:** consistent improvement across math reasoning tasks.
- **Operational metric:** per-benchmark accuracy in Tab. 1.
- **Aspect not measured:** statistical significance / seed variance. AIME24 = 30 problems, AIME25 = 30 problems. With 16 rollouts averaged per question, the per-question pass rate has nontrivial Monte Carlo noise; the per-benchmark accuracy then averages 30 noisy point estimates. Single-seed reporting on 30-problem benchmarks should be flagged. No bootstrap CIs reported.
- **Construct violation:** "consistently outperforms" is contradicted by Minerva: GRPO 41.5 vs P²O Teacher-Ref 36.4 (-5.1). One regression on six benchmarks ≠ "consistently."

### C4: "Context distillation is a prerequisite"
- **Construct claimed:** training on x̃ instead of x is fundamentally bad.
- **Operational metric:** Tab. 2 ablation: P²O w/o context distillation = 55.6 vs P²O = 65.2 vs GRPO = 60.5.
- This is the single best-supported sub-claim in the paper. The ablation's prediction (template dependency at eval time) is internally consistent: if the model learns to rely on x̃ at training, it fails at eval where x is presented. The 55.6 < GRPO 60.5 result is striking and the mechanism is plausible.

### C5: "Group prompt diversity helps"
- 65.2 vs 64.2 = +1.0 avg gain. Within the noise band suggested by AIME's small sample sizes.
- AIME24: +2.5, AIME25: +4.8. Larger but still uncontrolled for seed.

## 3. Reward / objective audit

| Component | P²O | GRPO baseline |
|---|---|---|
| Reward function | binary, `\boxed{}` + correctness (Sec 4.1) | identical |
| KL penalty | excluded ("one-step off-policy strategy, excluding the KL divergence penalty," Sec 4.1) | excluded (matched) |
| Group relative advantage | yes (GRPO core) | yes |
| Hard-sample mining (gradient on x, rollout on x̃ for hard subset) | yes — novel | no |
| GEPA prompt evolution | yes — novel | no |
| Mutation operator | Qwen3-4B (Self-Ref) OR **Kimi-K2** (Teacher-Ref) | n/a |
| Context distillation | yes — novel | no |

The proposed method's reward is identical to GRPO's. The "consistently outperforms" gain is therefore not reward-explained in the trivial sense; the gain comes from the additional mechanisms.

The Kimi-K2 mutation operator in Teacher-Ref is the central confound: it injects external-model trajectory information into the policy training signal. The paper presents Self-Ref as an internally-consistent variant, which lets the reader decompose the contributions, but the headline +4.7% number is the Teacher-Ref configuration.

## 4. Counter-hypothesis attempts

### CH1: Teacher-distillation explains the headline gain
**Hypothesis:** the +4.7% on DeepScaler is mostly access to Kimi-K2 as a stronger external CoT source, not the joint-optimization mechanism.

**Decomposition (Tab. 1, DeepScaler-5K):**
- GRPO: 60.5
- Self-Ref (Qwen3-4B mutator): 62.4 → joint-optimization contribution = +1.9
- Teacher-Ref (Kimi-K2 mutator): 65.2 → +2.8 from teacher swap

60% of the headline +4.7% is teacher-distillation. The joint-optimization mechanism alone delivers +1.9% averaged across six benchmarks.

**Refuted by paper's evidence?** No. The paper does not include a Kimi-K2-only SFT baseline that would isolate the teacher-distillation contribution. The paper presents both Self-Ref and Teacher-Ref but does not decompose the headline number this way.

**Status: unrefuted; central comment-anchored observation.**

### CH2: Evolutionary loop adds nothing beyond a fixed external CoT teacher
**Hypothesis:** GEPA's evolution is overkill; a static high-quality CoT prompt set (or direct Kimi-K2 SFT on hard samples) would match P²O Teacher-Ref.

**Refuted?** No baseline of this shape is presented. The Same-Template-In-Group ablation (64.2) tests whether templates *within a single training rollout group* should differ — but it doesn't test whether GEPA's evolutionary mechanism adds anything over a one-shot external CoT teacher.

**Status: unrefuted.** This is a missing-baseline / coupled-novelty concern.

### CH3: Seed noise on small benchmarks
**Hypothesis:** AIME24/25 deltas (+12.9, +11.7) are within seed noise on 30-problem benchmarks.

**Refuted?** The 16-rollout averaging reduces per-problem Monte Carlo noise but does not give multi-seed CIs. Standard practice for AIME claims in 2025+ is multi-seed runs with std reported (cf. DeepSeek-R1, Llama-Nemotron). Paper reports neither. The ablation in Tab. 2 keeps the same single-seed protocol.

**Status: partially unrefuted.** The +12.9% gain is large enough to plausibly clear single-seed noise on a 30-problem benchmark, but the paper does not establish this. The "consistently" claim is weakened by the absence.

### CH4: Minerva regression is a real signal, not noise
**Hypothesis:** The 41.5 → 36.4 Minerva drop on Teacher-Ref is real and indicates the joint-optimization recipe trades capability on some kinds of problems for others.

**Refuted?** No discussion in the paper. The "consistently outperforms" claim explicitly contradicts this single benchmark.

**Status: unrefuted.** Score-3-tier framing concern.

## 5. Mechanism-attribution audit

The paper introduces three coupled novelties:
- (a) Hard-sample mining (rollout-pass-rate threshold)
- (b) GEPA-evolved prompts as exploration aids
- (c) Context distillation (gradient on x, rollout on x̃)

**What Tab. 2 ablates:**
- (c) is ablated cleanly: w/o context distillation drops to 55.6 (below GRPO).
- The same-template-in-group ablation tests a sub-property of (b) but not (b) as a whole.

**What Tab. 2 does not ablate:**
- (b) holistically: P²O with hard-sample mining + context distillation but with a *random/fixed* prompt set instead of GEPA-evolved → would isolate GEPA's contribution.
- (a) alone: P²O with no GEPA, no context distillation, just hard-sample mining feeding GRPO with longer rollouts → would isolate the mining contribution.
- The Kimi-K2 contribution: Kimi-K2-SFT-only on hard-sample trajectories → would isolate teacher-distillation.

Multiple unrefuted alternative-mechanism explanations remain.

## 6. Multi-objective trade-off audit

P²O and GRPO both optimize the same scalar reward (binary correctness). No multi-objective trade-off in the reward function. The trade-off is in *compute*, addressed in §7.

## 7. Cost / wall-clock audit

The paper does not measure or report:
- GEPA reflection-call counts (Kimi-K2 API calls per iteration)
- Dev-set evaluation cost (300 examples per iteration, Sec 4.1)
- Total training wall-clock or token budget
- Comparison vs GRPO at matched compute

Fig. 3's x-axis "Steps" counts policy-update steps only. The parallel GEPA loop (Alg. 3 with budget Ctotal) is invisible in this comparison. The implicit "accelerates convergence" framing therefore cannot be validated.

This is a moderate cost-claim concern. The paper does not numerically claim "compute efficiency," so it doesn't trigger the score-2 cost-claim-without-measurement rule. Score-3-tier.

## 10. Defense paragraph (anticipated rebuttals)

**Rebuttal 1: "The Self-Ref vs Teacher-Ref decomposition is right there in Tab. 1 — we didn't hide it."**
True, but the abstract foregrounds the +4.7% Teacher-Ref number without decomposition. The Self-Ref number (62.4 → +1.9) is the "joint-optimization-only" delta. Reporting +1.9 in the abstract and decomposing the additional Teacher-Ref +2.8 as "with stronger external mutation operator" would be more honest framing.

**Rebuttal 2: "AIME single-seed is field-norm; many recent papers do this."**
Field-norm doesn't make it sound. DeepSeek-R1 and Llama-Nemotron report multi-seed AIME numbers; the absence here is conspicuous given the paper makes "+12.3% avg AIME" a contribution bullet.

**Rebuttal 3: "Kimi-K2-SFT baseline isn't a fair comparison — it requires Kimi-K2 trajectories at scale."**
Fair, but the paper's own Teacher-Ref already requires Kimi-K2 calls. Matching the Kimi-K2 budget in the SFT baseline is the appropriate fair comparison.

**Rebuttal 4: "Minerva is a known noisy benchmark; one regression doesn't break the consistency claim."**
Then "consistently" should be qualified or replaced with "5/6 benchmarks improve."

## 11. Theoretical context (out of Soundness scope)

The paper formalizes the bilevel optimization in Sec 3.2 and Eq. 3. The decomposition into context-distillation Phase 1 + GEPA Phase 2 is a clean alternating maximization. No theoretical claim about convergence, optimality, or rate is made; this is a methods paper, not a theory paper.
