## 2. Construct-validity audit

### Construct: "complementary strengths" across paradigms

| Method | F1 (avg) | Task | Candidate set provided? | Supervision |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| ClimateGraph | 0.79 (Tab 2) | Node classification on POI graph | **Yes** (manually curated POIs) | Supervised |
| RemoteCLIP | 0.480 (Tab 3 mean of 5 cities) | Tile-level zero-shot classification | No (raw 256×256 tiles) | Zero-shot |
| Rex-Omni | 0.206 (Tab 3 mean) | Detection from raw tiles (8-prompt ensemble) | No (raw tiles) | Zero-shot detection |
| Remote Sensing baseline | 0.418 (Tab 3 mean) | Pixel-wise NDBKI thresholding + morphological filtering | No (raw imagery) | Training-free |

**This is fundamentally not the same operational task.**
- ClimateGraph is given a graph of geolocated POIs and asked to classify each node.
- Image models are given raw imagery tiles and asked to detect kilns from scratch.
- F1 = 0.79 (node classification given candidates) vs F1 = 0.48 (detection from raw) cannot be compared directly.

The paper acknowledges this asymmetry in Sec 4.1 (verbatim quoted in scratch file) but does not surface it in the headline tables, and the abstract's "complementary strengths" framing collapses the distinction.

### Construct: "captures spatial and directional structure"

- Operational metric: F1 vs graph baselines (Tab 2):
  - ClimateGraph 0.79 (anisotropic kernel + bearing-angle attention)
  - SAGEConv 0.78 (inductive aggregation, no anisotropy, no attention)
  - GAT 0.62 (attention, no anisotropy)
  - GCN 0.62 (no attention, no anisotropy)
- Mechanism-attribution failure: SAGEConv (no anisotropy) is 0.01 below ClimateGraph. The "+17pp over GAT/GCN" headline credits "spatial and directional structure" with gains the ablation attributes mostly to the inductive aggregation (or to the multi-projection raster input features, which apply to all GNN models in this table).
- Missing ablation: ClimateGraph with K(θ) replaced by isotropic K(0). Without this isolation, the anisotropy contribution is bounded above at ≤1pp by the SAGEConv comparison.

## 3. Counter-hypothesis attempts

### CH1: The 0.79 vs 0.48 gap reflects task difficulty (node-classification given candidates vs detection from raw imagery), not model capability difference.

- Supporting evidence (paper's own Sec 4.1): "For graph-based learning, ClimateGraph is trained and evaluated on a single global graph constructed across all five countries... For image-based models, ... evaluation is conducted independently for each country using country-specific image tiles. These models operate on local image evidence without access to cross-country spatial context."
- Supporting evidence (Sec 3): POIs come from "manually curated annotations".
- Supporting evidence (Sec 5.3.5): Rex-Omni metrics are "percentage of images with ≥1 valid bbox from 8-prompt ensemble" — fundamentally a detection task.
- **Status:** unrefuted. The paper's own setup description confirms the task asymmetry. The "complementary strengths" framing implies the paradigms are comparable on the same axis when they are not.

### CH2: The anisotropy mechanism contributes ≤1pp; the +17pp gain is from inductive aggregation or input features.

- Supporting evidence (Tab 2): ClimateGraph 0.79 vs SAGEConv 0.78 (Δ = 0.01); ClimateGraph vs GAT/GCN Δ = 0.17.
- **Status:** unrefuted. The paper has no ablation that varies the anisotropic kernel directly. The credit attribution to "directional structure" is supported only against the weaker baselines (GCN/GAT).

### CH3: All four metrics reported as identical for every model in Tab 2 (0.79/0.79/0.79/0.79 for ClimateGraph; 0.78/0.78/0.78/0.78 for SAGEConv; etc.).

- Statistically unusual for a class-imbalanced task with weighted CE loss.
- Possible explanations: (a) micro-averaging on a perfectly-balanced confusion matrix — but the paper says it's macro-averaged; (b) reporting issue; (c) the test set is balanced and macro-F1 = micro-accuracy under specific confusion structure.
- **Status:** worth flagging as a presentation concern. Not a primary critique.

### CH4: ClimateGraph is supervised; image baselines are zero-shot.

- Supporting evidence: Sec 5.1.4 (ClimateGraph trained with weighted CE); Sec 5.2.4 (RemoteCLIP zero-shot via cosine similarity calibrated by logistic regression on 350 training tiles per city); Sec 5.3.5 (Rex-Omni zero-shot detection); Sec 5.4 (RS baseline training-free).
- The image models do receive minimal supervision (350-tile logistic regression for RemoteCLIP), but the bulk of their feature representations are pretrained on natural-image / general satellite corpora.
- ClimateGraph trains on the POI dataset directly.
- **Status:** asymmetry exists; paper doesn't highlight that ClimateGraph has the supervision advantage on top of the candidate-set advantage.

## 4. Multi-objective trade-off audit

- "Complementary strengths" is multi-axis (accuracy + scalability + interpretability + supervision regime). Headline tables compare only on F1.
- Compute cost: RS baseline ≈ 0.5s/tile (RTX 3070); RemoteCLIP 0.2s/tile (RTX 3090); Rex-Omni 8.2s/tile (A100); ClimateGraph **inference time not reported**.
- Supervision cost: ClimateGraph requires labeled POI graph (manual annotation); image models can be deployed zero-shot. This is the most consequential trade-off and is buried in the methodology.

## 5. Mechanism-attribution audit

ClimateGraph's coupled novelties:
- (a) Anisotropic kernel K(θ) parameterized as Fourier series (Eq. 3)
- (b) Geometry-aware attention with bearing angle φ(Δp_ij) (Eq. 4-5)
- (c) Multi-projection raster features (NDVI, NDBI, Spectral indices, etc.)
- (d) k-NN graph with directional bearing edges (Eq. 1)

Available ablations (Tab 2):
- ClimateGraph (full): 0.79
- SAGEConv (only inductive aggregation, no a/b/d): 0.78 — **Δ = 0.01 vs full**
- GAT (attention but no anisotropy or directional edges): 0.62 — **Δ = 0.17 vs full**
- GCN (no attention, no anisotropy): 0.62

The (SAGEConv vs ClimateGraph) comparison isolates the joint contribution of (a)+(b)+(d) at ≤0.01 F1. The "+17pp" framing credits the anisotropy mechanism with gains the ablation attributes to inductive aggregation or input features.

**Missing critical ablation:** ClimateGraph with isotropic K(θ=0) and SAGEConv with multi-projection raster features. Either would isolate the anisotropy contribution directly.

## 6. Framing-vs-demonstration audit

- **Framing (Abstract):** "complementary strengths across graph, foundation, and remote sensing approaches"
- **Demonstration:** Tables 2 and 3 invite direct numerical comparison; Sec 4.1 reveals the comparison is on different operational tasks (node classification vs detection from raw tiles). The framing collapses the task asymmetry.

- **Framing (Sec 6):** "yields substantial improvements over isotropic graph baselines such as GCN, GAT, and GraphSAGE"
- **Demonstration:** SAGEConv (a GraphSAGE variant) achieves 0.78 F1 vs ClimateGraph 0.79 — a 1pp difference, not "substantial".

- **Framing (Sec 6):** "directional relationships ... that are not accessible to tile-based image models"
- **Demonstration:** the directional mechanism contribution is bounded ≤1pp by the SAGEConv comparison. Foundation models like Rex-Omni do encode spatial relationships (via vision-language attention over coordinate tokens) — the framing implies a categorical advantage that the data does not support at the +1pp anisotropy contribution.

## 7. Headline-number denominator audit

- "+17% F1 improvement over isotropic baselines" — denominator GAT/GCN (0.62). Honest in absolute terms (17pp). The framing as "+17%" is ambiguous between absolute (17pp) and relative (27%); both are gains, but only over the weakest baselines.

## 8. Weaknesses found

- **[Major]** "Complementary strengths" framing across paradigms collapses a fundamental task asymmetry — ClimateGraph operates on a candidate POI set; image models do detection from raw tiles. The 0.79 vs 0.48 F1 gap is task difficulty as much as model capability.
- **[Major]** Mechanism-attribution failure on the anisotropy/directional-structure claim: ClimateGraph 0.79 vs SAGEConv 0.78 = 0.01 F1 improvement. The "+17pp" credit to anisotropy is over weak baselines (GCN/GAT) only.
- **[Moderate]** All four metrics in Tab 2 (Acc/Prec/Rec/F1) reported as identical for every model — statistically unusual under class-imbalanced + weighted-CE setup. Worth confirming.
- **[Moderate]** Compute-cost comparison is single-axis: ClimateGraph inference time is not reported alongside the image-model timings.
- **[Moderate]** ClimateGraph is supervised; image baselines are zero-shot. The supervision regime asymmetry is not surfaced in the headline comparison.
- **[Minor]** No seed counts or confidence intervals on F1.
- **[Minor]** The training/test split methodology for ClimateGraph is not described.
