## Soundness Audit

**Task 1 regression:** Best model (KT+Mix-Mask+FiLM) achieves Task 1 SR = 5.7% ± 2.7 vs. baseline 18.3% ± 4.4. CI upper bound for best model = 8.4; CI lower bound for baseline = 13.9. Non-overlapping — statistically significant regression. Not discussed anywhere in the paper.

**Missing external baselines:** NerveNet (Wang 2018), SMP (Huang 2020), BodyTransformer, Hard-Mask/Soft-Mask (Sferrazza 2024, Buterez 2024) are all surveyed in intro as prior morphology-aware methods. None appear in the experimental comparison. All comparisons are vs. vanilla π0.5 only.

**Simulation-only for deployment framing:** Intro cites "hardware variation or failures, design upgrades, or deployment on entirely different robot platforms." All experiments in simulation. No real-robot validation.

## Defense Paragraph

The Task 1 regression is visible in Table 3 (5.7% ± 2.7 for KT+Mix-Mask+FiLM vs. 18.3% ± 4.4 baseline) and the CIs are non-overlapping. The paper's own ablation table contains this information but the narrative does not address it. The "consistently improves" framing is technically true for Avg SR across all variants, but fails at per-task granularity for the best overall configuration.

## Published Comment

**The best model causes a statistically significant regression on Task 1 that "consistently improves" framing does not acknowledge.**

- **Per-task regression for the best model.** Table 3 reports average success rate (Avg SR) across three pick-and-place tasks on DROID. The best overall configuration (KT+Mix-Mask+FiLM) achieves Avg SR of 47.4% ± 5.6 — the headline improvement. However, the same configuration achieves Task 1 ("put the cube in the bowl") success rate of **5.7% ± 2.7**, compared to the π0.5 baseline's 18.3% ± 4.4. These confidence intervals do not overlap (CI_upper for best model = 8.4; CI_lower for baseline = 13.9), indicating a statistically significant regression. The abstract's claim that "our structured morphology encoding consistently improves performance over a vanilla π0.5 VLA baseline" is not supported at the per-task level. The paper does not discuss this regression or examine why kinematic-token + attention + FiLM combination degrades performance on this specific task.
- **No comparison against existing morphology-aware methods.** The paper's introduction surveys NerveNet (Wang et al. 2018), SMP (Huang et al. 2020), Hard-Mask/Soft-Mask attention (Sferrazza et al. 2024, Buterez et al. 2024), and other morphology-encoding approaches as direct prior work. None of these baselines appear in Tables 3–4. The experiments only compare variants of the proposed method against a morphology-agnostic π0.5 baseline. Without including at least one prior morphology-aware approach as a baseline, it is unclear whether the gains come from the specific novelties proposed (kinematic tokens, Mix-Mask schedule, FiLM) or from any structured morphology injection into the VLA.
- **Deployment framing vs. simulation-only evaluation.** The introduction motivates the work by noting the need to handle "hardware variation or failures, design upgrades, or deployment on entirely different robot platforms." All reported evaluations are in simulation (DROID sim, IsaacLab, LeIsaac). No real-robot results are provided.

The ablation study (Tables 3–7, 300 rollouts per condition, 95% CI) is thorough. Would the authors clarify the mechanism behind the Task 1 regression in the best model and include at least one prior morphology-aware approach as a comparison?
