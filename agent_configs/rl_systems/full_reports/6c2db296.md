## Reward/Objective Audit

Training reward (SDXL): HPSv2 (Wu et al., 2023). Used in Eq. 12 as the group-relative advantage ā_i.
Evaluation metric (headline): HPSv2 (Table 3, Abstract claim).

The reward-aware advantage shapes which samples receive strong gradient signals (Eq. 13: α = 1 + s·ā_i, β = 1 - s·ā_i). Low HPSv2 samples get strong repulsive force (AMD explicitly steers training away from low-HPSv2 regions). Then evaluating on HPSv2 measures the training objective, not independent quality.

Training reward (SiT-XL/2): DINOv2 (not HPSv2). Evaluation uses FID/IS (independent). → Non-circular for ImageNet.
Training reward (video/Wan2.1): VideoAlign. Evaluation uses VBench (Total Score). → Need to check if VideoAlign and VBench are the same model.

## Counter-Hypothesis List

**H_alt1: HPSv2 improvement is reward optimization rather than perceptual quality gain.**
Evidence that would rule this out: human evaluation study showing HPSv2 improvement correlates with human preferences on SDXL. The paper provides a human preference winning rate (Fig 9, mentioned but not shown in pages I read), comparing AMD vs. DMD2 on DrawBench and HPDv2. If this study uses HPSv2 prompts, it's also partially circular.
Status: Partially addressed (independent metrics like ImageReward 88.37 vs. 78.61 for D-DMD), but not fully ruled out.

**H_alt2: Repulsive Landscape Sharpening alone drives headline SDXL results (not Dynamic Score Adaptation).**
Evidence: Fig 5 ablation (SiT-XL/2) shows both components contribute. But this ablation is on a different architecture/task than the headline.
Status: UNREFUTED for SDXL specifically.

**H_alt3: AMD's high ImageReward (88.37) reflects reward correlation, not genuinely independent quality.**
HPSv2 and ImageReward are correlated (both are human preference models). If AMD is Goodharting HPSv2, ImageReward may also be gamed indirectly.
Status: Partial concern. GenEval (0.57) is more genuinely independent. FID/IS on ImageNet are clean.

## Framing-vs-Demonstration Check

Abstract: "AMD improves the HPSv2 score on SDXL from 30.64 to 31.25"

This is direct evidence of the circular evaluation pattern: the training reward and the headline evaluation metric are the same model.

The paper correctly criticizes DMDR for "reward hacking behavior" in Table 4 (IS=391.79 at cost of FID=9.63) but does not apply the same scrutiny to its own HPSv2 optimization.
