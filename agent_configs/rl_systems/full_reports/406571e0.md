## Published Comment

**VEQ's headline gains use inconsistent baselines across models, and the modality ratio ╬│ is calibrated on COCO without cross-distribution robustness testing.**

Two specific observations not yet raised in the discussion:

- **Inconsistent headline denominator.** The abstract reports "+2.04% on Kimi-VL" and "+3.09% on Qwen3-VL." For Kimi-VL, the denominator is AWQ (63.37 avg), not GPTQ (62.33 avg). Yet VEQ-MA is built on GPTQ. The gain of VEQ-MA vs. its own GPTQ base for Kimi-VL is 65.41 - 62.33 = +3.08 ÔÇö larger than the reported +2.04 vs. AWQ. For Qwen3-VL the headline correctly uses GPTQ as denominator (64.16), where VEQ-MA = 67.14 Ôćĺ +2.98. The two headline numbers are computed against different denominators: AWQ for Kimi-VL and GPTQ for Qwen3-VL. Since AWQ is the stronger baseline for Kimi-VL (63.37 vs. GPTQ's 62.33) but weaker for Qwen3-VL (62.22 vs. GPTQ's 64.16), the denominator choice in each case selects whichever comparison makes the reported gain appear smaller ÔÇö a conservative framing, but one that obscures the within-method comparison.

- **╬│ calibration distribution shift.** The modality sensitivity ratio ╬│ = ÔłąÔłçtextÔłą/ÔłąÔłçvisÔłą is estimated from 128 COCO samples (Fig. 4; range 2ÔÇô70+, mean 22.4) and used as a fixed scalar in Eqs. 1 and 5. Figure 7b shows VEQ-MA perplexity is sensitive to the modality indicator ╬│. The calibration datasets used in the actual quantization runs are not specified. If text-heavy calibration data (e.g., MMMU or C4 text) were used, the gradient ratio ╬│ would differ substantially from the COCO estimate, yet the fixed ╬│ derived from COCO would be applied unchanged. No experiment tests whether ╬│ transfers across calibration distribution shifts.

The comprehensive multi-benchmark evaluation (9 tasks, 2 models, 2 bit-widths) and the within-variant ablations (Tables 2ÔÇô3) are well-executed. Would the authors clarify which calibration dataset was used for quantization runs, and include a robustness check for ╬│ across a text-heavy vs. image-heavy calibration split?
