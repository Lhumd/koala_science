## Construct-validity audit (per claim)

### C1 — Sample efficiency
- Construct: "sample efficiency" — speed of learning with respect to data/samples.
- Operational metric: IL training-loss / μ MSE / σ MSE curves vs. number of imitation steps.
- Aspects measured: convergence rate while fitting a fixed expert dataset from a pre-trained MLP teacher (flybody MLP).
- Aspects NOT measured:
  - PPO learning curves for any of the four architectures (samples-to-reward-threshold; return at fixed sample budget; wall-clock to convergence).
  - Sample efficiency w.r.t. expert-dataset size.
  - Generalization sample efficiency (held-out task adaptation).
- Severity: HIGH. The paper's own framing as an "embodied reinforcement learning" controller and the existence of a PPO fine-tuning stage (Sec 3.2, Eq 9) make RL-phase sample efficiency the load-bearing facet — and it is unmeasured.

### C2 — Performance / accuracy
- Construct: "performance" — task success at velocity-tracking.
- Operational metric: Pos Err (m) and Angle Err (rad/s) at four (speed, yaw) command configurations (Table 1).
- Aspects NOT measured:
  - Energy / actuation cost (joint torque distribution, total work).
  - Action smoothness / jerk.
  - Real-fly transferability.
  - Long-horizon termination rates.
  - Performance under perturbation.
- Severity: MEDIUM. The paper's claim is bounded to velocity-tracking; the listed unmeasured aspects are not within the strict claim. Still, "performance" is overloaded.

### C3 — Wiring specificity for orientation control
- Construct: "wiring specificity is essential for orientation control."
- Operational metric: Angle Err comparison at the high-yaw condition (Table 1, speed=3, yaw=7): FlyGM 8.29, Rewired 13.55, ER-Random 125.36.
- Aspects measured: relative ordering of three graph constructions on yaw-tracking error.
- Aspects NOT measured:
  - "Specificity" within the connectome — does any specific subgraph (sensory→motor pathways, descending tracts) carry the load? No subgraph-ablation is reported.
  - Whether non-degree-preserving rewirings that respect modularity / small-world structure also recover FlyGM performance — i.e., "wiring specificity" could equally well mean "preserving the small-world topology" rather than "preserving exact synapses."
- Severity: MEDIUM. Conclusion follows from data, but the precise "wiring specificity" attribution can't be fully isolated from "general topology preservation."

### C4 — Stable control across diverse tasks without task-specific architectural tuning
- Construct: "stable control" + "no task-specific architectural tuning."
- Operational metric: qualitative (Fig 4-7 snapshots) + tripod gait pattern (Fig 9). The graph topology IS task-invariant (✓).
- Aspects NOT measured / inconsistent: Appendix A.3 reveals walking and flight tasks differ in encoder dim, decoder dim, output dim, and learning rate (1e-4 vs 1e-5, a 10× difference). The strict reading "graph topology unchanged" survives; the broad reading "no task-specific tuning at all" is contradicted by the per-task LR / I/O dim choices.
- Severity: LOW (framing-vs-demonstration is local, not load-bearing for the central architecture claim).

### C5 — Connectome organization is non-randomly optimized for embodied control
- Construct: very strong neuroscience claim.
- Operational metric: connectome > rewired > ER-random > MLP ordering.
- Aspects NOT measured: comparison vs. small-world preserving rewirings; comparison vs. evolutionary search baselines; vs. hand-designed CPG networks. The "non-random optimization" claim is supported only against unstructured rewirings; specific structural priors (modularity, hub-density) that the connectome happens to share with other biologically-plausible-but-non-Drosophila wirings are not separately tested.
- Severity: MEDIUM (over-broad neuroscience framing for a result that strictly demonstrates "exact connectome > unstructured rewiring").

## Reward / objective audit (full enumeration)
- IL loss (Eq 8): KL(N(μ_t, σ_t²) || N(μ_s, σ_s²)) + λ(t)(||μ_s − μ_t||² + α||log σ_s − log σ_t||²). λ anneals so distributional matching dominates later. α is a constant for scale balance.
- PPO (Eq 9): standard clipped surrogate + value MSE + entropy bonus. Task rewards inherited from flybody (Vaxenburg et al. 2025) — not enumerated in this paper. Reproducibility-impacting opacity, but symmetric across architectures so not a comparison-asymmetry concern.
- Set-relationship: not applicable — comparison is across architectures using identical reward.
- Orthogonal mechanism check: in IL, the auxiliary KL+MSE structure is identical across architectures, so any architecture-specific advantage cannot be attributed to an orthogonal regularizer.

## Counter-hypothesis attempts (≥2 per headline claim)

For C1 (Sample efficiency):
- **CH1.1:** "Sparsity, not connectome-specific wiring, drives the IL gain." Refuted by Rewired/ER-Random — both are sparse, both lose to FlyGM in Fig 3.
- **CH1.2:** "FlyGM matches the MLP teacher's action distribution faster because the connectome architecture happens to align with naturalistic fly motion (which the MLP teacher captures from mocap), making the IL fit easier specifically for FLY motion datasets — not because connectome is universally a good RL inductive bias." UNREFUTED. Paper does not test on a non-fly motion dataset or on RL learning curves where the MLP-teacher confound is absent.
- **CH1.3:** "RL-phase sample efficiency is comparable across the four architectures, and only the IL phase shows separation because IL fits a deterministic mapping while RL has to discover it." UNREFUTED — no PPO learning curves shown.

For C2 (Performance):
- **CH2.1:** "FlyGM tracks velocity better but uses much more actuation effort." UNREFUTED — joint torque / energy not reported.
- **CH2.2:** "Performance gain depends on the specific (speed, yaw) configurations chosen." Partially addressed: paper reports four configurations, with consistent FlyGM-best ordering on Angle Err. Position Err is mixed (Rewired wins on 2/4 configs).

For C3 (Wiring specificity):
- **CH3.1:** "Modularity, hub structure, or small-world property — not exact wiring — drives orientation control." UNREFUTED. Rewired preserves degree but breaks modularity/hub structure; a modularity-preserving rewiring control is missing.
- **CH3.2:** "Connectome's biological neurotransmitter polarity (Eq 1, signed weights) — not graph TOPOLOGY — drives the gain." Partially addressed: ER-Random and Rewired use unweighted unit-strength edges (Sec 4.1), so the comparison conflates topology with edge-weight specificity. The paper acknowledges this assumption but does not isolate weight-vs-topology contributions.

For C4 (Stable control):
- **CH4.1:** "Stability is implicit (no fall, completes velocity tracking) rather than measured." Confirmed: paper does not report perturbation recovery, fall rate, or termination statistics.

For C5 (Non-random optimization):
- **CH5.1:** "Any biologically-inspired structured wiring would beat random; connectome is just one such instance." UNREFUTED — no comparison vs. C. elegans connectome, mouse cortex motif, etc.

## Mechanism-attribution audit
- Two coupled novelties: (a) connectome-as-graph topology, (b) signed neurotransmitter weights (Eq 1, W_vu = N_exc − N_inh).
- Three baselines isolate (a): MLP, ER-Random, Rewired all use unweighted edges (Sec 4.1: *"all edges were treated as unweighted with unit strength"*). So the comparisons isolate "FlyGM with weights + connectome topology" against "non-connectome with unit weights."
- This means the FlyGM advantage could be due to **either** (i) the connectome topology, or (ii) the signed weights, or (iii) both. A "FlyGM-topology + unit-weight" baseline is missing — the paper does not run this. Without it, the wiring-specificity claim conflates topological wiring with signed-weight specificity.
- This is a classic coupled-novelty mechanism-attribution gap, but it is one tier weaker than the C1 critique (because the paper does acknowledge the unweighting choice).

## Multi-objective trade-off audit
- Reward terms in PPO are inherited from flybody and not enumerated in the paper. Cross-architecture comparison is on identical reward, so no asymmetry.
- Reported axes (per architecture): Pos Err, Angle Err. UNMEASURED axes:
  - Energy / joint-torque cost
  - Action smoothness / jerk
  - Behavioral diversity
  - Long-horizon termination / fall rate
  - Real-fly transferability
- Severity: medium — the paper's claims don't strictly extend to these axes, but a reader interpreting "stable control across diverse tasks" could reasonably expect at least termination rate.

## Framing-vs-demonstration audit (verbatim)
- **Abstract:** "stable control across diverse locomotion tasks WITHOUT TASK-SPECIFIC ARCHITECTURAL TUNING"
- **Appendix A.3:** "Both tasks use identical graph topology derived from the FlyWire connectome but DIFFER IN INPUT/OUTPUT DIMENSIONS AND NETWORK HYPERPARAMETERS due to behavioral constraints." Walking LR = 1×10⁻⁴; Flight LR = 1×10⁻⁵.
- The strict claim ("graph topology not tuned") survives; the broad claim is misleading. Soft framing concern, not a score-2 trigger because the central architectural object (the connectome) is genuinely invariant.

- **Abstract:** "FlyGM yields HIGHER SAMPLE EFFICIENCY"
- **Sec 4.1:** "We focused our evaluation on the imitation learning stage, which provided a stable environment to assess the structural advantages of the network from scratch."
- The reader is led to understand "sample efficiency" as the embodied-RL controller's data efficiency, but the demonstration is restricted to IL fit speed. **PRIMARY SCORE-2 TRIGGER.**
