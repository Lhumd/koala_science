## Construct-validity audit

### Construct A: "joint audio-visual reasoning" / "complex physical reasoning" (C4, C7)

- **Operational metric:** Accuracy on multi-choice classification with answer space {Left, Center, Right} (Tab 1 sample QA: *"Q: Can you tell which speaker the male voice originates from? A: Center"*).
- **What the metric does NOT capture:**
  1. Continuous spatial reasoning — the answer space is categorical (3-way), not the actual 3D position the FOA could in principle support.
  2. Ambiguity-stratified difficulty — no bin by candidate-position similarity, candidate angular separation, or acoustic confounders.
  3. 3-candidate vs 2-candidate breakdown — Tab 1 shows Task E has 24K of 2-cand + 5K of 3-cand samples. Tab 2 reports a single aggregate "Reasoning 2-speaker" number. The harder 3-cand subset (random = 33%) is buried in the average and not reported separately.
  4. Compositional / multi-step inference — "reasoning" framing implies multi-step inference; the operational task is single-step geometric matching.
  5. Robustness probes — no test under acoustic occlusion, similar-spectrum interferers, or adversarially-chosen candidates.
- **Critical observation:** Tab 5's "Ours w/o FOA Encoder" condition reaches 43.8% / 47.6%, which is *indistinguishable from* the Tab 2 random baseline (45.6% / 47.4%). The metric appears to be a binary information-presence detector — either the model has spatial-audio access (≈99%) or it doesn't (≈47%) — with no intermediate regime. This is consistent with the task being too easy to discriminate "good reasoning" from "poor reasoning."

### Construct B: "monaural representations are fundamentally insufficient for spatial disambiguation" (C5)

- **Operational metric:** Tab 5 — "Ours w/o FOA Encoder" hits 43.8% / 47.6% on Reasoning 1-spk / 2-spk vs full model 99.5% / 99.2%.
- **What the metric does NOT capture:** The ablation removes FOA entirely (audio→silence/empty input). It does not test against monaural-plus-visual systems with lip-motion attention or mouth-region attention, which are commonly demonstrated to solve speaker-source attribution from RGB alone in many cases (Qwen2.5-Omni baseline gets 35.8/44.0 in Tab 2 — well above random, consistent with this hypothesis). The harder-to-refute claim would frame: "monaural+RGB is insufficient compared to FOA+RGB-D," which is supported, vs "monaural is fundamentally insufficient," which oversells.

### Construct C: "Outperforms BAT in the more challenging overlapping-source task" (within C2)

- **Operational metric:** Tab 2 "Overlap DoA" — JAEGER 13.13° vs BAT 19.09°.
- **What the metric does NOT capture:** BAT uses binaural audio (2-channel). JAEGER uses 4-channel FOA. The improvement could reflect the additional channels rather than the Neural IV architecture. Tab 3's Classical-vs-Neural IV comparison is *internal* to JAEGER (FOA held fixed), so it isolates architecture-vs-representation but doesn't address the BAT-vs-JAEGER channel-count confound.

## Counter-hypothesis attempts

- **CH1 (geometric pipeline vs reasoning):** With FOA + RGB-D, the matching task could be solved by a near-deterministic geometric pipeline: estimate DoA from FOA, look up nearest visible speaker centroid, output its categorical label. **Refutation status:** NOT refuted. The paper presents no harder reasoning probe (compositional QA, multi-step inference, distractor-with-similar-DoA).
- **CH2 (saturated-by-design):** The matching task evaluates an information-presence boundary (random vs full info) rather than a continuous difficulty curve. **Refutation status:** NOT refuted. No difficulty-stratified analysis.
- **CH3 (FOA-vs-binaural confound on Overlap DoA):** JAEGER's beat on BAT could come from 4-channel vs 2-channel input. **Refutation status:** NOT refuted. No within-FOA non-Neural-IV baseline (e.g., a SELDnet on the same 4-channel input).
- **CH4 (task-routing curriculum):** Sec 5.1 says full encoders+projectors are trained only for the joint reasoning tasks. Joint reasoning gets the most permissive training schedule. This is a confound between architecture choices and training schedule. **Refutation status:** Partially refuted by Tab 5's component analysis (which holds the schedule fixed and removes individual modules), though the comparison to baselines (BAT, N3D-VLM, Qwen2.5-Omni) does not control for schedule.

## Mechanism-attribution audit

Two coupled novelties: Neural IV + 3D-aware positional encoding (depth-projected sinusoidal PE).

- **Tab 3:** Neural IV vs Classical IV cross-eval (train single, test overlap; etc.) — clean isolation of IV architecture.
- **Tab 4:** with vs without depth encoding for visual grounding (Task C) — clean isolation of depth.
- **Tab 5:** four conditions on reasoning — Neural/Classical × Depth/no-Depth, plus FOA-removed and Depth+FOA-removed.

Notable: in Tab 5, *Neural IV w/o Depth* underperforms *Classical IV w/o Depth* (94.9% vs 98.7% on 2-spk reasoning). When depth is removed, Neural IV is *worse* than Classical IV. This is interesting — it suggests the Neural IV's gain is depth-conditional. The paper does not flag or discuss this. A reader expecting Neural IV to be a general improvement is mildly misled by the headline framing.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit

JAEGER is supervised SFT (LoRA on Qwen2.5-Omni); no reward shaping. Trade-offs that warrant disclosure but are not reported:
- Inference latency vs baselines (BAT, N3D-VLM, Qwen2.5-Omni).
- Memory footprint with the additional Neural IV CNN + 3D PE.
- Training cost: 8 GPUs × 6k steps audio + 24 GPUs × 3k steps visual + 24 GPUs × 4k steps reasoning is described, but baselines' costs are not.

## Headline-number denominator audit

C4 (99.2%): denominator is all Task E samples (24K 2-cand + 5K 3-cand), reported as a single aggregate. The 3-cand subset (random=33%, harder) is 17% of the denominator. An aggregate 99.2% is consistent with 3-cand accuracy as low as ~95.0% (with 2-cand at 100%) or as high as ~100% (with 2-cand at ~99.0%). Readers can't tell. Soft denominator concern, not a flagrant switch.

## Silent baseline omission

None spotted. Baselines (BAT, N3D-VLM, Qwen2.5-Omni, Random) are consistent across tables.

## All weaknesses found (severity ranked)

1. **HIGH — Construct-coverage gap on the headline reasoning claim (C4/C7).** 3-way L/C/R matching with random ≈47% does not span "complex physical reasoning." Score-2 trigger.
2. **HIGH — FOA-vs-binaural confound (C2 vs BAT).** Cross-architecture comparison with different audio modalities; no within-FOA architecture baseline.
3. **MEDIUM — Disclosure gap on 3-candidate Task E subset.** 17% of Task E is 3-cand (random=33%), but only the aggregate is reported.
4. **MEDIUM — "Monaural fundamentally insufficient" (C5) overclaims.** Compared to no-spatial-audio condition, not to monaural+visual with lip motion.
5. **LOW — Neural IV's gain is depth-conditional** (Tab 5: Neural IV underperforms Classical IV when depth is removed). Not flagged in the discussion.
6. **LOW — Inference latency / cost vs baselines undisclosed.**
