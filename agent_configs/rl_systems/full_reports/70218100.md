# Stochastic Interpolants in Hilbert Spaces — Phase B full report (70218100)

## Headline empirical claims

1. **Abstract:** "By enabling generative bridges between arbitrary functional distributions, our approach achieves state-of-the-art results, offering a powerful, general-purpose tool for scientific discovery." → supported only by Table 2 (Section 6.2, 2D PDE-based Inference; 64×64 fixed discretisation).
2. **Section 1 / motivation:** Cites Chen et al. (2023); De Bortoli (2022); Stuart (2010) to assert that finite-dimensional DM/SI performance "may deteriorate as discretisation becomes finer," establishing the *raison d'être* of the framework. Operationalised nowhere in Section 6.
3. **Section 6.2 narrative:** "both our infinite-dimensional methods outperform FunDPS and DiffusionPDE" on Navier-Stokes, "with the ODE achieving the state-of-the-art." Supported by Table 2 last two rows.
4. **Section 6.1 ablation:** Time-change is "critical for numerical stability" — supported by Table 1 (1D Darcy variant).

## Reward / objective audit

There are two losses, Eqs. 18–19:
- **Lφ:** velocity loss `E[wφ(t) ‖α̇x₀ + β̇x₁ − φθ(xt,t)‖²_H]`
- **Lη:** denoiser loss `E[wη(t) ‖z − ηϕ(xt,t)‖²_H]`

Side-by-side with finite-dim SI (Albergo et al. 2023a, Eqs. 7–8 reproduced in Section 2.1), the proposed losses differ only in: (a) `‖·‖_H` replacing `‖·‖`; (b) z drawn from N(0, C) trace-class instead of isotropic Gaussian; (c) optional time-change θ(t) wrapping t.

**Set-relationship.** The proposed objective is *not* a strict subset — both methods optimise the same regression targets (interpolant velocity and noise). The substantive change is the *noise distribution* and the *time-change*, not the loss family.

**Sign of dropped terms.** No penalty terms are dropped; the loss family is essentially identical. This rules out the standard reward-overlap score-2 trigger.

**Orthogonal mechanism check.** Two orthogonal mechanisms could explain Navier-Stokes wins independently of the "infinite-dim" claim:
1. *Time-change as numerical-stability device.* Section 6.1 (Table 1) shows that change-of-time alone gives a 2,400× error reduction on 1D Darcy ODE (9433.4% → 3.8%) and 144× on inverse (2500.5% → 17.4%). The change-of-time is independently applicable to finite-dim SI but is not used in the "Finite-dim. SI" baseline rows of Table 2. If the same time-change were applied to the finite-dim SI, the gap might close or reverse.
2. *Trace-class noise as inductive smoothing.* Trace-class C induces a smooth-noise prior that may regularise gradients independently of the well-posedness machinery. A "Finite-dim. SI + smooth (non-isotropic) noise" baseline is missing.

## Counter-hypothesis attempts (≥2 per headline claim)

**Headline 1: "State-of-the-art" on PDE benchmarks.**
- *CH-1a (architecture confound).* Navier-Stokes wins might be attributable to U-NO (used for proposed) vs. U-Net (used for finite-dim SI baseline) — not to the infinite-dim formulation. The paper *itself* concedes (Section 6.2) that "U-Nets used in finite dimensions are better suited" for Darcy's discontinuous coefficients. By symmetry, U-NO might be better-suited to Navier-Stokes' smooth fields independent of dimensional formulation. **Unrefuted** — no apples-to-apples row where Finite-dim. SI uses U-NO or where Infinite-dim. SI uses U-Net.
- *CH-1b (NFE confound).* Table 2 shows DiffusionPDE at NFE = 2000, FunDPS and proposed at NFE = 100, with Finite-dim. SI at 100. The paper claims SOTA at 1/20 the NFE of DiffusionPDE, but does not report wall-clock or matched-NFE comparisons. A 20× compute disparity favoring DiffusionPDE on Navier-Stokes Forward would reverse the ordering. **Partially refuted** for Finite-dim. SI (matched at 100), but **unrefuted** for DiffusionPDE.

**Headline 2: Finite-dim approaches deteriorate as D → ∞ (motivation).**
- *CH-2a (no deterioration shown).* The motivating claim could be tested by sweeping resolution {32, 64, 128, 256} and reporting how Finite-dim. SI's error grows. The paper uses fixed 64×64 throughout. **Unrefuted** — the motivating premise is asserted via citations rather than demonstrated.
- *CH-2b (motivation might be vacuous at the tested resolution).* On Darcy Flow at 64×64, Finite-dim. SI achieves 1.1% error vs. proposed 1.9%/2.7%. If finite-dim deteriorated as D grew, the proposed method should already begin to outperform at higher D. The single-resolution result does not even hint at the predicted scaling. **Unrefuted.**

**Headline 3: Both proposed methods (ODE and SDE) outperform FunDPS/DiffusionPDE on Navier-Stokes.**
- *CH-3a (cherry-picked benchmark).* The Navier-Stokes wins are real (1.0/4.6 vs. FunDPS 2.8/6.1, DiffusionPDE 6.1/8.6), but the framing "both our infinite-dimensional methods outperform" applies *only* to Navier-Stokes; on Darcy Flow the proposed SDE/ODE are beaten by FunDPS forward (1.9 vs. 1.9 ODE proposed = tie; 2.3 SDE proposed > 1.9 FunDPS) and on inverse (FunDPS 3.6 vs. proposed ODE 2.7 — proposed wins; SDE 3.7 — proposed loses). The "outperform" framing is benchmark-specific. **Refuted on Navier-Stokes; unsupported as a general claim.**

**Headline 4: Time-change is critical for numerical stability.**
- *CH-4a (the "no time-change" baseline is artificial).* Table 1's "None (θ̇ = 1)" rows give catastrophic errors (9433.4% for ODE forward; 1395.8% for SDE forward). Standard practice in finite-dim SI uses early-stopping or interval truncation `t ∈ [δ, 1−δ]` rather than no mitigation. The "tremendous improvement" framing is true vs. the worst-case baseline but the comparison doesn't isolate whether time-change is uniquely effective vs. simpler interval truncation. **Partially refuted** — the table establishes that *some* mitigation is required, but not that the proposed time-change is the right one.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit

The paper does not claim "comparable performance" with fewer objective terms — both methods regress the same velocity and denoiser, so the standard multi-objective trade-off pattern (drop a penalty, improve the metric the remaining penalties target) does not apply at the loss level.

However, Table 2's NFE column reveals a **single-axis claim on a multi-axis comparison space**: a SOTA claim at NFE = 100 against a baseline at NFE = 2000 (DiffusionPDE) is not iso-compute. Wall-clock per inference, peak memory, and parameter count are not reported. A reader cannot tell whether the proposed method dominates on a Pareto sense or only on a fixed-NFE slice that favours the proposed method.

## Framing-vs-demonstration audit

- **Abstract framing:** "achieves state-of-the-art results, offering a powerful, general-purpose tool for scientific discovery."
- **Table 2 reality:** SOTA on Navier-Stokes forward (1.0) and inverse (4.6); LOSES to Finite-dim. SI on Darcy Flow forward (1.9 vs. 1.1) and inverse (2.7 vs. 1.1). The proposed method achieves SOTA on **2 of 4** reported settings. The abstract does not qualify "state-of-the-art" by benchmark.
- **Section 6.2 narrative:** "We attribute our underperformance on Darcy flow to the representation of the permeability inputs a as a binary field, which introduces sharp discontinuities and high-frequency components that are poorly captured by spectral convolutions in the U-NO due to Gibbs phenomena ... U-Nets used in finite dimensions are better suited to representing these discontinuities, likely contributing to their superior performance."
  - This is a candid admission of an *architecture-driven* failure mode. But Theorem 8's HC-Lipschitz requirement on the learned models (Section 4.2: "This bound requires Lipschitz continuity of the learned models, a condition satisfied by our neural operator architectures (Li et al. 2020; Rahman et al. 2022)") constrains architecture choice toward neural operators. Swapping in a U-Net is therefore not a free fix — it is in tension with the framework's regularity assumptions backing Theorem 8.
- **Motivating premise:** Section 1 "performance may deteriorate as discretisation becomes finer" → **never empirically tested.** A resolution-sweep is the canonical test and is absent.
- **Stochasticity framing:** Title is *Stochastic* Interpolants in Hilbert Spaces. The SDE variant (the headlined feature) is consistently the *worst* among the proposed methods on every Table 2 row. The deterministic ODE variant carries the wins.

## Mechanism-attribution audit

Two coupled novelties are introduced jointly:
1. The infinite-dimensional well-posedness framework (Theorems 2, 5, 6, 8).
2. The change-of-time θ(t) (Lemma 7, Section 4.1).

Section 6.1 ablates change-of-time on a 1D toy. Section 6.2 reports headline 2D results *without* reporting whether the Finite-dim. SI baseline is also using a comparable change-of-time. If the proposed time-change is independently effective and independently applicable to Finite-dim. SI, then the Navier-Stokes wins may stem from this *one* component rather than from the infinite-dimensional formulation. The paper's own Table 1 shows the time-change accounts for a 2,400× error reduction in isolation — that is the larger of the two contributions ablated anywhere in the paper. **The "Finite-dim. SI + matched time-change" row is missing from Table 2.**

## Defense paragraph (for the owner)

Two independent score-2 triggers fire on independent load-bearing claims, each anchored to verbatim text the authors cannot dispute:

1. **The abstract claims "state-of-the-art results" and Table 2 shows the proposed method losing to the paper's own Finite-dim. SI on 2 of 4 reported settings (Darcy Flow forward: 1.9 vs. 1.1; Darcy Flow inverse: 2.7 vs. 1.1).** The most direct rebuttal would be that "state-of-the-art" was meant only with reference to FunDPS / DiffusionPDE — but the abstract makes the unqualified claim, and the paper's own Section 6.2 narrative ("both our infinite-dimensional methods outperform FunDPS and DiffusionPDE" — note the qualifier *on Navier-Stokes*) shows the authors know the qualification is needed.

2. **Section 1 motivates the entire framework on the premise that finite-dim performance "may deteriorate as discretisation becomes finer" (citing Chen et al. 2023, De Bortoli 2022, Stuart 2010), and Section 6 uses fixed 64×64 throughout.** The most direct rebuttal would be that resolution-invariance is a theoretical property, not an empirical one — but the same paper provides empirical comparison with finite-dim SI at a single resolution, where finite-dim SI actually wins on half the tasks. The motivating premise is therefore not even hinted at empirically.

The Soundness rationale does not depend on theoretical content (which is the Theory sibling's lane). The score is grounded in claim-vs-Table 2 mismatches and in motivating-premise-vs-experimental-section mismatches. Both are anchored to verbatim quotes at specific section numbers and table cells.
