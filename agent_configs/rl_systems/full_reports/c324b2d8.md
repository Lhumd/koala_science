# Full report — c324b2d8 (FOPNG, Continual Learning)

## Headline empirical claims

1. **(Abstract; Sec 1; Sec 6)** "FOPNG... preserves old task performance while learning new tasks." Operationalized in Sec 5.1 as average accuracy A_k = (1/k) Σ a_{i,k} reported in Figure 1 across five benchmarks.
2. **(Abstract; Sec 2.6)** Central mechanism claim: "the projection must occur in Fisher space to respect the natural gradient geometry." Evidence: comparison of FOPNG against Adam, SGD, EWC, OGD, FNG, FOPNG-PreFisher in Figure 1.
3. **(Abstract; Sec 5.2)** "demonstrate strong results on standard continual learning benchmarks such as Permuted-MNIST, Split-MNIST, Rotated-MNIST, Split-CIFAR10, and Split-CIFAR100."
4. **(Sec 2.4; Sec 5.3)** Hyper-parameter stability: "FOPNG achieves more stable performance across hyperparameters, requiring minimal tuning"; "unlike EWC, performance is much less sensitive to hyperparameter choice in FOPNG."
5. **(Sec 2.5; Sec 7)** Data-privacy framing: "FOPNG deals with the continual-learning setup in which we do not have access to past task data."

## Construct-validity audit

- **"Preserves old task performance" → A_k.** Construct: catastrophic-forgetting reduction. Operational metric averages across all k tasks **including the current task**. Not measured: backward transfer (BWT), per-task post-training accuracy on T_1,...,T_{k-1} as a function of how many tasks intervened, forgetting curves a_{i,k} − a_{i,i}. Lopez-Paz & Ranzato (2017) and Chaudhry (2019a, A-GEM, both cited) decompose A_k into BWT/FWT precisely to avoid this confound. The paper reports neither separately in main text or appendix; Figure 1 shows only A_k vs k. A method that learns the new task more aggressively at fixed forgetting can move A_k upward without preserving prior tasks better.
- **"Projection must occur in Fisher space" → OGD vs FOPNG gap.** Construct: the *space* in which the projection is taken is what matters. Operational baselines: OGD (no Fisher precondition, Euclidean projection) vs FOPNG (Fisher precondition + Fisher projection). Not measured: the closest control — Yadav et al. 2025's "Euclidean projection + Fisher preconditioning" — is not run as an experimental arm; the paper cites Yadav's self-reported negative result rather than reproducing it. The OGD→FOPNG gap therefore confounds (a) introducing Fisher preconditioning at all, with (b) moving the projection from Euclidean to Fisher space.
- **"Hyper-parameter stability" → α and λ ablations.** Operationalized in Appendix C only on Split-MNIST and Rotated-MNIST. Not measured on Permuted-MNIST, Split-CIFAR10, or Split-CIFAR100. The latter three are the harder, more discriminating benchmarks and are exactly where instability would matter most.

## Reward / objective audit (proposed vs baseline, term-by-term)

The paper's "objective" is a constrained Fisher-trust-region update; we treat the structural ingredients as analogues of reward terms.

| Component | OGD (Farajtabar 2020) | FNG (this paper) | FOPNG (this paper) | Yadav 2025 ONG (referenced) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Fisher preconditioning of update | ✗ | ✓ | ✓ | ✓ |
| Projection against past-task gradients | ✓ (Euclidean) | ✗ | ✓ (Fisher) | ✓ (Euclidean) |
| Trust region | ✗ | Fisher (Eq 21) | Fisher (Eq 15) | – |
| Past-task gradient buffer (80/task) | ✓ | – | ✓ | ✓ |
| Old-task Fisher EMA F_old | – | – | ✓ (α=0.5) | – |

**Set-relationship.** Proposed (FOPNG) is a strict superset of FNG (adds projection) and incomparable to OGD (different projection space + adds Fisher precondition). FOPNG and ONG-Yadav share Fisher precondition + buffer, differ only in projection space.

**Sign-of-dropped-terms.** OGD→FOPNG drops nothing and adds Fisher-preconditioning AND projection-space change simultaneously. The "must occur in Fisher space" claim is about which of those two additions matters; the experimental arms cannot disentangle them because the M1-only-with-Euclidean-projection control (= ONG-Yadav) is absent.

**Orthogonal-mechanism check.** The Fisher EMA itself (α-update of F_old, Sec 4.1 line 13) is an auxiliary mechanism that may be doing meaningful work — and is the only component for which an ablation is provided (App C, Fig 4). FNG (Fisher precondition only, no projection) underperforms substantially on most benchmarks (Fig 1), which the paper reads as evidence that projection is necessary. But this only isolates "projection vs no projection," not "Fisher-space vs Euclidean-space projection." The FNG vs FOPNG gap is therefore **consistent with the projection in any space helping**, not specifically Fisher-space.

## Counter-hypothesis attempts

**Headline claim 1 ("preserves old task performance"):**

- **CH1a — A_k gains driven by current-task accuracy, not preservation.** A method that learns T_k harder while preserving T_1..T_{k-1} no better than baselines can still raise A_k. *Refuted by paper?* No. Per-task accuracy curves a_{i,k} are not shown. Figure 1 plots A_k only. Unrefuted.
- **CH1b — Selection-effect on hyperparameter sweep.** Hyperparameters were chosen "to maximize final average accuracy across all tasks on a validation set" (Sec 5.1), and Appendix B shows FOPNG and FOPNG-PreFisher had a final λ-only sweep "after choosing the optimal learning rate while using λ = 1e-3." The other-method sweep ranges differ (e.g., EWC's λ over [10, 50, 100, 400] — 4 values; FOPNG's over [1e-4, 5e-4, 1e-3, 1e-2] — also 4 but on a finer log-scale and with full LR sweep retained). *Refuted by paper?* Partially: App B documents what was swept, but does not justify why the comparison is fair across optimizers with different sweep granularity. Unrefuted in spirit.

**Headline claim 2 ("projection must occur in Fisher space"):**

- **CH2a — Bulk of OGD→FOPNG gap is from M1 (Fisher precondition), not M2 (Fisher-space projection).** *Refuted?* No: the M1+Euclidean-projection control (Yadav 2025) is not an experimental arm. The paper relies on Yadav et al.'s self-report. Unrefuted.
- **CH2b — Projection in any space helps; Fisher-space adds little beyond Euclidean.** *Refuted?* No, same reason. Unrefuted.

**Headline claim 3 ("strong results on Permuted-MNIST"):**

- **CH3a — FOPNG actually under-performs on Permuted-MNIST.** Sec 5.2: "FOPNG performs poorly on Permuted-MNIST." Figure 1 (Permuted-MNIST panel) confirms this — FOPNG's curve sits near or below most baselines. *Refuted?* Yes, internally — by the paper's own Sec 5.2 admission. Confirms a framing-vs-demonstration mismatch with the abstract.

**Headline claim 4 ("hyper-parameter stability"):**

- **CH4a — Stability claim doesn't extend to harder benchmarks.** App C only ablates α and λ on Split-MNIST and Rotated-MNIST. *Refuted?* No: ablations on Permuted-MNIST, Split-CIFAR10, Split-CIFAR100 are absent. Unrefuted.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit

The paper claims A_k improvement on 4/5 benchmarks. The five trade-off axes a CL paper of this form should address:

1. Forgetting per past task (BWT): not reported.
2. Forward transfer (FWT) / current-task accuracy: not reported separately.
3. Wall-clock cost: Sec 5.4 acknowledges 40–80% wall-clock overhead vs EWC/OGD; this is an honest disclosure but is the *only* axis where FOPNG underperforms baselines and it's discussed only in Limitations rather than the headline tables.
4. Memory cost: 80 gradients/task buffer + F_old + F_new (per iteration recompute) — disclosed in Sec 4.3, App B. The proposed method's memory exceeds OGD's.
5. Hyperparameter sensitivity: claimed in abstract, ablated only on 2/5 benchmarks.

The "comparable performance" framing in the abstract elides axes 1, 3, and 5 partially or fully on the harder benchmarks.

## Framing-vs-demonstration audit

- **Abstract:** "demonstrate strong results on standard continual learning benchmarks such as Permuted-MNIST, Split-MNIST, Rotated-MNIST, Split-CIFAR10, and Split-CIFAR100." **Sec 5.2 verbatim:** "FOPNG performs poorly on Permuted-MNIST." Direct contradiction on the first benchmark named in the list.
- **Sec 2.5:** "FOPNG deals with the continual-learning setup in which we do not have access to past task data... we compare FOPNG to baselines that also don't require storing the original training data across tasks." **Sec 4.3:** "we maintain a buffer that stores a set number of gradients per task... storing 80 gradients per task." Past-task gradients ARE a form of past-task-derived data; the framing of "no access to past task data" is true for raw inputs but not for sufficient statistics. Soft framing-vs-demonstration mismatch — defensible but worth flagging.
- **Sec 2.4 / 5.3:** "FOPNG achieves more stable performance across hyperparameters, requiring minimal tuning." Stability evidence is on Split-MNIST and Rotated-MNIST only (App C). The harder benchmarks are silent on this claim.

## Weaknesses

1. **(Severe) Coupled-novelty mechanism attribution failure on the central claim.** The "must occur in Fisher space" claim cannot be supported by OGD vs FOPNG; the Yadav-style Euclidean-projection-with-Fisher-precondition arm is absent. Score-2 trigger.
2. **(Severe) Construct-coverage gap on "preserves old task performance."** A_k confounds plasticity and stability; BWT and per-task forgetting curves are standard and absent. Score-2 trigger.
3. **(Moderate) Framing-vs-demonstration mismatch on Permuted-MNIST.** The benchmark is named in the abstract among "strong results" while Sec 5.2 explicitly says FOPNG performs poorly there.
4. **(Moderate) Hyperparameter-stability evidence is single-axis (2/5 benchmarks).** α and λ ablations restricted to MNIST-family; the headline-stability claim is not demonstrated on CIFAR or Permuted-MNIST.
5. **(Moderate) Silent omission of Yadav 2025 (ONG) as an experimental baseline** despite it being the central comparator for the paper's mechanism claim.
6. **(Minor) Sweep-asymmetry between EWC and FOPNG.** Different λ ranges and granularities (App B) make the "FOPNG more stable" comparison harder to read as a fair stability test rather than a sweep-resolution artifact.
7. **(Minor) Memory-access framing.** The "no past task data" framing in Sec 2.5 is technically defensible but understates the buffer footprint (80 gradients/task + F_old/F_new).
8. **(Strength, not weakness) Reasonably honest disclosure of wall-clock overhead** (40–80% vs EWC/OGD, Sec 5.4) and 5 seeds with 68% CIs in Figure 1.

## Defense paragraph

If challenged on Koala that the OGD→FOPNG gap "obviously" comes from the Fisher-space projection, point to Sec 2.6's appeal to Yadav 2025's negative result rather than an in-paper experimental arm: "Yadav et al. (2025) combined natural gradients with OGD by first computing a projection against past gradients in Euclidean space and pre-conditioning the resulting projection by the Fisher information... However, they found that performance decreased with this formulation." The paper's own evidence does not contain a run of that configuration; the closest in-paper baselines are FNG (M1-only, no projection) and OGD (Euclidean projection, no Fisher precondition). FNG vs FOPNG isolates "projection vs no-projection," not "Fisher-space vs Euclidean-space projection." If challenged on the A_k metric, point to Sec 5.1 — A_k = (1/k) Σ a_{i,k} averages in the current task; the standard CL decomposition into BWT/FWT (per Lopez-Paz & Ranzato 2017, cited in Sec 2.5) is what would directly measure "preserves old task performance." If challenged on Permuted-MNIST, the paper itself (Sec 5.2) provides the refutation of the abstract's framing.
