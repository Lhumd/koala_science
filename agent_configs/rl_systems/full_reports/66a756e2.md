# Phase B Full Report — TIMI: Training-Free Image-to-3D Multi-Instance Generation with Spatial Fidelity (66a756e2)

**Soundness score:** 2 / **Confidence:** 5
**Primary trigger:** Coupled-novelty mechanism-attribution failure — the title's headline contribution (ISG: Instance-aware Separation Guidance) alone *degrades* the headline SSR metric vs the no-guidance baseline (Tab. 2: 0.674 < 0.697); the load-bearing "w/o ISG, w/ SGU" ablation cell is missing, so the paper cannot demonstrate ISG contributes anything beyond what SGU alone would.
**Secondary triggers:** Construct-coverage gap on "spatial fidelity" (SSR is count-only, depth ordering / interpenetration / per-instance correspondence not measured); framing-vs-demonstration mismatch on "training-free" (4 hyperparameters chosen by sweeps on the eval set, no held-out validation); silent baseline omission (6+ multi-instance/scene methods cited but absent from results).

---

## Headline empirical claims

1. **C1 (Abstract / Sec 1):** "TIMI ... achieves *high spatial fidelity*" via a *training-free* framework. Headline numbers Tab. 1: best LCD (0.598), best FS-O (0.353), best SSR (0.809), inference ~59s.
2. **C2 (Sec 5.2.2):** "Despite being training-free, TIMI consistently outperforms the training-based approach MIDI in both global and local spatial fidelity." Load-bearing because it underwrites the entire "training-free" framing.
3. **C3 (Sec 1):** "ISG and SGU jointly promote local instance separation and global layout preservation." Mechanism attribution to two named modules (ISG = title contribution).
4. **C4 (Sec 5.2.2):** "TIMI also maintains high inference efficiency (~59s), comparable to the base model Hunyuan3D, and is significantly faster than both the training-based MIDI (~90s) and the compositional DPA (~783s)." Speed claim.

## Construct-validity audit — what is "spatial fidelity"?

| Operational metric | Definition | What it measures | What it does NOT measure |
|---|---|---|---|
| LCD | Centroid Chamfer Distance (Eq. 8-9) | How close predicted-instance centroids are to GT centroids | Per-instance shape correctness; depth ordering |
| CD-S, FS-S | Scene-level Chamfer Distance / F-Score | Aggregate point-cloud distance | Per-instance correspondence |
| CD-O, FS-O | Object-level CD/FS | Per-instance point-cloud distance — **but the paper never explains how predicted instances are matched to GT instances** for CD-O computation. Hungarian assignment? Nearest-centroid? Unspecified | Identity correctness; orientation |
| **SSR (Separation Success Rate)** | `min(N_pred, N_gt) / max(N_pred, N_gt)` (Eq. 10) | **Count match.** Scores 1.0 whenever instance count is right, regardless of whether instances correspond to GT, are positioned correctly, or are even the right objects | Instance positions, identities, depth ordering, contact/interpenetration |

**Aspects of "spatial fidelity" not measured:**
- Depth ordering / front-back relationships between instances (no metric for relative depth).
- Inter-instance contact / interpenetration (whether instances correctly overlap, support, or avoid each other in 3D).
- Per-instance correspondence to the input image (SSR ignores identity; CD-O matching procedure unspecified).
- Orientation / pose consistency of instances (e.g., chairs all facing the table).
- Scene-level scale/extent fidelity (CD-S confounds size error and position error).

**SSR is the only "instance distinctiveness" metric** and it is a count-match, not a separation or correctness metric. This is the construct-coverage failure on the headline claim.

## Counter-hypothesis attempts

1. **CH1 — Mask-quality leakage.** Sec 5.1 verbatim: "Instance masks are extracted using Grounded-SAM (Ren et al., 2024)." TIMI's wins might largely reflect Grounded-SAM mask quality on the chosen scenes; baselines (MIDI, DPA) may not consume masks the same way, so the comparison conflates "ISG/SGU is better" with "the mask input gives TIMI a strong prior the baselines lack." **Unrefuted**: no robustness ablation on mask quality.
2. **CH2 — SGU-alone explains the gain.** As noted in mechanism audit: missing "w/o ISG, w/ SGU" cell in Tab. 2 means a smoothing+momentum update on the latent might explain most of the improvement, with ISG's named contribution near-orthogonal. **Unrefuted.**
3. **CH3 — Hyperparameter overfitting.** The four parameters (l, t, α, σ) selected by sweeps on the eval set could explain the gap to MIDI; cross-dataset fixed-hyperparameter results not shown. **Unrefuted.**

## Mechanism-attribution audit (coupled novelty — primary trigger)

The paper introduces **two modules** (ISG, SGU) and credits both jointly. **Tab. 2 ablation** (verbatim):

| Variant | LCD | SSR | FS-O |
|---|---|---|---|
| w/o ISG, w/o SGU (Hunyuan3D 2.0 baseline) | 0.627 | **0.697** | 0.339 |
| w/ ISG, w/o SGU | 0.623 | **0.674** ← *DEGRADES* | 0.351 |
| **w/o ISG, w/ SGU** | **MISSING** | **MISSING** | **MISSING** |
| w/ ISG, w/ SGU (full TIMI) | 0.598 | 0.809 | 0.353 |

- **ISG alone DEGRADES SSR** vs the no-guidance baseline (0.674 < 0.697). LCD only marginally improves.
- **There is no "w/o ISG, w/ SGU" condition.** The paper cannot demonstrate that ISG contributes anything beyond what SGU alone provides.
- The only condition where ISG is "shown to help" is the joint condition, where SGU might be doing all the work.
- **Tab. 5** (SGU sub-ablation) shows SGU's components (GM, SR) progressively help — further suggesting SGU is the load-bearing component, not ISG.

The paper's central novelty by name is "**Instance-aware Separation Guidance** via cross-attention anchoring" — the title contribution. The ablation table is consistent with the load-bearing component being SGU, not ISG. Coupled-novelty mechanism attribution failure on a load-bearing claim.

## Multi-objective trade-off audit

The paper claims "high spatial fidelity" measured via 4 metrics (LCD, CD-S, FS-S, CD-O, FS-O, SSR). Tab. 6 α-sensitivity:
- α=0.1: LCD 0.598 (chosen as "best balance")
- α=0.2: LCD 0.523 — **13% better than the chosen value on LCD**

If LCD is the headline global-fidelity metric (it leads Tab. 1), why is the chosen α not the LCD-optimal one? Inconsistent — picking α=0.1 looks like a post-hoc tradeoff justification across multiple metrics.

## Framing-vs-demonstration audit

- **Abstract:** "*training-free* framework ... no additional fine-tuning."
- **Sec 5.1:** Hyperparameters: l ≤ 4 (ISG cross-attention layer count), t ≤ 15 (ISG timesteps), α = 0.1 (SGU modulation strength), σ = 1.5 (SGU smoothing).
- **Tab. 3 (l), Tab. 4 (t), Tab. 6 (α), Tab. 7 (σ):** Full ablation sweeps over each hyperparameter; **chosen value is the one that wins on the eval set** (highlighted/bolded as best).
- **No held-out validation set.** The "training-free" claim is contradicted by **hyperparameter selection on the test set itself** — selecting four hyperparameters on the eval data is a soft form of training; the "generalizability" claim ("these parameters work well across most cases") is not supported by any out-of-distribution validation experiment.

**Hidden input requirement.** Sec 5.1: "Instance masks are extracted using Grounded-SAM." The pipeline takes (image, instance masks) as input — not just an image. Fig. 1 caption says "TIMI generates multiple 3D instances *from a single image*"; abstract says "from a single image". The dependence on Grounded-SAM mask quality is silent in the abstract. If Grounded-SAM merges two adjacent instances or splits one, downstream SSR / CD-O would degrade — but no ablation tests robustness to mask quality.

## Silent baseline omission

Cited but not benchmarked in Tab. 1:
- Tang et al. 2024b/2025 (Diffuscene)
- Zhou et al. 2024 (Zero-shot scene reconstruction from single images with deep prior assembly)
- Rahamim et al. 2024 (Lay-A-Scene) — multi-instance 3D arrangement from text-to-image priors
- Han et al. 2025 (Reparo) — differentiable 3D layout alignment
- Yao et al. 2025 (CAST) — component-aligned 3D scene reconstruction from RGB image
- Ardelean et al. 2025 — generalizable 3D scene reconstruction from single view

CAST and Lay-A-Scene are particularly directly comparable. The paper compares only against MIDI, DPA, and Hunyuan3D 2.0 (a single-instance baseline that is not even an I2MI method). Three baselines for a 70-scene benchmark with 7+ uncited recent multi-instance/scene methods.

## Defense paragraph

If challenged on the coupled-novelty trigger: Tab. 2 verbatim has rows for "w/o ISG, w/o SGU", "w/ ISG, w/o SGU", and "w/ ISG, w/ SGU" — but **NOT** "w/o ISG, w/ SGU". The two ISG-isolating numbers (w/ ISG, w/o SGU): SSR=0.674 vs baseline 0.697 — ISG actively degrades the headline metric in isolation. The paper credits ISG as a named load-bearing contribution (it's literally in the title); the only ablation evidence for ISG helping is in the joint condition, where SGU could be carrying the entire benefit. The missing cell is the load-bearing absent ablation.

If challenged on the construct-coverage trigger: Eq. 10 verbatim defines SSR as `min(N_pred, N_gt) / max(N_pred, N_gt)` — purely a count match. No metric in Sec 5.1 measures depth ordering, inter-instance contact / interpenetration, or per-instance correspondence to the input image. CD-O's instance-matching procedure is unspecified.

If challenged on the "training-free" framing mismatch: Tab. 3 (l), Tab. 4 (t), Tab. 6 (α), Tab. 7 (σ) verbatim report sweeps over 4 hyperparameters with the bolded "chosen" value being the eval-set winner. No held-out validation.

The well-executed parts (clean diffusion-guidance formulation, ~59s inference, fast vs DPA's 783s, sensible LCD definition) are caveats per the calibration principle.
