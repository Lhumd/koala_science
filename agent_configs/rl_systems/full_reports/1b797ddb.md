## 2. Construct-validity audit

**C2 — closed-loop SR gain on Push-T.**
- Construct claimed: improvement attributable to the decoupled dynamics framework (Disentangled Dynamics Paradigm + LRM).
- Operational metric: Push-T MPC SR.
- Unmeasured aspects: (a) the matched comparison "DINO-WM + Sparse MPC Cost Mask" (see §4 below); (b) seed variance — Sec. F discloses "fixed random seed" and 400-episode evaluation, but no ±std for SR; (c) sensitivity of CEM hyperparameters across the comparison.

**C3 — multi-task SR.**
- Some margins are 1–2 points (Maze 100/98, Wall 98/96), well within plausible single-seed variance for 400-episode SR estimates. Without confidence intervals, "matches or surpasses" is hard to evaluate at the small-margin end.

**C4 — landscape smoothness.**
- The "smooth optimization landscape" claim is supported only by qualitative Figure 5 with two scenes. No quantitative landscape-roughness metric (e.g., gradient-norm distribution, local-minima count under random perturbation) is reported.

**C5 — low-rank structure.**
- The PCA argument compares LRM's predicted background-update PCs to ground-truth update PCs on Push-T. No held-out task; no comparison to a non-low-rank smoothing baseline (e.g., a small dense correction MLP) that would isolate whether the low-rank inductive bias matters or whether any sufficiently expressive correction module exhibits similar PCA structure.

## 3. Reward / objective audit

This is supervised dynamics learning + planning, not RL with explicit rewards. The relevant audit is on the planner cost function:

- Conventional CEM cost: `LMPC = MSE(ẑT, zgoal)`
- Proposed CEM cost (Sec. 3.4): `LMPC = MSE(ẑT ⊙ Mtask, zgoal ⊙ Mtask)` where `Mtask` is the binary pixel-diff mask between current obs and goal image.

The Sparse MPC Cost Mask depends only on (a) the current observation, (b) the goal image, (c) the pixel-diff threshold. It is **architecture-agnostic** at the planner level. Nothing in its definition references LRM, DDP, or any DDP-WM internal module. It can be applied around any latent world model's CEM loop.

This matters because the load-bearing comparison (Table 1, Push-T row, DDP-WM vs DINO-WM) holds the world-model architecture variable but does not control for whether the planner is using the proposed Sparse MPC Cost Mask.

## 4. Counter-hypothesis attempts

### CH1 (mechanism attribution): the Push-T closed-loop improvement is explained by the Sparse MPC Cost Mask, not by the decoupled dynamics framework.

**Evidence supporting CH1.** Table 7 (Push-T):

| LRM | MPC Mask | SR |
|-----|----------|-----|
| ✓ | ✓ | 98% |
| ✓ | ✗ | 90% |
| ✗ | ✓ | 70% |
| ✗ | ✗ | 62% |

- "LRM ✓, MPC Mask ✗" = full DDP-WM architecture (history fusion, dynamic localization, sparse main predictor, LRM) without the planner-side cost mask → 90%, exactly DINO-WM's number from Table 1.
- The 8-point Push-T headline gap (90% → 98%) lives entirely in the MPC Mask cell.
- The marginal contribution of the decoupled architecture, holding the planner-side trick fixed (both off), is **zero on Push-T**.

**Refutation attempt.** If the MPC Mask required architecture-side cooperation (e.g., the LRM's smoother landscape were necessary for the cost-mask trick to work in a sampling-based optimizer), then "DINO-WM + MPC Mask" would degrade. The paper does not report this experiment. The "LRM ✗, MPC Mask ✓" row (70%) is suggestive — Naive Sparse + MPC Mask underperforms — but Naive Sparse is not the dense baseline. The relevant test (DINO-WM + MPC Mask) is not in the paper.

**Status: unrefuted.** The matched comparison is missing.

### CH2 (LRM as generic gradient-flow regularizer rather than specifically a low-rank-structure capturer).

**Evidence supporting CH2.** The Appendix A PCA argument shows the LRM-produced updates exhibit similar PCA structure to ground-truth updates. This is consistent with two interpretations: (a) the LRM specifically captures a low-rank inductive bias (paper's claim); (b) the LRM is a sufficiently capable correction module that learns whatever structure exists in the training data, and a non-low-rank-constrained correction module of similar capacity would also.

**Refutation attempt.** A control with a small dense correction MLP (no low-rank inductive bias, matched parameter count) is missing.

**Status: weakly unrefuted, but secondary.** This is a less load-bearing concern than CH1.

### CH3 (Goodhart on Push-T): the SR metric counts T-block reaching a target pose threshold; could a degenerate strategy game this without genuine planning improvement?

The Push-T SR metric (from Chi et al., 2024) is well-established and typical. CEM is sampling-based; degenerate "creep" strategies are filtered by the cost function. **Refuted on standard interpretation.**

## 5. Mechanism-attribution audit

The paper introduces two coupled novelties:
- **N1 (architecture):** Disentangled Dynamics Paradigm + dynamic localization + sparse main predictor + LRM.
- **N2 (planner):** Sparse MPC Cost Mask.

The ablation Table 7 isolates them on Push-T, but the **headline against-baseline comparison (Table 1) does not control for N2.** The "DINO-WM + N2" cell is missing.

Without that cell, the paper attributes to N1 (the decoupled architecture) the closed-loop improvement that Table 7 attributes to N2 (the planner-side cost mask). This is a clean instance of the silent-baseline-omission pattern on a load-bearing comparison.

The (well-supported) efficiency story (C1) is unaffected — that is a comparison of computational resources, where the world-model architecture is the natural denominator.

## 7. Framing-vs-demonstration audit

- **Abstract framing:** "improves the MPC success rate from 90% to 98% compared to state-of-the-art dense models." A reader maps "comparison vs dense model" to "the decoupled framework vs the dense framework" — the MPC Mask is mentioned but not framed as decisive.
- **Methodology demonstration (Table 7):** The 8-point gap is fully in the MPC Mask cell; the framework alone matches DINO-WM at 90%.
- **Mismatch:** The framing implies architectural credit; the demonstration localizes credit to a planner-side trick that the dense baseline was not given.

This is the framing-vs-demonstration mismatch trigger from the rubric.

## 8. Headline-number denominator audit

- **9× speedup (C1):** Denominator is DINO-WM under matched environments and CEM iterations. Genuine and well-supported.
- **Push-T 90→98% (C2):** Denominator is "DINO-WM without MPC Mask" vs "DDP-WM with MPC Mask." Asymmetric.

## 9. All weaknesses found (severity)

1. **Major (load-bearing):** Silent omission of "DINO-WM + Sparse MPC Cost Mask" baseline; closed-loop SR gain attributed to the decoupled framework but localized in Table 7 to a planner-side trick. (Score-2 trigger.)
2. **Major (load-bearing):** Framing-vs-demonstration mismatch on Contribution 3 — the abstract credits the framework, the ablation credits the cost mask. (Score-2 trigger.)
3. **Moderate:** Single-seed reporting (Sec. F: "using a fixed random seed"); no ±std for SR/CD numbers in Tables 1–8; 1–2-point margins on Wall and Maze are within plausible single-seed sampling noise.
4. **Moderate:** Sim-only across all five tasks; no real-robot validation despite framing in terms of "real-time deployment" and "real robotic systems" (Sec. 1).
5. **Minor:** LRM-as-low-rank vs LRM-as-generic-smoother is not isolated by a non-low-rank correction-module control.
6. **Minor:** Landscape-smoothness claim (C4) supported only by 2-scene qualitative Figure 5; no quantitative landscape metric.
7. **Strength (independent of above):** The 9× FLOPs / 7.5× wall-clock efficiency story is well-measured, with concrete hardware (NVIDIA A5880, 2080 Ti), batch size, FLOPs, throughput, and full MPC decision time. This part of the contribution survives the audit cleanly.

## 10. Defense paragraph

If challenged on the Koala thread, key verbatim anchors:
- Section 4.2.1: "Table 1 shows that our method matches or surpasses the SOTA dense model on all tasks. The most significant gain is on the challenging Push-T task, where our 98% success rate substantially outperforms DINO-WM's 90%."
- Section 4.3 + Table 7 row "LRM ✓, MPC Mask ×" → 90% on Push-T.
- Section 3.4: "we first compute the pixel-wise difference between the current observation image and the goal image to generate a binary mask Mtask… we only consider the feature error in these task-relevant regions."

The arithmetic is closed: DINO-WM (Table 1) = 90% on Push-T; DDP-WM minus MPC Mask (Table 7) = 90% on Push-T; identical. The MPC Mask is defined entirely on observation/goal pixels (no DDP-WM-internal dependency). Without "DINO-WM + MPC Mask" reported, the closed-loop comparison is unidentified.

## 11. Theoretical context (out of Soundness scope)

The "low-rank background updates" hypothesis (Appendix A) is interesting and supported empirically on Push-T. Whether the result generalizes to scenes with global lighting/viewpoint changes (where context-driven updates may not be low-rank) is open. This is more of a Theory/Originality concern than a Soundness one.
