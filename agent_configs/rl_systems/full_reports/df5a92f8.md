## 2. Construct-validity audit

**Claim 1 — "near-lossless accuracy on LongBench, SCBench, AND AIME" (conjunctive).**

|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| LongBench (Tab 1) | Llama-3.1-8B | 50.0 | 50.3 | 50.2 | -0.0 | ✓ near-lossless |
| LongBench | Qwen2.5-7B | 47.6 | 47.4 | 47.1 | -0.5 | ✓ near-lossless |
| LongBench | Qwen2.5-32B | 48.4 | 48.1 | 47.7 | -0.7 | ✓ near-lossless |
| SCBench avg (Tab 2) | Llama-3.1-8B | 50.4 | 49.2 | 45.0 | -5.4 (10.7%) | ✗ not near-lossless |
| SCBench R.KV | Llama-3.1-8B | 79.0 | 72.2 | 58.0 | -21.0 (26.6%) | ✗ severe degradation |
| SCBench R.KV | Qwen2.5-7B | 70.4 | 69.2 | 59.4 | -11.0 (15.6%) | ✗ not near-lossless |

The abstract's conjunctive claim ("on LongBench, SCBench, AND AIME") is supported on LongBench (3/3 backbones near-lossless) but not on SCBench R.KV (21-point drop on Llama, 11-point drop on Qwen) or on AIME (6.7-point drop, 13.4% relative).

The body itself uses softer language: Sec 5.2 says SCBench drops are "slightly larger" attributed to SSID-like distribution mismatch; AIME is described as "DeltaKV remains effective on mathematical reasoning benchmarks". Both are more accurate characterizations than "near-lossless".

Per-batch ratios at equal BS:
- 128k BS=8: 148.4 / 143.2 = **1.04×**
- 256k BS=4: 115.9 / 70.2 = **1.65×** (honest); DeltaKV† at BS=4 = 120.6 / 70.2 = 1.72× (honest).
- 512k BS=2 vLLM = 33.1; Sparse-vLLM DeltaKV at BS=2 not in table; DeltaKV at BS=4 = 32.1 (slower per-batch than vLLM at BS=2).
- 900k BS=1 vLLM = 18.6; Sparse-vLLM DeltaKV† at BS=1 not in table.

The "2.05×" at 512k headline (67.7 / 33.1) compares **DeltaKV† at BS=4 vs vLLM at BS=2** — different batch sizes. The honest per-batch speedup at the same BS at 512k is unmeasured; at BS=4 the vanilla DeltaKV variant is *slower* than vLLM at BS=2 (32.1 vs 33.1).

The "up to 2×" qualifier in the abstract softens this; not a clean denominator switch but the framing implies a per-batch compute speedup that the data doesn't isolate.

**Claim 3 — "matching the results of OmniKV" on LongBench.**

Verbatim Table 1: DeltaKV is consistently 0.1-0.4 points below OmniKV on LongBench across all three backbones. Roughly comparable but slightly worse — and OmniKV is training-free while DeltaKV requires 8-14 GPU hours per backbone. This is a fair characterization at the LongBench level but understates the cost differential.

## 3. Reward / objective audit

- DeltaKV training: `L = L_rec + L_ntp` (MSE reconstruction + frozen-LLM NTP cross-entropy)
- Compared baselines:
  - **OmniKV: training-free** dynamic context selection
  - **SnapKV / PyramidKV / AdaKV: training-free** static eviction
  - **Quest: training-free** query-aware sparsity
  - **Palu: training-free** low-rank
- DeltaKV is the **only** baseline that requires training. Cost: 8 GPU hours for 7B/8B, 14 GPU hours for 32B, on a single RTX PRO 6000.
- Per-model retraining is required for new model variants (no zero-shot transfer claimed).

## 4. Counter-hypothesis attempts

### CH1: Throughput gain at 512k is largely batch-fitting, not per-batch speedup.

- Supporting evidence (paper's own Tab 4): at equal BS=8 at 128k, Sparse-vLLM DeltaKV/vLLM = 1.04×. At equal BS=4 at 256k, ratio = 1.65× (honest). At 512k, vLLM cannot fit BS>2, Sparse-vLLM with DeltaKV can fit BS=4. Headline "2.05×" combines per-batch speedup with batch-fitting capacity.
- **Status:** unrefuted by the paper's evidence at 512k. The vanilla DeltaKV at BS=4 at 512k = 32.1 t/s, slightly slower per-batch than vLLM at BS=2 = 33.1 t/s — the throughput gain at 512k is driven by DeltaKV† (Light decompressor) and by being able to fit larger batches.

### CH2: DeltaKV underperforms training-free OmniKV on multi-turn (SCBench) and reasoning (AIME).

- Supporting evidence (paper's own Tab 2 SCBench Llama avg): OmniKV 49.2 vs DeltaKV 45.0 — 4.2-point gap.
- Supporting evidence (paper's own Tab 3 AIME): OmniKV 46.7 vs DeltaKV 43.3 — 3.4-point gap.
- **Status:** the paper acknowledges these numbers in tables but the abstract framing "matching the results of OmniKV" is supported only on LongBench. The training-free OmniKV strictly dominates DeltaKV on accuracy on SCBench and AIME, while DeltaKV's selling point is the memory savings (KR≈45% vs OmniKV's 100%).

### CH3: DeltaKV† (Light decompressor) is the configuration that achieves the headline throughput numbers.

- Supporting evidence (paper's own Tab 4): every "DeltaKV vs DeltaKV†" comparison shows the Light variant ahead on throughput. At 128k BS=8: 148.4 vs 187.0 (with BS=16 capacity). At 256k BS=4: 115.9 vs 120.6. At 512k BS=4: 32.1 vs 67.7. At 900k: only DeltaKV† reported.
- **Status:** acknowledged in Sec 5.3. The Light decompressor is also slightly worse on accuracy (Table 1 DeltaKV vs DeltaKV† on Llama: 50.2 vs 50.3 LongBench — essentially the same; Table 2 SCBench Llama: 45.0 vs 46.3 — Light is actually better on SCBench). Counter-defense ok.

## 5. Mechanism-attribution audit

DeltaKV's three coupled novelties:
- **(a) Residual encoding via MLP/linear compressor f_c with strided reference selection** (Sec 4.1)
- **(b) Hybrid MSE + NTP loss** (Sec 4.2)
- **(c) Sparse-vLLM inference engine with custom kernels** (Sec 4.3)

Available ablations:
- **Tab 5 + Tab 11-12 (Llama-3.1-8B)** isolate (a). Verbatim component-wise:
  - Full DeltaKV LongBench avg: 50.2
  - "w/o f_c and f_d" (eviction only, no residual encoding): 46.7 (-3.5)
  - "w/o Ref. Tokens T" (no reference subtraction, just compress raw): 45.9 (-4.3)
  - Both components contribute meaningfully and roughly symmetrically.
- **Fig 6** isolates (b). Train-NTP-only achieves a different loss curve than Train-MSE-only and Train-Both. Total loss + NTP loss + MSE loss curves all confirm "MSE alone reduces NTP loss; NTP alone does not reduce MSE; combining works best".
- **Sparse-vLLM kernel cost** (Tab 4): Sparse-vLLM Full Attn at 128k BS=8 = 135.0 t/s vs vLLM Full Attn = 143.2 t/s — 5.7% framework overhead. Reasonable.

Mechanism attribution is **reasonably well-isolated** for components (a) and (b). Not a coupled-novelty attribution failure.

## 6. Multi-objective trade-off audit

- "Balance between accuracy, compression, and hardware efficiency" — claimed.
- **Accuracy:** multi-axis (LongBench, SCBench, AIME). Measured on all three, but framed as "near-lossless" when only LongBench supports that characterization.
- **Compression:** KR ≈ 45%, CR = 30%. Measured. (Note: KR is not "29%" as the abstract claims — the 29% is the residual cache ratio; combined with full-attention layers and reference-token storage, the actual KR is 45.2% per Table 8 calculation.)
- **Hardware efficiency:** throughput at 128k/256k/512k/900k. Measured; "2×" supported only at largest contexts and only with mixed-BS comparison.
- **Training cost:** 8-14 GPU hours per backbone. Disclosed but not folded into the efficiency narrative; competitor OmniKV is training-free.

## 7. Framing-vs-demonstration audit

- **Framing (Abstract):** "DeltaKV reduces KV cache memory to 29% of the original"
- **Demonstration (Tab 8 calculation):** the actual KR for the Llama 30%-budget setting is 45.2% (= 0.156 + 0.844 × 0.35). The "29%" figure refers to the d_c / 2d_k ratio (residual compression rate within compressed layers), not the overall KR. The body discloses this; the abstract conflates the two.

- **Framing (Abstract):** "near-lossless accuracy on LongBench, SCBench, AND AIME"
- **Demonstration:** as documented in Section 2 above, contradicted on SCBench R.KV and AIME.

- **Framing (Abstract):** "up to 2× throughput improvement"
- **Demonstration:** supported at 512k with mixed BS; "up to" qualifier honest.

- **Framing (Sec 5.2):** "matching the results of OmniKV"
- **Demonstration:** holds on LongBench only; behind on SCBench and AIME.

## 8. Weaknesses found

- **[Major]** Framing-vs-demonstration mismatch on the abstract's "near-lossless on LongBench, SCBench, AND AIME" claim. SCBench R.KV drops 21 points absolute on Llama (26.6% relative); AIME drops 6.7 points (13.4% relative).
- **[Major]** "29% of the original" memory framing in the abstract refers to the residual compression rate, not the overall KR. The actual overall KR is ~45% per the paper's own Table 8. The abstract's framing implies the headline savings are larger than they are.
- **[Moderate]** "2× throughput" headline at 512k uses different batch sizes between numerator and denominator (Sparse-vLLM at BS=4, vLLM at BS=2). The "up to" qualifier mitigates this but the per-batch speedup at equal BS at 512k is unmeasured.
- **[Moderate]** Training-free OmniKV strictly dominates DeltaKV on SCBench and AIME accuracy while DeltaKV requires per-backbone training. The "matching OmniKV" framing in Sec 5.2 holds only for LongBench.
- **[Minor]** No seed counts or confidence intervals on accuracy benchmarks (typical for KV-compression literature).
- **[Minor]** Sparse-vLLM kernel cost ablation (135.0 vs 143.2 t/s overhead) is reasonable; engineering work appears credible.
