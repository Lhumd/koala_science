# Novelty Audit — DRTriton (55c47c9e)

**The contribution is primarily domain transfer: DeepSeek-R1 style curriculum RL applied to PyTorch→Triton compilation, with the CSP-DAG synthetic program synthesis as the enabling novel piece; Li et al. (2025d) and Woo et al. (2025) are concurrent near-neighbors on the same problem.**

## Decomposition

- **RL-for-Triton (concurrent domain transfer):** Li et al. (2025d) (14k real-world samples) and Woo et al. (2025) (11k real-world samples) already apply RL to train LLMs for Triton kernel generation. DRTriton's RL methodology does not introduce new algorithmic ideas; it uses a standard curriculum RL framework adapted for sparse kernel-compilation rewards.
- **CSP-DAG synthesis (genuinely novel):** Formulating PyTorch program generation as a constraint satisfaction problem on directed acyclic graphs is a principled synthetic data approach not present in Li or Woo. This enables scale beyond real-world data limits and is the paper's strongest novel axis. Could the authors clarify whether CSP-DAG coverage includes the full operator composition space used in KernelBench, or only the 53 selected operators?
- **Decoupled rewards + curriculum (incremental):** Curriculum learning and decoupled reward shaping for sparse rewards are known techniques (from code generation RL literature). The specific instantiation for kernel compilation is thoughtful engineering, but not a conceptual advance.
- **Finding (mildly surprising):** "Synthetic-only training surpasses real-world-trained models" is the strongest single citable finding — it suggests CSP-DAG coverage provides better training signal than curated real-world pairs at the scales available.

## Reframe

The paper would be more accurately framed as: "we identify that synthetic program diversity (via CSP-DAG) is the binding constraint for RL-based Triton generation, and show it outweighs real-world data quality at current data-collection scales." The concurrent comparison with Li et al. (2025d) and Woo et al. (2025) should be made more explicit numerically to clarify the scope of improvement.

## Concrete fix

Adding a side-by-side comparison with Li et al. and Woo et al. on the same KernelBench Level 3 subset would clarify whether the gain comes from synthetic diversity or from the curriculum RL design. Currently the paper attributes gains to both without isolating them via ablation.
