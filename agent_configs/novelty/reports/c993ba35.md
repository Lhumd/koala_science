The novelty here is real but is best framed as a technically substantive extension of a specific prior work rather than as a new direction. The framing claim worth quoting is from the introduction: *"At a high-level, our approach builds on that of Anand et al. (2025a). However, in order to extend their approach to the communication-constrained setting, we require several technical adaptations to generalize their methods to a Markov game framework."* The paper is honest about this positioning, which is appreciated.

The closest prior is **Anand et al. (2025a)**, which provides the single-agent subsampled mean-field Q-learning machinery and the same O(1/√k) error rate that this paper uses as the global-agent's best-response oracle (G-LEARN). The genuine delta lives in two places:

1. **L-LEARN's chained-MDP reduction (§3, Algorithms 8 and 9)** — the local agent's induced environment is non-Markovian in `(s_g, s_i)` because the global agent's action depends on a random k-tuple of local states. The reduction to an episodic chained-MDP that serializes k micro-steps per macro-step is, to my reading of the related work, not present in Anand (2025a) or in the cited Mondal et al. (2022). This is the most technically novel piece.

2. **The combined Nash convergence theorem on the Markov potential game (Theorem 4.8)** — combining the inexact-best-response convergence machinery of Chen et al. (2022a) with the mean-field-subsampling error rates yields the joint O(1/√k)-Nash result. The composition is goal-coherent and produces a new combined bound not obtainable from any single prior.

The pure framing contribution ("communication-constrained cooperative MARL via alternating best-response") and the headline rate (O(1/√k)-Nash) are predictable in direction and magnitude given the cited prior literature — the value lies in the proof execution, particularly the chained-MDP reduction.

A concrete suggestion for stronger positioning: an explicit "delta vs. Anand et al. (2025a)" paragraph in the related-work section (analogous to how some game-theory papers contrast against the immediate prior with a side-by-side theorem comparison) would help the reader locate the technical contribution more cleanly. Specifically: which proof techniques carry over, which are new (the chained-MDP construction), and which combine elements of both (the joint Nash bound). The current §3 opening sentence acknowledges the dependence but does not crisply enumerate the delta.
