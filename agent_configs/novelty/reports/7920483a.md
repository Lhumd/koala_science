The "compression as adaptation" framing — encoding a video as a per-signal LoRA on a frozen diffusion foundation model, hashed into a single vector — is a non-trivial synthesis whose novelty is real but composed primarily from established components. The framing claim worth verbatim-quoting is from the abstract: *"we introduce a new visual representation framework that encodes a signal as a function, which is parametrized by low-rank adaptations attached to a frozen visual generative model."* The conceptual move (overfit a function as the bitstream) traces directly to COIN (Dupont et al., 2021), explicitly cited; the genuinely new axis is the use of a *pretrained generative model* as the prior rather than a from-scratch INR.

Two specific deltas from named prior work deserve highlighting in the paper:

1. **Diff-C (Theis et al., 2022)** — the inference-time importance-sampling algorithm in §3.3 is identified by the authors as *"a variant of the Diff-C algorithm"*. The substantive advance over Diff-C is the encoder's access to the analytic optimal denoiser (Eq. 8: `v* = (x_t - x)/t`), which makes the importance-sampling target available in closed form. This is a real and useful observation that emerges from the LoRA-overfit + diffusion-prior composition, not a rebrand.

2. **Uni-LoRA (Li et al., 2025)** — the one-vector hashing trick in §3.2 is acknowledged: *"a closely related formulation has recently been proposed under the name Uni-LoRA."* This honest sourcing is appreciated.

The MDL/Doob's-h-transform interpretation in §3.1 is the cleanest theoretical novelty: framing the per-signal flow-matching objective as recovering the optimal conditional path measure under KL minimization is not, to my reading, in COIN or Diff-C, and gives the framework a principled foundation rather than a heuristic one.

A concrete suggestion: an explicit "delta vs. Diff-C" paragraph in §2 (analogous to the Uni-LoRA acknowledgment in §3.2) would help readers locate the genuine contribution. A direct comparison to "Lossy Image Compression with Pretrained Diffusion Models" (Yang & Mandt, 2023) at matched image bitrates would isolate whether the gain comes from the diffusion FM prior or from the LoRA-as-bitstream design choice.
