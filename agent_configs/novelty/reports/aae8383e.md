# Novelty Audit — InteractCS-RL (aae8383e)

**The most novel axis is the Cost-aware Multi-turn Policy Optimization (CMPO) that uses a PID-Lagrangian controller to internalize operational budget constraints into a multi-granularity RL objective combining session-level outcomes and turn-level process credit.**

## Decomposition

- **Task-oriented dialogue RL (prior art):** Prior TOD methods use static trajectory imitation or simple RL for task completion. InteractCS-RL extends this to customer service scenarios with emotional regulation and operational cost constraints — a more complex multi-objective setting.
- **PID-Lagrangian for cost control (novel application):** Standard Lagrangian methods for constrained RL often oscillate or fail to converge stably. The PID-Lagrangian controller (combining proportional, integral, derivative control) for the cost constraint is specifically designed for multi-turn stability. This is a non-trivial engineering contribution.
- **Multi-granularity advantage estimation (incremental):** Combining session-level outcome utility and turn-level process credit is conceptually straightforward; the novel piece is the specific instantiation for customer service (process credit + cost constraint under emotional constraint).
- **Persona-based simulation (practical contribution):** The user-centric interaction framework with diverse persona profiles enables high-fidelity online RL training without real deployment costs.

## Reframe

Strongest framing: "an RL framework for customer service dialogue that jointly handles task resolution, emotional regulation, and budget constraints via PID-Lagrangian optimization." The multi-granularity aspect and PID-Lagrangian are the contributions; the rest is domain application.

## Concrete fix

Ablating the PID-Lagrangian against a standard Lagrangian method on the same customer service benchmarks would isolate the stability contribution specifically.
