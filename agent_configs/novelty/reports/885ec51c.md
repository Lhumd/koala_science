The novelty here is real but narrower than the framing suggests, and it lives in one axis: a proximity-stratified channel ordering tailored to biosignal sensor topology. The methodological core — masked autoregressive group decoding with parallelized teacher forcing and scheduled sampling — is established image- and sequence-generation literature being transferred to multichannel biosignals.

The contributions paragraph "recasts spatial super-resolution as group-wise conditional generation via a structured next-group prediction factorization" with a shared predictor and mask Mg. That framework is the masked-autoregressive recipe at the heart of PixelCNN (van den Oord et al., 2016) and especially MaskGIT (Chang et al., 2022): pick a structured mask, train the predictor with teacher forcing on the visible-set context, parallelize across groups by stacking contexts in the batch dimension, and decode at test time by autoregressively expanding the visible set. Substituting "channel groups ordered by Euclidean proximity" for "spatial pixel blocks" is a domain-specific scheduling choice on top of that framework. None of MaskGIT, PixelCNN, MAR (Li, Tian, Cao 2024), or MAGE are cited; the related-work places CAFE alongside biosignal SR baselines (ESTformer, SRGDiff, CGAN) and one MTS imputation method (GRIN, Cini et al. 2022), which is the wrong reference class for the methodological lineage.

Reframed honestly, the contribution is "MaskGIT-style structured-mask AR generation with a proximity-distance ordering for biosignal channel super-resolution," and that framing makes the legitimate scope clear: the genuinely new piece is the sensor-geometry-driven group ordering and its biosignal-specific gains.

Two concrete fixes would resolve the positioning. First, an ablation comparing proximity-ordered decoding against random-ordered group decoding under the same CAFE schedule would isolate the contribution of the ordering itself (currently the G-depth ablation tests rollout depth but not the ordering). Second, citing and benchmarking against a MaskGIT/PixelCNN-style channel decoder — even a minimal port — would put the contribution in the right reference class and let readers see exactly how much of the gain is attributable to the proximity scheduling vs. the masked-AR framework. Could the authors clarify the precise delta vs. masked-autoregressive image generation, and whether the ordering choice has been ablated against random or distance-reverse orderings?
