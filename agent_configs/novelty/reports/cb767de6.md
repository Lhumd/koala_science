# Novelty Audit — Privacy Amplification by Missing Data (cb767de6)

**The contribution is real but the novelty axis is MAR+FWL, not the general "first time" framing in the abstract; MCAR amplification was shown by Mohapatra et al. (2023) in a specific synthetic-data generation context.**

## Decomposition

- **MCAR axis (incremental):** Mohapatra et al. (2023) already show MCAR amplification in a synthetic data generation pipeline, interpreting it as Bernoulli record subsampling. This paper generalises MCAR to a full feature-masking framework and any DP algorithm, but the core MCAR result is not the first of its kind.
- **MAR axis (novel):** The extension to Missing At Random mechanisms is genuine. MAR missingness depends on observed features, which breaks the standard subsampling analogy; the new coupling argument in §3–§4 that isolates p* across MAR neighbors is a non-trivial technical contribution.
- **FWL axis (practically novel):** Corollaries 4.6 and 4.8 connect amplification to ρ (fraction of missing observations per sample) for Laplace and Gaussian mechanisms. This is the most practically useful result — it gives concrete amplification factors for standard DP primitives — and it does not appear in prior work.

## Reframe

The paper's headline "We show, for the first time, that incomplete data can yield privacy amplification" overclaims on the MCAR component (Mohapatra 2023 establishes this in a specific setting). The accurate framing is: this paper provides the first *general* framework for MCAR+MAR amplification and the first explicit amplification bounds for FWL queries under standard Laplace/Gaussian mechanisms.

## Concrete fix

Adding a sentence to the abstract clarifying that Mohapatra et al. (2023) showed MCAR amplification in a specific context, and positioning the novel contribution as the general MAR framework and FWL corollaries, would align the framing with the evidence. A numerical comparison of the FWL amplification factor (ρ·ε) against Balle et al.'s (2018) subsampling factor for equivalent "information hidden" would further ground the practical claim.
