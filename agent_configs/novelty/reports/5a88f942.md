# Novelty Comment: 5a88f942 — Private PoEtry

**Private PoEtry's core novelty is the formal Product-of-Experts connection applied to DP-ICL: each context example becomes one expert, soft log-prob aggregation replaces hard voting, and the PoE structure enables both a theoretical convergence guarantee and 30 pp empirical gains over Wu et al. (2024) — the primitive (Hinton 2002) is prior art, but its instantiation in the DP-ICL setting is new.**

Three axes of novelty decomposition:

- **Primitive (PoE model):** Hinton (2002) / Heskes (1997) introduced Product-of-Experts for probabilistic ensembles. Well-established prior art. The novel contribution is applying this primitive specifically to differentially private ICL, where each in-context example is treated as one expert and the exponential mechanism handles DP sensitivity — a connection not present in prior DP-ICL work.
- **Method (soft vs. hard aggregation):** Wu et al. (2024) aggregates predictions via subsampling and discrete majority vote, discarding the logit distribution. This paper aggregates clipped log-probabilities directly, retaining distributional information. The direction (soft > hard) is intuitive to practitioners, but the formal PoE grounding is what enables the convergence result rather than leaving soft aggregation as a mere heuristic.
- **Theory (convergence result):** The strongest contribution is a theorem showing that as the number of in-context examples J → ∞, the PoE-ICL distribution converges to the full-context LLM distribution. This prediction is enabled specifically by the PoE framing and is absent from prior DP-ICL work (Tang 2024 uses synthetic data generation; Wu 2024 uses discrete voting — neither framework supports this convergence claim).

Reframed honestly: framed as "soft aggregation outperforms hard voting," the contribution is directionally expected and moderately incremental. Framed as "PoE provides a theoretically grounded, parallelizable DP-ICL framework with formal convergence guarantees," the contribution is more substantial. The strongest piece is the convergence theorem, which is currently buried behind the empirical lead in the abstract.

A concrete improvement: an ablation comparing (a) soft log-prob aggregation without the PoE formalism vs. (b) the full PoE algorithm would isolate whether the 30 pp gain arises from the PoE structure specifically or simply from using full logit distributions instead of discrete votes. Additionally, elevating the convergence theorem (currently implicit in the theory section) to the abstract would better represent the paper's most original contribution. Could the authors clarify whether the 30 pp improvement holds uniformly or is concentrated on the task types where hard-voting priors (Wu et al.) were most suboptimal?
