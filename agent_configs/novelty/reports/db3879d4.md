The novelty here is real and substantive. The framing claim worth quoting is from the abstract: *"Our key mechanism, Dual-Timestep Scheduling, applies heterogeneous noise levels across tokens, creating an information asymmetry that forces the model to infer missing information from corrupted inputs."* This is a concretely defined mechanism that does not reduce cleanly to any single prior in the related work.

The closest neighbors are:

1. **REPA (Yu et al., 2024)** — the dominant external-alignment baseline, cited extensively. REPA aligns flow model intermediate features with frozen DINO features. Self-Flow's delta is the cleanest: replace the external encoder with an internal self-supervised mechanism. The 2.8× faster convergence vs. REPA + the scaling-law adherence (vs. REPA's plateau) is exactly the kind of empirical claim that warrants the methodological move.

2. **Jiang et al. 2025; Haghighi et al. 2025 — cited as concurrent "internal-feature self-alignment" work** — these methods rely on layer-wise asymmetry within the model. Self-Flow's delta is to introduce *token-level noise asymmetry* as a richer self-supervision source than layer-asymmetry alone, which the paper claims (and the experiments support) drives stronger representations.

3. **MAE (He et al., 2022) and BYOL (Grill et al., 2020) — cited** — Self-Flow can be read as a hybrid of these two SSL paradigms (mask-and-predict + predict-target-features) adapted to the flow-matching setting via continuous noise levels rather than binary masks. The conceptual ancestry is clear, but the specific instantiation within flow matching with two parallel forward passes is genuinely new.

Two observations worth raising:

(a) The "scaling-law adherence" claim (vs. REPA's plateau) is a strong empirical finding and the most differentiating result. Could the authors include the explicit scaling exponent comparison (Self-Flow vs. REPA vs. vanilla flow matching) at multiple model scales? Fig. 1 shows the convergence speedup but the scaling-law evidence is qualitative in the abstract.

(b) The relationship to RCG (Li et al., 2023, "Self-conditioned Image Generation via Generating Representations") — another "avoid external alignment" line — would be worth explicitly discussing in the related work for completeness.

A concrete suggestion: the dual-timestep mechanism's connection to MAE-style mask prediction is not made explicit in the related work, but it is the cleanest conceptual ancestry. An explicit "Self-Flow as a continuous-noise generalization of MAE-on-flow-matching" framing in §2 would help readers locate the technical move and would strengthen the citation magnetism by tying to a foundational SSL paper. The current framing ("we propose to directly integrate a self-supervised framework into flow matching") is correct but obscures the specific lineage.
