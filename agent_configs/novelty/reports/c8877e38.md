The novelty here is real but is best described as a clean methodological inversion plus substantial engineering scale, rather than a step-change in the data-synthesis literature. The framing claim worth quoting is from the abstract: *"DIVE, an evidence-driven recipe that inverts synthesis order, executing diverse, real-world tools first and reverse-deriving tasks strictly entailed by the resulting traces, thereby providing grounding by construction."* The inversion is concretely defined and well-motivated by the §3 tension between structural diversity and grounded validity.

The closest neighbors are:

1. **ToolBench / ToolLLaMA (Qin et al., 2023)** — cited as the canonical "query-first" recipe (generate task → check executability). DIVE's flip (execute → derive task) is a clean delta and resolves the verification bottleneck that ToolBench-style approaches incur.

2. **AgentInstruct (Mitra et al., 2024)** — uses simulated/seed-driven synthesis. DIVE's delta is replacing simulated tools with 373 validated real tools across 5 domains, which addresses the unverifiable-trace risk that simulation incurs.

3. **Liu et al. (2025a) "deep-research" pipelines** — cited as "extracting data from specialized pipelines is costly, as scaling diversity requires manually scaling pipelines." DIVE's decoupled-pool decomposition (tool / seed / exemplar) is the precise mechanism that removes this manual-scaling bottleneck.

Two observations worth raising:

(a) The "diversity > quantity scaling" finding (4× less data → better OOD) is a substantive empirical claim, but it should be positioned within the broader diversity-matters literature (Wang et al. 2023 Self-Instruct; Yauney et al. 2023 "Data Diversity Matters for Robust Instruction Tuning") rather than as a fresh discovery. The novelty is the controlled demonstration in the *agentic tool-use* setting with a verified tool pool; the underlying principle is established.

(b) The "+68% over the strongest 8B baseline" framing in the abstract is impressive but is implicitly scoped to the 8B model class. Could the authors clarify whether the diversity-scaling finding holds at larger model scales (Qwen3-32B+ or frontier-tier)? The current evidence supports the claim within the 8B regime.

A concrete suggestion: an explicit "delta vs. AgentInstruct" subsection in §2 would help; AgentInstruct is the most direct conceptual neighbor (multi-domain synthetic agentic data) and the differentiation (real tools + inverted order vs. simulated tools + query-first) is the heart of DIVE's methodological contribution. A side-by-side comparison table along these axes would make the positioning concrete.
