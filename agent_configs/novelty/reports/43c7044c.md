# UAOR: Uncertainty-aware Observation Reinjection for Vision-Language-Action Models

---

**UAOR's contribution is a training-free combination of (a) per-layer action-entropy gating from VLA uncertainty literature (Valle et al. 2025) and (b) FFN-as-key-value-memory observation retrieval from Geva et al. (2021), producing a plug-and-play inference-time observation refresh for VLAs; the genuine novelty is the uncertainty-triggered coupling of these two existing mechanisms, not either component alone.**

The paper decomposes into three axes:

- **Uncertainty metric (Action Entropy):** Builds directly on Token-Based Entropy (TB-E, Valle et al. 2025), adapting it to layer-wise averages over action or condition tokens. The metric itself is an incremental refinement of existing VLA uncertainty literature rather than a new primitive.
- **Reinjection mechanism (FFN-as-memory retrieval):** The key-value memory interpretation of FFN layers is taken directly from Geva et al. (2021) and applied in the VLA context. The paper cites this lineage explicitly. The application to observation features as the "memory" being queried is the transfer step.
- **Uncertainty gating (the coupling):** The non-obvious piece is the conditional triggering: reinjection happens only when action entropy at layer ℓ exceeds threshold γ, targeting the specific layer range where the paper empirically shows attention to observation tokens drops. This gating is not present in Geva et al. (2021) or in TB-E (Valle et al. 2025), and is what distinguishes UAOR from both "always reinject" and "never reinject" baselines.

The contribution is better framed as: *a training-free inference-time observation-refresh mechanism for VLAs, where the entropy signal both diagnoses observation-attention decay and gates the remedy.* The broader framing in the abstract — "helps VLAs better attend to observations during inference, enabling more confident and faithful action generation" — is accurate but undersells the specific diagnostic-to-intervention design choice.

One key comparison is missing: the paper does not compare UAOR against uniform reinjection (injecting at every layer without entropy gating). Without this ablation, it is unclear whether the uncertainty gating is the essential innovation or whether simply "more observation access" explains the gains. Could the authors report this baseline to isolate the gating component's contribution?
