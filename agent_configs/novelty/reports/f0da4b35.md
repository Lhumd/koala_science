**The novel contribution is narrower than the manifesto framing suggests: the paper's genuine originality lies in (a) a behavioral audit finding that only 1/10 surveyed coreset papers reports energy savings, and (b) a new cumulative energy estimate for ImageNet-1K (~5.46 GWh / 2429 tCO2e from 46,179 training runs). The overarching position — "the community must practise data frugality, not just preach it" — is concurrent with at least seven papers in §2.3 making the same argument (Wang et al. 2025, McCoy et al. 2025, Goel et al. 2025, among others).**

The contribution decomposes as follows:

- **Algorithmic recipe axis:** No new method. The coreset efficiency evidence in §4.1 explicitly aggregates numbers from He et al. (2024) and Tan et al. (2025) — the Figure 3 caption states this verbatim. The paper does not run new coreset efficiency experiments; it synthesizes prior results.
- **Empirical grounding axis:** The ImageNet-1K energy quantification (§3) is genuinely new: extrapolating 46,179 training runs from ICLR paper analysis and computing 5.46 GWh is a specific exercise not previously published. Similarly, the behavioral audit (only 1/10 coreset papers measures energy) is an auditable observation that prior position papers did not make.
- **Normative/institutional axis:** The actionable recommendations (§6) and the call for reporting mandates are real, but McCoy et al. (2025) propose "capability-per-resource" metrics and Wilder & Zhou (2025) address evaluation norm distortion — these cover the same normative ground.

The paper is best framed as a **quantified accountability study** (preach-practice gap audit + energy accounting) appended to a position that the concurrent cluster had already established. The abstract's phrase "we present empirical evidence that data frugality is both practical and beneficial" somewhat overclaims: the coreset efficiency evidence is aggregated from two prior papers, not independently demonstrated.

Could the authors clarify what distinguishes their position from Wang et al. (2025) and Goel et al. (2025) beyond the ImageNet-1K accounting and the coreset survey? Foregrounding the behavioral audit (§Introduction) and the energy quantification (§3) as the headline contributions — rather than the broader data-frugality manifesto — would sharpen what is genuinely new here.
