The novelty here lies on a narrow but defensible **method** axis — the Failure-Driven Augmentation (FDA) render-back primitive — while the Soft-Rejection Sampling half is essentially a continuous-verifier transfer of an existing self-training recipe to image-to-CAD.

The abstract frames the dual mechanism symmetrically: *"GIFT combines two mechanisms: Soft-Rejection Sampling (GIFT-REJECT), which retains diverse high-fidelity programs beyond exact ground-truth matches, and Failure-Driven Augmentation (GIFT-FAIL), which converts near-miss predictions into synthetic training examples."* The paper itself acknowledges the closest neighbor on the SRS half: *"GIFT differs in how it obtains the signal. Instead of relying on heuristic confidence scores or learned rewards, it uses a deterministic geometric kernel."* Read against STaR (Zelikman et al. 2022) and rejection-sampling-fine-tuning (Dong et al. 2023), SRS is "STaR with a soft IoU-interval acceptance threshold using a CAD verifier" — a substantive domain transfer (continuous, equivalence-class-aware verifier instead of discrete answer match), but the underlying principle is the same.

The genuinely new piece is FDA's inverse-rendering step (§3.1): rendering a failed program z back into a synthetic image c̃ = φ(d(z)) and training (c̃, z_gt) as a "geometric denoising" objective. This produces a hard-negative augmentation specific to executable-code → geometry domains that pure rejection sampling does not. Fig. 2d shows FDA contributes the larger lift (+53.8% problems-solved vs. +31.4% from SRS), consistent with FDA being the load-bearing primitive.

Reframed as *"a render-back / geometric-denoising augmentation for image-to-CAD that complements (rather than replaces) standard rejection-sampling fine-tuning,"* the contribution is honest. Framed as a new "dual" framework, it slightly inflates the SRS half.

A concrete fix that would tighten the positioning and head off reviewer doubts: (1) lead with the FDA render-back primitive in the abstract, with SRS as the supporting half; (2) add a head-to-head against an online geometric-RL baseline (e.g., the cited Wang 2025b / Chen 2025 / Li 2025c) — without it, the "RL alternative without instability" claim rests on indirect evidence; (3) state the relationship to STaR / RFT explicitly in the abstract, not just §3.1.
