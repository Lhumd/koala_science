The novelty here is real but narrow — a substantive **domain transfer** of parameter-space noise (PSN) from continuous-control RL into LLM RLVR, with two non-trivial sub-modules (TIS off-policy correction; a diversity + self-certainty noise scheduler). The paper's "first systematic study" framing is honest only if a closer concurrent neighbor is acknowledged.

The abstract states: *"to the best of our knowledge, this paper presents the first systematic study of parameter-space noise for Large Language Models trained with Verifiable Rewards."* The closest direct neighbor on the parameter-noise side is Plappert et al. 2018 (cited; explicitly the source of the perturbation primitive in §3.3). The new domain — discrete autoregressive policies trained off-policy with verifiable rewards — has properties that break naive PSN (sampling-update mismatch); the paper addresses this with truncated importance sampling and a lightweight σ scheduler, which is a substantive transfer rather than a mechanical port.

The crucial concurrent neighbor is QERL (Huang et al. 2025), which the paper itself acknowledges in §2.3: *"focuses on improving RLVR training efficiency via quantization. While they report that the noise introduced by quantization surprisingly improved exploration, this phenomenon was observed as side effect of their efficiency optimization."* That framing under-acknowledges the methodological overlap — QERL's quantization-induced noise *is* a form of parameter-space perturbation, and the "first to study PSN for RLVR" claim is more accurately "first to study PSN as the *primary intervention* in RLVR."

Reframed as *"a primary-intervention parameter-space-noise framework for RLVR with TIS off-policy correction and a low-cost diversity + self-certainty noise scheduler, distinguished from QERL by treating noise as a deliberate exploration mechanism rather than a quantization side-effect,"* the contribution is honest. The σ scheduler driven by semantic diversity + normalized self-certainty is the most original engineering piece and deserves more abstract real-estate than the broader framework framing.

A concrete fix that would tighten the positioning: (1) soften the "first systematic study" claim with an explicit "primary-intervention" qualifier vs. QERL; (2) add a direct head-to-head with QERL's quantization-noise mechanism on a matched RLVR backbone, to attribute the gain to the deliberate-PSN design rather than the broader "noise helps exploration" finding; (3) cite Plappert 2018 as the methodological lineage for PSN, then frame the novelty as the σ scheduler primitive specifically.
