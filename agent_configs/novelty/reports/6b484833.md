**ALIEN replaces iterative optimization in latent watermarking (Huang et al. 2024; Zhang et al. 2024) with a closed-form analytical derivation of a time-dependent modulation coefficient; the novel piece is deriving how watermark residuals propagate through the diffusion forward SDE/ODE, enabling sampler-agnostic (including irreversible) watermark embedding.**

**Novelty decomposition by axis:**

- **Problem setting and goal** — embedding watermarks in initial LDM latents before generation: shared with Huang et al. 2024 (optimization-based) and Wen et al. 2024 (direct modification). This axis is established prior art.
- **Key primitive — analytic derivation of watermark propagation dynamics** — new. Prior optimization-based methods (Huang et al. 2024; Zhang et al. 2024) approximate the modulation through iterative gradient search, accepting local optima and requiring reversible samplers (e.g., DDIM) to invert the generation. ALIEN works through the forward SDE/ODE dynamics directly and computes the time-dependent coefficient in closed form. This is the contribution that is absent from the prior literature.
- **New property — sampler-agnostic embedding** — new. Yang et al. 2024c and Gunn et al. 2024 constrain sampling to Gaussian-identifiable subspaces, limiting generative diversity; optimization-based methods require reversible samplers. ALIEN's analytic approach imposes no sampler constraint and works with irreversible solvers (DEIS, DPM-Solver). This universality property is not present in any prior class of methods.

**Reframe:** The core contribution is a principled analytical treatment of watermark signal propagation through the diffusion forward process rather than a new embedding architecture. This is more accurately described as a theoretical contribution with strong empirical validation — the 33.1% quality improvement and 14% robustness improvement over SOTA are consequences of the analytic grounding, not independent contributions. The paper's headline claim, "first analytical derivation of time-dependent modulation coefficient for watermark embedding in LDMs," appears accurate based on the related-work coverage.

**Concrete fix:** A direct comparison showing that ALIEN's analytical coefficient matches what iterative optimization converges to on reversible samplers (where both methods are applicable) would provide the strongest theoretical validation of the derivation. Additionally, the sampler-agnosticity property — arguably as important as the quality gains — is underemphasized in the abstract; elevating it to the headline would sharpen the contribution claim.
