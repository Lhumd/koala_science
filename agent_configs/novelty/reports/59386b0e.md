The novelty here is real but narrow — it lies primarily in the closed-form derivation that enables differentiable RL on discrete graph flow models, with the rest of the contribution being a clean composition. The framing claim worth quoting is from the abstract: *"We derive an analytical expression for the transition probability of GFMs, replacing the Monte Carlo sampling and enabling fully differentiable rollouts for RL training."* This is concretely defined and the derivation in §3 is the central technical contribution.

The closest priors are:

1. **DDPO (Black et al., 2023) and related RL-for-diffusion approaches** — solve the analogous problem in continuous diffusion via score-function reformulations. The Graph-GRPO derivation is the discrete-flow-matching-on-graphs analogue of this established move. The conceptual blueprint (replace stochastic sampling with a differentiable analytical form for RL gradients) is established; the specific derivation for the discrete factorized graph case is new.

2. **Liu et al. (2024) — RL with graph diffusion models, cited** — already extends RL to graph generative models. The delta vs. Liu 2024 is the substrate (discrete flow matching on graphs vs. graph diffusion) and the GRPO outer loop. The paper could more clearly diff against Liu 2024 — currently the related work treats it as a one-line reference.

3. **DeFoG (Qin et al., 2025) — cited as the base GFM** — provides the discrete flow matching graph generative model that Graph-GRPO trains with RL. The contribution is properly framed as RL on top of DeFoG.

A few observations:

(a) The noise-and-regenerate refinement strategy in contribution (2) is conceptually parallel to SDEdit (Meng et al., 2022) for continuous-diffusion image editing, applied to graphs and combined with reward-based selection of high-reward samples. SDEdit is not cited; an explicit "graph analogue of SDEdit" framing would situate the contribution within prior iterative-refinement work.

(b) The "SOTA on molecular optimization" claim is well-supported empirically, but the comparison spans three method families (graph-based RL, fragment-based RL, genetic algorithms) — pointing out which family Graph-GRPO most directly displaces (and by how much) would tighten the headline.

A concrete suggestion: an explicit comparison subsection in §2 with Liu et al. (2024) would crystallize the contribution. Specifically, the analytical-transition-probability derivation is the cleanest novelty axis, and contrasting it against Liu's gradient-handling approach for graph diffusion would make the technical delta explicit. Additionally, citing SDEdit and noting the conceptual parallel for the refinement strategy would honestly position the iterative-refinement contribution.
