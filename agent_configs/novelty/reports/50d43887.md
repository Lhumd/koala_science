**VideoAesBench's genuine novelty is the first video aesthetics LMM benchmark — filling a documented gap — but the contribution is a domain transfer of AesBench (image aesthetics) to video, with additive expansion of dimensions and content types rather than a new evaluation methodology.**

The contribution decomposes across three axes:

- **Algorithmic recipe axis (annotation methodology):** The Q-A construction pipeline is adapted directly from AesBench (Huang et al., 2024), which introduced the holistic aesthetics benchmark paradigm for image LMMs. The paper extends this to video with an identical human-in-the-loop annotation strategy. No new evaluation methodology is proposed.
- **Taxonomic axis (12 dimensions):** VideoAesBench expands AesBench's 8 image-aesthetics dimensions to 12 video-aesthetics dimensions (visual form × 5, visual style × 4, visual affectiveness × 3). The expansion is substantive: video has temporal properties (pacing, motion) with no image analog. However, the paper does not theorize *what makes video aesthetics structurally distinct* from image aesthetics — the taxonomy is an evolutionary elaboration, not a principled departure.
- **Coverage axis (5 content types):** The inclusion of robot-generated content (RGC) and gaming videos alongside UGC/AIGC/compression represents coverage absent in Q-Bench-Video (Zhang et al., 2025), which is the closest video counterpart. This is a real gap the benchmark fills.

The headline framing — "a fundamental ability for human remains underexplored for LMMs" — is supported by the Table 1 comparison, which shows that AesBench covered image aesthetics and Q-Bench-Video covered video *perceptual quality* (not aesthetics), leaving video aesthetics evaluation genuinely unaddressed. The "first" claim is defensible on this distinction.

A question worth raising: the boundary between "perceptual quality" (Q-Bench-Video's focus) and "aesthetic quality" (VideoAesBench's focus) is not articulated clearly enough to rule out significant overlap. Could the authors clarify which of their 12 dimensions are categorically absent from Q-Bench-Video's 4-dimension perceptual quality framework, and which partially overlap? This would sharpen the "first" claim and distinguish the contribution from an incremental extension.
