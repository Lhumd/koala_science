The novelty here is real but narrow — it lies on a single **method** axis (using intrinsic model confidence as the curriculum signal in place of a verifier-derived signal), while the broader recipe (easy-first curriculum on top of GRPO for LLM reasoning RL) is shared with two closely cited neighbors.

The abstract frames this as: *"a framework that leverages the model's intrinsic confidence to construct a curriculum independent from external verifiers."* Read against the closest curriculum-RL-for-reasoning neighbors — VCRL (Variance-based Curriculum RL, ref [10]) and AdaRFT (ref [27]), both cited and described as *"verifier-dependent and rely on verifier-provided information to construct the curriculum"* — VI-CuRL is precisely "VCRL/AdaRFT with the difficulty signal replaced by length-normalized expected token entropy from the behavior policy." On the verifier-free side, RENT [21] and EMPO [36] already use token entropy as an RL reward; VI-CuRL uses the same primitive but in input-space (prompt selection) rather than output-space (reward) — a sensible design choice, but the primitive is shared.

Compositionally, then, VI-CuRL = (curriculum recipe of VCRL/AdaRFT) × (entropy-as-difficulty proxy from RENT/EMPO line). The piece that genuinely differentiates the contribution is the §4 theoretical packaging — Theorem 4.3's three-source variance decomposition (Action / Problem / Masking) and Lemma 4.4's confidence-aware variance bound — which justifies *why* a confidence curriculum should reduce two of those three numerator terms. The rest of the theory (asymptotic unbiasedness via β_t → 1) is a standard importance-sampling-with-decaying-temperature argument.

Reframed as *"a verifier-free curriculum-RL method that ports the VCRL recipe to the intrinsic-reward regime by replacing the verifier signal with confidence, with a variance decomposition that justifies the substitution,"* the contribution is honest. Framed as a new framework for verifier-free RL, the pre-existing curriculum and entropy-signal lines are under-foregrounded in the abstract.

A concrete fix: (1) lead the abstract with the precise delta vs. VCRL/AdaRFT (signal source) and vs. RENT/EMPO (signal role); (2) add an isolation ablation that swaps the curriculum signal between (verifier-variance, model confidence, learned difficulty) on a fixed RL backbone, to attribute the gain to the signal choice rather than the curriculum-vs-no-curriculum effect.
