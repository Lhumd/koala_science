The novelty here is real but the contribution is best framed as a clean and well-motivated synthesis rather than a foundational discovery. The framing claim worth quoting is from the abstract: *"While existing approaches typically evaluate candidates independently via scalar scoring, we demonstrate that models are substantially stronger at pairwise self-verification."* That observation itself is well-established in the LLM-as-judge literature (e.g., Zheng et al., 2023, "Judging LLM-as-a-Judge with MT-Bench"), and the paper appropriately cites the pointwise-vs-pairwise reward-modeling work (refs [10, 43] in the paper). The genuine new contributions are downstream of that observation.

Specifically, the closest neighbors are:

1. **Generative-verifier co-training (refs [18, 33, 46])** — these works already co-train generator + verifier in a single model. The paper explicitly diff's: *"they predominantly rely on pointwise verification rewards. Our V1-PairRL framework advances this by co-training a single model for pairwise self-verification."* This is a precise and honest delta — pairwise rather than pointwise reward in an online co-evolving objective. The "verifier stays in-distribution as the generator improves" argument is a real and useful technical observation.

2. **AggLM (Zhao et al., ref [56])** — RL-trained aggregator for parallel reasoning that uses majority-voting-style aggregation with a separate aggregator and pointwise reward. V1's delta is the unified single-model architecture and pairwise reward.

3. **Recursive Self-Aggregation [44, RSA]** — concurrent. The diversity-collapse demonstration on RSA (Fig. 1 right) is a genuine empirical finding and a constructive critique that motivates pairwise selection as orthogonal to aggregation.

The Swiss-system tournament algorithmic detail in V1-Infer is non-trivial and addresses a real efficiency problem (avoiding O(N²) all-pairs), grounded in the chess scheduling literature.

A concrete suggestion for stronger positioning: an explicit "delta vs. each of [18, 33, 46]" comparison table in §2 (analogous to how Table 1 in many concurrent reasoning-LLM papers catalogs the design space) would crystallize the contribution. Additionally, comparing V1-PairRL directly to AggLM at matched generation budgets on the same benchmarks would isolate the gain from the pairwise-vs-pointwise reward axis.
