# Novelty Audit — When Scaling Fails: Audio Perception Decay (5c3f9b40)

**The paper identifies "audio perception decay" — an unintuitive phenomenon where extending reasoning length in LALMs degrades audio perception rather than improving it — as the reason test-time scaling (which succeeds in language) fails for Large Audio-Language Models.**

## Decomposition

- **Test-time scaling failure (counterintuitive):** The established paradigm is that longer reasoning chains improve performance. This paper shows the opposite for LALMs: "post-training models for structured reasoning trajectories results in marginal or even negative gains compared to post-training for direct answering." This is the strongest novelty signal — expert prediction (scaling helps) is directly reversed.
- **Audio perception decay mechanism (novel diagnostic):** The CAFE evaluation framework quantifies that the specific failure mode is audio perception degrading as reasoning extends — not a general reasoning failure, but a modality-specific one. Step-Audio-R1 (Tian et al. 2025) identifies over-reliance on textual reasoning; MMAR (Ma et al. 2025b) identifies audio errors as dominant. This paper adds the specific diagnosis that the decay is length-dependent.
- **MPAR2 solution (incremental):** The proposed remedy (multi-step perception-aware reasoning) addresses the identified failure. This is a useful contribution but secondary to the diagnostic finding.

## Reframe

The core contribution is the diagnosis: "test-time scaling fails for audio because longer reasoning degrades audio perception, measured by CAFE." The solution (MPAR2) follows naturally. The paper's title captures this well.

## Concrete fix

A direct comparison with Step-Audio-R1's modality-grounded approach on the same CAFE metrics would clarify whether MPAR2 specifically addresses the decay or whether Step-Audio-R1's approach also mitigates it. Currently it's unclear how differentiated the proposed solution is from prior modality-grounded methods.
