**AMD introduces the "Forbidden Zone" concept — regions where DMD's (Yin et al. 2024) real-teacher guidance fails and fake-teacher repulsion is insufficient — and provides the first explicit mechanism (low-reward detection + Repulsive Landscape Sharpening) to escape them; the unifying insight is that prior DMD variants (DMD2, Shao 2025, Jiang 2025) implicitly constrain the student away from Forbidden Zones without detecting them explicitly.**

**Decomposition by axis:**

- **Algorithmic recipe:** DMD's dual-teacher framework (Yin et al. 2024) is unchanged. AMD operates on top of DMD's real-teacher / fake-teacher decomposition without replacing the core distillation objective.
- **Failure mode (new conceptual contribution):** "Forbidden Zones" — regions where the real teacher is unreliable *and* the fake teacher's repulsive gradient is insufficient — is a new concept not present in DMD or DMD2. The paper defines it geometrically and uses it to reinterpret prior DMD variants as implicit avoidance strategies.
- **Escape mechanism (all three components new):** (1) low-reward scoring to detect Forbidden Zone membership at training time; (2) Repulsive Landscape Sharpening (RLS), where the fake teacher is retrained on corrupted samples to sharpen repulsion specifically in failure regions; (3) dynamic signal prioritization that up-weights repulsion over attraction when the student is in a Forbidden Zone. None of these three appear in DMD2 or the cited concurrent works.

**Reframe:** The strongest contribution is the Forbidden Zone unification perspective — it reframes prior ad-hoc DMD fixes (DMD2's stabilization scheme, Shao 2025's modifications, Jiang 2025's approach) as implicit manifestations of the same underlying training pathology. AMD's explicit detection turns this into a principled algorithmic criterion. The novelty is real and on the conceptual-framework axis; the base distillation architecture is entirely prior art.

**Concrete fix:** Quantifying what fraction of training steps enter Forbidden Zones — before and after AMD — would empirically ground the "Forbidden Zone" concept rather than relying solely on downstream quality metrics (HPSv2, FID) as indirect evidence. A diagnostic plot of Forbidden Zone entry frequency across training would also validate the unification claim by showing that DMD2 implicitly suppresses the same regions AMD detects explicitly.
