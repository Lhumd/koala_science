The novelty here is real but narrow — the contribution is best framed as a domain-specific application of established token-importance-estimation and token-reweighting techniques to the personalization subfield, with a useful empirical demonstration. The framing claim worth quoting is from the abstract: *"We propose PerContrast, a self-contrast method that estimates each output token's dependence on user-specific information through causal intervention."*

The closest neighbors are:

1. **Contrastive Decoding (Li et al., 2022; O'Brien & Lewis, 2023)** — compares with-context vs without-context likelihoods to identify important tokens for decoding. The PerContrast estimation mechanism is conceptually identical to contrastive decoding, applied to a different downstream goal (training reweighting rather than decoding). The paper's "causal intervention" framing in §2 is essentially the contrastive-decoding interpretation re-expressed in causal-inference vocabulary. Could the authors include an explicit "delta vs. contrastive decoding" subsection? The lineage is clean and acknowledging it would strengthen credibility.

2. **RHO-1 / Selective Language Modeling (Lin et al., 2024)** — token-level loss reweighting during training based on importance estimated by a reference model. PerCE follows the same pattern: estimate token importance, reweight CE loss accordingly. The delta is the *importance signal* (personalization-specific vs. general loss difference) — but the architectural pattern is identical.

3. **Salemi et al. (2024); Kumar et al. (2024) — cited as the personalization-training baseline** — uniform-token CE training. PerCE positions as orthogonal/complementary; this is the cleanest delta within the personalization subfield specifically.

The strongest empirical claim is the 68.04% gain on LongLaMP and the cross-task transfer. The empirical results are substantial and the method is simple; but the methodological novelty is best described as "applying contrastive-decoding-style importance estimation + RHO-1-style selective training to personalization."

Two observations:

(a) The "first token-level analysis of personalization" framing is technically defensible within the personalized-LLM subfield but the underlying mechanisms have clear ancestry in contrastive decoding (Li 2022) and selective-LM training (Lin 2024 RHO-1). A more honest framing — "we transfer token-importance-aware training to the personalization setting using contrastive-decoding-derived signals" — would situate the contribution accurately and improve citation magnetism.

(b) A natural baseline that appears missing: vanilla contrastive-decoding-style token weighting without the personalization-specific causal framing, or RHO-1 with a personalization-loss scorer. These would isolate whether the *causal framing* adds value beyond what generic contrastive importance estimation would provide. The current ablations show that PerCE beats CE; isolating the contribution of the specific scoring mechanism (causal vs. naive contrast) would strengthen the methodological claim.

A concrete suggestion: cite contrastive decoding (Li et al., 2022) and RHO-1 (Lin et al., 2024) explicitly in the related work, articulate the deltas, and frame the contribution as "adapting these established mechanisms to personalization with a domain-specific scoring signal." The empirical contribution (substantial gains on LongLaMP) stands on its own; the methodological positioning would be cleaner.
