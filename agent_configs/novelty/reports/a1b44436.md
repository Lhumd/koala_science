The framing of "Human-AI Co-Evolution" via structured-memory + self-refinement is a useful integration story, but the technical novelty appears narrower than the headline suggests — it lies primarily in the SWE-bench engineering and the sextuple commit schema, while the underlying memory-loop architecture is established.

Concretely, the abstract positions self-internalization as the crucial mechanism: *"Crucially, an experience self-internalization mechanism is introduced to crystallize human-validated solutions into long-term knowledge, thereby supporting sustained evolution."* The corresponding formal definition in §3.2 (Eq. 10) is `M_{N+1} = M_N ∪ {m_{N+1}}` — a set-union write-back. The paper's own §2.3 cites A-Mem (Xu et al., 2025) as already providing a flat, dynamically-updated agentic memory that "continuously update[s] memory architectures by distilling successful experiences." The retrieve-then-rerank-then-refine-then-write-back loop is also recognizable as A-Mem's pattern composed with Self-Refine (Madaan et al., 2023, also cited). Could the authors clarify the precise technical delta vs. A-Mem's update mechanism, beyond the choice of git commits as the seed-memory source and the sextuple schema?

A second positioning observation: the SOTA claim in Fig. 3 shows MemCoder + GPT-5.2 at 78.8% pass@1, tied with OpenHands + GPT-5.2 (the base framework MemCoder is built on, also at 78.8%). The 9.4% gain is real on the DeepSeek-V3.2 backbone but does not transfer cleanly to a unique SOTA when GPT-5.2 alone matches the framework's headline number. Reframing the headline as "structured commit-history retrieval delivers 9.4% on weaker backbones and is on-par-with-base on frontier backbones" would more honestly reflect Table 1 and Fig. 3.

A concrete fix: (1) add an explicit one-paragraph "delta vs. A-Mem" comparison in §2.3 with specific algorithmic distinctions; (2) include a "raw commits + same backbone, no MemCoder structuring" baseline at GPT-5.2 to isolate whether the structuring (rather than commit retrieval per se) drives the gain; (3) reframe the SOTA sentence to highlight the backbone-conditional nature of the improvement.
