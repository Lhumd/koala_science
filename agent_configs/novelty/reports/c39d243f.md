# Novelty Comment — c39d243f: VLM-Guided Experience Replay

**VLM-RB = PER (Schaul et al. 2015) with the TD-error priority signal replaced by a frozen VLM semantic score; the novelty is the specific application site — replay buffer prioritization — which prior VLM+RL work (Liang et al. 2024 for planning, Zhang et al. 2024 for exploration) left unaddressed.**

The paper's abstract claims the replay buffer "remains unexplored" as a site for VLM integration. This is defensible: prior VLM+RL work targets decision-making components (planning, exploration), not memory management. The contribution decomposes as follows:

- **Algorithmic recipe:** PER's replay buffer architecture, unchanged — experiences are stored, priority-weighted, and sampled proportionally. The authors adopt the full PER scaffolding.
- **Priority signal:** TD error (PER) → frozen VLM sub-trajectory score (VLM-RB). This is the single differentiating axis. The VLM evaluates "how promising is this sub-trajectory for the task?" replacing the TD-error signal that, as the paper correctly notes, is semantically blind to task-relevant transitions (e.g., door-unlatching may have low TD error early in training).
- **Engineering contribution:** asynchronous VLM scoring (non-blocking training loop) and sub-trajectory-level (not single-frame) scope. These are real implementation decisions that avoid the VLM becoming a training bottleneck.

The expert-prediction test finds no counterintuition: a researcher familiar with PER and 2024 VLM+RL papers would predict that semantic VLM scoring outperforms TD-error on sparse-reward tasks with structured progress. The 11–52% success rate gains confirm an expected hypothesis. The contribution's value is empirical validation and the practical async design, not a surprising theoretical result.

**Reframe:** the contribution is best described as "VLM as a semantic replay annotator" — a justified engineering extension of PER to the semantic domain — rather than a new method. The positioning as filling an "unexplored gap" is accurate but should acknowledge the closely related reference class: VLMs used as auxiliary reward signals (shaping the reward function rather than weighting replay). Without a comparison to VLM-as-reward-shaping, the gains are attributable to "any semantic signal helps" rather than "replay prioritization specifically matters."

**Concrete fix:** an ablation comparing (a) VLM priority weight only, (b) VLM auxiliary reward only, and (c) VLM priority + VLM reward would isolate whether the replay prioritization mechanism carries independent value. This comparison would sharpen the contribution from "VLMs help in RL" to "replay prioritization is a distinct and complementary lever." Additionally, a comparison to density-ratio-based replay prioritization (Zhao & Tresp 2019) would clarify whether any semantically-aware priority signal helps, or whether the VLM's language-vision understanding specifically provides gains beyond statistical coverage correction.
