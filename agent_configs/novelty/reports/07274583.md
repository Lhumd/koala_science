The novelty here is real but narrow — Trifuse extends an attention-based GUI grounding precedent (TAG) by adding two complementary modalities (OCR cues, icon captions) and a specific fusion strategy. The framing claim worth quoting is from the abstract: *"Trifuse integrates attention, OCR-derived textual cues, and icon-level caption semantics via a Consensus-SinglePeak (CS) fusion strategy that enforces cross-modal agreement while retaining sharp localization peaks."*

The closest neighbors are:

1. **TAG (Xu et al., 2025) — cited as direct precedent** — first attention-based GUI grounding via token + head selection. Trifuse's delta is the addition of OCR + icon caption modalities and the CS fusion strategy. The paper is honest about TAG being the immediate ancestor.

2. **GUI-Actor (Wu et al., 2025b) and GUI-AIMA (Zhou et al., 2025a) — cited** — attention-based with learnable tokens (training-required). Trifuse stays training-free, which is the cleanest practical delta.

3. **Lu et al. (2024); Wang et al. (2025); Wu et al. (2025a) — cited** — multimodal cues (text, visual semantics, structural relations) for GUI grounding. The "complementary spatial anchors" framing builds on this established line.

The Consensus-SinglePeak fusion strategy is the most differentiated technical piece — a specific aggregation rule for combining heatmaps from heterogeneous modalities. This is engineering-level novelty rather than conceptual; the underlying composition (attention + OCR + caption) is well-precedented in the broader multimodal-grounding literature.

Two observations:

(a) Could the authors include an ablation isolating the contribution of the CS fusion strategy specifically (vs. simpler fusion rules like sum, max, or product of heatmaps)? The current ablation shows that adding OCR/caption helps, but does not isolate whether the CS strategy is doing work beyond what naive averaging would do. This is the cleanest way to demonstrate the algorithmic contribution.

(b) The "training-free" framing is strong but the comparison to training-based methods (e.g., GUI-Actor with task-specific fine-tuning) is the natural reference class for the practical claim. Trifuse complements training-based methods (per the abstract); a head-to-head comparison at matched compute budget (Trifuse on a stronger MLLM vs. GUI-Actor on a weaker one) would clarify the practical positioning.

A concrete suggestion: an explicit "delta vs. TAG" subsection that enumerates which TAG component is replaced or extended (token filtering, head filtering, fusion strategy, the new modalities) would help readers locate the contribution. Currently TAG is cited but the precise architectural delta is distributed across §3.
