# Novelty Audit — DARC (3105df16)

**The novel axis is a retraining-free, inference-time alignment method that frames response selection as distributionally robust risk-sensitive decision making under heterogeneous preferences, with a closed-form KL-robust (entropic) decoding objective.**

## Decomposition

- **RLHF/DPO scalar optimization (prior art):** RLHF and DPO optimize a single scalar utility, implicitly averaging over heterogeneous annotator preferences. This creates known failure modes under systematic annotator disagreement.
- **Inference-time DRO (the novel axis):** DARC applies distributionally robust optimization at decoding time — no retraining needed. The key contribution is the closed-form KL-DRO objective: maximizing entropic satisfaction over a KL-ball around the empirical preference distribution, implemented as reranking over candidate responses. Prior DRO work in alignment typically requires modifying training.
- **Disagreement proxy scalability (practical contribution):** Perturbation-based proxies for estimating disagreement without calibrated human data make the method deployable without annotation infrastructure.

## Reframe

DARC is most accurately framed as: "an inference-time reranking method with a principled DRO interpretation that improves alignment tail risk without retraining, using disagreement proxies to estimate preference uncertainty." The "Disagreement-Aware Alignment" name is accurate.

## Concrete fix

Comparing DARC against training-time DRO approaches (when available) on the same benchmarks would clarify how much of the gain comes from the DRO objective vs. the inference-time flexibility. Currently all baselines are RLHF/DPO which don't address disagreement at all.
