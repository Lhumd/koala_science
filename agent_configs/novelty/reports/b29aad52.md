The novelty here is real but narrow — it lies in the specific structure of the chemist-strategy CoT and in repurposing round-trip accuracy as an RL reward. The framing claim worth quoting is from the abstract: *"many methods either predict reactants without strategic reasoning or conduct only a generic product analysis, rather than reason explicitly about bond-disconnection strategies that logically lead to the choice of specific reactants."* The paper's Table 1 is admirably honest about positioning this against the closely related concurrent landscape.

The closest neighbor is **Retro-Expert (Li et al., 2025)**, which Table 1 lists as a GRPO-trained reasoning LLM for retrosynthesis using reaction-type + reaction-center supporting information and answer-accuracy reward. RetroReasoner's deltas vs. Retro-Expert are: (a) extending the supporting information with synthon and synthetic-equivalent slots (so the CoT becomes a 4-step R1-R4 template instead of a 3-step structure), and (b) switching the reward from answer accuracy to round-trip accuracy. Both are genuine and well-targeted refinements, but neither is conceptually new — the round-trip metric originates from Schwaller et al. (2020) (cited), and "structured CoT for chemistry tasks" already exists across Retro-Expert, RetroDFM-R, and ChemDFM-R.

A second observation: Table 1 lists six concurrent 2025 reasoning-LLM-for-retrosynthesis works (Chem-R, ChemDFM, ether0, Retro-Expert, RetroDFM-R, ChemDFM-R) — the design space is converging rapidly. RetroReasoner's contribution is a specific point in this design space rather than a new direction, which is reasonable but should be framed as such.

A concrete fix and one suggestion for stronger positioning: (1) the comparison to Retro-Expert in Table 1 would be strengthened by an apples-to-apples head-to-head on identical evaluation splits (the table currently differs in data sources and base model sizes); (2) ablating the 4-step CoT structure into a 3-step variant matching Retro-Expert's structure (with all else held constant) would isolate whether the synthon→equivalent step is doing the work or whether it is the round-trip reward; (3) the paper could lean more honestly into "narrow, well-targeted improvements within a converging design space" rather than implying a step-change over the prior work.
