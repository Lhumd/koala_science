## 4g. Positioning Issues

1. **Overclaim relative to evidence:** The abstract says "we present empirical evidence that data frugality is both practical and beneficial, demonstrating that coreset-based subset selection can substantially reduce training energy consumption." However, Figure 3 caption states "The numbers are taken from He et al. (2024) and Tan et al. (2025)." The paper does not conduct new efficiency experiments — it aggregates others' results and presents them as its own empirical evidence. **Anchor:** "we present empirical evidence" (abstract) vs. "numbers are taken from He et al. (2024) and Tan et al. (2025)" (Figure 3 caption).

2. **Wrong reference class:** The paper frames its contribution as novel vs. the "growing body" of position papers while simultaneously acknowledging 7 concurrent papers making essentially the same argument. The genuine novel contribution (ImageNet-1K energy quantification + preach-practice gap survey) is narrower than the "data frugality manifesto" framing.

3. **Missing natural baseline:** The paper claims only 1/10 coreset papers measures energy savings, but doesn't compare its energy quantification methodology to existing dataset carbon accounting tools (e.g., Data Cards, Dataset Nutrition Labels) or the Common Crawl/Tailpipe methodology cited in §2.1.

4. **Inflated framing words:** "The ML community must move from preaching to practising data frugality for responsible development of AI." The normative claim is sweeping given that the empirical evidence section aggregates two existing papers and the position itself is shared by 7+ concurrent papers.

5. **Buried real contribution:** The most original piece is the preach-practice gap documentation (§Introduction: only 1/10 coreset papers reports energy savings) and the ImageNet-1K cumulative energy estimate (§3: 5.46 GWh / 2429 tCO2e). These are real contributions buried under the position-paper framing.

The paper's genuine novelty lies on two narrow axes:

The contribution exists but is narrower than the "manifesto for the community" framing implies.
