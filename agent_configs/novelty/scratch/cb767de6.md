## 4b. Closest-prior-work identification

- **Neighbor 1:** Mohapatra et al. (2023) [missing data in synthetic data generation] — "Most direct predecessor; they study MCAR missing data in synthetic data generation and interpret amplification as Bernoulli subsampling of records. This paper extends to general MCAR + MAR and any randomized DP algorithm, not just a specific synthetic data pipeline."
- **Neighbor 2:** Balle et al. (2018) [Privacy amplification by subsampling, NeurIPS] — "The coupling + α-divergence proof machinery is directly adapted; the technical core of Theorems 3.2 and 4.5 parallels Balle et al.'s subsampling analyses. The delta: subsampling selects a subset of records; missing data selects a subset of features per record, requiring new per-sample MAR coupling arguments."
- **Neighbor 3:** Das et al. (2022) [Imputation under DP] — "Das et al. show DP algorithms applied to imputed data yield privacy *degradation*; this paper identifies the opposite regime (no imputation, just masking) and formalizes amplification. The research question is symmetric but the technical path is distinct."

## 4c. Pseudo-novelty pattern check

- **Rebrand?** No — "missing data as subsampling" is not the full story. MAR amplification requires new coupling arguments that do not reduce to Balle 2018.
- **Domain transfer?** Partially — coupling/α-divergence tools from Balle 2018 are adapted. The new domain (feature-level masking vs. record-level subsampling) breaks the direct transfer, so the paper genuinely adapts them.
- **Composition without insight?** No — the composition of missing data mechanism D with DP algorithm A is analyzed rigorously; the mixture decomposition of AD is non-trivial.
- **Resolution bump?** No — FWL analysis introducing ρ dependence is a new conceptual result.

## 4d. Novel framing vs. novel science

The "missing data as amplification" framing enables new algorithmic design directions (explicitly using missingness to improve privacy budget) that the "missing data as impairment" framing did not. This framing does scientific work: it generates new predictions about which mechanisms amplify and by how much.

## 4f.2 Actual result and override

- Actual headline result: "we show, for the first time, that incomplete data can yield privacy amplification for differentially private algorithms" — specifically, MCAR and MAR mechanisms amplify privacy, with amplification factor controlled by p* (general) and ρ (FWL queries)
- Match assessment: partially matches prediction — MCAR direction matches; MAR amplification (especially for FWL, controlled by ρ) is partially surprising (expert would not predict clean MAR amplification without extra structure)
- Override clause that fires: **no override** — result was not clearly *opposite* of prediction; it confirms MCAR and extends to MAR with extra assumptions, which is "more than predicted" but not counterintuitive
- Justification: the expert would have predicted MCAR amplification; MAR amplification under assumptions 4.2/4.3 is mildly surprising but not opposite

## 4g. Positioning issues

- **"First time" overclaim (mild):** Abstract says "We show, for the first time, that incomplete data can yield privacy amplification." The Mohapatra et al. (2023) paper already showed MCAR amplification in a specific synthetic data generation setting. The paper acknowledges this in §5 but the abstract does not. The claim is accurate for the *general* MCAR+MAR framework, but not for MCAR per se.
- **Overclaim relative to evidence:** The paper frames MNAR as future work, so the "missing data" headline is implicitly restricted to MCAR+MAR. This limitation is mentioned in §6 (Limitations) but not in the abstract.
- **Missing natural baseline:** The paper does not compare amplification factors numerically to Balle et al.'s subsampling amplification for similar "fraction of information hidden." A side-by-side would clarify when missing data amplification is competitive with subsampling.
- **Buried real contribution:** The FWL analysis (§4, Corollary 4.6/4.8) connecting amplification to ρ is arguably the most practically useful result, but the abstract emphasizes the general framework (Contribution 2) over the concrete FWL bounds (Contribution 3).

## 4h. Originality score rationale

The strongest novel axis is the **general MAR amplification framework**: unlike Mohapatra 2023 (MCAR-as-Bernoulli-subsampling in a specific setting), this paper provides a systematic formal model of AD as a composition of missing data mechanism and DP algorithm, with amplification proofs under MAR via new coupling arguments. The FWL results (ρ-dependent amplification for Laplace/Gaussian) are practically valuable. The MCAR contribution is incremental relative to Mohapatra 2023. The technical tools borrow heavily from Balle 2018 but are legitimately adapted. The "first time" framing in the abstract slightly overclaims for MCAR, but the MAR framework is genuinely novel.
