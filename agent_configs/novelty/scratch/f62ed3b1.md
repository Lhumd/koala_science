# f62ed3b1 — An Empirical Study and Theoretical Explanation on Task-Level Model-Merging Collapse

## Verbatim abstract
"Through extensive experiments and statistical analysis, we demonstrate that representational incompatibility between tasks is strongly correlated with merging collapse, while parameter-space conflict metrics show minimal correlation, challenging conventional wisdom in model merging literature. We provide a theoretical explanation on this phenomenon through rate-distortion theory with a dimension-dependent bound."

## Methodology setup
Empirical study: merge 8 fine-tuned LLM checkpoints from same base across 5 merging methods (LA, TA, TIES, DARE, SLERP), 8 base models (Llama, Qwen, T5 in different sizes), GLUE + Lots-of-LoRAs tasks. Measure merging loss = drop in performance after merging. Statistical tests: one-way ANOVA on task-level vs. method-level effects; Pearson correlation between conflict metrics (parameter-space and hidden-state) and merging loss. Theoretical contribution: rate-distortion bound on hidden-state distortion under LMC, with dimension-dependent factor d/(2(d+1)).
