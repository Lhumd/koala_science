## 1. Selective read

**Abstract.** "PSN-RLVR ... perturbs policy parameters before rollout generation to induce temporally consistent, trajectory-level exploration ... we incorporate truncated importance sampling (TIS) ... a computationally efficient real-time adaptive noise scheduler ... Instantiated on GRPO, PSN-GRPO consistently expands the effective reasoning capability boundary across multiple mathematical reasoning benchmarks ... outperforming prior exploration-oriented RLVR methods (e.g., Pass-at-k-style training) while remaining orthogonal."

**Setup core.** GRPO-style RLVR with parameter-space noise on rollout policy π_θ̃; clean θ updated via off-policy gradient with TIS correction; lightweight scheduler tunes noise σ_t from semantic-diversity + normalized-self-certainty surrogates.

## 4b. Closest prior

1. **Plappert et al. 2018 (PSN paper, cited).** Closest neighbor: parameter-space noise for RL exploration. Delta: paper ports PSN to discrete-action LLM RLVR with TIS off-policy correction + diversity-based σ scheduler. Method axis is direct domain transfer.
2. **Fortunato et al. 2018 (NoisyNet, cited).** Closely related: learned parameter noise via factorized σ. Delta: PSN-RLVR uses fixed-σ Gaussian noise (not learned), tunes σ via separate scheduler. Less expressive than NoisyNet but cheaper.
3. **Huang et al. 2025 (QERL, concurrent).** Closest concurrent: incidentally observes quantization-noise → improved exploration in RLVR. Paper distinguishes: QERL "did not systematically analyze the noise dynamics, optimal injection strategies, or scaling laws required to leverage this effect for reasoning." This is the strongest claim the paper makes for its novelty over a concurrent direct competitor.
4. **Chen et al. 2025 (Pass@k training, cited; benchmarked).** Different exploration mechanism (objective-level pass@k optimization vs. parameter noise). Composable; PSN stacks on top.
5. **Zhu et al. 2025 (RLVR-Decomposed, cited; benchmarked).** Negative-sample-based exploration; orthogonal mechanism.

## 4c. Pseudo-novelty patterns

- Rebrand? **No** — PSN-RLVR is an honest descriptor.
- Domain transfer? **Yes, partial.** Plappert PSN → LLM RLVR is the central transfer. The new domain (discrete tokens, autoregressive multi-step, off-policy update) has properties that *do* break naive PSN (sampling-update mismatch), and the paper *does* address them via TIS + σ scheduler. Substantive transfer, not pure mechanical port.
- Composition without insight? **Partial** — PSN + TIS + adaptive σ is a 3-component composition. The σ scheduler driven by diversity + self-certainty is a small original element. Not pure additive engineering, not strongly insightful.
- Resolution bump? No.

## 4d. Novel framing vs novel science

The paper claims to be the "first systematic study of parameter-space noise for LLMs trained with Verifiable Rewards." This framing is honest if QERL's exploration finding is acknowledged as the closest concurrent, which the paper does. The systematic-study framing predicts the design-space ablations (where to inject noise, scaling with magnitude, action-space vs parameter-space) reported in §4.2 — the framing does scientific work.

## 4h. Originality rationale

Real but narrow novelty — single-axis (domain-transfer to RLVR with two non-trivial sub-modules) extension of an established method.
