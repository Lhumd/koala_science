# d3a0166f — C-kNN-LSH: Nearest-Neighbor Algorithm for Sequential Counterfactual Inference

Verbatim abstract: "Estimating causal effects from longitudinal trajectories is central to understanding the progression of complex conditions and optimizing clinical decision-making, such as comorbidities and long COVID recovery. We introduce C-kNN–LSH, a nearest-neighbor framework for sequential causal inference designed to handle such high-dimensional, confounded situations. By utilizing locality-sensitive hashing, we efficiently identify 'clinical twins' with similar covariate histories, enabling local estimation of conditional treatment effects across evolving disease states. To mitigate bias from irregular sampling and shifting patient recovery profiles, we integrate neighborhood estimator with a doubly-robust correction. Theoretical analysis guarantees our estimator is consistent and second-order robust to nuisance error. Evaluated on a real-world Long COVID cohort with 13,511 participants, C-kNN-LSH demonstrates superior performance in capturing recovery heterogeneity and estimating policy values compared to existing baselines."

### 4g. Positioning issues

- **Overclaim relative to evidence:** **Yes.** Abstract claims "C-kNN-LSH demonstrates superior performance in capturing recovery heterogeneity and estimating policy values compared to existing baselines." Without a ground-truth counterfactual (which is fundamentally unavailable on observational Long COVID data), "superior performance" is asserted via different-from-baselines curves — but different ≠ better. The §5.6 discussion says only "LMN yields a distinct counterfactual trajectory" — the abstract's "superior" is stronger than the body justifies.
- **Wrong reference class:** Mild. The baselines are OR/IPW/Local AIPW — global statistical estimators. The paper does not compare to recent neural CF estimators (Counterfactual Recurrent Network — Bica et al.; G-Net — Lim 2018; TARNet — Shalit et al. 2017; Causal Transformer — Melnychuk et al. 2022; SCIGAN; Time Series Deconfounder). Those are the natural reference class for "high-dimensional sequential CF" and would be expected baselines in a 2026 ICML submission.
- **Missing the natural baseline:** **Yes.** No ablation: (a) VAE-without-LSH vs. raw-features-with-LSH would isolate the role of VAE compression; (b) kNN without DR vs. kNN with DR would isolate the DR contribution; (c) any of CRN, G-Net, Causal Transformer would establish whether the gain is real or just methodological re-arrangement.
- **Inflated framing words:** "novel framework" (in §6 Conclusion). Mild — not "first to" or "paradigm shift."
