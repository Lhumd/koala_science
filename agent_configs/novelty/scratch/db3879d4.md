# db3879d4 — Self-Supervised Flow Matching for Scalable Multi-Modal Synthesis (Self-Flow)

## Verbatim abstract
"Strong semantic representations improve the convergence and generation quality of diffusion and flow models. Existing approaches largely rely on external models, which require separate training, operate on misaligned objectives, and exhibit unexpected scaling behavior. We argue that this dependence arises from the model's training objective, which poses a denoising task with little incentive to learn semantic representations. We introduce Self-Flow: a self-supervised flow matching paradigm that integrates representation learning within the generative framework. Our key mechanism, Dual-Timestep Scheduling, applies heterogeneous noise levels across tokens, creating an information asymmetry that forces the model to infer missing information from corrupted inputs."

## Methodology setup
Two parallel forward passes per sample: (1) heterogeneously-noised input (two noise levels across token subsets), (2) cleaner uniformly-noised input. Loss: standard flow loss on (1) PLUS a self-supervised term that predicts the (2)-pass representations from the (1)-pass intermediate features. The information asymmetry between the two noise levels gives the self-supervised signal — the model has to "infer missing information from corrupted inputs."

## 4b. Closest-prior-work identification

- **Neighbor 1: REPA (Yu et al., 2024)** — cited as the predominant external-alignment baseline. Aligns flow model intermediate features with frozen DINO features. Delta: Self-Flow removes the external encoder; uses information asymmetry across noise levels to drive representation learning internally. The central technical move (replace external alignment with internal self-supervised mechanism) is the cleanest delta.
- **Neighbor 2: Jiang et al. 2025; Haghighi et al. 2025 — cited** — opt to rely on the model's natively learned features and the semantic asymmetry between different layers (no external alignment, but no new SS objective). Delta: Self-Flow goes beyond layer-asymmetry by introducing token-level noise asymmetry as the self-supervision source.
- **Neighbor 3: MAE (He et al., 2022)** — masked autoencoding for self-supervised representation. Conceptually parallel: mask part of input → predict masked part. Self-Flow's noise-level heterogeneity is a continuous analogue of binary masking. Cited.
- **Neighbor 4: BYOL (Grill et al., 2020) and SimSiam** — predict target representations of one view from another view, without negatives. Self-Flow's "predict cleaner-input representations from mixed-input" is precisely this pattern with a flow-matching backbone. Cited.
- **Neighbor 5: RCG (Li et al., 2023) — "Self-conditioned Image Generation via Generating Representations"** — generates and conditions on self-extracted representations. Different mechanism (chained generation) but same goal (avoid external alignment). Possibly cited; relevant precedent.
