### 4c. Pseudo-Novelty Pattern Check

- **Rebrand:** No. The "Forbidden Zone" concept is not renaming an existing named phenomenon. The concept is novel: prior DMD literature does not use this framing or give a name to the specific failure mode. The counter-test passes: the new framing enables new predictions (e.g., any DMD variant should be evaluable as to whether it handles Forbidden Zones explicitly or implicitly), which the old vocabulary (just "instability" or "mode collapse") did not.
- **Domain transfer:** No. This is not a known method applied to a new domain; AMD operates in the same diffusion distillation domain as DMD.
- **Composition without insight:** Partially applicable. The three mechanisms (detection + RLS + prioritization) each build on existing components (reward models, fake teacher, loss weighting), but the composition is non-trivial: the unification insight explains *why* the combination works in a way that pure additive engineering would not predict. The RLS mechanism (training fake teacher on corrupted data to sharpen repulsion) is a non-obvious architectural choice motivated by the Forbidden Zone concept.
- **Resolution bump:** No. The contribution is not "we ran DMD at larger scale"; the Forbidden Zone framing is a conceptual contribution, not a scale-up.

### 4d. Novel Framing vs. Novel Science

The "Forbidden Zone" framing does scientific work: it enables the unification claim (prior DMD variants as implicit strategies) and motivates specific algorithmic choices (why sharpen the repulsive landscape specifically in corrupted regions). The framing makes new predictions: any DMD variant can be analyzed by whether it handles Forbidden Zones, and new fixes can be designed as explicit Forbidden Zone escape mechanisms rather than ad-hoc patches. This is framing that enables new experiments — verdict: the framing is doing scientific work, not mere vocabulary.

### 4g. Positioning Issues

- **Overclaim relative to evidence:** Moderate risk. The unification claim ("prior DMD variants are all implicit Forbidden Zone strategies") is stated broadly but is only empirically validated for specific cited cases (DMD2, Shao, Jiang). Whether *all* DMD extensions can be subsumed is not demonstrated. The abstract phrase "unified optimization framework" may overclaim the scope of the unification. Verbatim abstract: "we develop a unified optimization framework that reinterprets the past work." Experimental scope: three specific prior methods reinterpreted.
- **Wrong reference class:** Low risk. AMD correctly situates itself within DMD-style distribution-matching distillation, not as a replacement for all diffusion distillation approaches.
- **Missing the natural baseline:** Low risk for the main claim; the paper compares against DMD and DMD2 directly. However, a comparison specifically measuring Forbidden Zone entry frequency (before vs. after AMD) is absent — this would empirically ground the concept rather than rely on performance gains as indirect evidence.
- **Inflated framing words:** "unified optimization framework that reinterprets the past work" — the word "unified" is moderately strong but not egregiously overclaimed given the three specific prior works discussed.
- **Buried real contribution:** The strongest technical contribution is likely the Repulsive Landscape Sharpening (RLS) mechanism — training the fake teacher on corrupted data specifically to improve gradient quality in Forbidden Zones. This may be more novel than the abstract's "detection" framing suggests. Worth checking §3–§4.

### 4h. Originality Score Rationale

The strongest novelty in AMD is the Forbidden Zone conceptual framework combined with the Repulsive Landscape Sharpening mechanism. On the phenomenon axis: naming and formalizing a specific failure mode (Forbidden Zones = real-teacher unreliable + fake-teacher repulsion insufficient) in DMD is a genuine contribution — prior work knew about instability but did not geometrically characterize it. On the method axis: the three explicit mechanisms (detection, RLS, prioritization) are non-trivially composed and motivated by the concept, with RLS being the most architecturally novel. The closest prior (DMD2, Yin et al. 2024) addresses the same instability through a different fix, but AMD's contribution is providing the principled explanation for why DMD2's fix works, plus two additional mechanisms. The unification framing — reinterpreting prior DMD variants as implicit Forbidden Zone strategies — is the highest-value novelty claim and appears defensible for the three cited cases. The expert-prediction override does not fire (no directional reversal). Score 6: genuine novelty on the conceptual-framework and one mechanism axis; derivative on the base distillation architecture; framing scope is moderately ambitious but appears supported.
