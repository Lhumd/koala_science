## 4b. Closest-Prior-Work Identification

- **Neighbor 1: Huang et al. 2024 (optimization-based watermarking)** — "ALIEN replaces Huang et al.'s iterative optimization over the initial latent with a closed-form analytic derivation; the optimization search that Huang requires is collapsed into a single matrix computation, removing local-optima risk and the requirement for a reversible sampler."

- **Neighbor 2: Zhang et al. 2024 (optimization-based, Tree-Ring or similar)** — "Zhang et al. 2024 also uses iterative optimization in latent space; ALIEN's analytic solution achieves the same embedding goal without iterative search, enabling inference-time deployment on any sampler."

- **Neighbor 3: Yang et al. 2024c / Gunn et al. 2024 (Gaussian-constrained subspace sampling)** — "Yang et al. and Gunn et al. constrain sampling to identifiable subspaces, limiting generative diversity; ALIEN does not partition the latent space and therefore preserves full generative coverage."

- **Neighbor 4: Wen et al. 2024 / Ci et al. 2025 (initial latent modification)** — "Wen et al. and Ci et al. directly modify the initial latent without accounting for the forward diffusion dynamics; ALIEN's modulation coefficient explicitly compensates for how the forward SDE/ODE attenuates the watermark signal over time, which these methods ignore."

- **Rebrand:** No. The contribution cannot be described purely in prior vocabulary. The analytic derivation of a time-dependent modulation coefficient is a technical object absent from prior work; prior work either optimizes iteratively (fundamentally different computational path) or constrains sampling (fundamentally different strategy).

- **Domain transfer:** No. This is not a known technique moved to watermarking; the derivation is specific to the structure of the diffusion forward SDE/ODE and does not exist in another domain to transfer from.

- **Composition without insight:** Partially applicable. The overall pipeline combines known ideas (watermark in initial latent + diffusion sampling). However, the key component — the analytic derivation of the modulation coefficient — is not a straightforward composition; it requires working through the stochastic dynamics of the forward process, which produces a non-obvious closed-form result. The composition is not additive engineering.

- **Resolution bump:** No. The headline contribution is a new theoretical treatment (closed-form derivation), not a replication of prior results at higher scale or more conditions.

## 4d. Novel Framing vs. Novel Science

The paper introduces "analytic latent watermarking" as a framing. This framing does scientific work: it enables new predictions (the modulation coefficient can be derived for any forward diffusion schedule, not just those where iterative optimization converges) and enables new experiments (testing on irreversible samplers, which prior work cannot address). The framing is not merely vocabulary — it opens the design space for sampler-agnostic watermarking.
