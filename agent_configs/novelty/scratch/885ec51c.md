# 885ec51c — CAFE: Channel-Autoregressive Factorized Encoding for Biosignal Spatial SR

Verbatim abstract: "[...] we propose CAFE, a plug-and-play rollout generation scheme that reconstructs the full montage in geometry-aligned stages. Starting from the LD channels, CAFE first recovers nearby channels and then progressively expands to more distal regions, exploiting reliable local structure before introducing non-local interactions. During training, step-wise supervision is applied over channel groups and teacher forcing with epoch-level scheduled sampling along the group dimension is utilized to reduce exposure bias, enabling parallel computation across steps. At test time, CAFE performs an autoregressive rollout across groups, while remaining plug-and-play by reusing any temporal backbone as the shared predictor."

### 4b. Closest-prior-work identification

- **Neighbor 1: ESTformer (Li, Zeng, Wang, Yang 2025, KBS) — cited and used as primary baseline.** Transformer-based EEG super-resolution with global spatiotemporal attention. Delta: ESTformer is the global-coupling baseline CAFE argues against; CAFE is "ESTformer + proximity-ordered AR rollout" as a plug-and-play wrapper. The methodological wrapper (group AR with teacher forcing + scheduled sampling) is the contribution.
- **Neighbor 2: SRGDiff (Liu, Zhou, Yang, Yao, ICLR 2026) — cited and used as primary baseline.** Diffusion-based EEG SR. Delta: SRGDiff is the diffusion baseline; CAFE replaces noise-driven iterative refinement with structured channel-group AR. Different mechanism, same task.
- **Neighbor 3: GRIN / Cini, Marisca, Alippi (ICLR 2022) — cited.** Graph-neural-network multivariate time-series imputation. GRIN already uses graph structure (which encodes a kind of locality) for imputation. Delta: GRIN is graph-based and parallel imputation; CAFE is sequential along a 1D distance-ordered chain with explicit teacher forcing — the AR-structured-decoding piece is what CAFE adds vs. GRIN's graph-MP imputation.
- **Neighbor 4: van den Oord et al. 2016 PixelCNN / Chang et al. 2022 MaskGIT — NOT cited.** This is the methodological genus: structured-mask autoregressive decoding with parallelizable training via teacher forcing. CAFE's "next-group prediction with mask Mg, scheduled sampling along group dim" is essentially MaskGIT's mask-then-predict recipe with a domain-specific group ordering. **Missing baseline / missing reference class** — see 4g.
- **Neighbor 5: TimeMixer++ (Wang et al. 2025a) — cited.** Time-series backbone used inside the predictor. Not a direct competitor — sibling component.

### 4c. Pseudo-novelty pattern check

- **Domain transfer:** **Yes — substantial.** The masked-AR generation framework (PixelCNN/MaskGIT-style) is being transferred from images to multichannel biosignals. The new domain has properties — heterogeneous channel topology, sparse LD observations, noise — and the paper does adapt to them via the proximity-distance-based ordering. So the transfer is non-mechanical, but the methodological core (masked-AR decoding with parallel teacher-forced training and scheduled sampling) is directly imported.

Each component is established; the combination is non-obvious but each piece's contribution to the headline gain is not fully isolated (the ablation tests G but not "CAFE schedule with random ordering vs. proximity ordering").

### 4g. Positioning issues

- **Overclaim relative to evidence:** Mild. The "plug-and-play" claim is well-supported (3 backbones tested). The "consistently better" claim is supported by Fig. 2 across 6 datasets. The unspoken claim — "this is a novel decoding paradigm" — is the inflated piece, since masked-AR group decoding is the established paradigm being applied here.
- **Wrong reference class:** **Yes.** The paper benchmarks only against biosignal SR methods (ESTformer, SRGDiff, CGAN) and one general MTS imputation method (GRIN). The actual contribution — using masked autoregressive generation with structured ordering — sits inside the broader image-AR / masked-AR-generation literature. PixelCNN-style models, MaskGIT, MAR, MAGIC, and structured-mask diffusion all share the methodological core. The narrower "biosignal SR" reference class flatters the novelty.
- **Missing the natural baseline:** **Yes.** Two natural baselines are missing: (a) MaskGIT-style decoding adapted to channel masks — would isolate "AR group decoding" from "proximity ordering"; (b) random-ordered group decoding — would isolate "proximity ordering" from "group decoding." The G-ablation (depth) is present but the ordering ablation is absent.
- **Inflated framing words:** "We recast spatial super-resolution as group-wise conditional generation" (contributions) — "recast" is mild but supportable. No "first to" or "paradigm shift."
- **Buried real contribution:** No major buried contribution. The combination (proximity ordering + group AR + scheduled sampling) is sold straight.

### 4h. Originality score rationale

Bucket score: real but narrow novelty. The methodological core (masked-AR group decoding with teacher forcing and scheduled sampling) is established image-generation literature; the genuinely new piece is the *proximity-stratified channel ordering tailored to biosignal sensor topology* — one axis (setting/scheduling), three axes (method, training objective, generation paradigm) derivative. The reference class is too narrow (compares only to biosignal SR) and the natural ablation (random-ordered vs. proximity-ordered) is absent.

No surprising buried contribution.
