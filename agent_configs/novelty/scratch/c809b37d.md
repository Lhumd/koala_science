## 1. Selective read

**Abstract (verbatim).** "Generating executable CAD programs from images requires alignment between visual geometry and symbolic program representations … We introduce Geometric Inference Feedback Tuning (GIFT), a data augmentation framework that leverages geometric feedback to turn test-time compute into a bootstrapped set of high-quality training samples. GIFT combines two mechanisms: Soft-Rejection Sampling (GIFT-REJECT), which retains diverse high-fidelity programs beyond exact ground-truth matches, and Failure-Driven Augmentation (GIFT-FAIL), which converts near-miss predictions into synthetic training examples … reducing inference compute by 80%. It improves mean IoU by 12% over a strong supervised baseline."

**§3 Method setup (verbatim core).** Given image c and ground-truth CadQuery program z_gt, model p_θ(z|c). For each image, sample K candidates; compute geometric IoU f(z) = IoU(Execute(d(z)), Execute(d(z_gt))); two indicators w_srs (τ_valid ≤ f < τ_match → high-fidelity diverse) and w_fda (τ_low ≤ f < τ_valid → near-miss); SRS reuses the original image c paired with each diverse valid z; FDA renders failed z back to image c̃ = φ(d(z)) and pairs c̃ with z_gt.

### 4f.1 Pre-result prediction (BEFORE seeing results)

- Expert profile: A researcher who has worked on (a) self-training / rejection sampling / STaR (Zelikman 2022) for code/reasoning and (b) program-by-execution-feedback (e.g., AlphaCode, code-RL, execution-guided synthesis), and ideally has read CAD-Coder (Doris 2025) or similar image-to-CAD baselines.
- Predicted headline result: Augmenting an SFT image-to-CAD trainset with (i) high-IoU non-exact-string-match candidates re-paired with their original image (output diversity) and (ii) low-IoU candidates rendered back into images and paired with the GT code (input robustness), each obtained via a CAD geometric verifier, will produce modest-to-moderate IoU gains over plain SFT (5–15%) and substantially reduce inference-time-search compute when scaled. The two mechanisms compose roughly additively.
- Predicted directionality: Matches paper's likely framing (12% IoU gain, 80% compute reduction).
- Confidence in prediction: high. STaR / RFT-style "use verified successful samples" + rendering-based hard-negative augmentation are direct, well-known patterns. Application to image-to-CAD is the new wrinkle, but the recipe is predictable to the expert.

## 1 (continued). Introduction + Related work + Conclusion + Buried sweep

**Contributions list (verbatim §1).** "(i) We introduce GIFT, a scalable data augmentation framework for Image-to-CAD program synthesis that uses offline geometric feedback to convert inference-time samples into augmented supervised training data. (ii) We propose a dual augmentation strategy: Soft-Rejection Sampling (GIFT-REJECT) … and Failure-Driven Augmentation (GIFT-FAIL) … (iii) We show that this augmentation pipeline expands the effective training set substantially, improves single-shot accuracy over a strong supervised baseline, and reduces the reliance on expensive test-time sampling. (iv) We demonstrate through extensive analysis that GIFT improves robustness on complex geometries, narrows the amortization gap between pass@1 and pass@k, and remains competitive with more complex multimodal systems without requiring additional human annotation or specialized architectures."

**Related work (key cited neighbors).**

- **Zelikman et al. 2022 (STaR)** — explicitly cited as the closest "self-training/weak-supervision" neighbor; GIFT explicitly contrasts: "GIFT differs in how it obtains the signal. Instead of relying on heuristic confidence scores or learned rewards, it uses a deterministic geometric kernel to identify novel, valid alternative programs."
- **Dong et al. 2023 (Self-instruct/RFT-style)** — also cited in same neighbor cluster.
- **Schulman et al. 2017 / Guo et al. 2025 / Li 2025c / Kolodiazhnyi 2025** — RL alternatives; GIFT positions as offline alternative to online RL with geometric kernels.
- **Brown 2024 / Snell 2024** — inference-time scaling references; GIFT amortizes ITS into weights.

**Conclusion / headline (verbatim).** Mean IoU +12% over CAD-Coder-SFT baseline; 80% inference compute reduction at matched performance; competitive with more complex multimodal systems.

### 4f.2 Actual result and override
- Actual headline result: "+12% mean IoU over a strong supervised baseline" and "reduces inference compute by 80%" via amortizing ITS into weights, with separable contributions from SRS (output diversity, +31.4% problems-solved) and FDA (input robustness, +53.8% problems-solved per Fig. 2d).
- Match assessment: matches prediction
- Override clause that fires: no override
- Justification: result direction predictable for an expert who knows STaR/RFT + execution-feedback program synthesis. Magnitude is solid but not qualitatively surprising.

**Buried-contribution sweep (§3–§4).**
- §3.1 introduces a *dual* augmentation operating on disjoint IoU intervals (τ_low ≤ f < τ_valid for FDA; τ_valid ≤ f < τ_match for SRS). The disjoint-intervals design with explicit thresholds chosen from the IoU CDF (Fig. 9b) is a small but operationally clean detail.
- The clearest *original* element is **FDA's render-back step** (φ(d(z)) — rendering the failed program back into a synthetic image and pairing it with the original GT code, creating a "geometric denoising" objective). This is more than standard rejection sampling — it's an inverse-rendering hard-negative augmentation specific to executable-code → geometry domains. This is the load-bearing novelty.
- SRS alone is essentially soft-rejection sampling / multiple-correct-targets — a known idea (e.g., RFT on code, "all valid solutions" datasets in Lean/IMO work).

**Experiments skim.** GenCAD-Code (163k pairs) train, 8k test, 400 OOD. CAD-Coder-SFT 7B baseline. Table 1 shows clear pass@k vs. pass@1 gap that GIFT amortizes.

## 4b. Closest prior work (3–5 nearest neighbors with deltas)

1. **Zelikman et al. 2022 (STaR; cited).** Closest prior on the *self-training* axis. STaR samples reasoning chains, retains those leading to correct answers, fine-tunes on them. Delta: GIFT replaces "correct answer" with "high-IoU geometric match" (deterministic kernel vs. discrete answer match) and adds the FDA render-back hard-negative branch. The SRS half is essentially STaR with a soft (interval) match criterion; the FDA half is conceptually beyond STaR.
2. **Dong et al. 2023 (Self-instruct / RFT-style; cited).** Closest neighbor on rejection-sampling fine-tuning for code. Delta: same as STaR — geometric verifier vs. unit-test/answer-match. Not a new principle; new instantiation.
3. **Doris et al. 2025 (CAD-Coder; cited; baseline).** Closest neighbor on the *image-to-CAD program synthesis* axis. Delta: GIFT does not change architecture or base model; it adds an offline data-augmentation loop on top of CAD-Coder-SFT.
4. **Wang et al. 2025b / Chen et al. 2025 ("RL post-training for image-to-CAD"; cited).** Closest neighbor on *learning from execution feedback for image-to-CAD specifically*. Delta: GIFT is offline data augmentation, avoiding the GPU↔CPU communication overhead of online CAD-RL while preserving most of the benefit. Neighbor is in same domain with a related goal but different mechanism (online RL).
5. **Schulman et al. 2017 (PPO; cited as RL family).** Used as a generic reference; not a tight neighbor.

Note: I could not retrieve specific prior on *render-back / inverse-rendering hard-negative augmentation* for code-to-shape models. There is a clear analog in image-text contrastive ("hard negatives via re-rendering") and in vision-language reasoning ("synthetic image augmentation"), but I do not have a named prior I can defend with confidence — frame as observation.

## 4c. Pseudo-novelty pattern check

- Rebrand? **Partial.** "Soft-Rejection Sampling" is essentially "rejection sampling fine-tuning with a soft (interval) acceptance threshold via a deterministic verifier" — well-known from RFT literature. The new name does not enable new predictions the old framing doesn't already make.
- Domain transfer? **Yes, partial.** The SRS half is a transfer of STaR/RFT to image-to-CAD. The new domain has properties (executable program → geometric verifier returning a continuous score; programs are equivalence-class-rich beyond string-match) that the paper *does* address (the soft-interval threshold motivated by geometric-equivalence; the τ_valid = 0.9 / τ_match = 0.99 design from the IoU CDF). So the transfer is substantive on the SRS side.
- Composition without insight? **Mixed.** SRS + FDA composition is presented as additive and Fig. 2d shows the gains stack. The non-trivial element is that FDA targets an explicitly *disjoint* IoU interval — i.e., the two losses train on disjoint slices of failure space and are designed to be non-redundant. This is mild insight beyond pure additive engineering.
- Resolution bump? No.

## 4d. Novel framing vs novel science

The "amortizing inference-time search into weights" framing is mostly retrospective branding — STaR and RFT already amortize search into weights. The GIFT-FAIL render-back primitive is the framing that does scientific work: it predicts and enables a loss term (geometric denoising on synthetic images) that pure rejection sampling does not.

## 4e. Concurrent work calibration

Wang 2025b, Chen 2025, Li 2025c, Kolodiazhnyi 2025 are concurrent (~3 months window) RL-for-CAD references. Field is converging on "use geometric feedback to improve image-to-CAD post-SFT." Calibrate: the broad direction is in the air, but FDA's render-back primitive is a distinguishing element.

## 4g. Positioning issues

- Overclaim relative to evidence? **Mild.** "Reduces inference compute by 80%" is true at matched performance, but the headline could read as "anywhere/anytime"; it is benchmark-specific (GenCAD-Code/DeepCAD).
- Wrong reference class? **Mild.** Paper compares to "complex multimodal systems" without naming them in the abstract; in §4 the comparators are mostly CAD-Coder-SFT and ablations of GIFT itself. The "competitive with more complex multimodal systems" claim deserves explicit naming.
- Missing the natural baseline? **Yes.** The paper does not show a head-to-head against an *online RL with geometric reward* baseline (Wang 2025b / Chen 2025 / Li 2025c — concurrent), which is the closest direct competitor. Without that, "RL alternative without instability" is asserted but not compared.
- Inflated framing? Mild — "amortizes inference-time search into the model parameters" is standard self-training rhetoric; not egregious.
- Buried real contribution? **Yes.** The FDA render-back primitive (§3.1, Fig. 7) is the most novel piece but the abstract foregrounds the "dual mechanism" framing equally. Suggesting the authors lead with FDA's inverse-rendering objective would sharpen the contribution.

## 4h. Originality score rationale

Strongest novelty axis: the FDA *render-back* / inverse-rendering hard-negative augmentation as a geometric-denoising training objective for executable-program synthesis. The SRS half is well-precedented (STaR/RFT-style with soft thresholds); the application to image-to-CAD is a domain transfer that the paper does address (continuous-IoU thresholding). Net: real but narrow novelty — one axis (FDA's inverse-rendering primitive) genuinely new in this domain combination, the other axes (SRS = STaR variant, the framework framing) derivative.
