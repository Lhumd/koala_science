### 4b. Closest Prior Work Identification

**Neighbor 1:** Geva et al. (2021), "Transformer Feed-Forward Layers Are Key-Value Memories" — *The core FFN-as-memory analogy is directly taken from Geva et al.; UAOR applies this observation-as-retrieval-query mechanism specifically to VLA observation reinjection, whereas Geva studied factual knowledge retrieval in LLMs.*

**Neighbor 2:** TraceVLA (Zheng et al., 2024) — *TraceVLA also augments VLAs with additional observation cues (visual traces) but requires fine-tuning on 150K trajectories; UAOR achieves similar observation-reinforcement goals with zero training, making the training-free property the key differentiating axis.*

**Neighbor 3:** Dropout Decoding (Fang et al., 2024) — *Fang et al. use per-token uncertainty to guide visual-token dropout in VLMs (not VLAs); UAOR repurposes uncertainty-based gating from a pruning/dropout context to an injection context, inverting the mechanism: instead of dropping tokens under uncertainty, it reinjects them.*

**Neighbor 4:** Valle et al. (2025), Token-Based Entropy (TB-E) for VLA uncertainty — *TB-E introduces per-token entropy as a VLA uncertainty metric, which UAOR builds on for action entropy (per-layer average); UAOR's novelty over TB-E is the intervention triggered by the metric, not the metric itself.*

**Neighbor 5:** Karli et al. (2025) — *Leverages token-level uncertainty for human intervention timing; UAOR repurposes the same uncertainty signal to trigger automatic internal observation refresh rather than requesting human input.*

### 4c. Pseudo-Novelty Pattern Check

**Rebrand:** No. UAOR cannot be described in prior work's vocabulary without losing technical content — the uncertainty-gated FFN reinjection is not present in any single prior work. The training-free property is genuinely distinguishing vs. all cited observation-augmentation methods.

**Domain transfer:** Partial. The FFN-as-memory idea comes from NLP (Geva 2021) and is applied to VLA robotics. The new domain (action generation, robotics control) does have properties that the paper addresses: action entropy is a VLA-specific metric, the reinjection mechanism is adapted for discrete and continuous action heads, and the threshold calibration is model-specific. The paper does address domain-specific failure modes (dual-system vs. single-system architectures), so the transfer is substantive rather than mechanical.

**Composition without insight:** Partial, but the composition is non-trivial. The paper combines: (a) FFN-as-memory from Geva 2021, (b) per-token entropy gating from VLA uncertainty literature, and (c) training-free inference-time intervention. The non-obvious insight is that the uncertainty signal and the injection mechanism are coupled: high action entropy at layer ℓ implies lower observation attention, and injecting observation features precisely at that point (layer ℓ+1) addresses the causal gap. This is more than additive engineering — the coupling of diagnosis (entropy rise) with fix (observation reinjection at the subsequent layer) is the novel configuration.

**Resolution bump:** No. The contribution is a new intervention technique, not a rescaling of existing benchmarks.

### 4d. Novel Framing vs. Novel Science

The "working-memory decay" analogy and the FFN-as-memory framing are both borrowed vocabulary. However, the framing does enable new predictions: if VLA models "forget" observation information through forward passes at a predictable layer range (early high-uncertainty layers), then targeting reinjection at those specific layers should outperform global reinjection. This is a falsifiable prediction that the authors verify empirically (by showing entropy-rise in early layers correlated with attention drop). The framing is doing modest scientific work — it leads to the uncertainty-gating design choice, not just rhetorical decoration. However, the working-memory decay analogy is not formally grounded in the cognitive science literature beyond a loose motivational cite (Ballard et al. 1995).

### 4g. Positioning Issues

**Overclaim relative to evidence:** Mild. The abstract claims UAOR "consistently improves diverse VLA models across simulation and real-world tasks." The evidence covers a specific set of VLA architectures (OpenVLA-OFT, CogACT, π0) and a specific set of LIBERO task suites plus limited real-world tasks. The claim of "diverse VLA models" is reasonable but the scope is narrower than the word "diverse" implies — all tested models share a transformer backbone with VLM pretraining. Quote: "enabling more confident and faithful action generation" is unfalsified but also underqualified.

**Wrong reference class:** The paper compares itself to "observation augmentation methods" (TraceVLA, PointVLA, etc.) as the primary reference class, which is appropriate. The comparison is fair.

**Missing the natural baseline:** The most important missing baseline is **uniform reinjection at all layers** (without uncertainty gating). UAOR's key claim is that uncertainty-triggered reinjection is better than always reinjecting — this comparison is not shown in the main experiments, making it unclear whether the entropy gating is essential or whether simply "more observation access is better" explains the gains. This is the central ablation the paper needs.

**Inflated framing words:** "training-free and plug-and-play" is accurate and not inflated. No "first to" or "paradigm" language detected in abstract or introduction. Framing is relatively honest.

**Buried real contribution:** The theoretical analysis (Theorems 3.1–3.4 in §3.4) is substantive and appears after the empirical motivation. The theorems on mutual information increase and information bottleneck optimization are the cleanest contribution; the abstract mentions them briefly ("rigorous theoretical analysis") but does not lead with them. This is the more durable contribution than the empirical results.

### 4h. Originality Score Rationale

The strongest novelty axis is the **specific combination of uncertainty-gated (not always-on) observation reinjection through the FFN's key-value memory interpretation, applied training-free to VLA inference**. The closest prior (Geva et al. 2021) provides the FFN-as-memory insight; the closest observation-augmentation priors (TraceVLA, PointVLA) require training; the closest uncertainty literature (TB-E, Karli 2025) does not perform reinjection. The delta vs. all three is defensible: no single prior work combines the FFN-as-memory retrieval mechanism with uncertainty gating for training-free VLA observation refresh.

However, the individual components are all drawn from prior work. The composition is non-trivial but the paper's own related-work section documents most of the building blocks explicitly. The training-free property is the most practically valuable novelty axis. The missing uniform-reinjection baseline leaves the core innovation claim (that uncertainty gating is essential, not just adding more observation access) unverified.

Score: **5** — solid incremental contribution with a clear and defensible delta, a useful training-free property, and a theoretical grounding; not a major conceptual breakthrough, and the central ablation is missing.
