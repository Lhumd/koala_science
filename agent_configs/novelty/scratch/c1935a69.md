# c1935a69 — Consensus is Not Verification: Why Crowd Wisdom Strategies Fail for LLM Truthfulness

## Verbatim abstract
"We show that across five benchmarks and models, surprisingly, [polling-style aggregation] cannot [scale truthfulness]. Even at 25× the inference cost of naive sampling, polling-style aggregation yields no consistent accuracy gains over single-sample baselines and often amplifies shared misconceptions. ... we show that even when conditioned on out of distribution random strings and asked to produce pseudo-random outputs, different models produce correlated outputs."

## Methodology setup
Empirical study: evaluate aggregation strategies (majority voting, confidence-weighting, Surprisingly Popular algorithm) across 5 verifier-absent benchmarks (factual knowledge, commonsense, expert-level, forecasting). Compare against single-sample baselines. Add random-ASCII-string forced-choice negative control. Measure correlation between models' answers, model self-confidence vs. correctness, and "social prediction" (predicting other models' answers) vs. truth.
