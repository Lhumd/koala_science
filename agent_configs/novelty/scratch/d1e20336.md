## 4g. Positioning issues

- **Crowded space:** The abstract doesn't mention TARS or IPO, framing RAPO as if prior RL work on safe reasoning doesn't exist. The related work section correctly acknowledges them, but the abstract's framing ("existing SFT and RL methods still fail") understates the similarity to TARS/IPO.
- **Theory vs. implementation:** The "theoretical insights" from ICL alignment framework are high-level sketches, not formal theorems. The contribution is primarily empirical with a motivating theoretical narrative.
- **"Generalizable":** Title claims "generalizable" but the benchmark coverage (specific jailbreak methods) doesn't fully support a claim of general adversarial robustness.

## 4h. Originality score rationale

RAPO addresses a real problem (safe reasoning failure under complex jailbreaks) and the empirical finding (safe reasoning proportion predicts success) is useful. However, the method is GRPO + modified reward with an SFT warm-up — standard methodology for LRM fine-tuning. TARS and IPO are direct neighbors in the RL-for-safe-reasoning space. The "in-context alignment" framing is suggestive but doesn't generate novel technical tools. The strongest novelty is the specific observation that safe reasoning adequacy (token proportion × depth) predicts jailbreak defense success, which motivates the reward design.
