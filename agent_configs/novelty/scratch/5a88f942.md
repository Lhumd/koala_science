# Phase A Scratch: 5a88f942 — Private PoEtry: Private In-Context Learning via Product of Experts

## Abstract (verbatim)

> "In-context learning (ICL) enables Large Language Models (LLMs) to adapt to new tasks with only a small set of examples at inference time, thereby avoiding task-specific fine-tuning. However, in-context examples may contain privacy-sensitive information that should not be revealed through model outputs. Existing differential privacy (DP) approaches to ICL are either computationally expensive or rely on heuristics with limited effectiveness, including context oversampling, synthetic data generation, or unnecessary thresholding. We reformulate private ICL through the lens of a Product-of-Experts model. This gives a theoretically grounded framework, and the algorithm can be trivially parallelized. We evaluate our method across five datasets in text classification, math, and vision-language. We find that our method improves accuracy by more than 30 percentage points on average compared to prior DP-ICL methods, while maintaining strong privacy guarantees."

## 4b. Closest Prior Work

**Neighbor 1: Wu et al. (2024) — Privacy-Amplification-by-Subsampling (PbS)**
Delta: Wu et al. apply subsampling and either call the LLM hundreds of times or threshold discrete logits, discarding the logit distribution. This paper replaces discrete hard voting with continuous soft log-probability aggregation via the PoE framework. The technical gap is the move from hard majority vote (no gradient flow through logit distribution) to summed clipped log-probs (retains full distribution information under DP noise).

**Neighbor 2: Tang et al. (2024) — Synthetic data generation as DP bottleneck**
Delta: Tang et al. generate synthetic data via an LLM as a differentially private intermediary, then re-input that synthetic data. The bottleneck is the token-by-token generation of synthetic examples, which is computationally expensive and depends on the task. This paper sidesteps the synthetic data pipeline entirely by treating each real example as an independent expert, with per-example LLM calls that are parallelizable.

**Neighbor 3: Hinton (2002) / Heskes (1997) — Product-of-Experts model**
Delta: Hinton's PoE model is the technical primitive being applied. The connection of PoE to differentially private ICL is new — Hinton's original formulation was for probabilistic ensemble methods in neural networks, not for privacy-preserving inference with exponential mechanisms. The novel contribution is the specific instantiation of PoE in the ICL+DP setting and the resulting theoretical convergence analysis.

**Neighbor 4: Sun et al. (2025) — also uses synthetic data for private ICL**
Delta: Sun et al. (2025) continue the synthetic data generation approach, incurring similar bottlenecks as Tang et al. (2024). This paper's contribution is most sharply contrasted against the synthetic-data line of work by demonstrating an approach with no synthetic generation step.

## 4c. Pseudo-Novelty Pattern Check

- **Rebrand:** NO. The Product-of-Experts model (Hinton 2002) is explicitly acknowledged as prior work. The contribution is a new application of PoE to the DP-ICL setting with new theoretical analysis, not a renaming of PoE under a new label. The contribution cannot be described in prior DP-ICL vocabulary (Tang 2024, Wu 2024) without invoking the PoE structure.

- **Domain transfer:** PARTIAL — but with new domain-specific contributions. Applying PoE (an ensemble method) to LLM inference with differential privacy is a domain transfer. However, the privacy setting introduces non-trivial adaptations: (1) the exponential mechanism replaces standard renormalization, (2) the clipping of log-probabilities is a domain-specific modification required for DP sensitivity bounds, and (3) the theoretical convergence proof is tailored to the ICL setting. The paper does not simply run Hinton's algorithm on a new dataset; it modifies it for DP guarantees.

- **Composition without insight:** NO. The combination of PoE + exponential mechanism is not additive engineering. The interaction produces a non-obvious property: the algorithm achieves DP guarantees while converging to the same distribution as full-context ICL. Neither PoE alone nor the exponential mechanism alone produces this guarantee — the specific composition is required.

- **Resolution bump:** NO. The headline contribution is not "we ran the same experiment at scale" — it is a new algorithm formulation with distinct theoretical properties and a 30 pp empirical gap versus direct predecessors.

- **Missing the natural baseline:** POTENTIAL. The paper does not include an ablation comparing (a) soft aggregation WITHOUT the PoE framing vs. (b) the full PoE algorithm. The abstract and introduction emphasize the empirical improvement (30 pp) and the PoE connection, but the theoretically interesting claim — that PoE-ICL converges to full-context ICL distribution as J → ∞ — is not the headline. This is arguably the most intellectually interesting contribution and is buried relative to the empirical lead. The comment can constructively suggest elevating this theoretical result.
