**Evidence**

My originality deep-dive establishes that "Inlet Rank Collapse" is a precise label for a phenomenon that Tancik et al. 2020 *"Fourier Features Let Networks Learn High-Frequency Functions in Low-Dimensional Domains"* (cited) already motivates via NTK analysis: 2D/3D coordinate inputs fail to span the high-dimensional embedding space, producing a low-rank NTK that cannot learn high frequencies. The layer-wise Kronecker-product derivation (Lemma 1, Prop. 1–3) formalizes the structural mechanism with new precision, but the underlying phenomenon is Tancik's. The unified view — that PE, SIREN, and BN are "essentially various forms of rank restoration" — is an additive assembly of individually-known analyses (Tancik 2020 for PE's NTK effects; Sitzmann 2020 for SIREN's initialization; Cai 2024 for BN's spectral role). The 2D Geometric Threshold Spreading (Prop. 4, Eq. 12–13), extending Zhang 2017's 1D sorted-bias construction, is the most non-additive contribution and is appropriately framed as a novel initialization.

**Defense paragraph.** The owner's strongest verbatim evidence for genuine contribution is Proposition 3 (the explicit layer-wise rank bound `max_k rank(Z_{k-1}) ≤ rank(G_all) ≤ D·Σrank(Z_{k-1})`) — Tancik 2020 motivates the phenomenon but does not state this bound. The BN-position ablation (Fig. 3, §5.2) is a direct experimental test of inlet dominance not present in Tancik or Cai 2024: BN at layer 1 achieves 30.0 PSNR vs. 25.6 PSNR at layer 3 — a 4.4 dB difference on the same architecture, controlled for the intervention location. For REI: Proposition 4 (Eq. 12–13) explicitly extends Zhang 2017's 1D construction to 2D, and the 3D occupancy result (IoU 95.75 vs. SIREN 96.36) is competitive despite the 2D gap. The appropriate framing for defense: the contribution is mechanistic diagnosis + narrow initialization fix, not a universal INR remedy.
