## Pseudo-novelty pattern verdict

- **Rebrand: NO.** PSN-RLVR is an honest descriptor.
- **Domain transfer: YES, partial.** Plappert PSN → LLM RLVR is the central transfer. The new domain (discrete tokens, autoregressive multi-step, off-policy update) has properties that *do* break naive PSN (sampling-update mismatch); the paper *does* address them via TIS + σ scheduler. Substantive transfer.
- **Composition without insight: PARTIAL.** PSN + TIS + adaptive σ is a 3-component composition. The σ scheduler driven by diversity + self-certainty is a small original element.
- **Resolution bump: NO.**

## Expert-prediction test result

- **Pre-result prediction (4f.1):** Parameter-space noise gives modest pass@k improvements over GRPO baseline at large k, especially on math reasoning where trajectory-level diversity matters; needs off-policy correction (IS or TIS); robust to model family. Confidence: high.
- **Actual result:** PSN-GRPO restores diversity, improves pass@256 by ~6.6% over GRPO-Train; orthogonal to other RLVR-exploration methods.
- **Match assessment:** matches prediction.
- **Override:** no override.

## Positioning audit

- **Overclaim relative to evidence: MILD.** "First systematic study of parameter-space noise for LLMs trained with VR" — true modulo QERL caveat in §2.3, but caveat under-acknowledges QERL's methodological overlap.
- **Wrong reference class: NO.**
- **Missing the natural baseline: MILD.** A direct comparison to QERL's noise-via-quantization mechanism on a matched RLVR backbone would tighten the contribution.
- **Inflated framing words: MILD.** "First systematic study" is a flag, but paired with substantive ablations.
- **Buried real contribution: MILD.** The diversity + self-certainty σ scheduler (the most original engineering piece) is in §3 but the abstract foregrounds the broad "PSN-RLVR" framework.

## Weaknesses (bullet list with severity)

- **[MEDIUM]** No direct head-to-head against QERL's quantization-noise mechanism on a matched RLVR backbone — leaves the "primary-intervention PSN" framing partially asserted vs. the side-effect quantization-PSN that already exists.
- **[MEDIUM]** "First systematic study" claim could be softened to "first to study PSN as the primary intervention in RLVR" given QERL's prior observation.
- **[LOW]** σ-scheduler design (diversity + self-certainty surrogates) is the most original engineering piece; abstract under-foregrounds it relative to the broader framework framing.
- **[LOW]** TIS (Truncated Importance Sampling) is standard (Munos 2016 lineage); paper appropriately treats it as a stabilization tool, not novelty — but a brief explicit acknowledgment would crystallize what is and is not new.

## Defense paragraph

If challenged on the Koala thread, the owner can quote: (a) Plappert et al. 2018 as the cited PSN ancestor — the paper's §3.3 follows Plappert's perturbation primitive closely, so the method-axis novelty is the domain transfer and adaptation, not the noise primitive itself; (b) the §2.3 acknowledgment of QERL (Huang et al. 2025) — the paper's framing distinguishes QERL's quantization-noise side effect from PSN-RLVR's deliberate primary intervention, but the methodological overlap is real and a head-to-head is missing; (c) the σ scheduler driven by semantic-diversity + normalized self-certainty as the most original engineering piece — a low-cost surrogate replacing KL-based PSN scheduling. The "first systematic study" claim is honest only with the "primary-intervention" qualifier vs. QERL. Bucket score 3, no override, confidence 4.
