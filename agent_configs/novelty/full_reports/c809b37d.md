## Pseudo-novelty pattern verdict

- **Rebrand: PARTIAL.** "Soft-Rejection Sampling" is essentially "rejection sampling fine-tuning with a soft (interval) acceptance threshold via a deterministic verifier" — well-known from RFT. The new name does not enable predictions the old framing does not.
- **Domain transfer: YES, partial.** SRS half is a transfer of STaR/RFT to image-to-CAD. The new domain has properties (executable program → continuous geometric verifier; equivalence-class-rich) that the paper *does* address (soft-interval threshold motivated by geometric-equivalence; τ_valid = 0.9 / τ_match = 0.99 from the IoU CDF). Substantive transfer on the SRS side.
- **Composition without insight: MIXED.** SRS + FDA is presented as additive and Fig. 2d shows gains stack. Non-trivial element: FDA targets an explicitly *disjoint* IoU interval — the two losses train on disjoint slices of failure space and are designed to be non-redundant. Mild insight.
- **Resolution bump: NO.**

## Expert-prediction test result

- **Pre-result prediction (4f.1):** Augmenting an SFT image-to-CAD trainset with (i) high-IoU non-exact-string-match candidates re-paired with their original image and (ii) low-IoU candidates rendered back into images and paired with the GT code, each obtained via a CAD geometric verifier, will produce 5–15% IoU gains over plain SFT and substantially reduce inference compute. Confidence: high.
- **Actual result:** +12% mean IoU; 80% inference compute reduction; FDA contributes the larger lift (+53.8%) vs. SRS (+31.4%).
- **Match assessment:** matches prediction.
- **Override:** no override.

## Positioning audit

- **Overclaim relative to evidence: MILD.** "Reduces inference compute by 80%" is true at matched performance, but the headline could read as "anywhere/anytime"; it is benchmark-specific (GenCAD-Code/DeepCAD).
- **Wrong reference class: MILD.** "Competitive with more complex multimodal systems" without naming them in the abstract; the §4 comparators are mostly CAD-Coder-SFT and ablations of GIFT itself.
- **Missing the natural baseline: YES.** No head-to-head against an *online RL with geometric reward* baseline (Wang 2025b / Chen 2025 / Li 2025c — concurrent), the closest direct competitor. The "RL alternative without instability" claim is asserted but not compared.
- **Inflated framing words: MILD.** "Amortizes inference-time search into the model parameters" is standard self-training rhetoric.
- **Buried real contribution: YES.** The FDA render-back primitive (§3.1, Fig. 7) is the most novel piece but the abstract foregrounds the "dual mechanism" framing equally. Leading with FDA's inverse-rendering objective would sharpen the contribution.

## Weaknesses (bullet list with severity)

- **[MEDIUM]** No head-to-head against online geometric-RL baselines (Wang 2025b, Chen 2025, Li 2025c) — leaves "RL alternative without instability" claim partially asserted.
- **[MEDIUM]** SRS half is largely a continuous-verifier transfer of STaR/RFT; the abstract presents SRS and FDA symmetrically but their conceptual novelty differs sharply (FDA is the genuinely original primitive).
- **[LOW]** Threshold choices (τ_valid = 0.9, τ_match = 0.99) are motivated by the IoU CDF but their sensitivity is not exhaustively ablated.
- **[LOW]** "80% inference compute reduction" headline is benchmark-specific; out-of-distribution generalization is on a smaller (400-sample) test set.

## Defense paragraph

If challenged on the Koala thread, the owner can quote: (a) Zelikman et al. 2022 (STaR) as the closest self-training prior — the paper's §3 explicitly distinguishes via "deterministic geometric kernel vs. heuristic confidence scores or learned rewards"; (b) the SRS-half is "STaR with a soft IoU-interval acceptance threshold using a CAD verifier" — substantive domain transfer (continuous, equivalence-class-aware verifier instead of discrete answer match), but the underlying principle is the same; (c) the genuinely new piece is FDA's inverse-rendering step (§3.1): rendering a failed program z back into a synthetic image c̃ = φ(d(z)) and training (c̃, z_gt). Fig. 2d shows FDA contributes the larger lift (+53.8% vs. +31.4% from SRS), consistent with FDA being the load-bearing primitive. Bucket score 3, no override, confidence 4.
