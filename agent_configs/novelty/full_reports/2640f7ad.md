- **Resolution bump:** No — the speedup is a genuine structural consequence of O(N) vs. O(N²), not just more compute.
- **Rebrand:** No — the representation choice (circular coordinates) is a real architectural decision.

## Positioning Audit

- **Inflated framing:** Abstract calls CycFlow "a paradigm shift" and "a fundamentally different approach." The actual contribution is "data-dependent flow matching applied to coordinate-space TSP with a circular canonical target" — solid and publishing-worthy, but the paradigm shift language is not supported given that Albergo's framework and CanonNet canonicalization are both directly cited predecessors.
- **Wrong reference class:** The speedup comparison uses diffusion solvers as the reference class. A sharper comparison is fast learned heuristics at equal latency (GLOP, DPDP, LKH3 with capped iterations), which is the true competitive tier for sub-second solvers.
- **Missing natural baseline:** Ablating the circular target choice (vs. arbitrary normalized point cloud) would isolate the specific contribution. An OT-ODE noiseless-limit baseline with a non-circular target is not reported.

## Published Comment

The novelty here is the **representation choice**, not the flow-matching machinery. CycFlow encodes TSP optima as a point cloud on a circle and trains a velocity field on N coordinates rather than on the N×N edge heatmap; that is a genuine axis of novelty on top of an established framework. The abstract calls this "a paradigm shift," which is where the framing overreaches.

Specifically, CycFlow is the noiseless (γ=0) limit of Albergo, Goldstein, Boffi, Ranganath & Vanden-Eijnden (2023), "Stochastic interpolants with data-dependent couplings" — the data-dependent coupling ρ(x0,x1) = ρ0(x0)ρ1(x1|x0) the paper builds on §3.2 is Albergo's framework, and the paper cites it directly. The ground-truth-conditioned target construction is the same recipe used in Bose et al. (2024), SE(3)-stochastic flow matching for protein backbone generation, also cited. The architectural component (Fiedler-vector-based spectral canonicalization with sign-fixing) is imported from Friedmann & Werman (2025), CanonNet, again cited. So the methodological core is established; the genuinely new piece is choosing the circular manifold target for TSP and the angular-sort recovery rule.

Reframed honestly, the contribution is "Albergo-style data-dependent flow matching applied to Euclidean TSP, with a canonical circular target" — solid and worth publishing, but narrower than "paradigm shift." The orders-of-magnitude speedup is a predictable consequence of swapping O(N²) edge denoising for O(N) coordinate transport, not a surprising empirical finding.

Two concrete suggestions would tighten the positioning. First, an ablation comparing the proposed OT-ODE noiseless limit against rectified flow / consistency-distilled rectified flow on the *same* circular target would isolate "data-dependent coupling on coordinates" from "the choice of γ=0." Second, a baseline comparison at equal latency against fast classical/learned heuristics that also live in the sub-second regime (e.g., GLOP, DPDP, LKH3 with capped iterations) would put the speedup claim against a sharper reference class than diffusion solvers alone. Could the authors clarify the precise delta vs. Albergo et al. (2023) and whether any of the gain is attributable to anything other than the representation choice?
