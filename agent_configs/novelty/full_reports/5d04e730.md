| # | Neighbor | Specific overlapping result | Delta |

| 2 | **Stoica et al. 2024b (TSVM, cited)** | Gradient-based task-vector-aligning pre-merge step via SVD without extra adaptation data | RI requires unlabeled auxiliary data but enforces output-space orthogonality on actual inputs (a stronger functional constraint); RI gains stack on top of TSVM (Fig. 1) |

## Pseudo-novelty pattern verdict

- **Rebrand: PARTIAL.** ξ closely tracks Ortiz-Jimenez's disentanglement-error with a substantive twist (representation drift vs. scaling response). Sharpened, not invented from scratch.
- **Domain transfer: NO.** Same domain (CLIP/ViT image classification merging).
- **Composition without insight: PARTIAL.** Combining task-preservation (standard distillation-style) + cross-task null-action loss + unlabeled auxiliary data. The combination is operationally clean and the gains stack on top of multiple mergers — not pure additive engineering.
- **Resolution bump: NO.**

## Expert-prediction test result

- **Pre-result prediction (4f.1):** A lightweight pre-merge per-expert adaptation that orthogonalizes task vectors with respect to other tasks' input-image manifolds (using unlabeled auxiliary data) gives modest, consistent gains (1–4%) over TIES/AdaMerging/DARE on ViT/CLIP image-classification merge benchmarks. Robust across data sources. Confidence: high.
- **Actual result:** "RI consistently boosts the performance of state-of-the-art merging methods by up to 3.8%, with up to 2.3% improvement in OOD generalization." Matches Fig.1 (small but consistent improvement across TA/TIES/KnOTS/Iso-C/Iso-CTS/TSVM, simultaneously reducing measured ξ).
- **Match assessment:** matches prediction.
- **Override:** no override.

- **Inflated framing: NO.** No "first to," no "fundamental," no "we discover" — measured.
- **Buried real contribution: MILD.** The dual-loss formulation in §5 / Eq. 2 (task-preservation + cross-task null-action) is the load-bearing operational primitive but the abstract foregrounds the *metric* (ξ). The metric is the smaller contribution; the loss is the bigger one.

## Weaknesses (bullet list with severity)

- **[MEDIUM]** Abstract foregrounds ξ over the dual-loss; the dual-loss is the load-bearing operational contribution, ξ is a sharpened restatement of disentanglement-error. Reordering would tighten positioning.
- **[MEDIUM]** No head-to-head against concurrent task-vector-aligning methods (Marczak 2025, Choi 2025) — leaves the "first task-agnostic" framing partially asserted.
- **[LOW]** ξ is presented as a new metric; explicit relation to Ortiz-Jimenez's disentanglement-error in the abstract (not just §2) would credit ancestry properly.
- **[LOW]** Auxiliary-data-source ablations show robustness, but the optimal-source-selection strategy is left unstudied — practitioners will want a recommendation.
