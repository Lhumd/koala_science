## Pseudo-novelty pattern verdict

- **Rebrand: NO.** "Unlearning by design" is a precise descriptor.
- **Domain transfer: YES — substantive.** Memory networks → unlearning. The new task (instance-level unlearning) requires (a) per-instance addressing during training and (b) deletion-without-weight-update at inference. The paper does address these via the exemplar-token mechanism.
- **Composition without insight: MILD.** MUNKEY = memory-augmented transformer + per-instance learnable keys + deletion-as-unlearning. Conceptually clean; the per-instance learnable key is the operational original element.
- **Resolution bump: NO.**

## Expert-prediction test result

- **Pre-result prediction (4f.1):** A memory-network architecture where each training instance gets a learned key; unlearning = delete the key. Outperforms post-hoc baselines (which fight catastrophic forgetting) on instance-level unlearning. Should generalize to image classification benchmarks. Confidence: high.
- **Actual result:** MUNKEY beats post-hoc baselines on natural images, fine-grained recognition, medical datasets; instance-level unlearning is zero-shot.
- **Match assessment:** matches prediction.
- **Override:** no override.

## Positioning audit

- **Overclaim relative to evidence: MILD.** "First application of external memory mechanisms specifically designed for machine unlearning" — moderately strong; defensible if "specifically designed" is read strictly.
- **Wrong reference class: MILD.** Compares to "post-hoc baselines" but a comparison to architectures that *also* decouple memorization (Memorizing Transformers, RETRO-style retrievers) would strengthen the claim.
- **Missing the natural baseline: MILD.** A k-NN classifier over a memory bank with deletion = drop entries would be the simplest "unlearning by design" baseline. Not benchmarked.
- **Inflated framing words: MILD.** "Paradigm shift" is a flag but the paper backs it up with experiments.
- **Buried real contribution: NO.**

## Weaknesses (bullet list with severity)

- **[MEDIUM]** No retrieval-based classifier baseline (k-NN over memory bank with deletion = drop entries) — the simplest "unlearning by design" alternative.
- **[MEDIUM]** Memorizing Transformers (Wu et al. 2022) is cited but its closer architectural relationship to MUNKEY (vs. NTM/MemNets) is not foregrounded — it is the better architectural neighbor for the abstract.
- **[LOW]** "Paradigm shift" framing is strong; the structural novelty (per-instance learnable key) deserves more abstract real-estate than the broader framing.
- **[LOW]** "First application" claim could be softened to "first end-to-end design with deletion as the primitive operation" to head off challenges from prior associative-memory-based approaches.
