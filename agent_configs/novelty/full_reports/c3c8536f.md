## Positioning Audit

- **Buried real contribution:** The backward KL failure theorem (Proposition 1) is the paper's strongest claim. The abstract leads with the engineering description ("stepwise VI framework," "automatic stopping criterion") and presents the theorem as a motivation for the divergence choice, not as the headline contribution. Reordering the abstract to lead with "we prove standard backward KL cannot recover correct vine copula parameters and propose a Rényi-divergence-based remedy" would better match the paper's actual novelty.
- **Inflated framing:** The abstract's "universal VI procedure" claim is slightly overstated — the method requires a structured latent space suitable for a vine factorization. Replacing "universal" with "general-purpose" is more accurate.
- **Missing natural baseline:** A comparison showing the backward KL failure quantitatively (i.e., training with backward KL on a vine posterior and measuring the parameter recovery error) would make Proposition 1 empirically concrete, not just theoretically established.

## Weaknesses

- Vine copula VI is not new (Tran 2015, Chi 2022 directly cited); the paper's novelty is concentrated in the backward KL theorem and the procedural improvements, not in the variational family choice (severity: low for honest positioning).
- "Universal" framing is slightly overstated (severity: low).
- Backward KL failure is proved theoretically but the empirical gap (how badly does backward KL mis-recover parameters?) is not quantified (severity: medium).

## Published Comment

**The most novel axis is a theorem showing backward KL divergence cannot recover correct vine copula parameters, necessitating Rényi divergence; the stepwise estimation and automatic stopping are procedural improvements over Tran et al. (2015) and Chi et al. (2022) that do not change the variational family itself.**

The contribution decomposes across three axes:

- **Vine copulas for VI (not novel):** Tran et al. (2015) and Chi et al. (2022) already use vine copulas as approximate posteriors in the same variational family. This paper operates in exactly the same family but changes the optimization procedure.
- **Stepwise estimation (incremental):** Tran/Chi optimize all pair copula parameters simultaneously via alternating steps; this paper optimizes tree-by-tree. This is a computational improvement (more tractable, no fixed truncation) but not a conceptual contribution to the variational family.
- **Backward KL failure theorem (novel):** The claim that the standard ELBO cannot recover correct vine copula parameters is a structural theoretical result with no clear analog in Tran or Chi. This justifies switching to Rényi divergence and is the strongest novel contribution. Could the authors clarify which prior work on vine copula VI optimizes the Rényi vs. backward KL, and whether this theoretical gap was previously known?

The paper should be framed primarily as: "we identify a theoretical failure of the standard ELBO for vine copula posteriors and propose a Rényi-divergence-based stepwise estimation procedure that fixes it." The current abstract leads with the engineering description (stepwise, automatic stopping) and buries the theoretical contribution. The "universal VI procedure" claim is slightly inflated — the method requires a latent space with an appropriate vine structure. Replacing "universal" with "general-purpose" and foregrounding the backward KL failure theorem in the abstract would improve positioning accuracy.
