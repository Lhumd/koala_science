## Theorem / formal-claim inventory

### Definition 4.1 (GRPO Advantage Collapse), p. 7, Eq. 10
A_j = (R(τ_j) − μ_g) / (σ_g + ε_stab). Collapses to 0 when σ_g → 0.

### Proposition 4.2 (Vanishing Gradient in Peaked Policies), p. 7
Statement: Let π_ref be trained on optimal demonstrations. Suppose for each step t (and history h_t* on the optimal trajectory), SFT achieves
  D_KL(π*(·|h_t*) || π_ref(·|h_t*)) ≤ ε_sft        (Eq. 11)
Then for G independent trajectories sampled from π_ref:
  P(τ_1 = τ_2 = ... = τ_G = τ*) ≥ e^{−GTε_sft} ≥ 1 − GTε_sft        (Eq. 12)
On the event {all τ_j = τ*}, R(τ_j) identical, so σ_g = 0 and A_j = 0.

### Proposition 4.3 (Variance Guarantee via Reward Conditioning), p. 8, Eq. 13
Statement: In RC-GRPO, each τ_j is conditioned on r_j ~ P_sample(r). If π_ref learns modes such that |E[R(τ)|r_i] − E[R(τ)|r_j]| ≥ ε for r_i ≠ r_j, then
  E[σ_g²] ≥ κε²        (Eq. 13)
where σ_g² = (1/G) Σ_{j=1}^G (R(τ_j) − μ_g)², κ = (G−1)/G · p(1−p) for binary P_sample with parameter p.

## Assumption inventory
- A1 (Dirac optimal): π*(·|h_t*) is a Dirac measure at a_t* (Eq. 11 D_KL is from a Dirac to π_ref).
- A2 (Per-step KL bound): D_KL(π*(·|h_t*) || π_ref(·|h_t*)) ≤ ε_sft for each step t on the optimal trajectory.
- A3 (Deterministic environment): implicit in the proof — P(τ = τ*) = ∏_t π_ref(a_t* | h_t*) requires that conditional on a_t = a_t*, the next observation o_t is deterministically equal to o_t*. Standard for tool-calling but not stated.
- A4 (i.i.d. group rollouts): trajectories sampled independently from π_ref.
- A5 (Mode separation): for Prop 4.3, |μ_h − μ_l| ≥ ε where μ_h = E[R(τ)|r=high], μ_l = E[R(τ)|r=low]. This is an assumption on the trained RCTP policy π_ref — not derived from the RCTP objective itself.

## Load-bearing lemma list
- Lemma A.2 (KL → probability bound): If π* Dirac at a*, D_KL(π* || π) ≤ ε ⇒ π(a*) ≥ e^{−ε} ≥ 1 − ε.
- Lemma A.3 (Law of Total Variance): Var(X) ≥ Var_Y(E[X|Y]).
- Lemma A.4 (Expected group second central moment): For X_1, ..., X_G i.i.d. with finite variance, E[S_G²] = (G−1)/G · Var(X), where S_G² = (1/G) Σ (X_j − X̄)².

## Appendix proofs cross-check
- Lemma A.2 proof (Eq. 15): D_KL(π* || π) = Σ_a π*(a) log(π*(a)/π(a)) = 1 · log(1/π(a*)) = −log π(a*). ≤ ε ⇒ π(a*) ≥ e^{−ε}. Then e^{−ε} ≥ 1 − ε for ε ≥ 0. ✓
- Prop 4.2 proof (Eqs. 16–19): Per-step π_ref(a_t* | h_t*) ≥ e^{−ε_sft}. Product over T: P(τ = τ*) = ∏_t π_ref(a_t* | h_t*) ≥ e^{−Tε_sft} ≥ 1 − Tε_sft. For G i.i.d. samples: P(all = τ*) = (P(τ = τ*))^G ≥ e^{−GTε_sft} ≥ 1 − GTε_sft. On that event, R identical, σ_g = 0, A_j = 0, gradient term zero. ✓
- Lemma A.4 proof (Eq. 22): E[S_G²] = E[X²] − E[X̄²] = (Var(X) + μ²) − (Var(X)/G + μ²) = Var(X)(1 − 1/G) = (G−1)/G · Var(X). ✓
- Prop 4.3 proof (Eqs. 23–25): By LTV, Var(X) ≥ Var_r(E[X|r]) = p(1−p)(μ_h − μ_l)² for binary r ~ Bernoulli(p). Under |μ_h − μ_l| ≥ ε: Var(X) ≥ p(1−p)ε². By Lemma A.4: E[σ_g²] = (G−1)/G · Var(X) ≥ (G−1)/G · p(1−p)ε². κ = (G−1)/G · p(1−p). ✓

## Theory-score rationale (theoretical-rigor only)

The two propositions are correctly stated and correctly proved. The toolkit is standard (KL → density bound, law of total variance, Cochran-style identity for the empirical second central moment of i.i.d. samples). The assumptions are clearly enumerated. The propositions support the paper's central narrative about advantage collapse and variance restoration via reward conditioning.

Three substantive concerns from a theoretical-rigor standpoint:

1. **Exact-vs-near collapse gap.** Prop 4.2 controls the *exact-collapse* event {τ_1 = ... = τ_G = τ*} via a tail bound on probability ≥ 1 − GTε_sft. The empirical phenomenon being targeted is *near-collapse* — groups with small but nonzero σ_g. With a normalized advantage A_j = (R(τ_j) − μ_g)/(σ_g + ε_stab), the gradient is dominated by ε_stab whenever σ_g ≪ ε_stab, even if not all trajectories are identical. Prop 4.2 does not bound σ_g itself, so the chain "high SFT confidence ⇒ low effective gradient" is established only on the exact-match measure-1 event, not on the smoother near-match regime that the empirical analysis (Table 4, Fig. 2) actually documents. A continuity-style result E[σ_g²] ≤ f(ε_sft) would close this gap, but is not provided.

2. **Mode-separation assumption in Prop 4.3.** The variance lower bound requires |E[R(τ)|r=high] − E[R(τ)|r=low]| ≥ ε in the trained π_ref. This is an assumption on the *outcome* of RCTP fine-tuning — not a consequence of any of the paper's stated objectives. The paper provides no theoretical or empirical estimate of ε. If RCTP fails to instill mode separation, the lower bound κε² → 0 and Prop 4.3 is vacuous. The theory thus only addresses the second of two required conditions ("RC injects variance"); the first ("RCTP achieves mode separation") is implicit.

3. **Determinism of the environment** is used implicitly in Prop 4.2's proof to write P(τ = τ*) = ∏_t π_ref(a_t* | h_t*). For multi-turn tool calling with stochastic API responses (rare but possible), this would not hold and the bound would weaken.

Novelty is marginal. The Prop 4.2 mechanism (peaked policy ⇒ collapsed group ⇒ zero advantage) is qualitatively known from prior vanishing-gradient analyses (Razin et al. 2024 cited). The Prop 4.3 result is a one-line application of the law of total variance. The contribution is the framing — using these to motivate reward-conditioning as a structural variance injection — not the theorems themselves.

Score: 5. Correct, defensible, but modest in depth. Both propositions are well-executed but neither is an independently novel theoretical result; both have stated gaps relative to the empirical claim they purport to support.
