# Scratch: 10c1b45d — How Attention Sinks Emerge in LLMs (P0-Sink Circuit)

## Theorem / formal-claim inventory

The paper has no labeled theorems/lemmas/propositions. The theoretical content lives in two derivations, both in §4:

### Derivation D1 — pre-norm sensitivity (§4.1, p. 6–7, Eqs. 1–5)
- Eq. 1: Norm(x) = x / r(x).
- Eq. 2: r(x) = sqrt((1/d) x^T x).
- Eq. 3: Jacobian J_Norm(x) = (1/r(x)) I − (1/d) (1/r³(x)) x x^T.
- Eq. 4: δx = δx_∥ + δx_⊥, where δx_∥ = α x and δx_⊥ · x = 0.
- Eq. 5: Norm(x+δx) − Norm(x) ≈ J_Norm(x) δx = δx_⊥ / r(x).
- Conclusion (informal, p. 7): "when r(x) is large, the effect of orthogonal perturbations δx_⊥ on the normalized vector becomes proportionally smaller."

### Derivation D2 — cone model and attention output norm (§4.2, p. 7–9, Eqs. 6–11)
- Eq. 6: cone model. Each value vector v_i = α u + sqrt(1−α²) s_i, where u ∈ R^d is a fixed unit cone axis, ‖s_i‖₂ = 1, s_i ⊥ u, s_i sampled uniformly on the (d−1)-dim unit sphere orthogonal to u, independent across i.
- Eq. 7: E[v_i^T v_j] = 1 if i=j (deterministic, in fact), α² if i≠j.
- Eq. 8: c = Σ_{i=0}^{l−1} p_i v_i (attention output as weighted sum of values).
- Eq. 9: ‖c‖² = Σ p_i² ‖v_i‖² + Σ_{i≠j} p_i p_j v_i^T v_j.
- Eq. 10: E_v[‖c‖² | p] = Σ p_i² + α² Σ_{i≠j} p_i p_j = α² + (1−α²) Σ p_i² (using Σ p_i = 1).
- Eq. 11: E[‖c‖²] = α² + (1−α²) E[Σ p_i²].

## Assumption inventory
- A1 (cone model): v_i = α u + sqrt(1−α²) s_i, with s_i ⊥ u, ‖s_i‖=1, uniformly distributed on S^(d−2) (the unit sphere in u⊥), independent across i.
- A2: pre-layer RMS normalization yields ‖x_i‖₂ = 1 for input vectors. (Stated p. 7.)
- A3 (used after Eq. 11, not part of cone model): "attention tends to be sparse across positions [Zucchet et al. 2025, ref [21]]." Used to claim E[Σ p_i²] decreases monotonically with sequence length l.

## Load-bearing lemma list
- D1's role: argues that increasing the norm of the position-zero hidden state stabilizes its direction against gradient noise. Used to motivate why a high-norm P0 state is a "useful" device for the model to maintain a stable position-zero anchor. Not central to the sink phenomenon itself.
- D2's role: argues that the position-zero attention output retains a larger norm than other positions because position 0 has only a single value (itself) under causal masking, while later positions average over l terms whose pairwise correlations dilute the effective norm. This is the load-bearing theoretical justification for the "causal-masking asymmetry" claim that is the paper's headline contribution (Contribution 1: "P0 sink arises from causal-masking asymmetry rather than [BOS] semantics").

## Algebra check
Eq. 7 verified: For i ≠ j, v_i^T v_j = α² u^T u + 2α sqrt(1−α²) (cross terms with s_i, s_j) + (1−α²) s_i^T s_j. The cross terms vanish in expectation because u^T s_i = u^T s_j = 0 deterministically; E[s_i^T s_j] = 0 for i ≠ j by independence and isotropy in u⊥. So E[v_i^T v_j] = α². ✓
For i = j: v_i^T v_i = α² + 0 + (1−α²)·1 = 1 deterministically. ✓
Eq. 9 → 10: condition on p, take E over v: sum p_i² · 1 + α² Σ_{i≠j} p_i p_j = sum p_i² + α² ((Σ p_i)² − Σ p_i²) = sum p_i² + α²(1 − sum p_i²) = α² + (1−α²) sum p_i² (since softmax weights sum to 1). ✓
Eq. 11: take outer expectation over p. ✓

The algebra of D2 is fully correct under A1–A2.

## Theory-score rationale (theoretical-rigor only — do NOT invoke empirical results)

The paper has one real theoretical contribution: the cone-model moment computation in §4.2 (Eqs. 6–11). Under the stated assumptions A1–A2, the derivation is algebraically correct. The conclusion E[‖c‖²] = α² + (1−α²) E[Σ p_i²] is a textbook moment computation for weighted sums of cone-distributed vectors with one common direction.

However, the load-bearing chain from Eq. 11 to the headline claim — that the position-zero attention output has larger norm than later-position outputs as sequence length grows — has a clear gap. The paper writes immediately after Eq. 11: "attention tends to be sparse across positions [21]. This sparsity implies that the second-order statistic E[Σ p_i²] typically decreases monotonically as sequence length l increases." This is not a derivation from the cone model. Within the cone model, the attention weights p are treated as exogenous; the model alone produces no monotonicity statement for E[Σ p_i²] as a function of l. The monotonicity is invoked as an external, citation-supported empirical fact, then bolted onto the result.

Position-zero asymmetry is real (the position-zero output is just v_0, deterministic norm 1, no averaging dilution), but the way the paper formalizes the asymmetry-vs-l story leaves the key step on the empirical side rather than the theoretical side. A tighter version would have either (a) added an explicit assumption on how p depends on l (e.g. an upper bound on entropy as l grows) and derived monotonicity inside the model, or (b) phrased the result as a conditional bound parameterized by Σ p_i² without claiming a monotonicity that the model does not deliver.

D1 (pre-norm sensitivity) is correct but slight — a Jacobian computation plus an order-of-magnitude observation, not load-bearing for the sink mechanism.

The novelty of D2 is marginal: the cone model is a standard way to model anisotropic features, and the moment expression Σ p_i² + α²(1 − Σ p_i²) is a one-line corollary once the assumptions are stated. The contribution is interpretive — using this small calculation as a lens on the P0-sink phenomenon — rather than a new theoretical result.

Score: 5 (weak but defensible).

- Novelty is marginal: cone-model moment computation is textbook.
- Independence/isotropy of s_i is a strong assumption in light of the well-known anisotropy and positional correlations of LLM hidden states; the paper does not discuss robustness.
