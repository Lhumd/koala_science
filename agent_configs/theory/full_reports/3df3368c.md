## Theorem inventory

The paper does not formally state numbered theorems/lemmas. The load-bearing theoretical claim is the four-step argument in Section 3.1 (main text, page 4) and Appendix 7.3 (page 13–15), titled "Gaussian Mixture Regression: A Theoretical Basis for OVQ-attention" / "Linking GMR and OVQ-attention".

The four-step argument (p.13, lines 707-724):
1. Their algorithm implements standard GMM initialization (data-point seeding, k-means++-flavored).
2. Under the assumption Σ_n = I·(1/β), the first batch EM step on a GMM is equivalent to a batch K-means update.
3. The first EM update after initialization is equivalent to a Newton update on the NLL.
4. Generating predictions using GMR after initialization and one EM update is equivalent to OVQ-attention.

## Assumption inventory

- **A1** (p.13, line 692-697): "Σ_n = I·(1/β) for all n, where β is a scalar." Justification: simplifying covariance, common in textbook GMM treatments. An additional implication used: Σ_n^{vk} = 0 (zero off-diagonal).
- **A2** (p.13, line 698): q_T and μ_n^k are unit norm.
- **A3** (p.14, line 740-744): "setting the precision β = ∞ is justified, since immediately after initialization, the mean of each Gaussian component equals the one data point assigned to it, so there is no empirical variance between mean and its assigned data point." This is a transient, initialization-time condition.
- **A4** (p.14, line 740-741): Uniform prior π_n = 1/N (justified because c_n = γ_n = 1 for all n components after initialization).

## Load-bearing lemma list

- "First batch EM after initialization ≡ batch K-means" (Sec 7.3 step 2, eq 38-42). Relies on A1, A3, A4.
- "K-means update ≡ Newton step on NLL" (Sec 7.3 step 3, p.14). Cited from Bottou & Bengio 1994.
- "GMR prediction ≡ OVQ-attention" (Sec 7.3 step 4, eq 43-48). Relies on A1, A2; uses the count vector c_n in place of mixing prior π_n.

## Theory score rationale

Each derivation in Sec 7.3 is mathematically correct under its stated assumptions. The issue is the **scope of the load-bearing equivalence**, not its correctness:

- The GMR↔OVQ-attention equivalence is proved only for "GMR after initialization and one EM update" (the title of step 4, p.13 line 722-724). The argument relies on assumption A3 (β=∞), which the paper justifies as a *transient* initialization-time condition (line 741-743): "immediately after initialization, the mean of each Gaussian component equals the one data point assigned to it." This justification specifically does not survive subsequent updates: once a component absorbs more than one data point, its mean is no longer that single data point, empirical within-cluster variance becomes nonzero, and the argument for β=∞ no longer applies.

- However, OVQ-attention performs online updates *throughout each sequence* (eq. 19, applied at every chunk of every position). The "ongoing" updates lack a corresponding equivalence proof. The closest the paper comes is "deviations in cluster assignments are not observed empirically to negatively effect performance" (p.14, line 750-752, citing Bottou & Bengio 1994), which is empirical robustness of online K-means, not theoretical equivalence with the GMR target the section claims to provide.

- A second, more subtle point: in Sec 7.3 step 2, β is set to ∞ to derive K-means↔EM equivalence; in Sec 7.3 step 4 (eq 47-48), β is the *finite, learned* scalar used in the prediction softmax. These two β-values appear in the same proof chain. The paper does not explicitly address that β has different roles (transient ∞ for clustering update justification; finite learned for attention prediction). The reader is left to assume these are consistent because both are written as "β".

- The assumption A1 (isotropic Σ_n = I/β with shared β across components) is strong but stated honestly. The paper does not discuss alternatives or implications.

- No matching lower bound on the gap between OVQ-attention and the idealized GMR target. No quantitative analysis of approximation error after t updates.

Net: the theory is **technically correct but its scope is narrower than the section title suggests**. The proof justifies the algorithm at one specific moment (post-initialization, first EM step) rather than its sustained operation over a long sequence. This is a real but not fatal flaw — it places the contribution at "weak but defensible" rather than "broken" or "sound." The paper acknowledges the gap (line 746-752) but does not bridge it theoretically.

**Score: 4** (weak but defensible — load-bearing equivalence theorem is correct, but its scope is genuinely narrower than the prose framing implies, and there is no theoretical bridge to the algorithm's sustained operation).
