# Scratch: 1b92ce54 — InSight: Efficient RLVR Training via Weighted Mutual Information Data Selection

## Paper sketch
Online data-selection method for RLVR. Models per-prompt success rate $\phi_\tau$ as Beta-distributed; computes a weighted-MI acquisition score; selects top-M prompts each step; runs GRPO. Empirical: planning, math, general reasoning benchmarks; 0.6B–7B LLMs.

## Theorem inventory (verbatim, with proof location)

- **Eq. 8** (main, p.4): $\Delta V(\tau) = \frac{\alpha\beta}{(\alpha+\beta)^2(\alpha+\beta+1)^2} = \frac{\bar\phi_\tau(1-\bar\phi_\tau)}{(n+1)^2}$ where $n = \alpha+\beta$. Derivation: App. A (Eqs. 18–25).

- **Eq. 11** (main, p.4): Closed-form Beta-entropy: $H(\Phi_\tau) = \ln B(\alpha,\beta) + (\alpha+\beta-2)\psi(\alpha+\beta) - (\alpha-1)\psi(\alpha) - (\beta-1)\psi(\beta)$. Derivation: App. B (Eqs. 26–30).

- **Eq. 13** (main, p.5): Multi-rollout MI $I(R_{1:K}; \Phi_\tau) = H(\Phi_\tau) - \sum_{s=0}^K \binom{K}{s} \frac{B(\alpha+s,\beta+K-s)}{B(\alpha,\beta)} H(\Phi_\tau | S_\tau=s)$. (Just expansion of MI = H − sum.)

- **Proposition 5.1** (main, p.5): "Let $\Phi_\tau \sim \text{Beta}(\alpha_\tau, \beta_\tau)$ and $R \in \{0,1\}$ be a Bernoulli reward conditioned on $\Phi_\tau$. Define $n_\tau = \alpha_\tau + \beta_\tau$. Then as $n_\tau \to \infty$, $I(R; \Phi_\tau) \approx \mathcal{O}(1/n_\tau)$." Proof: App. C (Eqs. 31–35).

## Assumption inventory

- **Beta–Bernoulli conjugacy** (Eqs. 1–5): for each datapoint $\tau$, rewards $r_{\tau,j}^t$ are independent Bernoulli($\phi_\tau$) given a latent success rate $\phi_\tau \in [0,1]$. Prior $\phi_\tau \sim \text{Beta}(\alpha_\tau^0, \beta_\tau^0)$, posterior updates by counts.
- **Fixed latent assumption** (implicit): $\phi_\tau$ does not vary across training steps $t$. The "$\phi_\tau^t$" notation in Eq. 1 makes this look explicit, but the conjugacy formulas only work if the same $\phi_\tau$ generates all rewards.
- **Temporal discounting** (Eq. 6): $\alpha^{t+1} = \lambda\alpha^t + (1-\lambda)\alpha^0 + S_\tau^t$, $\beta^{t+1} = \lambda\beta^t + (1-\lambda)\beta^0 + K - S_\tau^t$. Stated as a heuristic for stability; not derived from any non-stationary likelihood model.
- **App. D.2 setting**: $\lambda = 1.0$ for all reported experiments. With $\lambda = 1$, Eq. 6 reduces to plain accumulation $\alpha^{t+1} = \alpha^t + S_\tau^t$, $\beta^{t+1} = \beta^t + K - S_\tau^t$.
- **Asymptotic regime for Prop. 5.1**: $n_\tau \to \infty$. Beta → Gaussian approximation used for entropy.

## Load-bearing lemma list

- Eq. 21 (Beta-Bernoulli posterior): $\phi|r \sim \text{Beta}(\alpha+r, \beta+1-r)$. Standard, correct.
- Eq. 25 (final variance-reduction formula): result of cancellation between prior and posterior variances. Verified independently (see proof check below).
- Eq. 32 (App. C): $I(R;\Phi_\tau) \approx \frac{1}{2}\mathbb{E}_R[\ln(\text{Var}(\Phi_\tau)/\text{Var}(\Phi_\tau|R))]$ — relies on Beta → Gaussian step (Eq. 31).
- Eq. 33 (App. C): replaces $\text{Var}(\Phi_\tau|R)$ by $\text{Var}(\Phi_\tau) - \Delta V(\tau)$ — concentration argument, informal.
- Eq. 34 (App. C): Taylor expansion $\ln(1/(1-x)) \approx x$ for small $x$. Standard.

## Appendix proofs cross-check

**App. A (Eq. 8 derivation).** Verified independently.
- Prior variance: $\text{Var}(\phi) = \alpha\beta/[(\alpha+\beta)^2(\alpha+\beta+1)] = \alpha\beta/[n^2(n+1)]$. ✓
- Posterior under $r=1$: $\text{Beta}(\alpha+1,\beta)$, variance $(\alpha+1)\beta/[(n+1)^2(n+2)]$.
- Posterior under $r=0$: $\text{Beta}(\alpha,\beta+1)$, variance $\alpha(\beta+1)/[(n+1)^2(n+2)]$.
- Marginal $\Pr(r=1) = \alpha/n$, $\Pr(r=0) = \beta/n$.
- $\mathbb{E}_R[\text{Var}(\phi|R)] = \frac{\alpha}{n} \cdot \frac{(\alpha+1)\beta}{(n+1)^2(n+2)} + \frac{\beta}{n} \cdot \frac{\alpha(\beta+1)}{(n+1)^2(n+2)}$
   $= \frac{\alpha\beta[(\alpha+1) + (\beta+1)]}{n(n+1)^2(n+2)} = \frac{\alpha\beta(n+2)}{n(n+1)^2(n+2)} = \frac{\alpha\beta}{n(n+1)^2}$.
- $\Delta V = \frac{\alpha\beta}{n^2(n+1)} - \frac{\alpha\beta}{n(n+1)^2} = \alpha\beta \cdot \frac{(n+1) - n}{n^2(n+1)^2} = \frac{\alpha\beta}{n^2(n+1)^2}$. ✓ Matches Eq. 25.
- This equals $\bar\phi(1-\bar\phi)/(n+1)^2$ since $\bar\phi(1-\bar\phi) = \alpha\beta/n^2$. ✓ Matches Eq. 8.

**App. B (Beta entropy).** Standard textbook formula, no independent issue.

**App. C (Prop. 5.1 proof).** Result correct, but proof informal.
- Step 1 (Eq. 31): Beta → Gaussian. Informal. Holds asymptotically for large $\alpha, \beta$ (CLT-like).
- Step 2 (Eq. 33): "$\text{Var}(\Phi_\tau|R)$ concentrates tightly around its expectation." Informal. For binary $R$, $\text{Var}|R$ takes only 2 distinct values: $(\alpha+1)\beta/[(n+1)^2(n+2)]$ and $\alpha(\beta+1)/[(n+1)^2(n+2)]$. Their difference is $(\beta-\alpha)/[(n+1)^2(n+2)]$, which is small relative to the mean only when $|\beta-\alpha| \ll \alpha\beta/n$ — i.e., when $\alpha \approx \beta$. For unbalanced priors, this approximation degrades, but the leading-order $1/n$ scaling survives.
- Step 3 (Eq. 34): Taylor expansion $\ln(1/(1-x)) \approx x$. Correct for small $x$.
- Step 4 (Eq. 35): plug in numbers. $I \approx \Delta V/[2 \text{Var}(\Phi_\tau)] = \frac{1}{2(n+1)}$. ✓.

**Independent verification of Prop. 5.1.** $I(R;\Phi_\tau) = h_2(\bar\phi_\tau) - \mathbb{E}_{\Phi_\tau}[h_2(\Phi_\tau)]$ where $h_2$ is binary entropy. By Taylor expansion of $h_2$ around $\bar\phi_\tau$: $\mathbb{E}[h_2(\Phi_\tau)] \approx h_2(\bar\phi_\tau) + \frac{1}{2}h_2''(\bar\phi_\tau) \text{Var}(\Phi_\tau)$. With $h_2''(\bar\phi) = -1/[\bar\phi(1-\bar\phi)]$ (in nats):
$I \approx \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{\text{Var}(\Phi_\tau)}{\bar\phi(1-\bar\phi)} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{\alpha\beta/[n^2(n+1)]}{\alpha\beta/n^2} = \frac{1}{2(n+1)}$. ✓
This direct derivation avoids the Gaussian-approximation + concentration steps in App. C. The result is correct.

## Theory score rationale (theory-grounded only)

The paper has three formal results:
1. Closed-form variance reduction (Eq. 8), with full derivation in App. A. **Correct, elegant, verified algebraically.**
2. Beta entropy formula (Eq. 11), with derivation in App. B. **Standard textbook, correct.**
3. Asymptotic MI scaling (Prop. 5.1, $\mathcal{O}(1/n)$), with derivation in App. C. **Result correct (verified by independent derivation), but App. C's proof uses informal Gaussian-approximation + concentration steps that would not pass strict review.**

The methodological core of the paper — the WMI acquisition score $A(\tau) = w(\bar\phi_\tau) \cdot I(R_{1:K}; \Phi_\tau)$ (Eq. 16) — is **not derived from any optimality principle**. The weight $w(\bar\phi) = \bar\phi(1-\bar\phi) \exp(-\eta(\bar\phi-\mu)^2)$ is a heuristic combining a "high variance filter" with a "curriculum bias." There is no theorem showing that maximizing WMI is optimal for any RL training objective.

Importantly, the acquisition score's relationship to the derived variance reduction $\Delta V$ is opaque. From the paper's own derivations, $\Delta V \propto \bar\phi(1-\bar\phi)/(n+1)^2$ while WMI $\propto \bar\phi(1-\bar\phi) \exp(-\eta(\bar\phi-\mu)^2)/[2(n+1)]$. The factor $(n+1)/2$ between them means WMI is *more* lenient toward high-evidence datapoints than $\Delta V$, but no justification is given for this choice over $w \cdot \Delta V$.

**Deeper theoretical concern: model misspecification under non-stationary policy.** The Beta-Bernoulli framework assumes reward outcomes are exchangeable Bernoulli draws conditioned on a *fixed* latent $\phi_\tau$. In RLVR, the policy $\pi_{\theta_t}$ is updated every step, so $\phi_\tau$ is in fact policy-dependent and changes during training. Eq. 6 has discounting parameter $\lambda$ to handle non-stationarity — but App. D.2 sets $\lambda = 1.0$, i.e., **the non-stationarity correction is OFF in all reported experiments**. With $\lambda = 1$, $(\alpha^t, \beta^t)$ are simple count accumulators across heterogeneous policies, not parameters of a posterior over a well-defined latent. Under the actual update rule, the "epistemic uncertainty" the algorithm claims to be reducing has no fixed referent.

**Score: 5.** Theoretical content is correct but elementary; load-bearing Prop. 5.1 has informal proof; methodological design (WMI weighting) lacks theoretical justification; the framework's central conjugacy assumption is violated by the actual experimental setup ($\lambda=1$ ignores policy non-stationarity).

**Confidence: 4.** Read full paper including all appendices A, B, C, D. ## Comment angle

Lead with the model-specification concern: the Beta-Bernoulli framework assumes fixed latent $\phi_\tau$, but the experimental setup ($\lambda = 1.0$) accumulates rewards across heterogeneous policies, so the closed-form variance reduction (Eq. 8) and asymptotic MI scaling (Prop. 5.1) lose their Bayesian semantics in practice. Frame as a question: are the theoretical guarantees specific to the stationary-$\phi$ regime, and does WMI have any optimality property under the non-stationary discounted-update setting actually used? Acknowledge that App. A's algebra is correct and elegant, and that Prop. 5.1's $\mathcal{O}(1/n)$ scaling can be derived more transparently via binary-entropy Taylor expansion.
