## Theorem inventory

- **Proposition C.1 (appendix p.16, Unbiased estimation of the uniform-descendant objective).** For a rooted tree under node q, defining µ(q) = (1/|L(q)|) Σ_ℓ z_ℓ over leaves L(q), and µ(c) = (1/|L(c)|) Σ_{ℓ∈L(c)} z_ℓ for each child c ∈ C(q): µ(q) = Σ_{c∈C(q)} (|L(c)|/|L(q)|) µ(c). The unique unbiased combination of child means uses subtree-size weights.
- **Proposition C.2 (appendix p.16, Minimum-variance weighting under simple noise).** Same setup; assume z_ℓ = µ_ℓ + ε_ℓ with i.i.d. zero-mean noise, common variance σ². Among unbiased estimators µ̂(q) = Σ α_c µ̂(c) with Σ α_c = 1, the choice α_c = |L(c)|/|L(q)| uniquely minimizes Var[µ̂(q)].

## Assumption inventory

- **Prop C.1:** lineage structure is a rooted tree; z_ℓ ∈ R is well-defined per leaf.
- **Prop C.2:** independent leaf noise, zero mean, common variance σ². Unbiasedness of µ̂(q) of the linear-combination form Σ α_c µ̂(c).

## Appendix proof cross-check

- **Prop C.1 proof (p.16).** Each leaf belongs to exactly one child subtree; partitioning Σ_ℓ z_ℓ across children gives Σ_c |L(c)| µ(c). Dividing by |L(q)| = Σ_c |L(c)| yields the decomposition. **Trivially correct** — pure algebraic rearrangement.
- **Prop C.2 proof (p.16).** Variance computation: Var[Σ α_c µ̂(c)] = Σ α_c² σ²/|L(c)|. Lagrangian minimization of Σ α_c²/|L(c)| subject to Σ α_c = 1 gives α_c ∝ |L(c)|. **Textbook minimum-variance unbiased estimator argument** — standard result in any introductory statistics text (e.g., weighted mean of independent observations with heteroscedastic variances).
