## Assumption inventory

- **Assumption 4.1 (p.4, line 230)**: "For prompts x_0 ∼ D it holds with probability 1 that V(f*(x_0)) = 1."
- **Assumption 4.2 (p.4, line 237)**: "For any j ≠ j' and input x, it holds that σ_j(x) ≠ σ_{j'}(x)." Paper claims this is "always possible to satisfy [...] by expanding the vocabulary [...] V × [J] containing tuples of the form (v,j)" — but this artificial expansion is not how real LLMs work.
- **Assumption 5.3 (p.6, line 296)**: "Let θ^(0) be the initial policy parameters, and let Θ_+ := {θ : ∀s ∈ [S], P_θ(A_{s,τ(s)}) ≥ P_{θ^(0)}(A_{s,τ(s)})}. There exists a constant α > 0 such that for any step s ∈ [S], any task j ∈ [J], and any parameter vector θ ∈ Θ_+: ρ_{s,j}^θ < 1 if j ≠ τ(s), and ρ_{s,j}^θ ≥ 1+α if j = τ(s)."
- **Implicit assumption (orthonormal positional task embeddings, p.4 line 196)**: "We assume that the collection {h_{s,j}}_{s,j} is orthonormal, ensuring that task selection at one step does not interfere with task selection at other steps."
- **Implicit assumption (linear-in-θ fine-tuning regime, p.4 line 213)**: "f̃_θ(x) ≈ f̃_{θ^(0)}(x) + ∇_{θ^(0)} f̃_{θ^(0)}(x)·(θ − θ^(0))" — NTK-style linear approximation, citing Jacot et al., Lee et al., Allen-Zhu et al., Arora et al., Jang et al.

## Theory score rationale (anchored)

**Score: 5.** The proofs are technically correct under the stated assumptions, the contribution (task-advantage ratio characterization, O(S²/ε²) iteration complexity under uniform advantage, parity hardness, and a concrete failure case) is non-trivial, and the framing is honest about being a "behavioral abstraction". Theorems 5.2 and 5.4 are clean characterizations within their setting.

**What knocks it down:**

1. **Lemma A.3's prefix-independence consequence is a load-bearing simplification.** The proof shows π_t(σ_j(x_{s-1}) | x_{s-1}) does not depend on x_{s-1} — it depends only on ⟨θ, h_{s-1,j}⟩. Under the orthonormality + Assumption 4.2 setup, the policy's per-step task-selection probability is completely prefix-independent. This means the abstraction reduces autoregressive reasoning to memoryless per-step task selection (modulo step indexing); the only coupling across steps is via the multiplicative verifier V at the end of the rollout, not via any computational dependence between intermediate decisions. Real LLMs have rich prefix-dependent attention; the paper does not explicitly flag that its analysis discards this.

2. **No matching lower bound on iteration complexity.** Theorem 5.4 gives upper bound T = O(S²/(αε²)), and Prop 6.1 shows α = 1/2^{d−1} for parities, so T = O(2^{2(d−1)} S²/ε²) for parities. But there's no general lower bound saying T = Ω(S²/(αε²)) under uniform task-advantage. A COLT-style review would want this.

3. **Linear-in-θ fine-tuning regime (NTK).** The paper assumes f̃_θ ≈ f̃_{θ^(0)} + ∇f̃·(θ−θ^(0)). NTK results are asymptotic in width and don't describe finite-width RLVR. The paper cites NTK theory but doesn't argue applicability to RLVR.

4. **Assumption 5.3 (uniform task-advantage on Θ_+).** Requires α > 0 uniformly across all θ ∈ Θ_+. The paper provides examples where it holds (Prop 6.2) but no general theory of when this invariance holds during RLVR training.

**What it doesn't have:** broken proof, missing assumption, dimension mismatch, vacuous bound, or false novelty claim. The proofs check out.
