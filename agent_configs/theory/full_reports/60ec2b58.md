## Theorem inventory

**Theorem 3.1 (Value Approximation Error Bound)** [page 4, line 184]:
Let r : S → R be a reward function with bounded variation and r_k its reconstruction using the first k eigenvectors. Let v* and v_k* be their corresponding optimal value functions. Then,
  ||v* − v_k*||_∞ ≤ ||r||_G / ((1−γ) √λ_k),
where λ_k is the k-th eigenvalue.

Identical statement to 3.1; full proof provided.

**Lemma B.1 (Parseval's Theorem)** [page 12]:
Σ_i f(i)^2 = Σ_i f̂_i^2 (cited from Shuman et al. 2016 — well-known).

**Lemma B.3 (Reconstruction Bound)** [page 13]:
For any signal f with bounded variation, the squared reconstruction error using the first k eigenvectors satisfies
  error(f, k) = Σ_{i>k} f̂_i^2 ≤ ||f||_G^2 · λ_k^{-1}.
Cited as Zhu & Rabbat (2012) — standard graph signal processing result.

**Definition B.2 (Graph Total Variation)** [page 13, Eqn 9]:

## Appendix proofs cross-check

Proof of Theorem B.4 in Appendix B.2 (pp. 14–15). Two-step structure:

**Step 1 (Eqns 14–16):** Bound ℓ_∞ reward approximation error.
- ||r − r_k||_2^2 = Σ_{i>k} r̂_i^2 by Parseval (Lemma B.1).
- ≤ ||r||_G^2 / λ_k by Lemma B.3.
- ||r − r_k||_∞ ≤ ||r − r_k||_2 ≤ ||r||_G / √λ_k =: ξ.

**Step 2 (Eqns 17–23):** Propagate via Bellman contraction.
- v*(s) − v_k*(s) ≤ max_a Σ p(s'|s,a)[(r(s') − r_k(s')) + γ(v*(s') − v_k*(s'))].
- Take supremum, use max_a fag − max_a g ≤ max_a (f − g):
  ||v* − v_k*||_∞ ≤ ||r − r_k||_∞ + γ ||v* − v_k*||_∞.
- Rearrange: (1 − γ) ||v* − v_k*||_∞ ≤ ||r − r_k||_∞.
- Substitute Step 1 (Eqn 24): ||v* − v_k*||_∞ ≤ ||r||_G / ((1 − γ)√λ_k).

The proof is clean. No identifiable error in any step. Eqn 23 (using ||r − r_k||_∞ directly) is tighter; Eqn 24 (using the spectral form) is the "looser but more direct" interpretation per the paper's own remark.

## Theory score rationale

**Correctness.** Theorem 3.1 is correct. The proof is a clean composition of two textbook results:
- Step 1: graph spectral approximation bound from Zhu & Rabbat (2012), cited as Lemma B.3.
- Step 2: standard reward-error → value-error propagation via Bellman contraction (textbook, e.g. Bertsekas, Puterman).
No proof step is broken; no assumption is hidden or missing.

**Novelty.** Modest. Both components are well-known. The "novel" element of Theorem 3.1 is the application of graph signal processing bounds to MDP value approximation — but the theorem itself introduces neither a new proof technique nor a new bound. The contribution to the literature is the *method* (LK), and Theorem 3.1 is interpretable motivation rather than a stand-alone theoretical contribution.

**Tightness / scope.** The bound has admitted looseness:
1. The ||·||_∞ ≤ ||·||_2 step in Eqn 16 loses up to √|S|. For large state spaces this can dominate.
2. The smoothness measure ||r||_G is computed w.r.t. P_π_data (the data-collecting policy). The bound's tightness therefore depends implicitly on π_data, but this dependence is not formally analyzed. A reward that is "smooth" under uniform random π_data may not be smooth under a more concentrated policy, so the bound's geometry shifts with the offline dataset choice — a real subtlety for the LK pipeline since DMC experiments use APS/Proto/RND datasets.
3. No matching lower bound is provided. The paper does not characterize tightness regimes.

**Assumption transparency.** The "bounded variation" condition (Eqn 10) is mild and discussed. The reversible-MDP restriction is stated upfront. The implicit dependence of L (and hence λ_k and ||r||_G) on π_data is acknowledged in §4.1 but its quantitative consequences for the bound are not addressed.

**Score: 5.** Weak but defensible: proof technically correct, novelty marginal (composition of known results), loose constants, and implicit π_data-dependence in the smoothness measure that is not formally analyzed.
