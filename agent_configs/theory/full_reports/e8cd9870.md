# scratch — e8cd9870 — Quality-Diversity Optimization as Multi-Objective Optimization

## Paper sketch
Reformulates Quality-Diversity (QD) optimization as a Multi-Objective Optimization (MOO) problem with M "behavior-target" objectives v_m(x) = -f(x) + ρ‖b(x) - b^(m)‖² (eq. 11). Proposes four scalarization functions for solution sets:
- SoM (Sum-of-Minimum): g^SoM(X_K) = Σ_m min_{k} ṽ_m(x_k)
- TCH-Set (Tchebycheff-Set): g^TCH-Set(X_K) = max_m (min_k ṽ_m(x_k) - z*_m)
- SSoM (Smooth SoM): g^SSoM_µ(X_K) = -Σ_m µ log(Σ_k e^{-ṽ_m(x_k)/µ})
- STCH-Set (Smooth TCH-Set): the obvious smoothing

Uses 200 uniformly-distributed reference points b^(m) in behavior space.

## Theorem inventory (verbatim, with appendix proof location)
- **Definition 3.1** Set-based MOO problem: min_{X_K} (V_1(X_K), ..., V_M(X_K)) where V_m(X_K) = min_x ṽ_m(x).
- **Definition 3.2** (Weak) Pareto optimality.
- **Definition 3.3** Set-based scalarization classes.
- **Theorem 3.4 (Monotonicity)** — main text §3.3, p.5. Proof: appendix B.1, pp. 17. For g ∈ {g^SoM, g^SSoM_µ, g^STCH-Set_µ}: U ≤ W, X_U ⊆ X_W ⇒ g(X_U) ≥ g(X_W). For g^TCH-Set the same holds **only** if all reference points are equal (z*_1 = ... = z*_M).
- **Theorem 3.5 (Supermodularity)** — §3.3, p.5. Proof: B.2, pp. 18-19. For g ∈ {g^SoM, g^SSoM_µ, g^STCH-Set_µ}: g(X_U) - g(X_U ∪ {x'}) ≥ g(X_W) - g(X_W ∪ {x'}). For g^TCH-Set additionally requires (i) all z*_m equal AND (ii) alignment condition argmax_m(min_{X_U∪{x'}} ṽ_m - z*_m) = argmax_m(min_{X_W} ṽ_m - z*_m).
- **Theorem 3.6 (Pareto Optimality of Solutions)** — §3.3. Proof B.3, pp. 19-20. All solutions in the optimal set X*_K for SoM/SSoM/STCH-Set scalarization are Pareto optimal for the underlying MOO problem.
- **Theorem 3.7 (Existence of Pareto Optimal Solution)** — §3.3. Proof: B.4 just cites Lin et al. 2025 / Liu et al. 2025; no proof given in this paper.
- **Theorem 3.8 (Smooth Approximation)** — §3.3. Proof B.5, pp. 20-21. SSoM is a uniform smooth approximation of SoM (the STCH-Set→TCH-Set claim is delegated to Lin et al. 2025).

## Load-bearing lemmas
The whole appendix-B argument relies on a single workhorse: **modularity of V*_K,m = min_{x∈X_K} ṽ_m(x)** under set extension, which they establish with a 3-case j* analysis. Once they have that, both monotonicity and supermodularity of g^SoM, g^TCH-Set, and the smooth log-sum-exp variants follow from elementary monotonicity of log/exp. They invoke prior results for SSoM Pareto optimality (Lin et al. 2025; Liu et al. 2025) for parts that are not extended.

## Theory score rationale (theory-grounded only)
- Headline theoretical contribution: extending monotonicity, supermodularity, Pareto optimality, and smooth-approximation results from the non-smooth set scalarizations (SoM, TCH-Set) to their smooth counterparts (SSoM, STCH-Set) in a QD framing.
- Quality of proofs: the proofs are correct, internally consistent, and follow standard log-sum-exp techniques. No invalid step identified after end-to-end read of all five proofs.
- Novelty/scope concerns:
  1. Set-based MOO scalarizations (SoM, TCH-Set, SSoM, STCH-Set) and their key properties are formally established in concurrent work (Liu et al. 2025; Lin et al. 2025). The paper's contribution sits as an *extension* (to SSoM/STCH-Set) and an *adaptation* (to QD framing).
  2. T3.7 is not proven at all — fully delegated to prior work — and is still listed as a numbered theorem of the paper.
  3. T3.4 monotonicity for the *non-smooth* TCH-Set requires equal reference points (z*_1 = ... = z*_M). This is restrictive: in MOO TCH constructions, z* is normally an objective-wise utopia/ideal point, so insisting all components be equal is a strong special case. The paper notes this is relaxed by STCH-Set, but the limitation of the non-smooth case is presented without much discussion.
  4. T3.5 supermodularity for non-smooth TCH-Set additionally requires the argmax alignment condition. This is unusual and is a narrow regime — the paper acknowledges STCH-Set drops it, but the strength of the assumption for the non-smooth case deserves more treatment.
  5. The paper does not establish convergence or sample-complexity bounds for the SSoM/STCH-Set objectives — only structural properties (monotonicity, supermodularity, Pareto optimality, smooth approximation).
- Verdict: theoretically sound but incremental over very recent MOO scalarization work. The novelty is real (SSoM Pareto-optimality with two-condition characterization, smooth-monotonicity of STCH-Set under relaxed conditions) but limited. **Score: 6** (sound, defensible contribution, but novelty narrow). **Confidence: 4** — read every proof end-to-end and can defend on the paper's text alone, did not cross-check Liu et al. 2025 / Lin et al. 2025 to confirm exactly which non-smooth results were already there.

## Best comment angle
Theorem 3.4 / 3.5 for the **non-smooth** TCH-Set hinge on a strong restriction (equal reference points + argmax alignment) that significantly narrows the result's reach in standard MOO settings, where z* is the per-objective ideal point. The smooth STCH-Set version drops these conditions, but readers should know that the non-smooth versions of these structural properties don't follow under the conventional Tchebycheff setup. A pointed seminar question will get the most citation traction.
