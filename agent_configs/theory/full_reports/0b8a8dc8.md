# scratch — 0b8a8dc8 — ASBM (Adjoint Schrödinger Bridge Matching)

## Paper sketch
A two-stage generative-modeling framework based on Schrödinger Bridges with non-memoryless base SDEs. Stage 1: optimal coupling construction via SOC (data-to-energy view, alternates Adjoint Matching + Corrector Matching). Stage 2: backward bridge matching under the learned coupling. Plus a distillation procedure to a one-step generator. Empirical: image generation (CIFAR-10, ImageNet, FFHQ).

## Theorem inventory (verbatim, with proof location)
- **Proposition 3.1** (main text, p.3): "If the base path measure p_base is memoryless, then the optimal path measure p* of the SB problem is also memoryless: p*(X0,X1) = p_data(X0) p_prior(X1)." Proof: Appendix A, ~10 lines.
- **Theorem A.1 (SOC optimality)** (Appendix A, p.12): the optimal control u*_t(x) = -σ_t ∇V_t(x) where V_t(x) = -log E[exp(-g(X1)) | X_t = x], and the joint-distribution formula p*(X0,X1) = p_base(X0,X1) exp(-g(X1) + V_0(X0)). **No proof given** — cited to Bellman 1954 / HJB.
- **Theorem B.1 (Optimal control in SB problem)** (Appendix B, p.13): characterizes u*_t, v*_t via SB potentials φ_t, φ̂_t. **No proof given** — cited to Pavon & Wakolbinger 1991, Chen et al. 2021c, Caluya & Halder 2021.
- Distillation derivation in Appendix C (eq 42–45) uses data-processing inequality + Girsanov; standard, no formal theorem statement.

## Assumption inventory
- p_base path measure has well-defined marginals; X_0 ~ p_data initial constraint (eq 22).
- For Proposition 3.1: memoryless condition (eq 8 / 25): p_base(X0, X1) = p_base(X0) p_base(X1).
- For SOC↔SB equivalence (implicit in Prop 3.1's claim p*(X1) = p_prior): terminal cost is chosen such that the SOC optimum's terminal marginal matches p_prior. The specific choice g(x) = log(p_base_1(x)/p_prior(x)) (eq 31) is given only in Appendix B.

## Load-bearing lemma
The single load-bearing identity is the SOC optimal joint distribution (eq 24): p*(X0,X1) = p_base(X0,X1) exp(-g(X1) + V_0(X0)). Everything in Proposition 3.1's proof reduces to plugging the memoryless factorization p_base(X0,X1) = p_base(X0)p_base(X1) into this.

## Appendix proofs cross-check
- Theorem A.1: not proved in paper. The HJB derivation is classical for the optimal control u*_t = -σ_t ∇V_t. The specific joint distribution formula eq (24) is a Girsanov-type Radon-Nikodym derivative against the base measure. No source cited specifically for the joint-distribution formula; it is standard in stochastic optimal control under reference-measure formulations.
- Proposition 3.1 proof:
  - Memoryless ⇒ V_0(X0) = -log ∫ p_base(X1) exp(-g(X1)) dX1 = constant in X0. ✓
  - Substituting into eq (24): p*(X0,X1) = p_base(X0) p_base(X1) exp(-g(X1)) · const. ✓
  - Factorization ⇒ X_0 ⊥ X_1 under p*. ✓
  - For p*(X1) = p_prior, need g such that p_base(X1) exp(-g(X1)) ∝ p_prior(X1), i.e., eq 31. The proposition statement claims p*(X1) = p_prior but this part of the conclusion requires the specific terminal cost from Appendix B. The argument as stated in the main-text proof shows independence (factorization), and the marginal identification goes through under the standard terminal-cost choice.
- Theorem B.1: not proved in paper, fully cited to prior work.
- Distillation (eq 42–44): data-processing inequality D_KL(p^ξ_0 || p^φ_0) ≤ D_KL(p^ξ || p^φ), then Girsanov turns the path-space KL into drift-matching. Standard; no bug.

## Theory score rationale (theory-grounded only)
- Headline theoretical claim: Proposition 3.1, "memoryless base ⇒ independent optimal SB coupling".
- Quality of the one proof in the paper: correct under the natural reading. Algebra checks out, factorization is clean, the marginal-identification step implicitly uses the standard SOC↔SB terminal-cost equivalence.
- Other theorems (A.1, B.1) are recapped from prior work without proofs. The distillation derivation is standard data-processing-inequality + Girsanov.
- Scope of theoretical contribution: a single observation that motivates the methodological design ("memoryless = independent = uninformative coupling, hence diffusion is a wasteful special case of SB"). The observation is a corollary of standard SOC machinery, not a new technical insight.
- Novelty issues:
  1. The "diffusion = special case of SB with memoryless base" framing is well-known in the SB literature (Bortoli et al. 2021, Chen et al. 2022, Liu et al. 2023). The independence corollary is a clean restatement but not previously absent from the literature in spirit.
  2. The proof relies entirely on a delegated identity (eq 24) which the paper does not prove.
- The proof of Proposition 3.1 itself is correct (verified algebraically). The argument is short and clean.

## Comment angle
The conclusion "p*(X1) = p_prior" in Proposition 3.1 implicitly requires the specific terminal-cost choice g(x) = log(p_base_1(x)/p_prior(x)) (eq 31), which is only introduced in Appendix B. The factorization-into-independence half of the proof goes through for any g, but the marginal identification (p*(X1) = p_prior) is only valid under the SOC↔SB equivalence terminal cost. A pointed question on this can clarify whether the proposition is intended to be a structural property of any SOC formulation with memoryless base, or specifically of the SOC formulation that recovers SB.
