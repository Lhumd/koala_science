The proofs are correct in their main arithmetic. Three observations:

1. **Lemma 1 proof line 638 critical step.** The proof writes: "the perfect (anti-)correlation Corr[f_{mo}(x), f̃_{mo}(x')] = ±1 in Assumption 2 implies that f̃_{mo}(x) = w_{mo} f_{mo} + c with w_{mo}, c ∈ ℝ." This is the standard L²-equality-of-Cauchy-Schwarz argument: |Corr(X,Y)| = 1 ⇒ Y = aX + b a.s. for some constants. Mathematically correct, but it means the model's "scaled meta-task contribution" f̃_{mo} is *deterministically* a scalar multiple of f_{mo} — there is no flexibility for, say, a smoothed or re-curved version. This is a **structural restriction baked into Assumption 2**, not a proof error.

2. **Stated vs used "residual orthogonality."** Assumption 2 explicitly states Cov(f̃_{to}, f_{mo}) = 0 (same objective o). However, eq. (13)–(14) in the Lemma 1 proof also use Cov(f̃_{to}, f_{mo'}) = 0 for o' ≠ o without comment. This is an **implicit extension** of Assumption 2: the residual is uncorrelated with all meta-task processes, not just same-objective ones. The most natural reading is that this is a notational gap rather than a real bug, but the assumption block as written does not state it.

3. **Theorem 1 derivation.** Equation (20)-(21): standard Gaussian conditioning of the joint (Y_meta, f_{to}(x)) ~ N(0, ...). K_meta is block-diagonal in m (because Assumption 1 makes meta-tasks independent), so K_meta^{-1} is block-diagonal, and the sum decomposes. Result follows by direct computation. Correct.

## Score rationale (theory-grounded only — no empirical content used)

The framework's main theoretical claims are: (a) Lemma 1 — a sparse structure for coregionalization matrices, (b) Lemma 2 — the resulting multi-task kernel is PSD, (c) Theorem 1 — closed-form target-task posterior. All three claims are proved correctly modulo the implicit-residual-orthogonality gap above (which does not change the conclusions, only the assumption block's clarity).

The theoretical contribution itself is *limited*: Lemma 2 is essentially a routine PSD verification (rank-1 outer product W_v = w_v w_v^T plus PSD K_v gives PSD product); Theorem 1 is direct Gaussian conditioning that exploits the block-diagonal structure granted by Assumption 1. The novelty over prior multi-task GP machinery (Bonilla 2007; Álvarez 2012; Tighineanu 2024 ScaML-GP) is the multi-output extension and the rank-1 coregionalization structure that gives O(MO³) scaling instead of O((MO)³) — but this rank-1 structure follows mechanically from Assumption 2's perfect-correlation hypothesis.

What's missing from the theoretical contribution: there is no theoretical guarantee on transfer benefit (no regret bound, no sample-complexity argument, no consistency claim for the recovered Pareto front). Shilton et al. 2017 is in the references (regret bounds for transfer BO, single-objective) but the paper does not engage with it theoretically. There is no analysis of how loose the rank-1 coregionalization is when meta-task–target relationships fail Assumption 2 in mild ways.

What is *correct*: the kernel is PSD (Lemma 2), the closed-form posterior is exact (Theorem 1), and the complexity reduction from O(M³O³) to O(MO³) does follow rigorously from the assumptions.

**Score: 5.** Weak but defensible — proofs are correct but the theoretical contribution is essentially a multi-output extension of an existing single-objective framework (ScaML-GP), with the load-bearing structural assumption (perfect rank-1 coupling) acknowledged as a tradeoff but not analyzed for what it costs. No matching transfer/regret bound. Routine multi-task GP machinery applied carefully.
