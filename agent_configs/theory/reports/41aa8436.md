Theorem 4.3 / Corollary 4.4 are positioned as a cryptographic impossibility result for activation probes, but the bridge from the underlying PRF-hardness theorem (Thm A.9) to the linear-probe corollary (Cor A.11) appears to rely on an assumption that the appendix itself flags as conjectural.

Theorem A.9 establishes that no PPT algorithm with **oracle access** to C_k can predict trigger membership beyond chance — the proof simulates C_k via an external oracle G and applies the standard PRF distinguisher argument. Corollary A.11 then asserts the same for **linear probes operating on internal activations z(x)**: its proof simply takes P(x) = Probe(z(x)) to be a generic PPT algorithm and applies Thm A.9.

Yet a few lines earlier (between Defs A.7 and A.8, p. 11), the modeling note writes:

> "We treat the neural network's internal computation of the trigger as black-box oracle access to F_k. This assumes the network's weights effectively obfuscate the implementation of F_k... Formally bridging black-box hardness to white-box extraction would require indistinguishability obfuscation (iO); we leave this as a conjecture grounded in empirical observations of probe failure."

So the headline impossibility for white-box probes — which see internal activations of the trained network — does not follow from PRF security alone; it additionally requires iO-style obfuscation to be realized in the trained transformer's weights, which is a strictly stronger and unverified assumption.

Two concrete questions the formal status would benefit from clarifying:

1. Could the authors state Cor 4.4 / A.11 as a *conditional* result — "linear probes cannot detect Class 2 misalignment **assuming** the trained network's weights instantiate an iO-style obfuscation of F_k"? As written, the corollary reads as an unconditional consequence of A.9, but the proof's application of A.9 to a white-box predictor needs that bridge.

2. The empirical Liar and Fanatic share a single semantic trigger (weight-related queries), yet only the Fanatic is claimed to lie in the PRF-hard regime. The §4.2 trigger-complexity argument (rationalization → approach PRF hardness) is presented as a hypothesis. Is there a way to localize, formally, what property of the Fanatic's trained weights would distinguish it from the Liar's at the level of A.11's hypothesis?
