# Sign Lock-In: Theory–Novelty–Construct-Validity Audit

**Bottom line:** The stopping-time formalism (Theorem 3.6) is genuinely novel within stochastic-approximation-applied-to-deep-learning, but four independent gaps — across all four ICML rubric dimensions — break the deployment-relevant claims. (i) Proposition D.10's sufficient condition for Assumption 3.4 fails structurally for AdamW; (ii) Proposition D.3's deployment bridge is vacuous on ≈27 % of Gaussian-init coordinates; (iii) the empirical "discovery" reproduces Zhou et al. (NeurIPS 2019) and Gadhikar & Burkholz (ICLR 2024) under the lazy-training regime the paper sets up; (iv) the aggregate `(ĥ, ĝ)` statistic averages over a 20× parameter-class spread, the billion-scale sweep is `~4 × 10⁶×` under-trained, and no end-to-end bpw × accuracy experiment closes the title's compression claim.

---

## 1. Theory: Prop. D.10's SGD-only sufficient condition fails for AdamW (Soundness)

The "schedule-aware SGD-based" sufficient condition for Assumption 3.4 (re-entry bound `g_T`) is **Proposition D.10** (App. D.3, l. 1184), whose proof chain (Lemma D.6 → D.7 → D.8 + D.9 → D.10) requires Assumption D.5(iii): `E[g_t | F_t] = ∇L(v_t)` with bounded conditional variance `E[‖g_t − ∇L(v_t)‖² | F_t] ≤ ξ²`. AdamW's update `g_t^Adam = m̂_t / (√v̂_t + ε)` violates this in two independent ways:

- **Numerator (momentum) is biased:** `m̂_t = m_t / (1 − β_1^t)` is an EMA; `E[m_t | F_t] = β_1 m_{t−1} + (1 − β_1) ∇L(v_t) ≠ ∇L(v_t)` for any `β_1 > 0`. Bias-correction restores `E[m̂_t]` over the full history, **not conditional on `F_t`**.
- **Denominator (second-moment normalization) breaks variance bound:** `1/√v̂_t` has heavy tails during warmup and on rare-token gradients; Lemma D.8's Cauchy-Schwarz step (l. 1300–1311) uses `E[‖g_t‖² | F_t] ≤ ‖∇L(v_t)‖² + ξ²`, which has no finite `ξ²` under heavy-tailed `1/√v̂_t`.

Remark D.11 ("similar arguments apply to SGD-family optimizers such as Adam," l. 1003) is asserted, not proved. **A revised theorem must either restrict the empirical claims to SGD or supply Prop. D.10's analog under the actual AdamW recursion (Eqs. 10–11), with `δ_upd^Adam` written down for the App. C.5 schedule.**

## 2. Theory: Proposition D.3's deployment bridge is vacuous on the at-risk subpopulation (Soundness)

Prop. D.3(B) bounds the deployment-relevant `E[p_T^init] ≤ h̄_T + δ_upd` where `h̄_T := (1/N) Σ_i P[τ_{Δ,i} ≤ T]`. For any coordinate with `|w_0(i)| ≤ Δ` at t=0, `τ_{Δ,i} = 0` and `P[τ_{Δ,i} ≤ T] = 1` deterministically.

With `σ_init = 0.02` (App. C.4, l. 741) and `Δ ≥ η_max G` set to bound the SGD step (Prop. D.10 remark, l. 1232–1235), Δ is comparable to `σ_init/3` for `η_max ≈ 10⁻³` and gradient-norm clip `G = O(1)`. Under Gaussian init, `P[|w_0| ≤ Δ] = erf(Δ/(σ_init √2)) ≈ 0.27`. **The bound `h̄_T + δ_upd` collapses to a trivial constant ≥ 0.27 on roughly 27 % of weights — precisely the at-risk subpopulation.** Theorem 3.6 is informative only on the complementary 73 %, where signs rarely flip under any reasonable analysis.

## 3. Originality: Zhou (2019) + Gadhikar (2024) + lazy-training trivialization (Originality)

**Two canonical priors are missing from the main body:**

- *Zhou, Lan, Liu, Yosinski. "Deconstructing Lottery Tickets: Zeros, Signs, and the Supermask." NeurIPS 2019.* Replacing winning-ticket weights with `sign(w_0) × c` recovers near-original accuracy — direct operationalization of "signs are what training preserves."
- *Gadhikar & Burkholz. "Masks, Signs, and Learning Rate Rewinding." ICLR 2024.* Empirically establishes sign persistence under SGD in lottery tickets.

App. B (l. 562–570) gestures at lottery tickets only via NTK / lazy-training references and never engages with the sign-only ticket result. With these named, Contribution #1 ("Empirical discovery," l. 65–73) becomes **multi-architecture confirmation** of a known phenomenon.

**Lazy-training trivializes Theorem 3.6 quantitatively.** `σ_init = 0.02`, `ρ = 10⁻³` ⇒ outer-to-outer flips require traversing a band 20× narrower than typical `|w_0|`. NTK / lazy-training results (Chizat & Bach 2018; Du et al. 2018; Jacot et al. 2018) bound cumulative parameter movement at `O(1/√width)`. For `d_model ∈ [512, 4096]` (Table 1, p. 17), this is `[0.016, 0.044]` — comparable to `σ_init` but rarely traversing `2ρ = 2 × 10⁻³`. Theorem 3.6 is then a quantitative restatement of NTK-scale stability under the paper's own initialization conventions.

## 4. Soundness: KS test is at the marginal-rejection threshold, not "indistinguishable" (Presentation/Soundness)

Fig. 2(b) reports `D = 0.077` (TinyLlama-1.1B), **`0.123` (ResNet18)**, `0.049` (MLP-Mixer-B16). Two-sample KS critical value with `s = 256` (App. C.3, l. 684):

- α = 0.01: `D_crit ≈ √(− ln(α/2) / s) ≈ 0.144`
- α = 0.05: `D_crit ≈ 0.120`

**`D = 0.123` for ResNet18 *rejects* the i.i.d.-Rademacher null at α = 0.05 and is barely under the threshold at α = 0.01.** The Abstract phrasing "spectrally indistinguishable from an i.i.d. Rademacher baseline" overstates the test result; the correct rendering is "consistent with, not strong evidence for."

## 5. Significance: Sign lock-in is parameter-class-stratified by 20× — aggregate `(ĥ, ĝ)` masks the bulk-population behavior (Significance)

Fig. 2(c) plateau values at step 5000:

| Parameter class | Flip rate `p` | XOR mask entropy `H(p)` (bits/param) |
|---|---|---|
| Output LM head, output embedding | ≤ 0.01 | ≤ 0.081 |
| Input token embedding | ~ 0.08 | ~ 0.402 |
| Transformer blocks 1–12 (the bulk) | 0.18 – 0.22 | **0.680 – 0.760** |
| Aggregate (paper's reported `flip_mean`) | ~ 0.10 | 0.469 |

The transformer blocks dominate every model in the scale sweep, and they sit at `H ≈ 0.72` bits/parameter — *higher* than the aggregate `H = 0.469`. **The "deep lock-in regime" framing in the Conclusion (l. 904–905) is a property of the aggregate, not of the bulk-parameter population that any compression budget actually depends on.** A revised analysis must report `(ĥ_l, ĝ_l)` per parameter class.

## 6. Significance: Billion-scale validation is `~4 × 10⁶×` under-trained (Significance)

App. C.5 (l. 815–818, l. 866) for the Fig. 4 / Table 1 sweep: data = Tiny Shakespeare, sequence length 64, **batch size 1**, **T = 1000 optimizer steps**, AdamW at constant `η = 3 × 10⁻⁴`, no warmup. Token budget: `1000 × 1 × 64 = 64 000`.

For 12.9B params, **Chinchilla-optimal token budget (Hoffmann et al. 2022) ≈ 20 × params ≈ 2.6 × 10¹¹ tokens** ⇒ the sweep runs at `2.5 × 10⁻⁷` of optimal, **`~4 × 10⁶×` under-trained**. The reported `(ĥ, ĝ) → 0` with parameter count is then most parsimoniously *"larger models barely move from initialization at this token budget."*

## 7. Significance: The mechanism→compression chain is never closed end-to-end (Significance)

The paper's logical arc — signs persistent → lock-in theory predicts this → gap-init + log-barrier reduces flip rate → sub-bit compression enabled — leaves step (iv) **asserted but not run**. No experiment reports bpw × downstream accuracy. Fig. 5's perplexity-vs-flip-rate is on TinyCharLM (`d_model = 128`, 2 layers, 0.4M params; App. C.4 l. 738) — perplexity *of the unquantized model with regularization*, not a compression cost. The "1-point increase in perplexity" Abstract headline is a regularization side-effect on a 0.4M-param char-LM.

The PRNG-seed + XOR Pareto baseline is unevaluated: at `p = 0.10`, `H(p) ≈ 0.469` bits/param at zero PPL cost. **For the regularizer to be Pareto-improving, Fig. 5's curve must dominate `(0.469 bits, 0 PPL)`** — comparison absent. From-scratch ternary/binary networks (BitNet b1.58, Ma et al. 2024; OneBit, Xu et al. 2024) bypass the storage-of-S construction the paper labels the "wall," so the Conclusion's universality claim (l. 425–426) is false against contemporary literature.

## Asks for the rebuttal

1. Prove or cite the AdamW analog of Prop. D.10 with the unbiasedness/variance-bound substitutes spelled out for the App. C.5 schedule, with `δ_upd^Adam` populated.
2. Report the empirical fraction of coordinates with `|w_0| ≤ Δ` at t=0; bound `P[s_T ≠ s_0]` directly conditional on `|w_0| > Δ`.
3. Bootstrap `(ĥ, ĝ)` and likelihood-ratio test the geometric null against an exponential-with-cutoff alternative.
4. Cite Zhou (2019) and Gadhikar (2024) in the main body and re-frame Contribution #1 as cross-architecture confirmation + first formal stopping-time treatment.
5. Rephrase the KS finding as "consistent with, not strong evidence for, an i.i.d. Rademacher null" and disclose the α=0.05 rejection for ResNet18.
6. Report `(ĥ_l, ĝ_l)` per parameter class (LM head, embeddings, blocks) for at least the Fig. 4 scale sweep, with `H(p_l)` per class.
7. Re-run the scale sweep with Chinchilla-aware token budgets, or restrict the scaling claim's scope to the actual training regime studied.
8. Run the closing compression experiment: bpw × downstream accuracy under the proposed scheme on transformer blocks, vs. BitNet b1.58 / OneBit / PRNG+XOR baselines.
