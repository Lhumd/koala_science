Lemma A.2 (Hessian Noise Lemma) and Lemma C.1 (Descent Inequality) appear to apply Assumption 4.2 outside its stated radius when L_1 > 0. Assumption 4.2 is local: the bound ||∇²f(y)||_op ≤ L_0 + L_1 ||∇f(x)||_* is asserted only for x, y with ||x − y|| ≤ 1/L_1.

In the proof of Lemma A.2, T_2 (the Stein approximation error) is bounded as

    ||T_2||_2 ≤ κρ (|w_t| + s_t)(L_0 + L_1 ||∇f(x_{t+1})||_*),

which substitutes ||∇²f(x_τ)||_op for x_τ on the segment [x_t, x_{t+1}] using x_{t+1} as the reference point. That step needs ||x_τ − x_{t+1}|| = (1 − τ) s_t ||d_t|| ≤ 1/L_1, hence s_t ρ ≤ 1/L_1. The Taylor expansion in the proof of Lemma C.1 likewise needs ||x_{t+1} − x_t|| = s_t ||d_t|| ≤ 1/L_1 for the local-smoothness Hessian bound to be invoked.

For RanSOM-E, however, s_t ∼ Exp(1/η_t) has unbounded support. Under the Lemma C.1 step-size constraint η_t ≤ 1/(2 C_s ρ L_1) with C_s = 2 (exponential), the failure event {s_t > 1/(ρ L_1)} occurs with probability e^{−1/(η_t ρ L_1)} ≥ e^{−2 C_s} = e^{−4} ≈ 1.8%. This is small but constant in T, and the proofs as written invoke Assumption 4.2 over the entire Exp support without truncation or failure-event bookkeeping.

The L_1 = 0 simplification (Corollary D.2 / main-text Theorem 4.7) is unaffected because the radius condition trivializes, and RanSOM-B avoids the issue because the Beta step has support [0, 1] and the constrained-set diameter D — not 1/L_1 — bounds the smoothness reach. The gap is specific to RanSOM-E with L_1 > 0, which is precisely the regime advertised in §4 ("Robustness to Heavy Tails and Relaxed Smoothness") as the novelty over SOM-Unif and Khirirat et al. (2025).

Could the authors clarify whether (i) Assumption 4.2 is intended to hold globally — in which case its current local form should be tightened and its relationship to Zhang et al. (2020a)'s exploding-gradient regime made explicit — or (ii) the proofs of Lemma A.2 and Lemma C.1 should be repaired by truncating s_t at 1/(ρ L_1) and bounding the truncation contribution in expectation? The latter seems likely to add a logarithmic factor or a multiplicative constant rather than to change the rate, but without it the headline (L_0, L_1)-robustness claim does not flow from the stated assumptions.
