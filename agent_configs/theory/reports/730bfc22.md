A clarification request on the per-transition gradient estimator that load-bears the Õ(ϵ⁻²) MLMC sample-complexity claim (Lemma 6.1 / Theorem 7.1).

In §6.1 the per-transition estimator is defined as

  ∇ξF(ξ, Z_t) = (1/(1−γ)) · ϕ(Z_t)/Pξ(s_{t+1}|s_t,a_t) · [rτ(s_t,a_t) **+** γ V̂^{π_{θ*}}_{Pξ,τ}(s_{t+1})],

consistent with the IS-form gradient derivation in the proof of Lemma 4.4 (Appendix B, Lemma B.8), which expands the bracket as `r(s,a) − τ log π_θ(a|s) + γ V^{π_θ}_{Pξ,τ}(s')` — a clean `rτ + γV` form.

In the proof of Lemma 6.1 (Appendix E.2, immediately after "Proof. We first define our estimator..."), the per-transition estimator is defined as

  ∇ξF(ξ, Z_t) = (1/(1−γ)) · ϕ(Z_t)/Pξ(s_{t+1}|s_t,a_t) · [rτ(s_t,a_t) **−** γ V̂^{π_{θ*}}_{Pξ,τ}(s_{t+1})],

with a minus sign in front of γ V̂. The same minus sign is then propagated through the variance bound (Eq. 158) and the median-of-means construction.

The bias decomposition that follows (Eqs. 147–152) takes the form `E[∇ξF(ξ,Z)] − ∇ξF(ξ) = (γ²/(1−γ)²) · E[(ϕ/Pξ) · (V − V̂)²]`, which is the right form only if both the estimator definition and the target gradient share the same sign convention on the γ V̂ term. Under the appendix's `−γV̂` definition, the expectation under the stationary distribution recovers a quantity that differs from the gradient formula in Eq. 20 / Lemma B.8 by an additive `2γ E[(ϕ/Pξ) V]/(1−γ)` term that does not vanish.

Could the authors confirm which sign convention is intended, and — under whichever definition is correct — verify that the bias step in Eqs. 147–152 still yields the stated `γ²C_ϕ²ϵ_v²/((1−γ)²p_min)` bound? The Õ(ϵ⁻²) advertised improvement over fixed-horizon estimators rests entirely on this bias-bound step, so resolving the sign would let the rest of the composition into Theorem 7.1 follow cleanly.
