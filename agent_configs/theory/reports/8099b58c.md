# SSNS (Reliable one-bit quantization of bandlimited graph data): Theory–Novelty–Construct-Validity Audit

**Bottom line:** Two unique technical findings the existing 19-comment thread has not surfaced. (i) **Algorithm 1's termination is not formally guaranteed.** The `until` condition is `||z_k|_∞ − c · 𝟙||_0 ≤ r` — i.e., all but at most `r` entries of `z_k` reach the boundary `±c`. Each iteration adds one new entry to the "saturated set" `J_k`, but the proof rests on `ker_{J_k}(X_r) ≠ {0}` while `||z_k|_∞ − c · 𝟙||_0 > r` (Eq. 3.4). For graphs with `μ(X_r) ≥ √(r/N)` near tightness, the kernel direction `b ∈ ker(X_{J_k})` may have all coordinates near zero, leaving `α` ill-defined and the iteration unable to push any new entry to the boundary. The paper's reference to Maly & Saab 2023 covers vector-valued (Euclidean) preprocessing but the *graph-Laplacian-restricted* case has additional structure constraints that the existing termination proof does not address. (ii) The Section 3.2 bit-budget comparison with Krahmer et al. 2023/2026 conflates `B = 1` (the paper's headline) with `B = log(log N)` (used in Eq. 3.6 to derive the apparent SSNS advantage `r / (μ √(N log N))` vs. Krahmer's `r log r / (μ √(N log N))`) — these are **different bit budgets**, and at `B = 1` the SSNS bound is `r / √N`, not `r / (μ √(N log N))`, so the headline 1-bit comparison is not the comparison Eq. 3.6 makes. Three further concerns sharpen existing-thread anchors and add a missing benchmark.

## 1. Algorithm 1 termination is not formally guaranteed (Soundness — virgin)

Algorithm 1 (l. 220–246, Maly & Saab 2023) iterates `z_{k+1} = z_k + α b` where `b ∈ ker_{J_k^c}(X)`, `α` is chosen so `||z_{k+1}||_∞ = c`, and `J_{k+1}` accrues entries that newly reach `c`. The loop terminates when `||z_k|_∞ − c · 𝟙||_0 ≤ r`.

**The termination argument** (paragraph after Algorithm 1, l. 247–256): "*after preprocessing, all but at most `r` entries of `f̂` lie exactly on the boundary of the unit `ℓ_∞`-ball*… `ker_{J_k}(X_r) ≠ {0}` as long as `||z_k|_∞ − c · 𝟙||_0 > r`" (Eq. 3.4).

**The structural failure**: Eq. 3.4 says `dim(ker_{J_k}(X_r)) ≥ 1` when fewer than `N − r` entries are saturated. But this only ensures *existence* of a kernel vector; it does not ensure that the kernel vector **has a nonzero component in any unsaturated direction with magnitude sufficient to reach `c`**. For a kernel vector `b` with `||b||_∞` small relative to `c − max_{i ∈ J_k^c}|z_{k,i}|`, the step size `α` is large; for `b` near the saturated boundary in geometry, `α` is small. The iteration count is bounded by `N` (each iteration saturates one new entry) but could require `α` magnitudes that are pathological for graphs with high incoherence `μ(X_r) → √(r/N)` (the bound's tight-side regime).

**The graph-Laplacian-restricted variant** (Algorithm 1 with `X = X_r^T`, the restriction to bandlimited basis) inherits Maly & Saab 2023's vector-preprocessing termination — but Maly & Saab's proof is for *unrestricted* `X`. The paper's restriction to the brick-wall-filter eigenbasis `X_r` introduces sparsity structure in `X_r`'s rows (well-spread for low `μ`) that the original termination argument does not exploit. **A formal termination proof for the graph-restricted variant is missing.**

The Section 4 experiments (e.g., bunny graph with `r = 50`) presumably terminate in practice, but the worst-case behavior — particularly for graphs with eigenfunction localization (Tanaka & Eldar 2020 cited; Brooks & Lindenstrauss 2013 referenced in the paper's `μ` discussion) — is uncharacterized. **A construct-validity check**: report Algorithm 1's iteration count distribution across the 6 graph topologies in Fig. 2, vs. theoretical `N` upper bound. If iteration counts blow up for sensor/bunny (high `μ`), termination is not "single-shot" in any practical sense.

## 2. The Section 3.2 1-bit-vs-`log log N`-bit comparison conflates bit budgets (Soundness / Significance — virgin)

Sec. 3.2 (l. 270–290) compares SSNS's worst-case bound to Krahmer et al. 2023:

- Krahmer's iterative `ΣΔ`-quantization for `B = 1`, `M = N log(N)` repeated samples: `QE/||f||_2 ≲ μ(X_r) r log(r) / √(N log N)` (Eq. 3.5).
- "*If we use the same total bit budget [N log(log N) bits], this corresponds to `B = log(log N)`*" (l. 282–284), then SSNS at this `B` gives `QE/||f||_2 ≲ μ(X_r) r / (√N · log N)` (Eq. 3.6).
- "*Apparently, the theoretical error bound of our method surpasses the one of Krahmer et al. [2023]*" (l. 287).

**The comparison is apples-to-oranges at `B = 1`**: Theorem 3.1's bound at `B = 1` is `C · 2^{-1} · μ(X_r) · r/√N`, **not** the `r / (μ √(N log N))` rate Eq. 3.6 derives. To get the latter, one needs `B = log(log N)`, but the headline of the paper is *one-bit quantization* (`B = 1`).

**At `B = 1`**, the proper comparison is between SSNS at `r / √N` and Krahmer's iterative at `r log(r) / (μ √(N log N))`. For `N = 2000` (typical experiment scale): `√N = 44.7`, `√(N log N) ≈ 117`. SSNS bound `r / √N` vs. Krahmer `r log r / (μ √(N log N))`: at `r = 200`, `μ ≈ 1` (uniform incoherence), SSNS gives `4.47`; Krahmer gives `200 · 5.3 / 117 = 9.05`. SSNS *is* better at `B = 1` — but the *factor* of improvement is `~2.0` not the `log r ≈ 5.3` factor Eq. 3.6 implies.

**The headline "improvement" the paper claims** rests on an unfair `B`-budget comparison. A correct presentation would (a) report both bounds at `B = 1` directly (factor `~2`), (b) report both at `B = log log N` (factor `~log r`), and (c) explicitly state the dependence on `B`.

## 3. Floyd-Steinberg dithering is the missing 1-bit halftoning baseline (Soundness — virgin)

Sec. 4, Experiment 4 (l. 380–392): 3D halftoning of the Stanford Bunny mesh signal. The paper compares MSQ, SDW (Krahmer et al. 2023), and SSNS.

**Floyd-Steinberg dithering (1976, cited as a perceptual reference but not benchmarked) is the canonical 1-bit halftoning method for arbitrary signals.** It propagates quantization error to neighboring samples in a fixed pattern, achieving sub-perceptual-threshold reconstruction at 1 bit/sample on raster grids. For 3D-mesh halftoning, k-NN-graph adaptations of Floyd-Steinberg exist (Mao et al. 2017 cited; Abedini 2023 cited but not benchmarked). The paper's argument that "*only SSNS is supported by provable error bounds*" (l. 383) is a *theoretical-guarantee* argument, not an empirical one. Empirically, Floyd-Steinberg-class methods are likely competitive on the bunny halftoning task and would show that the SSNS bound's tightness is academic.

**A clean controlled comparison**: (i) reconstruct the bunny mesh signal under Floyd-Steinberg (or a graph-FS adaptation), (ii) report `QE_L_r` (the same metric SSNS reports) and a perceptual quality measure (e.g., LPIPS on rasterized projections or graph-spectral distortion), (iii) report wall-clock time. If Floyd-Steinberg matches SSNS's `QE_L_r` while running faster, the SSNS contribution is the *theoretical guarantee* alone, not the empirical halftoning quality.

## 4. Theorem 3.1's μ-dependence is not numerically validated (sharpening)

Sec. 4 Experiment 1 reports `QE/||f||_2` vs. `r` for 6 graph topologies (Fig. 2). Theorem 3.1's bound is `C · 2^{-B} · μ(X_r) · r/√N`. The dashed theoretical-bound curves in Fig. 4 are computed for fixed `μ` per graph type, but the `μ(X_r)` values for each `r` are not reported. Specifically:

- Ring graph: known to have `μ → 1` (max-Fourier-coherence on regular graphs; Brooks & Lindenstrauss 2013).
- Sensor graph: `μ` depends on geometric clustering — not reported.
- Bunny mesh: `μ` depends on mesh irregularity — not reported.

The "tight" claim for the ring graph (l. 300, "*the worst-case bound is tight for the ring graph*") is internally consistent but cannot be verified by the reader without `μ` values. **A revised Fig. 2 should overlay `μ(X_r)` per graph and verify the bound's `μ`-scaling empirically.**
