The framework's headline contribution is anytime-valid detection via a "test supermartingale", but I could not locate a proof that the cumulative wealth process W_n = Π_{t=1}^n e*(v_t, s_t) is in fact a supermartingale under the sequential null in any of §3, §4, or Appendices A, B, C.

What the appendix establishes is a single-step result: Lemma A.7 shows the optimal e* of Theorem 4.1 satisfies Σ_s p_0(s) e*(v, s) = 1 for every v ∈ V. This is per-step validity *if* the conditional distribution of s_t given F_{t-1} under H_0 equals p_0 and s_t is conditionally independent of v_t given F_{t-1}. Neither condition is stated as an assumption in §3 nor verified for the autoregressive watermark-generation protocol the paper inherits from SEAL.

In standard hash-based watermarking, s_t is a deterministic function of past tokens via a secret key, so under H_0 (human-written text) the conditional law of s_t given F_{t-1} is determined by the protocol — it is not p_0 unless the protocol is explicitly designed to enforce this, and even then conditional independence with v_t requires a separate argument. Could the authors point to where the per-step conditional null E_{H_0}[e*(v_t, s_t) | F_{t-1}] ≤ 1 is established for the seed-generation protocol underlying the experiments?

A related observation: Theorem 4.3 invokes Theorem B.1/B.2, both of which are partial-sum results requiring a positive conditional drift (J_t → J_∞ > 0). These are H_1-side stopping-time results — they do not provide the Type-I error guarantee that the abstract attributes to Ville's inequality. The static optimality of Theorem 4.1 is correct and its closed form is clean, but the bridge from single-step e-value to anytime-valid sequential test seems to be assumed rather than proved. Clarification on where the supermartingale property is verified, or on the precise sequential null model under which it holds, would tighten the central claim considerably.
